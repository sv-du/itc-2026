## About the Program

Use the art of performance as a powerful tool for social change, personal growth and community building. Discover how to craft stories that engage, inspire and challenge audiences. Whether facilitating workshops, creating educational theatre or leading community-based projects, you’ll gain the skills to connect with people, spark dialogue, and empower others through the transformative power of theatre.

## The Polytechnic Approach

See the spark of inspiration ignite in the artists of tomorrow. Go beyond performance to explore theatre as a tool for learning, social change and community connection. Whether working with students in secondary schools or collaborating with local theatre organizations, you’ll gain hands-on experience in creating and leading educational programs that inspire and empower diverse audiences. Through workshops and practical teaching opportunities, you’ll develop the skills to create meaningful, inclusive experiences that foster creativity, critical thinking and collaboration. With a foundation in both the arts and the practice of teaching, you’ll be equipped to shape the next generation and engage communities through the transformative power of performance.

### Community Engagement Coordinator

Organize workshops, outreach programs and events that bring theatre into underserved or diverse communities.

### Theatre Workshop Facilitator

Integrate drama and role-playing as tools for conflict resolution, team building and empowerment.

### Arts Program Director

Manage and oversee theatre or arts programs for schools, museums, cultural centers or community organizations.

### Civic Engagement Advocate

Partner with local governments, school districts, or non-profits to influence decision-making on the funding and implementation of arts programs in schools and communities.

## Courses

The Theatre in Education and Community Engagement option is for those who are passionate about using theatre as a tool for education and social change. This track prepares you to teach theatre in secondary schools or to work with community-based theatre organizations. The curriculum emphasizes the development of pedagogical skills, as well as the ability to create and implement theatre projects that engage and empower diverse communities. You will gain practical experience through workshops, student-driven projects, and participation in mainstage productions. You will be equipped to pursue careers in educational theatre, community engagement, or further study in theatre education.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

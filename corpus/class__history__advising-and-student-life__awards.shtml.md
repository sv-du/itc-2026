## Celebrating Success

The History Department gives six annual undergraduate awards, and one graduate award, to recognize outstanding achievements. Click on the boxes below to learn about the different awards and award winners! (Pictured: Tuere Storks (MA '24, Ibrahim Award Winner) and Dr. Michael Slaughter)

![Tuere Storks and Michael Slaughter](../img/storks-slaughter-2.jpeg)

## Awards and Awardees

[### Pemstein Award

The Walter Pemstein Award is given to the graduating senior(s) with the highest CPP in their cohort.](#modal-0 "Pemstein Award")

### Pemstein Award

×

**Type of Award**

Outstanding Senior

**Requirements**

History major; Senior standing; graduated in fall or spring of same academic year; highest CPP GPA in cohort; must graduate no later than spring of same academic year as award

**Decided By:**

All History Department tenured and tenure track faculty attending the meeting for this purpose

[### Brundage Award

The Brundage Award is given to the student who has written the most outstanding senior thesis of the year.](#modal-2 "Brundage Award")

### Brundage Award

×

**Type of Award**

Senior Thesis

**Requirements**

History major; best completed senior thesis in academic year; nominated by faculty who have taught senior thesis in academic year

**Decided By**

Selection committee consisting of tenured and tenure track History faculty who have not taught senior thesis in academic year of award

**Past Recipients**

* 2024-25: TBA
* 2023-24: Bryce Malally, "From Picket Line to Political Forefront: Elizabeth Gurley Flynn's Dual Legacy"
* 2022-23: My T. Quach, "From Saigon to Chinatown: A Historical Study of the Chinese-Vietnamese American Community in Southern California"
* 2021-22: Anthony Clark, "Coercive Volunteerism: The Government, Women and the Nationalist Drive for Food Production in California during World War I"
* 2020-21: Nicholas Alanis, "Dismantling Chinese Exclusion: Chinese Diplomats, the American Republics, and the Fight against Discrimination in the 1930s and 1940s"
* 2019-20: Destinee Sparks, “Reclaiming My Time:  Slave Compensation Suits in Virginia, Missouri, and Louisiana, 1776-1860”
* 2018-19: Kristin Kosareff, "'To Combine the Old with the New': Molokan Women in America, 1904-2004" AND Danielle Tate, “How Alexander the Great used Persian Culture to Elevate the Kingship”
* 2017-18: Patricia O'Leary, "Fontucky: Making Steel & Race in Kaiser's Fontana, 1943-1983"
* 2016-17: Matthew Callebs, "Sailors and the State: Resistance to British Naval Impressment in the Atlantic during the Long Eighteenth Century"
* 2015-16: Isabel Melendez, "The Power of Pawns: An Analysis of Parisian Identity and Loyalty during the Anglo-Burgundian Occupation, 1420-1436"
* 2014-15: Isis Quan, ”The Women of the Kojiki and Nihongi”
* 2013-14: Kevin Laurell, “African Warrior Culture: The Symbolism and Integration of the Avtomat Kalashnikova in Mozambique and Zimbabwe”
* 2012-13: Jonathan Lee, “Back to the Future: the Flying Car & Postwar America”
* 2011-2012: Raquel Robles, “Triumph Over Adversity:  A History of San Francisco’s (Other) Great Bridge”
* 2010-2011: Gabriel Gutierrez

The Sean Yu Award is given to the student who has written the most outstanding paper on a topic related to environmental history during the past academic year.](#modal-3 "Yu Award")

### Yu Award

×

**Type of Award**

Outstanding project in environmental history

**Requirements**

Best essay/project connected to environmental history, produced for a CPP History class during the academic year; nominated by any History faculty

**Decided By**

Selection committee consisting of tenured and tenure track History faculty who did not teach a course with a nominated project

**Past Recipients**

The Ibrahim Award is given to the graduating MA student with the highest GPA in their cohort.](#modal-4 "Ibrahim Award")

### Ibrahim Award

×

**Type of Award**

Outstanding Graduate Student

**Requirements**

All History Department tenured and tenure track faculty attending the meeting for this purpose

* 2017-18: David Dorantes

[### Lloyd Award (historic prize)

The Lloyd Award was given for the best historiography paper submitted for HST 300/3300.](#modal-5 "Lloyd Award")

### Lloyd Award

×

**Type of Award**

Historiography

**Requirements**

History major; best historiographical paper produced in HST 300/3300 in academic year; nominated by faculty who have taught HST 300/3300 in academic year. Awarded in Fall.

**Decided By**

Selection committee of tenured and tenure track History faculty who have not taught HST 300/3300 in academic year of award

**Past Recipients**

* 2023-24: Kelsey Hughes, "Working Women, Labor Reform and the Triangle Shirtwaist Fire of 1911: A Historiographical Study"
* 2022-23: Sawsana Beshai, "The Study of Medicine by and for Women in Ancient Egypt"
* 2021-22: My T. Quach, "The Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882"
* 2020-21: Nayely Alcantara Vargas, "Malintzin, Myth, and Woman: A Historiographical Study"
* 2019-20: Jennifer Escobedo Sigala, “Trujillo: Understanding The Dictator’s Rise to Power and Methods of Control"
* 2018-19: Destinee Sparks, "African Americans in Baseball:  A Historiography"
* 2017-18: Robert Salcido, "Entertainment and Culture:  the Business of the Walt Disney Company"
* 2016-17: Richard Arguelles, "Historiography on the Paris Commune"
* 2015-16: Gregory Paguaga, "A Historiography on the Spanish Inquisition"
* 2014-15: David Dorantes , “His Truth is Marching On: A Historiography of John Brown”
* 2013-14: Jocelyn Murillo, “Parallels in Feminist Movements in Nicaragua and Beyond: A Historiographical Study.”
* 2012-13: Gina Risetter
* 2011-12: Joann Conger, “The Fall of Anne Boleyn: A Historiographical Study”

Close

[### Digital History Award

The Digital History Award is given to the student who has produced the best digital history project in one of the Digital Research Project classes (HST 4494, 4495, 4496, or 4497.](#modal-6 "Digital History Award")

### Digital History Award

×

**Type of Award**

Digital History project

**Requirements**

History major; best digital history project produced in HST 4494/4495/4496/4497 in academic year of the award

**Decided By**

Selection committee of tenured and tenure track History faculty

**Past Recipients**

* 2022-23: Jared Buck, "[Country Music's African Origins](https://storymaps.arcgis.com/stories/3628d13473f44e979a68e39c61bb2a46 "Buck project")"
* 2021-22:
  + Jacob McConnell, "[The Wearing of the Green: The evolution of the Irish-American Identity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iYnfTerj8I "McConnell Youtube link")"

# Supplemental Instruction

**What is SI?**

Supplemental Instruction was created in 1973 at the [University of Missouri, Kansas City](https://info.umkc.edu/si/) by Dr. Deanna Martin. It is a non-remedial approach to learning that supports students toward academic success by integrating “what to learn” with “how to learn.” SI consists of regularly scheduled, out-of-class group study sessions driven by students’ needs. Sessions are facilitated by trained peer leaders (SI Leaders) who utilize collaborative activities to ensure peer-to-peer interaction in small groups. SI is implemented in high-risk courses in consultation with academic departments and is supported and evaluated by trained LRC supervisors. SI is voluntary and is offered to all enrolled students in the targeted course.

**[What is SI video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qL_7_jbBGdI&ab_channel=UMKC "link to UMKC YouTube video describing SI")**

**Goals of SI:**

* Improve learning, while increasing retention within targeted historically difficult courses
* Improve student performance in targeted historically difficult courses
* Increase retention and graduation rates of students

**SI Works!**

Validated by the U.S. Department of Education in 1992; reaffirmed in Dawson, et al. in 2014:

* SI participants receive a lower rate of D, F, and Withdrawals.
* SI participants earn higher mean final course grades.
* SI participants persist (reenroll and graduate) at higher rates.

### Faculty Requests

The LRC can provide content and exam review workshops, as well as Supplemental Instruction, to provide further support to your students.

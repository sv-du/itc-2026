# Smartphone Security Education

## About

**The smartphone market has grown rapidly in recent years** while the traditional desktop PC market has remained relatively stagnant. Ubiquitous network connectivity and the ever growing number of apps available facilitates the adoption of this new technology. However, **as the popularity grows, so does the security risk.** Although this market is growing rapidly, education remains mostly in the realm of traditional computer designs, even when it comes to security.

**Many students graduate with Computer Science (CS) or related degrees without any experience in smartphone security** at the operating system or application level. This is **greatly due to the difficulty in finding platform and resources for smartphone security education.**

We discuss the development of a smartphone security education platform, sample educational resources (labs) that can be supported by the platform, and **evaluate the efficacy of the developed platform and resources in promoting experiential learning** in the domain of smartphone security education. The proposed platform is a hardware-software integrated system that will **enable students to remotely connect to the platform**, choose a smartphone security lab exercise supported by the platform and **learn by performing the lab activities on real smartphone devices.** The platform and resources will provide the students with the experiential learning opportunity through a hands-on experience that can inspire creative thinking and the motivation for future research.

## Project Details

### Overview

*Android*

Users can connect to Android devices via an Internet dashboard/webpage toperform labs and instrumentation in real-time. Devices have vanilla builds of AOSP that has an embedded custom monitoring application used to facilitate interactions with the controller. Device operations can be monitored and controlled by a user via *websockes* connection.

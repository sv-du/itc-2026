## About Me

Dr. Rachel Baumsteiger is an assistant professor in the psychology department at Cal Poly Pomona. Before coming to CPP, Dr. Baumsteiger earned a B.A. from the University of South Florida, earned an M.A. and Ph.D. from Claremont Graduate University, worked as a postdoctoral associated and then associate research scientist and the Yale Center for Emotional Intelligence, and worked as a lecturer at CPP, CSUN, CSULA, Chaffey College, Harvey Mudd College, and the University of Connecticut. Dr. Baumsteiger is currently directing the [Prosociality and Well-being Lab](https://nam11.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.prosocialityandwellbeinglab.com%2F&data=05%7C02%7Crwilson%40cpp.edu%7Ca1473fc16cae43ee50f408dc5017fcac%7C164ba61e39ec4f5d89ffaa1f00a521b4%7C0%7C0%7C638473311149863964%7CUnknown%7CTWFpbGZsb3d8eyJWIjoiMC4wLjAwMDAiLCJQIjoiV2luMzIiLCJBTiI6Ik1haWwiLCJXVCI6Mn0%3D%7C0%7C%7C%7C&sdata=9Hc%2FVeXErgGFKpF6eEgoyqYjXNl%2B%2FVEMoHJYHkgqqJY%3D&reserved=0), which focuses on understanding and promoting prosocial (helping) behavior and psychological well-being, especially among adolescents and young adults. Dr. Baumsteiger teaches courses in developmental psychology, positive psychology, and moral psychology, as well as courses in qualitative and quantitative research methods.

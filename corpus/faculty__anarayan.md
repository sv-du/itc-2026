# Anjana Narayan

Sociology Professor

Sociology Department, College of Letters, Arts, and Social Sciences

## Email

anarayan@cpp.edu

## Phone number

909-869-3914

## Office location

[5-248](https://www.cpp.edu/maps/?id=1130#!m/276183)

## Office hours

T TH | 9 AM - 11:00 AM

* [About Me](index.shtml)

## Teaching

SW 3322- Family Violence (Fully Asynchronous)

## Research Interests

**Gender, Ethnicity, Immigration, Transnationalism**

## Education

* **Phd. Sociology, University of Connecticut**
* **MSW, Tata Institute of Social Sciences, India**

## Recent Publications

Roy, R., B. Purkayastha, and A. Narayan. 2025. "Gendered Patterns of Counter-dissemination: Hindu Women Navigating Religious Nationalism in the United States." *Sociological Bulletin* . <https://doi.org/10.1177/00380229251349577>.

Narayan, Anjana, and Lise-Helene Smith. 2024. "Diaspora Assistance." Pp. xx–xx in *The Handbook on Humanitarianism and Inequality*, edited by Silke Roth, Bandana Purkayastha, and Tobias Denskus.

Narayan, Anjana, and Erica Morales. 2023. "Centering Intersectionality in Transnational Research."  in *Research Handbook on Intersectionality*, edited by Mary Romero. Cheltenham, UK: Edward Elgar Publishing.

Narayan, Anjana, Bandana Purkayastha, and Koyel Khan. 2021. "Intersectionality." Pp. 143–157 in *The Routledge Handbook of Research Methods in the Study of Religion*, 2nd ed., edited by Steven Engler and Michael Stausberg. Abingdon, UK: Taylor and Francis.

Narayan, Anjana, and Gabriele Plickert. 2021. "Connecting the Unconnected." *Sozial Extra* 45(3):167–173.

Kibria, Nazli, Bandana Purkayastha, Anjana Narayan, Koyel Khan, and Farhan Navid Yousaf. 2021. "Living Religions Across Transnational Spaces: Experiences of South Asian American Hindu and Muslim Women." *Contemporary South Asia* 29(2):243–256. https://doi.org/10.1080/09584935.2021.1915243.

Morales, Erica, Anjana Narayan, and William Atienza. 2019. "Vulnerable Vets? How Gatekeeping and Stereotypes Shape Access to Student-Veterans in the Qualitative Interview Process." *Qualitative Report* 24(4):717–730.

Narayan, Anjana, and Lise-Helene Smith. 2019. "Gender, Religion and Virtual Diasporas." *Economic and Political Weekly* 54:77–85.

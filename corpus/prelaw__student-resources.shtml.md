### Judicial Internship

Get firsthand experience in the legal system through the Cal Poly Pomona Judicial Internship. You’ll observe court twice a week, meet judges and lawyers, visit places like a forensics lab or court of appeals, and meet biweekly with a professor to reflect on your work.](https://www.cpp.edu/class/political-science/participate/judicial-internship.shtml)

[Card 2 of 3

![girl student in a law lecture hall](img/pre-law-mock-stock.jpg)

### Mock Trial

Join the Bronco Mock Trial Team to build courtroom skills and compete in regional tournaments. You’ll practice trial lawyering, present evidence, question witnesses, and sharpen your arguments. Earn credit in PLS 3080/3080A. Tryouts are held in the summer.](https://www.cppmocktrial.com/tryout)

Card 3 of 3

![student in suits giving a speech](img/pre-law-ethics-stockimage.jpg)

### Ethics Bowl

In Ethics Bowl, you’ll debate real-world ethical issues, defend your views before judges, and compete with other teams. It’s great prep for careers in policy, law, or government. Enroll in PHL 4820 in fall. Interested? Contact Dr. Itzel Garcia in spring.

## PreLaw Curriculum

The PreLaw curriculum helps you build the skills law schools look for—clear thinking, strong writing, and a solid understanding of how legal systems work. You’ll take courses in political science, ethics, constitutional law, and logic, all designed to sharpen your ability to analyze complex issues and make persuasive arguments. Whether you’re aiming for law school or a career that values sharp reasoning and communication, this path gets you ready.

### PreLaw Curriculum Overview

You can explore recommended PreLaw courses across the University in the [course catalog search](https://catalog.cpp.edu/content.php?catoid=69&navoid=5948 "https://catalog.cpp.edu/content.php?catoid=69&navoid=5948").

[School's Stats](https://www.abarequireddisclosures.org/barPassageOutcomes "School's Stats")

Another tool: [Law School Transparency](https://www.lawschooltransparency.com/schools)

To find a list of ABA (American Bar Association) accredited law schools, go to:
[ABA Accredited Schools](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_education/resources/aba_approved_law_schools/ "ABA Accredited Schools")

Some law schools in California are accredited by the State Committee of Bar Examiners. These are not ABA accredited. To find these, go to: [The State Bar of California](https://www.calbar.ca.gov/Admissions/Law-School-Regulation/Law-Schools "The State Bar of California")

Do not attend an unaccredited school!

### Gauging Your Odds

Several websites are now available that will give you a sense of your chances of being admitted to a given law school:

* **[Law School Numbers](https://lawschoolnumbers.com/)** – A community-powered site where applicants share their LSAT, GPA, and outcomes, helping others see how their stats might compare.
* **[MyLSN](https://mylsn.info/)** – A searchable database that visualizes admissions data based on LSAT and GPA, built from user-submitted information.
* **[Top Law Schools](https://top-law-schools.com/)** – A well-known forum and resource hub where applicants and students discuss law school admissions, personal statements, rankings, and more.

### LSAC PreLaw Undergraduate Scholars (PLUS) Programs

Each year, LSAC partners with host law schools to prepare aspiring legal professionals for law school success. Through a unique immersive learning experience, the LSAC PLUS Program offers selected participants a window into what law school is really like while providing supportive insights about the law school enrollment journey.

The PLUS Program is specifically aimed at undergraduate students and prospective law school applicants one year away from applying who are from minoritized groups that are underrepresented in the legal profession. There is no cost to apply or to attend the program if selected. All PLUS participants who successfully complete the program will receive a $1,000 stipend and an LSAC fee waiver.

[Click here for more information, host school deadlines, and application](https://www.lsac.org/discover-law/diversity-law-school/prelaw-undergraduate-scholars-plus-programs#msdynttrid=x-JAh7kRrvr9TnxucCpGHgJn3zQsPWTcAMw1PdFD6m0).

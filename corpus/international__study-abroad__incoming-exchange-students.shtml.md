# Incoming Exchange Students

## J-1 Incoming Exchange Student Admissions

Expand/Collapse All J-1 Incoming Exchange Student Admissions

### Plan

1. Only students from our exchange partner universities can participate in the exchange program. Our current exchange partners are:

* France: Paris School of Business
* Germany: Hochschule Biberach
* Germany: Leibniz University Hannover
* Spain: Public University of Navarre
* South Korea: Dankook University
* South Korea: University of Ulsan
* Taiwan: National Taiwan University of Science and Technology
* Taiwan: National Yunlin University of Science and Technology
* UK: Strathclyde University, Scotland

     2. Check eligibility and requirements with the exchange program coordinator at your university.  Have your coordinator nominate you to Cal Poly Pomona by the exchange deadline:
                Fall: March 15
                Spring: September 15

### Requirements from Cal Poly Pomona: (All documents in English)

1. Copy of Passport page showing your photo, full legal name, and place of birth
2. Proof of Financial Support - A current bank letter or statement in English must accompany your application showing at least $10,000 U.S. dollars on deposit for each semester (term) you will attend Cal Poly Pomona.
3. Official Transcripts (in English)
4. Passport Size Photo (Size 2 inches x 2 inches)
5. Official TOEFL Score
   * All students must include proof of English language proficiency by submitting a recent computer-based TOEFL test score of at least 71 internet-based, 197 computer-based, or 527-530 paper-based.
   * Students from the following countries are exempt: Australia, Canada, Germany, New Zealand, and the U.K. More information about the TOEFL test can be found at [http://www.ets.org/toefl/](http://www.ets.org/toefl/ "http://www.ets.org/toefl/")

### Submit

* After nomination, CPP will invite you to apply via a private application link.
* Acceptance and offer Letter will be issued to accepted students along with DS-2019 document.
* Student applies for J-1 student exchange visa in home country.

## After Being Accepted

Expand/Collapse All After Being Accepted

### Pre-departure

* We will contact you to make the necessary arrangements for your study here at Cal Poly Pomona.
* Exchange students normally choose to stay at our Village Apartments due to its close proximity to campus.  The Village Apartments holds some spaces specifically for our exchange students, however you are also welcome to find your own accommodations, but please keep in mind the distance and travel times to campus. Please apply for housing ONLY after receiving your acceptance letter and student identification number.
* Health Insurance Coverage. You must have health insurance coverage for your entire length of stay in the United States.  Further information about insurance will be provided after acceptance.
  + Students are required to purchase an approved policy through the Cal Poly Pomona International Center upon arrival at a competitive premium of approximately $650 per semester (this fee is subject to change)
* How to Purchase health insurance with Cal Poly Pomona. {More information coming soon.}

### Post-Arrival

* Bring the following to the International Center, Building 1, Room 104 once you arrive:
  + Passport
  + DS 2019
  + [J-1 Exchange Student Information Form](https://www.cpp.edu/international/docs/J-1_Exchange_Visitor_Info.pdf) ![PDF](img/icon_pdf.gif)
* Attend exchange student orientation (Dates are TBA)

## J-1 Incoming Exchange Student Estimated Expenses

Expand/Collapse All J-1 Incoming Exchange Student Estimated Expenses

### Estimated Cost for a Semester (4 months)

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  | **Living on -Campus** |
| Tuition and fees\* (Fees only, tuition is waived for exchange partners) | $850 |
| Books, course materials, supplies, and equipment | $450 |
| Living expenses, including food and housing costs | $6000 |
| Miscellaneous, personal expenses | $2000 |
| Transportation | $700 |
| TOTAL | **$10,000** |

NOTE: All exchange students are excluded from paying campus tuition.
\*Students on exchange via bilateral exchange agreements directly with Cal Poly Pomona are only responsible for the Student Success Fee. All other exchange students are responsible for paying all campus fees. Please see the term schedule of fees **[here.](https://www.cpp.edu/student-accounting/tuition/undergraduate-tuition-and-fees.shtml)**

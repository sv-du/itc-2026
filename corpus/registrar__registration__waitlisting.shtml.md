# Waitlisting

## Waitlisting for Classes

Cal Poly Pomona allows waitlisting for most classes. Waitlisting lets you “get in line” for a seat in a class that’s currently full. Waitlists are processed daily while registration is open and remain active through the last day of the add/drop period.

**Important:**

* Unit limits & waitlisting (Fall/Spring):
  + During priority & general registration: enroll up to 16 units and waitlist up to 10 units.
  + During the add/drop period: enroll up to 18 units and waitlist up to 10 units.
  + Waitlist units do not count toward your total unit limit.
* Unit limits & waitlisting (Summer/Winter)
  + Summer: may enroll up to 12 units (waitlist up to 10 units).
  + Winter: may enroll up to 6 units (waitlist up to 4 units).
* After add/drop ends, all remaining waitlisted courses are purged
* Once your schedule is finalized, drop any waitlisted courses to avoid accidental registration or charges if a seat opens.

**How Waitlisting Works**

1. Join the list: During registration, if a class is full, you’ll be offered the option to add yourself to its waitlist if applicable.
2. Get confirmation & position: You’ll be notified if you were successfully added and can see your waitlist position in [BroncoDirect](https://www.cpp.edu/~broncodirect/) → Student Schedule.
3. Manage your list: You may remove yourself from a waitlist anytime while registration is open.
4. Automatic enrollment when seats open: When waitlists are processed, students move from waitlisted to enrolled if all of the following are true:
   * You meet all prerequisites.
   * There are no co-requisites required for the course you’re waitlisted for.
   * There is no time conflict with your current schedule.
   * You are not already enrolled in another section of the same class.
5. Multiple waitlists: If you are waitlisted in multiple sections of the same course and you become enrolled in one section, your remaining waitlisted sections will be automatically dropped.

**Tips & Reminders**

* Keep an eye on your BroncoDirect and CPP email—if you’re auto-enrolled, tuition/fee deadlines and add/drop rules apply.
* If you’ve created your ideal schedule, remove any waitlisted classes to prevent surprise changes.
* After add/drop ends, any classes still on your waitlist will be automatically removed (purged).

**New Feature: Optimize Your Waitlist Experience!**

We're excited to introduce a new BroncoDirect feature, Waitlist Wizard. If you opt in to using the Waitlist Wizard, BroncoDirect will attempt to enroll you in any other compatible section of the course you are waitlisted for -- helping you get the classes you need faster.

 How It Works:

* When you opt in, the Waitlist Wizard will look for **any open section of the course** that fits your schedule.
* You may opt out at any time **until you are successfully enrolled in a section**. Once you are enrolled, you are no longer on the waitlist, and your opt-in ends automatically.
* The process runs **frequently throughout the day (about every hour, on the hour)**. Runs may be delayed or skipped during scheduled system maintenance or unexpected outages.
* Periodically, the system will automatically search for open sections that fit your schedule and attempt to enroll you.
* If you don’t meet requisites, have time conflicts, or have a registration hold, you will not be enrolled and will remain on the waitlist.
* If you are already enrolled in one section of the course, you will not be enrolled in another (since you cannot take multiple sections of the same course).
* If the Wizard successfully enrolls you into an open section, your original waitlisted section will be **automatically dropped.**
* If you no longer wish to opt-in after being enrolled into an open section, you will not be automatically added back into the originally waitlisted course. You will need to manually drop the new course, and re-add the old, waitlisted course. You will also lose your waitlist position number of the old course.
* If you're already enrolled in any section of the course, you will not be enrolled in another one since you can't be enrolled in multiple sections of the same course.
* You remain responsible for checking your schedule and making any schedule adjustments by published general registration and Add/Drop deadlines. You can review the [Academic Calendar](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/calendar/index.shtml) for deadlines.

 Instructions to Opt-In:

* Log in to [MyCPP](https://my.cpp.edu/)
* Open BroncoDirect Student Center - **Classes & Registration**.
* Select **Waitlist Wizard**.
* Check the **Opt In** box of the courses you want to include.
* Click **Save** to confirm.
* Need help? [**Watch the step-by-step GIF tutorial**](../img/waitlist-wizard-gif.gif)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many units may I waitlist?

Students may waitlist for each term as follows:

* **Fall** and **Spring** term waitlist unit limit up to **10** semester units.
* **Winter** term waitlist unit limit up to **4** semester units.
* **Summer** term waitlist unit limit up to **10** semester units.

Note: Waitlist units are not included in the total unit limit

### Can you waitlist for two sections of the same class, or waitlist for another section of the same class in which you are already enrolled?

Yes, however a student will not be enrolled from the waitlist if they are already enrolled in another section of the same class.

In addition, if you are waitlisted for one section, and later enrolled in another section of the same class, you will be automatically dropped from the waitlisted section.

### Do the classes I waitlist for count towards my total unit cap?

No, waitlisted classes do not count towards a student’s total enrollment.

### What are my chances of getting a seat in a class for which I am waitlisted?

This depends on a number of factors, most notably how many students drop the course for which you are waitlisted.

### I am trying to waitlist for a class and unable to , why is this?

There are several reasons a student may not be able to waitlist for a class. Two common reasons a student may be unable to waitlist for a class are: 1. the class has a co-requisite and 2. The student has not met a prerequisite for a class.

### If I have finalized my schedule, do I need to remove myself from the waitlist?

It is strongly recommended that a student drop waitlisted classes, once the student's schedule is finalized. If a space opens up in a class, and you have not reached your enrollment limit, you will be enrolled in the class, and responsible for completing and paying any associated fees relevant to it.

### Will I be able to waitlist for a class that has a co-requisite that I have already taken and passed?

You will need to get a permission number from your department to waitlist a class in this scenario.

### Waitlist Wizard - Student FAQs

**General Questions**

1. **What is the Waitlist Wizard?**
    The Waitlist Wizard is a tool that helps manage class waitlists. It checks for open seats in classes and, if eligible, moves you into the class automatically.
2. **Do I have to use the Waitlist Wizard?**
    No. You can choose to opt-in. If you don’t, you’ll remain on the waitlist as usual and will need to monitor enrollment yourself.
3. **How do I opt-in to the Waitlist Wizard?**
    You’ll see an option on your student self-service page. You can opt-in for all eligible courses or select specific courses.
4. **Can I change my mind after opting in?**
    Yes. You can return to the self-service page at any time to update or remove your opt-in selections.

**Eligibility & Enrollment**

5. **Will my waitlisted section be dropped after I am enrolled in an open section through the Waitlist Wizard?**
    Yes. Once the **Waitlist Wizard** successfully enrolls you into an open section of the course, your **waitlist status for the original section will be dropped**. This prevents you from holding multiple seats in the same course.
6. **Am I guaranteed a spot in the class if I use the Waitlist Wizard?**
    No. The Waitlist Wizard only moves you into a class if a seat opens up and you meet all registration and enrollment requirements.
7. **What if there’s a conflict (e.g., time overlap, max units, prerequisites)?**
    The Wizard cannot override rules. If a seat opens but you don’t meet requirements, you won’t be moved into the class.
8. **Will I lose my place on the waitlist if I don’t opt-in?**
    No. You’ll stay on the waitlist as usual. The Wizard just gives you an automated option if you opt-in.
9. **How does the Waitlist Wizard decide who gets the spot?**
    It follows the standard waitlist order (first-come, first-served, based on your position in line).

**Communication & Notifications**

10. **How will I know if I’ve been enrolled in a class?**
     You’ll receive an email communication and you can always check your schedule in the Student Center.
11. **What if the Wizard tries to enroll me but fails?**
     If the Wizard cannot add you due to restrictions (such as time conflicts or unit limits), you’ll remain on the waitlist.

**Special Situations**

12. **Can I opt-in to just one class instead of all of them?**
     Yes. You can choose specific courses or opt-in to all eligible courses.
13. **Does opting-in cost anything or affect my tuition?**
     No extra cost. Tuition is adjusted normally based on your final enrollment.
14. **What happens if I manually enroll in a class I opted into with the Wizard?**
     The Wizard will see that you’re already enrolled and won’t try to add you again.
15. **What if I’m waitlisted for multiple sections of the same course?**
     You’ll need to decide which section(s) you want to opt-in for. The Wizard only acts on the selections you make.
16. **Can I see the status of my opt-in choices?**
     Yes. Your self-service page will show which classes you’ve opted-in for.

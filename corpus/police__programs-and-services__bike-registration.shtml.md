* Tips

## Safety Tips

![](bike-rack-logo.png)

### Use Designated Bike Racks

Avoid theft by utilizing permanent, fixed anchored bike racks located around campus. Refrain from securing your bike to small trees, handrails, posts, or chain-link fences. You can find a map of designated bike rack locations [here](https://www.cpp.edu/maps/?id=1130#!ce/67378?ct/25103,25111,67378,67379,67380,67399,67402,67403,67404,67405,67406,68310?s/?mc/34.0523827388059,-117.81447426634276?z/16?lvl/0?share)

![](lock-logo.png)

### Always Lock Your Bike

Whether you are storing your bike in a garage, residence hall room, apartment, or briefly leaving it unattended, utilizing a lock is imperative.

![](u-lock-logo.png)

### Choose a U-Shaped High-Security Lock

Opt for a sturdy U-lock for enhanced protection, as lightweight cable or chain locks may not provide sufficient security and can be easily cut. For added security, consider using both types of locks.

![](bike-lock-logo.png)

### Properly Use the Lock

Maximize security by positioning your bike frame and wheels to occupy as much open space within the U-portion of the lock as possible. A tighter lock-up makes it more challenging for thieves to use a pry bar and open the lock.

![](helmet-logo.png)

### Secure Components and Accessories

Pay special attention to components that can be easily removed, such as quick-release wheels or seats. Bike shops offer security devices for added protection.

* Registering

**Bicycle Registration:**

**Registering your bicycle at Cal Poly Pomona is a crucial step in safeguarding your bike and deterring theft.**

![](shield-logo.png)

## Deterrent to Theft

Having your bike's serial number on file makes it more challenging for thieves to resell.

![](ownership-logo.png)

## Proof of Ownership

In case you forget your lock combination or lose your key, having your bike registered serves as proof of ownership when requesting assistance

![](recover-logo.png)

## Recovery if Stolen

In the unfortunate event of loss or theft, registered bikes have a higher likelihood of being returned if located.

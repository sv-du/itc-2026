## About the Program

Pursue traditional questions about the nature of truth, knowledge, justice and reality while examining the relevance of these questions to modern problems. In grappling with these questions, our program equips you with skills in high demand with employers: communication, critical thinking, assessing pros and cons, distilling complex data, and more.
The Philosophy program offers two options to serve your academic and career goals: General Philosophy and Law and Society.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Ask big questions about existence, collaborate on projects that apply philosophical ideas to fields like tech or medicine, debate hot-button ethical issues, engage in co-curricular learning activities, or analyze real-world case studies in business and law. Our programs prepare you to thrive in a fast-changing world, and you will graduate with highly adaptable, transferable skills.

## Program Emphasis

Card 1 of 3

### General Philosophy

Dive deep into critical and synthetic thinking. You’ll learn how to dissect complex ideas, connect the dots across disciplines, and develop well-rounded perspectives—skills that are invaluable in fields like science, cognitive studies or media.

Card 2 of 3

### Law and Society

Combine philosophical rigor and real-world problem-solving with a focus on ethical, social and political challenges. This path preps you for careers in law, urban planning or public policy by teaching you to analyze legal concepts and tackle societal problems with reason and compassion.

## Courses

The Philosophy Department has organized its programs to connect the traditional concerns of philosophy with the directions and needs of contemporary society. Philosophy instruction enhances skills which are crucial for success in a wide range of professions, in particular critical thinking skills (analytical and reasoning skills) and verbal skills (skills in writing and oral presentation).

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

## Department of Philosophy

### Philosophy Club

The Philosophy Club brings philosophy students together to share ideas, meet professors and engage in lively debates.
It’s a space for students from all majors to dive into philosophical discussions, whether you want to speak up or just listen. Join us for thought-provoking conversations and the chance to connect with fellow thinkers!

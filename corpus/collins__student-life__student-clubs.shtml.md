# Student Clubs and Organizations

## Connect Beyond the Classroom

Get to know your peers, meet Collins alumni and other hospitality professionals and gain leadership experience by getting involved in one or more of our student organizations. You are encouraged to join at least one to start making connections in the industry and have some fun outside the classroom.

## Explore Clubs

[### Hospitality Management Council

Oversee student organizations at Collins and act as liaison between Collins and ASI.](#modal-0 "HMC")

### HMC

×

The Hospitality Management Council (HMC) is the central representative body for student organizations within Collins and liaison between them and the university. Its mission is to promote unity and connection through the college. HMC oversees Collins clubs’ activities, ensures compliance with campus requirements, coordinates funding and works closely with the Bronco Leadership Center.

Close

[### SHFM

Connect with the innovative world of workplace hospitality such as company cafeterias, on-site gyms and more.](#modal-1 "SHFM")

### SHFM

×

The Society for Hospitality and Foodservice Management (SHFM) educates students about the Workplace Amenities and Foodservice industry – an area that creates workplace amenities that foster employee retention and greater work-life balance. Students have access to:

* Chapter meetings with topics ranging from “a day in the life” to a strategic outlook for the industry
* SHFM National Conference
* Research on industry trends
* Podcasts
* Live Q&A sessions with industry leaders
* Networking opportunities
* Webinars and virtual information series

Close

[### Club Mgmt Assn of America

It’s a club for the club industry! Explore club management with a side of fun field trips.](#modal-2 "CMAA")

### CMAA

×

Students are introduced to the club industry with opportunities for professional interaction, assistance in finding jobs, attending conferences, taking club tours and volunteering at Collins College events. Along with general meetings, CMAA members attend activities like the Magic Castle Tour, Bowling Social, Pacific Club Tour, Riviera Country Club Tour and more!

**Email**: calpolycmaa@live.org

Close

[### Eta Sigma Delta

Eta Sigma Delta is an international hospitality management honor society.](#modal-3 "ESD")

### ESD

×

Eta Sigma Delta (ESD) recognizes hospitality students for outstanding academic achievement, exemplary service and demonstrated professionalism, distinguishing them in the eyes of educators, recruiters and industry executives. Membership with ESD is by invitation only.

Events and activities include meetings, socials, Midterm Madness, study sessions and volunteer opportunities at Collins College events.

ESD students are student members of the International Council on Hotel, Restaurant, and Institutional Education (ICHRIE) until the December after they graduate from their program. They will then be eligible to apply for full ICHRIE membership.

**Email**: esd.cpp@gmail.com

Close

[### NSMH

Open to all students, the National Society for Minorities in Hospitality (NSMH) fosters diversity and support for underrepresented students in hospitality.](#modal-4 "NSMH")

### NSMH

×

The National Society for Minorities in Hospitality's (NSMH) purpose is to educate, encourage and empower underrepresented students interested in pursuing hospitality careers.

The club assists its members with the recruitment, retention, support and advancement of underrepresented students in the hospitality industry to encourage professional development and passion for all segments.

Members participate in general meetings, E-board meetings, regional and national conferences and volunteer at Collins College events, socials and fundraisers.

National Membership Fee: $25 (required)

Close

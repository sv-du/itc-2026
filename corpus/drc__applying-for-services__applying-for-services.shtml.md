# DRC Registration Process

## Registration Steps

### Step 1: Submit an Application

To begin this process, navigate to [myDRC](https://cascade.accessiblelearning.com/CPP/ApplicationStudent.aspx) to complete a **New Student Application**. You'll be required to log-in with your Bronco username and password. If possible, please upload your disability documentation\* when submitting your application.

**\*IMPORTANT:** Please note that you should not delay meeting with us out of concern for not having disability documentation. As needed, we will discuss specific documentation needs during your Intake appointment. Our first priority is meeting with you, not reviewing the paperwork.

Please review our [General Guidelines for Disability Documentation (DOCX)](../documents/general-documentation-guidelines-may20.docx) for more information about documentation.

### Step 2: Schedule an Appointment

After you submit an application for DRC services, an email will be sent to your CPP email to schedule an Intake Meeting appointment. The purpose of the Intake Meeting is to learn first-hand your functional limitations and accommodation needs. Students can meet with their Access Specialist either in person or via Zoom. It is a requirement to have your Zoom camera on for virtual meetings.

### Step 3: Intake Appointment

The Intake appointment is a critical step toward determining eligibility.

During the appointment, a DRC Access Specialist will chat with you about the barriers you are experiencing, reasonable accommodations to address the barriers, and your next steps.

Intake appointments for incoming freshmen and transfer students for Fall semester will not beginning until late May or early June prior to the starting term. Incoming students are encouraged to apply once they have accepted admission to Cal Poly Pomona and the DRC will keep their application available until intakes begin.

### Step 4: DRC Orientation

After a student's intake appointment, a DRC Orientation is scheduled with a Bronco Access Mentor or DRC staff member to review with the student on how to use their accommodations.

### Step 5: Request your Accommodations

The first step to using accommodations **each term** is to submit accommodation **requests for specific classes.** Accommodations do not go into effect until they have been **requested on the myDRC portal** and the **Faculty Notification Letter** has been sent to the instructor.

## New Student Frequently Asked Questions

### I need help filling out my application, what can I do?

Students call or visit the office for assistance completing the application, or read our [How to Apply Tutorial](https://sway.cloud.microsoft/hqh0aIB5ftial5cC?ref=Link).

### When can I register with the DRC?

Students can start the process to apply for DRC once they receive a Bronco ID number and CPP login credentials. Please review the application steps for details.

### I just applied for DRC Services, what should I do next?

Once DRC has reviewed your application, you will receive an email to your CPP Outlook to submit your availability for an intake appointment. Please wait until you receive this email to schedule your intake appointment. Incoming Fall students will be contacted in late May, early June to schedule intake during the summer. Incoming Spring students will be contacted in January to schedule intake during the month of January.

### I haven't accepted my offer to CPP, can I still apply for DRC Services?

If you have received your Bronco ID number and CPP login credentials, you can submit a DRC application.

### Do I need to re-apply with DRC every year?

Students are not required to re-apply every year if you qualify for accommodations on a permanent basis. If your eligibility is determined to be temporary, due to a temporary disability or due to insufficient documentation you will need to discuss how to continue eligibility with our staff. However, students must submit accommodation requests at the beginning of every semester for each of their classes. Once accommodation requests have been submitted, the DRC will notify professors of student accommodations via CPP outlook.

### I received accommodations in the past from another school. Will I receive the same accommodations at CPP?

Accommodations do not transfer automatically from one school to another. Accommodations are reviewed and determined on a case-by-case basis through an interactive process between the student and a DRC Access Specialist.

### Are there special admissions to CPP for students with disabilities?

No. All students must apply through the same admissions process and meet the admissions criteria. Please contact the Office of Admissions for more information.

### How do I provide my documentation?

Documentation may be provided in 3 ways:

* Submitting the documentation while completing the online application
* Upload through the link provided in the DRC email when your application has been reviewed
* Bring a copy to the DRC Office

### What kind of documentation do I need to submit to be approved for accommodations?

Please provide any available documentation that indicates a history of disability diagnosis and/or functional limitations. For more information regarding the requirements for disability documentation, please check out our [documentation guidelines website](https://www.cpp.edu/drc/applying-for-services/disability-documentation.shtml).

### Are intake appointments done only in-person?

Intake appointments may be done in-person or online via Zoom. To ensure a successful meeting, cameras are required for all Zoom meetings.

### Do I receive priority registration as a DRC Student?

DRC students are not automatically granted priority registration. Students may request priority registration as an accommodation. To be eligible, the student must be registered with the DRC and have a disability-related need which meets one or more of the following criteria:

* The student’s approved accommodations require significant time for DRC to arrange (interpreting, alternate format conversion, etc.); and/or
* The student has extraordinary class scheduling needs due to health restrictions and/or medical treatment (such as chemotherapy, renal dialysis, etc.).

### I am on the waitlist for housing, can DRC help me?

The Disability Resource Center (DRC) works in collaboration with University Housing Services (UHS) and Cal Poly Pomona Enterprises (CPPE) Student Housing to coordinate appropriate accommodations for students with documented disabilities or health conditions. Please note, the DRC reviews housing requests and approves accommodations as needed. DRC Housing accommodations are considered on a case-by-case basis and are determined by the nature of the student’s disability and the functional impact it has within the campus living environment. The DRC does not have the authority to prioritize students on housing waitlists. Approval of housing accommodations does not guarantee housing placement and is contingent upon a student’s standing with University Housing Services and/or CPPE Student Housing.

Students requesting housing accommodations must:

* Follow standard housing application and contracting procedures,
* Submit appropriate documentation to the DRC,
* Be approved by the DRC for a specific housing accommodation.

Because on-campus housing is in high demand, the DRC strongly encourages students to submit their housing applications as early as possible.

If you are unable to secure on-campus housing, you may consider nearby CPPE Student Housing options such as [University Village](https://cppvillage.com/) or [The Current in Pomona](https://thecurrentpomona.com/). We encourage you to explore these communities early, as availability may be limited closer to the start of the semester/academic year. Please note the [Bronco Express Shuttle](https://www.cpp.edu/transportation/commuting-to-campus/bronco-shuttle.shtml) does stop at both of these CPPE Student Housing locations.

For details about housing applications and procedures, please visit the [University Housing Services](https://www.cpp.edu/housing/), [University Village](https://cppvillage.com/)  and [The Current](https://thecurrentpomona.com/) websites.

### I am a current CPP student, how long does it take to receive accommodations after submitting the application?

Once the application is submitted, it takes about 1-2 weeks to complete the 3-step DRC registration process. Students must attend an intake appointment before accommodation eligibility is determined.

### I've been away from CPP and I'm ready to re-enroll. How do I re-establish eligibility for accommodations?

Please email drc@cpp.edu to reactivate your DRC profile. Depending on the length of absence, a new intake appointment may be required to discuss accommodation eligibility.

### What will happen during my intake appointment?

The intake appointment is a critical step in the interactive process between the DRC and the student. The nature of the student’s functional limitations will be discussed and eligibility for academic adjustments, auxiliary aids, and/or services will be determined based on the available information. You may bring a member of your support team with you; we will ask for your permission for them to attend before the meeting begins.

### How do my professors know I have accommodations?

Each term, the DRC student must request access to their accommodations using the myDRC portal. DRC will email a Notification of Accommodation letter to each instructor. The responsibility of providing academic accommodations does not go into effect until the email is sent. Services are not retroactive. If notification is presented in the middle of the term, accommodations are required from that point forward.

### Will my professors be notified of my disability/diagnosis?

Professors are not provided with the disability or diagnosis. They are only informed of the accommodations a student selected for their course.

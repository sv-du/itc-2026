## Who I Am

My name is Omar E. Mora, and I’m an Associate Professor in the Civil Engineering Department at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona. I have a background in land surveying and geomatics engineering, with degrees from Cal Poly Pomona (B.S.), Purdue University (M.S.), and The Ohio State University (Ph.D.). I'm also a licensed Professional Land Surveyor in California and hold FAA certification to operate drones commercially.

My research focuses on Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS), photogrammetry, Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR), deep learning, and geospatial solutions for civil, construction, and environmental engineering.

I’m an active member of several professional organizations, including ASPRS, CLSA, NSPS, and ASCE, which help me stay engaged with the latest developments in the field.

I’m an educator shaped by the mentors who believed in me and guided my path. Their influence sparked not only my passion for teaching but also a deep commitment to giving back. I carry their legacy forward by sharing my knowledge, supporting others, and working to inspire the same sense of curiosity, confidence, and purpose in the next generation that my mentors inspired in me.

When I’m not in the classroom, you’ll usually find me hanging out with family and friends or enjoying some sports. I love playing soccer and cheering on my favorite teams like AC Milan, Cruz Azul, and the Columbus Crew. I’m also a big fan of the Los Angeles Lakers, Kings, Dallas Cowboys, Chicago White Sox, and The Ohio State Buckeyes so there’s always a game on my radar!

Besides sports, I enjoy going for walks, staying active with some exercise, gardening, and trying out new foods whenever I can. I’m pretty approachable and always up for a good chat, so don’t be shy. Always open to a friendly hello when you see me.

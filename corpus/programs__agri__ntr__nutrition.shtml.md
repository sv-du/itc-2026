## About the Program

Channel your love of food and nutrition into helping people live healthier lives, making discoveries about diet and disease, or pre-professional training for students who plan to apply for medical school, pharmacy school, or other medical programs. A bachelor's degree in nutrition can lead to graduate school and careers as a registered dietitian, registered dietetic technician, health coach, registered healthcare professional, researcher or scientist, teacher, consumer advocate, and more.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Build a strong foundation in biology and chemistry, then dive into analysis and experimentation with food in our kitchens and labs. Learn basic culinary techniques to create diets that meet different dietary needs, work with industry-standard software, conduct dietary and health assessments, and work in the community to create culturally relevant healthier habits. Through these hands-on courses and service-learning opportunities, you will be well-prepared for graduate studies, dietetic internships or careers in the science, food industry, healthcare and wellness fields.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Clinical Dietitian

Utilize medical nutrition therapy to accurately assess nutritional needs, determine nutritional diagnoses, and create dietary interventions.

### Community of Public Health Dietitian

Focus on improving the health and nutritional well-being of populations by developing, implementing, and evaluating nutrition programs and policies that address public health concerns.

### Wellness Coach

Guide individuals in making sustainable lifestyle changes, addressing nutrition, exercise and mental health to help them achieve their personal wellness goals.

### Sports Nutrition Specialist

Work with athletes and those who love exercise to optimize performance and recovery through personalized nutrition plans tailored to their specific needs.

### Health Educator

Develop and deliver educational programs that teach individuals and communities about nutrition, wellness and disease prevention, empowering them to make healthier choices.

### Nutrition Researcher

Conduct studies exploring the relationship between diet, health and disease, contributing to advancements in nutrition science and influencing public health policies.

### Food Science Manager

Oversee the planning, preparation, and delivery of meals in institutional or commercial settings while ensuring adherence to nutrition guidelines and food safety regulation.

## Program Emphasis

Previous
Next

### Dietetics

Students who complete the Cal Poly Pomona Didactic Program in Dietetics can apply for post-baccalaureate dietetic internships to become registered dietitians (RD) or dietetic technicians, registered (DTR).

### Nutrition and Health

Nutrition and health allows you to learn more about disease prevention, mental and/or physical health, biology, public health, sports medicine or other areas, while health coach allows you to learn more about overall health, such as wellness and the psychological aspects of health.

### Nutritional Science

Promotes an appreciation for the multidiscipline nature of modern nutrition research. Prepares you with a sound scientific understanding of complex nutrition issues related to research, medicine and health, including animal nutrition.

## Courses

The Nutrition program has options in Dietetics, Nutrition Science, and Nutrition and Health. Nutrition Science offers emphases in Animal Nutrition and Pre-Professional. Nutrition and Health offers emphases is Nutrition and Health and Health Coach. Our undergraduate programs prepare students for challenging and rewarding careers in the professional fields of Nutrition and Dietetics, with opportunities in the private sector as well as government agencies. Our programs also provide a strong academic background for graduate study and research.

Our Didactic Program in Dietetics (DPD) is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Education in Nutrition and Dietetics (ACEND).

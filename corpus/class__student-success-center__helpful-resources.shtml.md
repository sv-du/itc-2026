### Personal Wellbeing and Basic Needs

Your social, mental, physical, and emotional health matters. CPP has many resources to help you thrive, and not just academically.

* [Broncos Care Center](https://www.cpp.edu/broncoscarecenter/)

  + "The Care Center is a first-stop location for any student seeking assistance with basic needs and wellbeing services. We seek to normalize resource seeking and create a culture of care where all students have the tools and resources to achieve their personal and academic goals."
* [Bronco Resource Hub](https://www.cpp.edu/orientation/resource-hub.shtml)

  + "Explore what it's like to be on campus and get acquainted with the online tools you'll be using as a CPP student. The Hub is meant to introduce you to the many areas of campus."
* [Self-Services](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/self-services/index.shtml)

  + "You can find many self-service tools on the Cal Poly Pomona website that will help you access important university information, administrative services and business transactions."
* [YOU at CPP](https://www.cpp.edu/healthwellbeing/youatcpp.shtml)

  + "YOU at CPP is an online platform designed to connect you with personalized content on many topics that affect you as a college student - from tips on boosting your immunity against stress to getting a solid grasp on finances and budgeting."

### A-Z Glossary of Academic Terms

Corequisite? Fully asynchronous? Synthesis course?

If you come across a term that you don't understand, check Student Success Central's [A-Z Glossary of Academic Terms](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/a-z.shtml). You can use Command+F on Mac, Control+F on Windows, or check your phone's browser settings to search for a particular term on the page.

Is the term not listed or you still need help understanding it? Send us an email at class-advising@cpp.edu or stop by our drop-in hours.

### Permission Numbers

**Our Center cannot issue permission numbers for any courses.**

The department who offers the course you’re trying to add will give you a permission number if available and only if you meet the prerequisites. You can find contact information by navigating to [the appropriate college and department website](https://www.cpp.edu/academics/colleges-departments/index.shtml). You will make your request to the faculty member (instructor) of the course, if they are listed on BroncoDirect, or the department's staff.

Make sure to provide them with your Bronco ID number and the class/section number of the course you're requesting a permission number for.

### Classes to Take for Your Major

The coursework you are required to complete to earn your degree is called your curriculum. Your curriculum is based on the academic year that you began your current major. You can find this information at the top of your Degree Progress Report (DPR) on BroncoDirect. The Bronco Advising Center has a YouTube tutorial, [Viewing Your DPR and Academic Information](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zXpEbJ3rApg), if you need help navigating to it. All of this information is found on an official document called the curriculum sheet.

* [All Curriculum Sheets & Roadmaps](https://www.cpp.edu/academic-programs/academic-advising/tools/sheets-roadmaps/index.shtml)

  + Select your curriculum year, select College of Letters, Arts, and Social Sciences, and select your major/option to view the curriculum sheet.

You will continue to follow that specific curriculum regardless of changes to future curriculums for your major that the department makes. Your curriculum year will only change if you change your major or if you petition to change your curriculum year.

### Changing Your Major

The [Change of Major Guide](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/change-of-major.shtml) is intended to help you understand the process of changing your major and the resources available to you as a CPP student.

Our advisors do change of major advising during our drop-in hours.

### All Things Graduation

Everything you need to know about graduation at CPP can be found on the [Steps to Senior Success Guide](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/senior-success.shtml).

Our advisors have created a [Pre-Graduation Worksheet](img/pre-grad-check-worksheet.pdf) that can assist you with making sure you're on track to graduate. Our advisors offer Senior Graduation Check appointments, which you can schedule on [CPP Connect](schedule-appointment.shtml).

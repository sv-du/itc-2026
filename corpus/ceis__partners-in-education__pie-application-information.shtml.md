# Pie Application Information

**$5,000 PIE Fellowship Award!**

## **Applications Open Summer 2026**

**Application deadline**: **Available Summer 2026**
**Application Link: Available Summer 2026**

1. You will first fill out the general application.
2. Once you have completed the general application you will be routed to the PIE Application.
3. You must complete both applications and submit by the deadline to qualify.
4. If you have any issues accessing the Scholarship Portal or about applying please contact:scholarships@cpp.edu

### **Eligibility Requirements:**

* Minimum **3.0** **GPA**upon entering the credential program.
* Fully admitted into the credential program**.**
* Working towards your **first** credential.
* **NOT** currently employed as an intern teacher or in a teacher residency program.
* Will be enrolled in clinical practice for **SPRING 2026 OR FALL 2026.**
* 1 Letter of Recommendation.
* Eligibility approval from Financial Aid Office.

### **Please Note:**

* This award is for living expenses. It does not cover your tuition.
* Students awarded fellowships are not eligible for reimbursements for terms of clinical practice already completed.
* Awards will not be given if students decide to participate in a paid clinical practice program (i.e. teacher residency or intern program).
* ***Accepting this award may/may not affect your financial aid package.***

### **Awardee Requirements:**

* Remain in good standing in the program during your coursework and clinical practice.
* Write a thank you note to the PIE Board.
* Initiate and maintain communication with your assigned PIE Board Member.
* Attend the PIE Fellows Awards Ceremony on February 2026.

## Financial Aid & Scholarship Questions

### What’s the process for getting “eligibility approval from the Financial Aid Office”? Do I need to submit anything?

You apply for the Partners in Education (PIE) Fellowship through the [Bronco Scholarship Portal (BSP)](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/scholarships/cal-poly-pomona-scholarships.shtml) using a direct URL provided by the department in an email to students preparing for clinical practice. After the committee reviews applications, the PIE Coordinator will contact the Office of Financial Aid & Scholarships to confirm potential candidates have room in their Cost of Attendance for the fellowship. The total amount of financial aid cannot exceed your Cost of Attendance. Turnaround time is typically 3–5 business days after The Office of  Financial Aid and Scholarships receives the request.

### Who can apply for the PIE Fellowship?

Applicants must meet the following eligibility criteria:

* minimum **3.0 GPA** upon entering the credential program;

* **fully admitted** to the credential program;

* **first credential** in progress;

* **not employed** as an intern teacher or in a teacher residency; and

* **enrolled in clinical practice in Fall 2025 or Spring 2026**.

* PIE also requires **eligibility approval from the Financial Aid Office**.

### When are applications open?

PIE posts its specific dates on the PIE site each year. The application cycle opens between September through October for award period for the following Spring and next Fall.

### Will accepting the PIE Fellowship up to $5,000 for living expenses affect my other financial aid (grants, scholarships, loans, work-study)?

It depends, the total amount of assistance from all sources cannot exceed the Cal Poly Pomona yearly Cost of Attendance (COA). If the addition of the PIE Fellowship would put you over that budget, Financial Aid will adjust other aid in the most advantageous way for you, often starting by reducing work-study and loans before grants when possible.

### Do I still need to file the FAFSA or California Dream Act Application if I expect to receive the PIE Fellowship?

Yes. File every year. It keeps you eligible for other aid and lets us coordinate PIE fellowship with your package; PIE fellowship may not cover all living costs or every term.

### If the PIE Fellowship causes a reduction to my other aid, can I lessen the impact?

It depends. If your actual expenses are higher than what’s in your cost of attendance breakdown, you can submit a Special Circumstance/COA Adjustment Request through the [Student Forms portal](https://cpp.studentforms.com/),  If approved, this may create room for PIE fellowship without reducing other aid.

### Does the fellowship cover tuition or fees?

No. The PIE Fellowship is designated for living expenses only therefore the funds will be refunded via your chosen method (direct deposit or check).

### How and when is the $5,000 paid?

PIE Fellowship lists the award as “up to $5,000.” At CPP, scholarships typically release to your account ~10 days before the start of the awarding term(s).

### The fellowship is intended for clinical practice in Spring 2026  and Fall 2026 and doesn’t reimburse past terms. Is there any flexibility

PIE Fellowships are tied to the award period in the official agreement. Exceptions are rare and, if considered, are coordinated by the department (and sometimes the donor).

### If my clinical placement is delayed or canceled, what happens?

The department notifies Financial Aid/PIE Coordinator about eligibility changes. If you no longer meet eligibility (e.g., placement canceled), the fellowship may be canceled or delayed accordingly. (Students don’t need to submit a separate form for this.)

### How will I know if PIE is in my aid package?

After the PIE Coordinator sends the recipient list, Financial Aid posts the fellowship to your BroncoDirect Award Summary (allow 3–5 business days). You’ll receive a system-generated email within 24 hours of posting confirming the change.

### Who do I contact with questions?

Email scholarships@cpp.edu and mention “PIE Fellowship” in the subject line, be sure to  include your Bronco ID within the body of the email.

### Are there any tax implications? Will I get a 1098-T?

Scholarships/fellowships are reported as financial aid resources. CPP issues Form 1098-T annually to eligible students to help with tax filing; forms are available in BroncoDirect/mailed each January (on or before the federal deadline). Please consult a tax professional about your specific situation.

### Will there be additional disbursements or holds when PIE is awarded

It depends. Holds or adjustments can occur if, for example, your aid eligibility changed after an appeal, you have outstanding To-Dos, or there’s a balance due. Aid (including scholarships) releases  starting 10 days before classes when all requirements are complete. Check your To-Do List and Account Activity in BroncoDirect.

### Can Financial Aid help me adjust my budget (Cost of Attendance) if my costs change?

Yes. You can submit a Special Circumstance/COA Adjustment Request through the [Student Forms portal](https://cpp.studentforms.com/), with documentation for additional, allowable expenses. A financial aid counselor can help you understand what qualifies and how to submit a complete, timely request. It is easy to make a virtual appointment on CPP Connect.

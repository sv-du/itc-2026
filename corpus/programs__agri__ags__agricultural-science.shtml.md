## About the Program

A degree in agricultural science will equip you with the knowledge and skills needed to address critical challenges in sustainable food production, environmental stewardship and global food security.
Blending science, technology and hands-on experience, this interdisciplinary program prepares you to work in general agriculture, animal science, agribusiness, agricultural education and research.

## The Polytechnic Experience

Prepare for a career in the fast-changing agricultural industry. Learn by doing in labs, fields, and greenhouses. Gain hands-on skills in crop and animal care, precision tech and sustainability. Connect with employers through internships and real-world projects. Build problem-solving skills for today’s ag challenges.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Agribusiness and Management

Roles in marketing, finance, supply chain management, laws and lay making, and business development within agricultural companies.

### Animal Science and Livestock Management

Careers in animal nutrition, breeding, veterinary science, animal production and farm management.

### Education and Extension Services

Extension education, farm advisor, consultant, and promoting agricultural best practices as extension agents or agricultural educators in traditional middle school and high school classrooms.

### Government and Policy

Developing agricultural policies, food safety inspector, administrative or government positions in local, state, national or international agriculture sectors working with regulatory agencies on current issues such as food security.

### Plant Science

Positions in agronomy, crop consulting, pesticide application, and soil health management to enhance productivity and sustainability.

### Research and Development

Working on advancements in biotechnology, pest management, soil health, climate change, food system innovations and environmental impacts of the agricultural industry on the planet.

### Sustainable Agriculture and Environmental Conservation

Jobs in sustainable farming practices, resource conservation, and environmental consulting.

### Future Agriculture Technologies

Studying and developing advancements in precision agriculture, data analytics, and agricultural technology, and taking on roles in ag-tech, climate-smart agriculture, and global food systems.

## Courses

Cal Poly Pomona's Bachelor of Science in Agricultural Science offers a diverse curriculum, including courses in basic biology, general chemistry, sustainable agriculture, animal production, agribusiness accounting, and the ethical aspects of food, nutrition, and health disparities. This broad range of courses provides a well-rounded education, preparing students for various aspects of the agribusiness field. Students are encouraged to consult with their advisors to select appropriate electives that align with their career goals. The complete curriculum and detailed course descriptions are available in the University Catalog.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

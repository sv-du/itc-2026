# FAQs

Expand/Collapse All for faq number 1

## What is the price of a semester parking permit?

Semester parking permits are $231.

## When are parking permits required?

Parking permits are required at all times, including weekends and holidays.

## Why do I have to pay for parking?

Under State law, all parking on university campuses is subject to the payment of a fee. The parking program is fully self-supporting; there are no tax payer funds used to fund the parking program. Your purchase of a parking permit enables the University to build and mantain the lots, provide requent patrols to parked vehicles, and purchase supples and equipment necessary to operate the parking program.

## What do I do if I lock my keys in my car or if my car won't start?

All vehicles with a valid parking permit are eligible to receive motorist assistance. This assistance includes free battery jumps and free vehicle unlocks. Dial 909-869-3070 for all requests.

## What are the code blue phones?

Blue Phones are located throughout campus parking lots and the parking structure. Blue Phones have been installed for your safety and provide a convenient direct link to University Police Dispatch.

To report malfunctions with a Code Blue Phone please call 909-869-3070.

View a [map](https://www.cpp.edu/~parking/documents/Uploads/Code-Blue-Map-2015.pdf) of the code blue phone locations (PDF).

## Can I request a safety escort?

Yes, please contact University Dispatch at 909-869-3070.

## Are there any alternatives to driving to campus?

Consider commuting by bus, bicycle, or foot.

**Bus -** Discounted Foothill Transit bus passes are available for purchase to all students.

**Train -** Discounted Metrolink train passes are available for purchase to all students. Additionally, students receive a 25% discount on ticket purchased at any Metrolink station. There is a **free** shuttle service from the Pomona North Metrolink Station (205 Santa Fe St., Pomona, CA 91767) to Cal Poly Pomona.

Discounted tickets/passes may be purchased at the ASI Games Room located at the Bronco Student Center, Building 35. For more information, call 909-869-4467.

**Carpool -** Matching services are available.

Some restrictions apply. Bus and train resources/schedules are available at the Rideshare located in the Bronco Student Center, 2nd fllot, office 2131. For more information, please contact the Rideshare office at 909-869-3233.

## Are there benefits to carpooling?

Students: Get close parking during the morning hours. See the police and procedures for the Student [Rideshare Program](https://www-test.cpp.edu/transportation/commuting-to-campus/rideshare.shtml).

Faculty and Staff: We have carpool spaces for faculty and staff as well. Please see the [Rideshare Office](https://www-test.cpp.edu/transportation/commuting-to-campus/rideshare.shtml) for a complete policy description and a Rideshare application.

## Are there areas on campus to secure bikes?

Yes, there are bike racks throughout campus.

Additionally, there is a free, secure bike parking with 24 hour access located in parking structure #2 located Southeast of iPoly High School (Bldg. 128).

For more information, please contact the Rideshare office at 909-869-3233, located in the Bronco Student Center, 2nd floor, oddice 2131.

## Where are the shuttle stop locations and operating hours?

The Bronco Express shuttles are a convenient way to get around the campus. **All shuttles are free.**

For real time status for the Bronco Express Bus Routes and real-time approval updates, please visit the <http://www.broncoshuttle.com>

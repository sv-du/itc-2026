# Undergraduate Programs in English & Spanish

## Earn a B.A. in English or Spanish

**English majors** can choose to focus on Applied Linguistics, Education, or Literary Studies. English majors are immersed in literary and cultural study, learning about how language functions and changes over time, and getting lots of practice in reading deeply, thinking critically, building arguments ethically, and communicating purposefully across a variety of contexts.

If you're interested in Applied Linguistics and Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages (TESOL), consider our blended B.A. + M.A. program—learn more below!

**Spanish majors** receive a holistic foundation in Linguistics, Literature, Cultural Studies, and Interpretation and Translation. They can take a range of PolyX classes, attend talks with contemporary Hispanic writers and scholars, engage in community work through internships and collaborations with public humanities groups in LA, and contribute to research and translation projects on campus.

## Learn more about our major programs

### English – Applied Linguistics

Ever wonder why language works the way it does? Applied Linguistics is the systematic study of human language and the everyday concerns related to language use. Courses teach the structural organization of languages, the mental processes involved in language acquisition and use, the ways humans learn additional languages in and out of the classroom, and the interaction between language and society. Linguists work as speech therapists, translators, interpreters, language database analysts, to name just a few career paths.

If you'd like to earn a B.A. + M.A. in Applied Linguistics and Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages (TESOL) in as few as 5 years, consider our blended degree program!

[Learn more about Applied Linguistics and our blended degree program here](../majors/apls.shtml)

### English – Education

Do you want to be a middle school or high school English teacher? The English Education curriculum prepares students to enter single subject credential programs and become middle school or high school English language arts teachers. The curriculum includes literature, linguistics, rhetoric, writing, and pedagogy courses that prepare students for a career in secondary education.

[Learn more about English Education here](../majors/eng-ed.shtml)

### English – Literary Studies

Do you love reading and dissecting fiction, poetry, and drama? The Literary Studies major option provides students with a strong background in writing and linguistics while engaging with literature across time periods, geographies, and literary movements. This option prepares students for a wide variety of career paths, including law, higher education, marketing, grant writing, video game narrative development, and more.

[Learn more about Literary Studies here](../majors/lit-studies-ba.shtml)

### Spanish

¿Te fascina el español? ¿Quieres conocer más sobre el mundo hispano, su historia y cultura, y ser un agente de cambio en las comunidades que te rodean? Our Spanish major offers courses in language, literature, culture, linguistics, history, cinema, and writing. You’ll meet writers, scholars, and alumni at our events, and you’ll have opportunities for community engagement through collaborations with public humanities groups in the LA area, internships in the legal and medical sectors, and study abroad programs. Majors go on to work as teachers, video-game or court translators, interpreters, government employees, and in business and nonprofit organizations.

[Learn more about Spanish here](../majors/spanish.shtml)

## Taking the Right Language Course

All English majors are required to take at least one language course at the 2000/Intermediate level, and Spanish majors, depending on their preparation, may need to take Spanish language courses. If you are not sure where to start in learning a language (or, if you already have some familiarity with an language and want to know which class to take to build on that knowledge), see our [Language Placement information](language-placement.shtml).

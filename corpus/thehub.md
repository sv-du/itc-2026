[### Feb 11 2026

Food and Nutrition Workshop

This workshop offers an in-depth exploration of foundational food and nutrition principles, empowering participants to make informed, evidence-based decisions about their dietary habits. Through an engaging and educational format, attendees will learn how nutrition impacts physical health, energy levels, and overall well-being.

Participants will gain practical, research-informed strategies for mindful and balanced eating, including how to evaluate food choices, understand individual nutritional needs, and apply sustainable habits that support long-term wellness. Attendees are encouraged to bring their nutrition-related questions and concerns, as faculty experts Dr. Sangeeta Shrivastava and Dr. Fatheeema Begum Subhan will be available to address questions and provide guidance throughout the session.

5:00 PM](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/food-nutrition-workshop-tickets-1979937429149?aff=oddtdtcreator)

### The Community Innovation Hub is dedicated to invigorating Pomona's workforce through comprehensive Workforce Development initiatives.

This center plays a pivotal role in:

* Enhancing Skills: By offering a range of programs focused on upskilling and reskilling, the Hub equips community members with the necessary tools and knowledge to thrive in various STEAM disciplines.

* Empowering Entrepreneurs and Local Businesses: The Hub serves as a resourceful beacon for small businesses and entrepreneurs, providing access to essential services, co-working spaces, and equipment for innovation and growth.

* Strengthening Community Ties: In response to the significant employment challenges exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic, the Community Innovation Hub stands as a cornerstone for economic revival, fostering a skilled, diverse workforce that supports and sustains local business ecosystems.

## University Receives $1M for Community STEA2M Innovation Hub CPP News

It's full steam ahead for Cal Poly Pomona's Bronco STEA2M Innovation Hub, after a $1 million federal funding request by U.S. Rep. Norma Torres was approved in a sweeping 2022 appropriations bill signed earlier this month by President Joe Biden. The project - an entrepreneurial and small business hub that will provide workforce training programs and a full complement of workspaces and fabrication equipment - was folded into a $14.3 million request to invest in 10 Inland Empire community programs. The hub is slated to open shop in downtown Pomona. Its location will be announced at a later date.

[Read more on CPP News](https://www.cpp.edu/news/content/2022/03/university-receives-1m-for-community-stea2m-innovation-hub/index.shtml "Read more on CPP News. ")

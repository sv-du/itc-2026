## AED

Sudden cardiac arrest can strike anyone, anytime, anywhere—including on the Cal Poly Pomona campus. In such emergencies, every second counts, and quick access to an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) can mean the difference between life and death. The Campus AED Safety Program is designed to ensure that these lifesaving devices are readily available, properly maintained, and supported by trained individuals across the campus community. By raising awareness, enhancing accessibility, and promoting training in AED use, the Office of Environmental Health and Safety aims to create a safer environment where immediate response to cardiac emergencies is both possible and effective.

## Information

### CPP AED Program Manual

[CPP AED Program Manual (PDF)](../all-pdfs/aed-forms/cpp-aed-program-draft-v3-1.pdf)

### AED Location

AED locations on CPP campus can be viewed by clicking on the [PulsePoint AED website](https://aedregistry.pulsepoint.org/index.php) and the [CPP AED Locations List (PDF)](../all-pdfs/aed-forms/aed-location-campus-2025.pdf).

For more accurate information, please download the [PulsePoint AED app](https://www.pulsepoint.org/download) on a cell phone or tablet since the AED locations can be viewed better on those devices than on a laptop/computer.

### AED Demo and Training Certificates

For more information on requesting AED demonstrations, please see the [Office of Emergency Management's](https://www.cpp.edu/em/) website or contact em@cpp.edu.

For more information on AED training certificates, please contact ASI, at asitraining@cpp.edu.

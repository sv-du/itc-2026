# Records FAQ

## FAQs

### What is a Degree Progress Report (DPR), and where can I access it?

A Degree Progress Report, or DPR, is a tool in your BroncoDirect that helps identify which requirements have been satisfied in your curriculum. It is similar to the digital format of your curriculum sheet. The DPR allows you to see which requirements are covered by a certain class.

To access your DPR, you will need to log in to BroncoDirect. From BroncoDirect, go to your Student Center. The top left-hand corner of your Student Center should say 'Academics'. There will be a drop-down tab that says, 'Other Academics'. Once clicking the drop-down tab, click on Degree Progress Report. If you need help understanding your DPR, you can meet with an academic advisor from your college. If you think there are some discrepancies within your DPR, you can contact tga@cpp.edu.

Did this answer your question? If not, please email your inquiry to registrar@cpp.edu. A Registrar's Office representative will reach out to help you further.

### My DPR does not show all my classes. Who can fix it?

Sometimes there are minor errors in the system where you should be receiving credit for a class, but the requirement does not indicate ‘satisfied.’ If this occurs, please verify with your academic advisor or contact tga@cpp.edu.

Did this answer your question? If not, please email your inquiry to registrar@cpp.edu. A Registrar's Office representative will reach out to help you further.

### How do I know my credits will transfer to CPP?

To determine if your credits will transfer from another institution, contact your college academic advisor at CPP or search the class by clicking the link: [ASSIST](https://assist.org/) or [TES by CollegeSource](https://tes.collegesource.com/) to see if any agreements exist.

Did this answer your question? If not, please email your inquiry to registrar@cpp.edu. A Registrar's Office representative will reach out to help you further.

### How do I change my major/minor or add a double major?

If you are considering changing your major/minor or adding a double major, you should connect with the department of your desired major. Departments have different requirements to change majors. Certain Departments may require a Change of Major Meeting. Once ready to change your major, submit a Change of Major form. This is an e-form located on the Registrar's Office webpage.

The Change of Major form can be found under ‘Academic Forms’. For more information, contact the Registrar's Office or refer to the Student Success Central guide on how to change your major. For instructions on how to change your major, please refer to our guide.

Did this answer your question? If not, please email your inquiry to registrar@cpp.edu. A Registrar's Office representative will reach out to help you further.

### Where can I find forms and petitions?

You may find various forms and petitions by visiting the Registrar's Office website. Click 'Academic Forms' on the site.

Did this answer your question? If not, please email your inquiry to registrar@cpp.edu. A Registrar's Office representative will reach out to help you further.

### I need an official letter that verifies my current enrollment, class standing, GPA, or graduation status. Where can I get an official letter?

An active student who needs an enrollment verification letter for enrollment status or GPA verification may log into the Student Center and request a Student Self-Service certification via the National Student Clearinghouse <https://secure.studentclearinghouse.org/sssportalui/faces/SSSPortal>.

If you are no longer an active student, you may email the Bronco Advising Center (BAC) at bac@cpp.edu.

Did this answer your question? If not, please email your inquiry to registrar@cpp.edu. A Registrar's Office representative will reach out to help you further.

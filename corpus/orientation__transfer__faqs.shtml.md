# Frequently Asked Questions

## About Orientation Day

### Is orientation mandatory?

Yes, Orientation is a mandatory enrollment requirement for all new undergraduate students. **You must complete all required components. Failure to complete any required component will result in your CPP acceptance being withdrawn.**

1. Make your orientation reservation. REQUIRED
2. Complete the CPP Pre-Orientation Modules. REQUIRED
3. Attend Orientation Day. REQUIRED

As you go through your orientation experience, you will learn about important Cal Poly Pomona programs, departments and services; meet other new students; and register for your first-semester courses!

More opportunities for meeting new students, interacting with your Orientation Leaders, and connecting with campus departments will be available throughout the summer. Check our social media, website, and your emails for more information!

### When does orientation for Fall '26 transfers take place?

* Orientation for Fall 2026 admits will take place beginning July.
* The availability of orientation sessions is dependent on your academic major. Please review date availability via your Bronco Applicant Portal.
* Make your Orientation reservation starting April 2, 2026.
* Your CPP Pre-Orientation comes in three components:
  + Modules 1 & 2 will be available before your Orientation Day.
  + Module 3 will be available after your Orientation Day.
* You need to complete module 1 and 2 prior to your orientation day.
* Complete module 3 by the start of the Fall '26 semester.
* More opportunities for meeting new students, interacting with your Orientation Leaders, and connecting with campus departments will be available throughout the fall. Check our social media, website, and your emails for more information!

### Is there an orientation for my parents, family members or other supporters?

Students are able to bring up to two adults (18 and over) guests. The cost is $85 per person. You can add your guests to your Orientation date at the time of reservation. Please visit [Parent & Family Engagement](https://www.cpp.edu/family/index.shtml) for more information.

### What will in-person orientation look like?

Orientation is a full day, so please plan to be on campus all day. Students will have an opportunity to meet with their academic advisor in a group advising session and register for up to 15 units the day of orientation. Students will also learn about campus resources, engage with orientation leaders, and be supported as they begin their transition to campus.

### What if I cannot attend the provided orientation date?

Attending orientation is a mandatory admissions requirement. Students with documented evidence of their inability to accommodate an orientation date are reviewed on a case-by-case basis. Students will utilize the Bronco Applicant Portal to review all orientation options.

### When will I receive my Bronco One Card?

If you have submitted an approved photo ahead of time to the Bronco Card Office, then you will be able to pick up your Bronco Card on your in-person Orientation Day. For information on what qualifies as an acceptable photo, as well as how and where to submit a photo, please visit the [Bronco Card Office website](https://broncoonecard.com/bronco-one-card-undergraduate-students/).

### How do I cancel my orientation reservation and withdraw my application to CPP?

If you no longer have plans to attend Cal Poly Pomona, please make sure to cancel your reservation on BroncoDirect and complete the following [Admission Withdrawal Form](https://nam11.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fforms.office.com%2Fr%2FahVzzfAzsk&data=05%7C01%7Ckmflores%40cpp.edu%7Ca355a841440142a7ea3008da9b60d113%7C164ba61e39ec4f5d89ffaa1f00a521b4%7C0%7C0%7C637993137383686459%7CUnknown%7CTWFpbGZsb3d8eyJWIjoiMC4wLjAwMDAiLCJQIjoiV2luMzIiLCJBTiI6Ik1haWwiLCJXVCI6Mn0%3D%7C3000%7C%7C%7C&sdata=sKSUjEk3eXlknm3QAOs%2FysSszxXPA%2FUv6ltnUgHMqP0%3D&reserved=0).

## Making Your Orientation Reservation

### When can I make my reservation for orientation?

Make your orientation reservation starting April 2, 2026. You need to submit your Intent to Enroll and pay your Enrollment Confirmation Deposit (if applicable) before being able to make a reservation. Making a reservation for orientation will sign you up for the CPP Pre-Orientation Modules and your official Orientation Day. You are required to make a reservation as part of your mandatory enrollment requirement. **If you do not make a reservation, your admission will be withdrawn.**

### How do I make my reservation for orientation?

You make your reservation through the Orientation Reservation system in BroncoDirect. When you make your reservation, you will select an open date for your major as your Orientation Day. To make your reservation:

* Log in to your [Bronco Applicant Portal](https://applicant.cpp.edu).
* Go to the Orientation tab.
* Click the *Schedule Orientation* box.
* Click on the orientation date you prefer*.*
* Follow the prompts to confirm your reservation.

You will receive a confirmation email when your reservation is confirmed.

### How do I submit my intent to enroll?

If you are receiving an enrollment deposit waiver, you still need to submit your intent to enroll.

[Follow These Steps](https://applicant.cpp.edu)

### How can I request accommodations to fully participate in orientation?

If you need accommodations to fully participate in Orientation, be certain to enter the details of your need when you make your orientation reservation. Depending on the nature of your request, you will receive a response from either the Disability Resource Center or the Office of New Student Programs & Family Engagement. If you forgot to include your accommodation request in your reservation, please [contact the Office of New Student Programs & Family Engagement](https://www.cpp.edu/orientation/contact-us.shtml) as soon as possible but no later than 2 weeks before your on-campus orientation day. Depending on the nature of your accommodation, you may need to [register with the Disability Resource Center](https://www.cpp.edu/drc/applying-for-services/applying-for-services.shtml) in order for your request to be approved.

All videos in the CPP Online Orientation are closed captioned. If you would like to have the CPP Online Orientation text read aloud, we recommend viewing the orientation using the Chrome browser with the [Read Aloud extension](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/read-aloud-a-text-to-spee/hdhinadidafjejdhmfkjgnolgimiaplp?hl=en "link to read aloud extension information (opens new window)") enabled.

### How do I claim my BroncoName/CPP email address?

Your BroncoName is your Cal Poly Pomona user name, the primary identifier for campus-wide computer systems. It is also the part preceding @cpp.edu in your email address. You will need to claim your BroncoName before making your Virtual Orientation Experience reservation.

Go to the [CPP Account Claiming website](https://apps.cpp.edu/idm/user_claim.aspx "link to CPP Account Claiming website (opens in new window)") to claim your BroncoName. You will need your BroncoNumber (your student ID number) and your claim code, which is your birthdate using the YYMMDD format.

For questions about or help with the Account Claiming process, contact the IT Service Desk at (909) 869-6776 or helpdesk@cpp.edu.

## Completing the CPP Pre-Orientation Modules

### By when do I have to complete the Pre-Orientation modules?

Complete Module 1 and 2 before your Orientation Day to ensure your ROS hold is removed and you will be able to register for your first-semester courses. Module 3 must be completed by the start of the Fall '26 semester. Successfully completing the CPP Pre-Orientation modules means that you have viewed all sections and passed the final test with a score of 80% or higher. Not completing the CPP Pre-Orientation modules puts you at risk for not being able to register for future semesters.

### How do I complete the Pre-Orientation modules?

The CPP Pre-Orientation modules are self-paced courses that you can complete at your convenience. The pre-orientation takes about two hours to complete. You do not have to complete them all at one time. We recommend you take some breaks throughout the program. You can save your progress and return to complete it at a future date.

The CPP Pre-Orientation is divided into three modules to provide you with important new student information when it is most relevant to you.

* Module 1 and 2 prepare you for your on-campus orientation experience and course registration:
  + Module 1 will be available TBD.
  + Module 2 will be available TBD.
* Module 3 prepares you to begin your first-semester at CPP and provide important information about campus life.
  + Module 3 will be available TBD.

Be sure to complete module 1 and 2 before your Orientation Day to ensure your ROS hold is removed and you can register for your first-semester classes. You can use any Internet-connected device to complete the modules (computer, tablet or smartphone).

Successfully completing the entire CPP pre-orientation modules means that you have viewed all sections and passed the final test with a score of 80% or higher. Not completing the Pre-Orientation puts you at risk for not being able to register for future semesters.

All videos in the modules are closed captioned. If you would like to have the text read aloud, we recommend viewing the orientation using the Chrome browser with the [Read Aloud extension](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/read-aloud-a-text-to-spee/hdhinadidafjejdhmfkjgnolgimiaplp?hl=en "link to read aloud extension information") enabled.

## Attending Orientation Day

### What if I miss my Orientation Day?

**If you do not attend your scheduled Orientation Day, your admission to CPP will be withdrawn.**If you wish to be considered for a reinstatement after being withdrawn, you will need to file a formal appeal with the Office of Admissions.

**If you can no longer attend your Orientation Day,**[contact the Office of New Student Programs & Family Engagement](https://www.cpp.edu/orientation/contact-us.shtml)**AS SOON AS POSSIBLE.** Be certain to include your Bronco ID number so we can assist you quickly.

If you no longer have plans to attend Cal Poly Pomona, please make sure to complete the following[Admission Withdrawal Form](https://nam11.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fforms.office.com%2Fr%2FahVzzfAzsk&data=05%7C01%7Ckmflores%40cpp.edu%7Ca355a841440142a7ea3008da9b60d113%7C164ba61e39ec4f5d89ffaa1f00a521b4%7C0%7C0%7C637993137383686459%7CUnknown%7CTWFpbGZsb3d8eyJWIjoiMC4wLjAwMDAiLCJQIjoiV2luMzIiLCJBTiI6Ik1haWwiLCJXVCI6Mn0%3D%7C3000%7C%7C%7C&sdata=sKSUjEk3eXlknm3QAOs%2FysSszxXPA%2FUv6ltnUgHMqP0%3D&reserved=0).

## Academic Advising and Course Registration

### Will I meet with an academic advisor?

Advising and registration is conducted in a small group format by major. In a small group setting, you will meet with academic advisors and registration assistants from your major and college. Advisors and registration assistants will provide guidance on your first-semester coursework. If you need to, you can complete your course registration over several days, since your registration window is open for one week. If you have in-depth questions about your academic plan, you can schedule an advising appointment for a later date.

### When do I register for my first semester courses?

You will register for your first-semester courses during Advising & Registration on your Orientation Day. Your registration window will remain open for one week so you can make any adjustments to your courses if needed and you will be able to make changes during the add/drop period at the start of the semester.

### How do I know what classes to register for?

Information on what you need to register for is available in module 2 of the CPP Pre-Orientation and in your[CPP Connect Planner](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/cppconnect/index.shtml). Your advisors will also discuss what classes you will need to register for on Orientation Day. Be sure to set up your Planner prior to your Orientation Day so you are well-prepared for advising and registration.

### When will my Orientation Registration (ROS) hold be removed from my student account?

You must complete module 1 and 2 of the CPP Pre-Orientation in order for your ROS hold to be removed. Once you complete module 1 and 2, your Orientation Registration (ROS) hold will be removed within 2 business days. Be certain to complete module 1 and 2 before your Orientation Day to ensure that your hold is removed prior to course registration.

You will also need to complete Module 3 by the start of the Fall 2026 semester.

Successfully completing the Pre-Orientation modules means that you have viewed all three parts, all sections, and passed the final test with a score of 80% or higher.

### When will my Orientation Advising (OAV) hold be removed from my student account?

You must attend Orientation Day for your OAV hold to be removed. Your OAV hold will be removed prior to the start of your registration window on Orientation Day once your attendance is recorded.

### Can I change my major before the start of the semester?

The deadline to change your major as part of the admissions process has passed. Your next opportunity to change your major will come after your first semester at CPP. You must reserve for a Orientation Day for your admitted major. However, you can let your advisor know of your interest in changing majors so that they can provide you with guidance. Review the [Cal Poly Pomona Change of Major Guide](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/change-of-major.shtml "link to CPP Change of Major Guide (opens a new window)") for more information about the change of major process.

## Orientation Fee

### Do I have to pay my student orientation fee in order to finalize my orientation reservation?

**No, you do not have to pay your student Orientation fee in order to finalize or confirm your Orientation reservation****.** Please be certain to make your orientation by the reservation deadline, regardless of your ability to pay the fee at the moment. The fee will appear on your student account as a separate charge from your tuition charges (as financial aid is not able to be applied directly to the Orientation Fee). Be sure to pay the fee by the due date indicated to avoid any holds for non-payment.

### When is my student orientation fee due?

Your student Orientation fee is posted as soon as you make your Orientation reservation. You will see it listed as ORS Fee on your student account. The fee is due by the date listed on your student account but you are able to pay it earlier if you would like.

### Is the student orientation fee refundable?

Refunds are granted if the following conditions are met:

* You have not yet completed your Orientation.
* Your refund request was emailed to orientation@cpp.edu no later than **two weeks** before your Orientation Day. Be certain to include your BroncoNumber in your email.

Refunds are not granted automatically and you must contact Orientation via email to request a refund. You are not eligible to receive a refund after completing Orientation, despite any change in your admission status or plans to attend Cal Poly Pomona.

### Can I waive the student orientation fee?

The student Orientation fee is a mandatory enrollment fee that cannot be waived. The fee will appear on your student account as a separate charge from your tuition charges (as financial aid is not able to be applied directly to the Orientation Fee). Be sure to pay the fee by the due date indicated to avoid any holds for non-payment.

### Is parking included in the cost of orientation?

Yes, the cost of parking is included in your student Orientation fee. You will receive a reminder email prior to your reservation date that will include steps to register your vehicle as well as a campus map, so you know where to park.

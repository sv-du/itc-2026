## Overview

### Training Resources for Accessible Technology

Cal Poly Pomona, along with other organizations, provide training resources that aim to teach accessible technology best practices.

![Two people sit at a computer desk taking notes, while another person points at something on the monitor](img/trainingstockimage.png)

## Cascade CMS & Web Accessibility

### Cascade Content Management System (CMS)

Cascade is the university's free Content Management System (CMS) that allows you to easily build and maintain your website. Features include: login and access management, training sessions, and support requests for website updates.

Further information and resources are available at the [Cal Poly Pomona Cascade CMS webpage](https://www.cpp.edu/cascade/index.shtml).

### Campus Digital Accessibility Canvas Course

A training course on accessible web content is available for Cascade publishers. Visit the [Cal Poly Pomona Digital Accessibility Course on Canvas](https://canvas.cpp.edu/courses/66042) for registration.

More information on web accessibility for site publishers can be found in [Web Accessibility](web-accessibility.shtml).

## Canvas LMS & Accessible Instructional Material

The Cal Poly Pomona Center for the Advancement of Faculty Excellence (CAFE) actively supports faculty, tenure-line, and lecturer faculty, in all stages and facets of their career at Cal Poly Pomona. Thy provide orientation for new faculty, pedagogical resources, collaborative opportunities, confidential consultation and assist with developing accessible instructional materials.

More information is available at the [CAFE Working With Accessibility Webpage](https://www.cpp.edu/cafe/teaching-and-learning/accessible-instructional-materials.shtml).

## Accessible Documents Training: Microsoft Word, PowerPoint, and Adobe Acrobat PDFs

### WebAIM Document Training Course

The WebAIM Document Training course is available to all Cal State University employees and provides the core fundamentals for creating accessible Microsoft Word and PowerPoint documents, as well as some best practices for PDF documents. An optional Excel module is also available.

#### Brief Overview of the Course:

* The course is fully online and consists of four self-paced modules. Each module has a series of sections that includes quizzes and concludes with an open book exam.
* Expect to dedicate 2 to 4 hours per module. Upon course registration, participants will have 180 business days (excluding holidays) to complete all modules.
* After finishing the course, participants will receive a certificate of completion.

For a full description of the course, see the recorded [WebAIM Course Kickoff Session on Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5B4Fy78DKg) (48:53)

[Register for the WebAIM Document Accessibility Course](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5B4Fy78DKg)

### Digital Document Training Webinar Recording

This sessions aims to teach the knowledge and tools to develop and remediate digital documents for accessibility. Participants will learn practical strategies for creating accessible PDFs, Word, and PowerPoint files, ensuring that everyone, regardless of ability, can engage with your materials. You will leave with actionable tips that are immediately applicable to your everyday work.

[Digital Document Training Recording](https://streaming.cpp.edu/media/Making%2BDigital%2BDocuments%2BAccessible%2BPresentation%2BRecording%2BSession%2B2/1_v596212k)

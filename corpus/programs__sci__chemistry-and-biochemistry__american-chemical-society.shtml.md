## About the Program

Understand the building blocks of the universe through the intersection of chemistry and physics. You will engage in broad, advanced study of many areas of chemistry including general, organic, physical, inorganic and chemical analysis.
Link theory with practice as you work side-by-side with experienced faculty in our state-of-the-art laboratory facilities. With the American Chemical Society emphasis, you will be prepared for your dream career in research, industry or graduate studies.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Be a catalyst of innovation through faculty-led research opportunities and publish your work in academic journals alongside leaders in the field. Get hands-on training with spectrometers, liquid chromatography, gas chromatography, mass spectrometer,centrifuges, lasers and other modern equipment to experiment with a wide range of chemistry applications. You won't just study chemistry in a book or classroom. You will become a chemist through labs and practical experience.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Research and Development Chemist

Develop new products and processes for energy, food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, electronics, pollution control and more.

### Pharmacologist

Investigate how drugs get absorbed, metabolize and affect living organisms to improve pharmaceuticals and overall health outcomes. Design clinical trials, assess toxicity and drug resistance and more.

### Pharmaceutical Sciences

Develop and manufacture pharmaceutical products, including drugs and diagnostic tests, to assist in the treatment of health conditions.

### Clinical Lab Science

Conduct laboratory tests and examine samples to help diagnose diseases and inform their treatment.

### Medical Careers

Prepare for entry into professional programs such as Medical Doctor, Pharmacist, Physician Assistant, etc.

### Environmental Science

Investigate chemical processes in the environment, assess human impact and help develop solutions to environmental issues.

### Government Agencies

Conduct research, work in regulatory agencies or inform public policy from federal to local levels.

### Quality Control and Assurance

Ensure company processes and products meet all necessary requirements and standards. Detect issues in products (QC) or prevent and detect quality problems (QA).

### Chemical Education

Prepare for credential and postgraduate programs and share your passion for chemistry with learners of any age. Opportunities include teaching in elementary and secondary schools, two-year colleges, four-year institutions, or Ph.D-granting institutions.

### Intellectual Property/Law

Assess new chemical compounds, formulations or optimized processes to determine if they qualify for patent protection or use your expertise to review cases of potential patent infringement.

### Graduate and Professional School

Continue your studies with advanced degrees in chemistry or applied sciences or continue into professional programs, including medical, pharmacy, physician’s assistant or nursing school.

## Featured Sub-Discipline: Physical Chemistry

Physical chemistry gives an understanding of how chemical processes work at the macroscopic and microscopic levels. Thermodynamics and kinetics teach us how molecules interact with each other while quantum chemistry provides knowledge of how molecules behave at the atomic level. These insights give knowledge that allow us to create new ideas or innovate current ones.

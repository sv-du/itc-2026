## About the Program

Combine your passion for crops or livestock with business skills to manage anything from a family farm to a global company. The only program of its kind in California, it offers a strong foundation in agribusiness—covering marketing, accounting, food laws, and more—while preparing you to navigate industry challenges and tailor your path to your career goals.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Work in operations at the W.K. Kellogg Arabian Horse Center or market new CPP products at the farm store. Create business plans, compile financial statements and use analytics to guide strategy. Develop agri-food items or tackle industry challenges in competitions sponsored by the USDA, National Grocers Association, NAMA and the Food Distribution Research Society.

### Food Procurement Broker

Connect people and products to manage, expand and market everything from fruits and vegetables to meat, dairy and processed food products.

### Trained Farm Advisor

Help local farmers and ranchers improve their practices and productivity with your expertise.

### Commodities Trader

Analyze markets for crops like grapes, lettuce or almonds and make valuable trade relationships on an international or local level.

## Courses

Cal Poly Pomona's Bachelor of Science in Agribusiness and Food Industry Management provides a comprehensive foundation in agribusiness management, covering essential areas such as accounting, marketing, retailing, wholesaling, personnel management, and food laws and regulations. The curriculum is designed to equip students with the skills needed to navigate the legal and ethical principles of the industry. Students are encouraged to consult with their advisors to select appropriate electives that align with their career goals. The complete curriculum and detailed course descriptions are available in the University Catalog.
The curriculum is designed to provide you with an understanding of business functions in application, theory and practice.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

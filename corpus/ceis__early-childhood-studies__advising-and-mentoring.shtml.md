### Academic Policies and Degree Progress Report

×

**Academic Policies**

[Academic Policies](https://catalog.cpp.edu/content.php?catoid=53&navoid=4006) provide information as to what students must complete to obtain their degree Graduation Requirements:

1. 120 units are required to graduate
   * 80 units of lower division (LD) which are 1000 and 2000 level courses and 40 units of upper division (UD) which are 3000 and 4000 level courses.
   * 48 units of GE courses totoal in which 9 will be UD
   * 72 units of ECS courses total in which 30 will be UD courses (need 1 more additional UD to meet the total 40 units)
2. American Cultural Perspectives Requirement- - The American Cultural Perspective Requirment is a graduation requirment.
   * HS 2202 GE Area D1 counts as American history and government and American Cultural Perspectives requirement
3. Transfer
   * You can only transfer up to 70 units You are required to take 50 units at CPP.
   * If you take more than 70 units at a community college or decide to continue to take coursework at a community college to make progress, the transfer coursework will satisfy curriculum course requirements, however, the units will be adjusted.

**Degree Progress Report**

The DPR is a tool in your Bronco Direct Student Center that provides information about the completion status of your degree requirements.

1. [Degree Progress Report & Transfer Credit Information](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/degree-progress-report/index.shtml)
2. [How to access your DPR](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/dpr.shtml)

## Step 4

Explore the ECS course requirements and roadmaps
Learn More

### ECS Course Requirements

×

\*A grade of C or better is required for all your major ECS courses.

All students take the core 15 ECS courses that are focused on providing students with the competencies to work with young children and families using and anti-racist framework that values all children and families. ECS 2100/A is a core course all students will take, in this course students will be placed in an approved ELC settings in which 60 hours will be completed with children and families under the supervision of a mentor teacher and faculty member.

There are 6 options in the ECS department. The option you choose (or have chosen) depends on your career path choice. For each option you will find the following link that will help you understand the courses you are required to take.

* Curriculum Sheet: Helps identify what courses are needed to obtain your BA in ECS
* Road Map: Helps you identify when to take courses, which semester the course is offered (courses are not offered every semester), and the required pre-requisites (most courses have pre-requisites so that you have the knowledge and skills that help you with the next course). Use CPP Connect to help plan your path towards graduation

View details of the 6 options on our [Curriculum page](curriculum.shtml).

## Step 5

Explore career paths in early childhood

### Career Paths

×

There are several career options in early childhood including:

* Infant-toddler teacher
* Preschool teacher
* Elementary teacher (after post-baccalaureate program)
* Careers in home visiting
* Family support programs
* Post-baccalaureate degree programs,

The following website was created by students in ECS to inform future students about the various career pathways in early childhood: [Early Childhood Career Paths](https://sites.google.com/view/earlychildhoodcareerpaths/home).

**Child Development Permit**

All state and government early learning programs require early childhood teachers to hold a child development permit (some private early learning programs may require it as well). You can get your permit as soon as you complete 6 units in ECS (this qualifies you for an Assistant Permit). After you complete your BA in ECS you can apply for you Child Development Site Supervisor Permit.

Levels of child development permit:

* [Child Development Permit Information](https://www.ctc.ca.gov/credentials/leaflets/child-development-permits-%28cl-797%29)
* [Child Development Permit Recording](https://streaming.cpp.edu/media/CHILD%2BDEVELOPMENT%2BPERMIT%2B%26%2BPOMONA%2BR%26R/1_wwnzygsa)
* [Child Development Permits Worksheet](https://www.ctc.ca.gov/docs/default-source/leaflets/cl723a.pdf)
* [Questions on the Child Development Permit](https://www.ctc.ca.gov/credentials/faq/faq-child-dev)

**Cal Poly Pomona Teacher Credential**

For students seeking to be an elementary teacher, Cal Poly Pomona has a multiple subject teacher credential.

* Visit the [CPP Credential Program website](https://www.cpp.edu/ceis/credential-program/preliminary-multiple-subject-credential.shtml) for more information
* Contact Jeanne Reynaga, CPP Teacher Preparation and Recruitment Advisor, for questions about the CPP credential program

## Academic Advisors

ECS majors connect with the Student Success Center in the College of Education and Integrative Studies for guidance and registration questions. At the CEIS Student Success Center, you can meet with an advisor, find out about services, and learn about upcoming workshops. College Advisors provide information on detailed academic questions concerning your academic plan for graduation; submitting petitions and appeals; recommendations on courses; updating your academic plan; clearing advising holds; and transfer credits. The designated College Advisor for ECS students is Marlene Melinte (pictured to the left). Email ceisadvising@cpp.edu or visit 94-275 to schedule an appointment with her.

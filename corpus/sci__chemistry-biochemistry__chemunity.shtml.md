# Chemunity

Chemunity aims to:

* Highlight and support diversity in chemistry & science
* Foster community among chemistry students, faculty, and staff at CPP
* Educate about barriers and historical inequities in chemistry & science

## 2025-2026 Events

![](jmv_profile_2025.jpg)

### Prof. Jesús Velázquez

**Thursday, September 18: 12:00pm-1:00pm**

**Room: 8-345**

In collaboration with SMACS, Professor Jesús Velázquez from UC Davis will be coming to talk to students about his career and research. Research in the Velázquez group is centered on the design of well-defined dimensionally reduced materials, ranging from monolayers, bilayers, nanocrystalline thin films, and free-standing mesoporous monoliths. He will meet with interested students for Q&A following the seminar from 1:00pm-2:00pm.

![](elisa-olivas_uc-irvine.png)

### Elisa Olivas

**Friday, October 10: 11:00am-12:00pm**

**Room: 4/1-314**

Elisa Olivas from UC Irvine will be coming to talk to students about her research. A Cal Poly Pomona alumni, she worked on single crystal X-ray diffraction of various bis-imidazolium salts with Dr. Stieber. Interested in crystallization of inorganic materials, she joined the Patterson lab at UCI to understand how encapsulated enzymes influence the nucleation and growth mechanisms of metal-organic-frameworks. She will meet with interested students for Q&A following the seminar in 8-344.

![](dr-vern-oconnor_csuf.jpg)

### Dr. Vern O'Connor

**Friday, November 7: 11:00am-12:00pm**

**Room: 4/1-314**

Dr. Vern O'Connor from CSU Fullerton will come speak with students about her research. Dr. O'Connor is a postdoc in the Cammarota lab and lecturer at CSUF whose research focuses on investigating catalytic hydrodefluorination of perfluoroalkyl arenes through data science–driven NHC ligand design, integrating mechanistic insight, high-throughput experimentation, and statistical modeling to accelerate reaction optimization. She will meet with interested students for Q&A following the seminar in 8-344.

### Dr. Arianne Hunter

***Tues. Mar. 21, 2023: 12-1 pm***

*“The Power of Effective Communication in Science”*

Learn about forensic chemistry, communication, and career options from Dr. Arianne Hunter. There will be pizza for attendees.

Room: 8-345

Talk Abstract

### Talk Abstract

×

*Understanding the importance of effective communication is essential in advancing science in the Information Age. Scientists communicate inwardly with colleagues such as lab mates, professors, and advisors. But more importantly, scientists communicate their findings with government funding agencies and the general public to show the impact of their work on society.*

*Dr. Arianne C. Hunter learned about the importance of effective science communication after reading the book “Don’t Be Such A Scientist” by Randy Olson during her 2nd year in graduate school while preparing for her candidacy exam. After applying the tactics in that book, she graduated from the University of Oklahoma as the first black woman with a Ph.D. in organic chemistry. During her time in graduate school, she was a SMART Department of Defense Fellow and after graduation, she was placed at the Defense Forensic Science Center in Atlanta, GA, to fulfill her service agreement from the award. While in this position, she lived in Kuwait, conducting forensic analysis on evidence retrieved by US soldiers and our allies from violent extremist organizations. In the Spring of 2022, Dr. Hunter returned to academia as an NSF Ascend and Presidential Post-Doctoral Fellow at The California Institute of Technology, where she studies the application of electrochemistry and data chemistry in asymmetric organometallic catalysis.*

*During this talk, Dr. Hunter will discuss her journey in science as a forensically trained organic chemist and how she has used the power of effective communication to advance her career.*

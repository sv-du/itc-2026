#### Digital Community Archiving on a Budget

We explored how digital archiving keeps community stories alive and accessible. Guest speakers shared practical tips for preserving history on a budget. Watch the full event soon on our YouTube channel!

### Friday, 10/31: 9:30 AM- 10:30 AM

#### Video Editing 2

#### Human-to-Human: Interviewing Across Disciplines Panel

The panel, held in Building 162 Room 1002, brought together experts from journalism, oral history, social science, and podcasting to share best practices for conducting insightful and ethical interviews, offering fresh perspectives on understanding humanity through conversation.](https://youtu.be/03ljEVrL8YY?si=l-ntl9PioeXidzc8  "opens a new window")

[![Carlos Montemayor (San Francisco State)](img/fall2025-cultivating-attention-in-the-age-of-ai.png)

### Monday, 09/29, 10 AM - 11:15 AM

#### Cultivating Attention in the Age of AI

Held in Library 015-1807, the event featured Carlos Montemayor of San Francisco State University, who presented “Cultivating Attention in the Age of AI,” examining how artificial intelligence is reshaping human attention and its implications for learning, focus, and critical thinking.](https://youtu.be/JtLCIeNxsAU?si=gotFinLyGtwtiOBw  "opens a new window")

Dr. Dana Marshall led a workshop in Building 163 Room 1004 and on Zoom focused on understanding how AI tools work and how to use them ethically. Food was provided for in-person attendees, and the session recording is available on YouTube.](https://youtu.be/9jSzRmgeAw8?si=WYwjL3x7LPYnLsLW  "opens a new window")

#### Video Editing 101: Foundations of CapCut

Brian Tran, a DHC@CPP Student Fellow and Marketing major, led a beginner-friendly workshop on CapCut. ## Past Events - Spring 2025

#### OAI Faculty and Staff Summer Conference 2025

The Academic Innovation Summer Conference will focus on “The AI-Driven Future of Higher Education and Work,” featuring keynotes, panels, and workshops that explore how AI is shaping education and the workforce. CPP faculty and staff may attend at no cost - thanks to support from the NSF INVEST grant.

Applications due: March 21, 2025](https://www.cpp.edu/dhc/project-based-learning-workshop.pdf%20 "opens a new window")

#### Shannon Vallor, "Can We Live With AI?"

As AI becomes more embedded in our lives and impacts our environment, Shannon Vallor asks: Can we live with AI? Whether you're tech-savvy or just curious, this talk will challenge how you think about AI and its role in shaping our future.](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLwqGdKJVCJjHPQZ14vhC6BWRKfvdG4hRN  "opens a new window")

![Empowering Students in the Age of AI](img/ai-fair-hackathon-event.png)

### Thursday, April 17: 2:15PM - 3:45PM

#### Empowering Students in the Age of AI: Literacy, Access, and Equity

This talk explores how students are using and perceiving generative AI tools like ChatGPT in education. It examines barriers—both societal and institutional—that affect access and attitudes, highlighting existing disparities. Based on research with U.S. college students, the discussion will focus on how educators can promote equitable and inclusive AI use in learning.

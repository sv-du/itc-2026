# Join a Club or Organization

## Discover what moves you. Get involved. Make your mark.

College is more than classes—it’s where your interests come alive. With hundreds of clubs and organizations, there’s a place for every passion, cause, and curiosity. Whether you want to lead, create, compete, serve, or explore something entirely new, getting involved helps you turn ideas into action and classmates into lifelong friends.

![Club fair from above](../img/2025/hero.png)

## Learn more about involvement at Cal Poly Pomona

[### Search on myBAR

Learn how to find organizations on our involvements website, mybar.cpp.edu.](#modal-0 "Search for Organizations on myBAR")

### Search for Organizations on myBAR

×

Log in to [myBAR (cpp.edu)](https://mybar.cpp.edu/) and type one of your interests into the search bar. Find an organization you may be interested in joining and contact the primary contact for the organization. You can also scroll to the bottom of this page to see the currently registered clubs and organizations (updated weekly).

If you cannot find an organization that has your interests, contact us at leadership@cpp.edu. We will help you start it!

[Find an organization on myBAR now](https://mybar.cpp.edu/organizations)

Close

[### Create an Organization

Don’t see your passion on myBAR? Let us help you bring it to life by starting a new organization.](#modal-1 "Creating an Organization")

### Creating an Organization

×

**Have a hobby, passion, or interest you don’t see represented on campus? Start your own club at Cal Poly Pomona!** Whether you're into niche board games, K-pop dance, sustainable fashion, or anything in between, you have the power to build a community around what you love. Starting a club is easier than you think — bring your ideas, gather a few like-minded Broncos, and make your mark on campus life!

Contact us at leadership@cpp.edu and you must attend a new club workshop. New club workshops occur in September and February. Check the [Bronco Leadership Center's myBAR events page](https://mybar.cpp.edu/organization/blc/events) to see if there are any upcoming workshops.

You will need to have attended a new club workshop, a sustainability plan, five student members, a faculty or staff advisor, and by-laws. Check out the [Club and Organization Home](../student-leaders/club-org-toolkit.shtml) for templates.

Close

[### What is myBAR?

myBAR is the official platform for exploring campus involvement and managing student organization operations.](#modal-2 "myBAR Explained")

### myBAR Explained

×

myBAR—your *Bronco Activity Record*—is the central hub for discovering campus organizations, departments, events, and more. Club leaders also use myBAR to manage official organization documents, maintain member rosters, and conduct annual elections.

Close

[### Club Fairs and other BLC events

Don’t miss the next Club Fair—see upcoming dates on the myBAR Events page.](https://mybar.cpp.edu/organization/blc/events)

[### Organization List

See which student organizations are currently registered and active this academic year.](#modal-3 "Details about the Organization List")

### Details about the Organization List

×

Though myBAR is one way of viewing all clubs and organizations, you can verify that each orgnaization is registered using the list on the webpage.

Unless otherwise listed, all organizations are recognized for **August 2025-May 2026**. All organizations must re-register every year. Most organizations register in the March - August window for recognition in the entire next Academic Year. You can also [view our registration timelines](https://cpp.cascadecms.com/entity/open.act?type=page&id=bd1b36be0a00005e1390c3ec937ea64a&confId=b2af10d40a00005e3af5b6a359a1e7f0) for complete details.

Close

[### Other ways to get involved

Learn more about councils, instructionally related activities, and Associated Students, Inc.](#modal-4 "Councils, Instructionally Related Activities, and Associated Students, Inc.")

### Councils, Instructionally Related Activities, and Associated Students, Inc.

×

Club councils represent groups of student organizations by interest area, offering leadership development, guidance, and a collective voice while helping clubs stay connected and in good standing with the university. You can find more information about councils [on their myBAR pages](https://mybar.cpp.edu/organizations?query=council).

Instructionally Related Activities (IRA) support programs and events that enhance students’ academic experience beyond the classroom, such as lectures, workshops, and student-run media. [Check out the list of IRAs](https://www.cpp.edu/cppira/approved-programs.shtml).

ASI, Inc. (Associated Students, Inc.) is the official student government organization that advocates for students, provides services, and funds campus programs and clubs. [Learn more about getting involved in ASI.](https://asi.cpp.edu/student-government/get-involved/become-a-student-leader/)

Close

## About the Podcast

Established in 2018 at Cal Poly Pomona, the California Center for Ethics and Policy (CCEP) investigates pressing national and global challenges—such as climate policy, healthcare, artificial intelligence, immigration, and racism—through a Californian lens. Our podcast brings together students, artists, philosophers, social scientists, and advocates to debate, tell stories, and share ideas.

## Season 4

We are pleased to announce the launch of Season 4 of CCEP’s ethics and policy podcast, on misinformation and how it affects the many facets of our lives.

![](img/banners/podcast/1.png)

### Episode 1 - Scroll for your Health

CPP student fellows Katie Priest and Charlotte Hill examine the effects of misinformation specifically as it relates to health information in a post-COVID world.

[Listen to Episode 1](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/the-multiverse-of-misinformation-scroll-for-your-health/ "Listen to Episode 1. ")

![](img/banners/podcast/2.png)

### Episode 2 - Financial Literacy in the Age of Misinformation

CPP student fellow Joshua Ramirez will discuss the phenomenon of online financial misinformation with Angel Campos, President of the CPP Student Managed Investment Club, and David Rivera, President of the CPP Finance Society.

[Listen to Episode 2](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/the-multiverse-of-misinformation-episode-2-financial-literacy-in-the-age-of-misinformation/ "Listen to Episode 2. ")

![](img/banners/podcast/3.png)

### Episode 3 - CreepyPastas

CCP student fellow Anna Bachman takes us on a trip down memory lane by exploring the chilling world of Creepypastas.

[Listen to Episode 3](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/the-multiverse-of-misinformation-episode-3-author-meets-critics-with-desiree-lim/ "Listen to Episode 3. ")

![](img/banners/podcast/s4e4.png)

### Episode 4 - Misinformation and Democracy

CCEP hosted a panel on February 16, 2024, titled Misinformation and Democracy, which focused on the threat that misinformation poses to democracy, with a special emphasis on the 2024 primary and general elections in the State of California.  The panelists for this discussion were Hannah Cole, the Humanities and Communication Librarian at Cal Poly Pomona, Dr. Hyungjin Gill, Assistant Professor of Communication at Cal Poly Pomona, and Katie Priest, a Philosophy and Communication major at Cal Poly Pomona, journalist with the Poly Post, and one of this year’s CCEP Student Fellows.

[Listen to Episode 4](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/the-multiverse-of-misinformation-episode-4-misinformation-and-democracy/ "Listen to Episode 4. ")

### Season 1 - Securing Justice

Season 1 of our podcast, Securing Justice, focuses on housing insecurity, an issue that affects millions in the United States and is particularly acute here in California. This season explores housing insecurity through a variety of means--panel discussions, interviews, and creative works by faculty and students. From artists and activists to philosophers and policy wonks, we hope to provide listeners with a varied and nuanced look at how housing insecurity affects the lives of Californians, and what we--and you--can do about it.

![CCEP Podcast](https://www.cpp.edu/class/ethics-and-policy-center/img/banners/podcast/generic-podcast.png)

##### Episode 1 - Housing Justice: From Trump to Biden

CCEP brings together four panelists to discuss the role and impact of the federal government on housing insecurity under the Trump and Biden administrations.

[Listen to Episode 1](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/episode-1-housing-justice-from-trump-to-biden/)

![CCEP Podcast](https://www.cpp.edu/class/ethics-and-policy-center/img/banners/podcast/generic-podcast.png)

##### Episode 2 - Representations of Place, Home, and Security

A panel discussion hosted by CCEP, in which we invited three creatives--a filmmaker, a visual artist, and a theater maker, examine the themes of representations of place, home, and security.

[Listen to Episode 2](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/episode-2-representations-of-place-home-and-security/)

![CCEP Podcast](https://www.cpp.edu/class/ethics-and-policy-center/img/banners/podcast/generic-podcast.png)

##### Episode 3 - Moving Forward in Affordable Housing: The Role of Architects

CCEP’s 2020-21 student fellows, Itzia Salinas Cortes interviews Jim Broeske, an architect with 40 years of experience working in Southern California, to better understand the role that architects can play in addressing housing affordability.

[Listen to Episode 3](https://ccepsecuringjustice.podbean.com/e/moving-forward-in-affordable-housing-the-role-of-architects/)

##### Episode 4 - The Eviction Crisis on the Horizon: Rent Relief and Pandemic Recovery in California

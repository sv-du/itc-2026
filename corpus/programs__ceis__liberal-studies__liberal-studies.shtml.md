## About the Program

Venture into the exciting pursuit of interdisciplinary knowledge and explore a wide range of subjects, from arts and humanities to math and natural sciences.
Choose from four program options to fit your goals: General Studies, Pre-Credential, Integrated Teacher Education Program (ITEP) in Special Education for Mild to Moderate Support Needs, ITEP in Special Education for Extensive Support Needs.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Regardless of emphasis, you will take ten core courses that introduce you to the fundamentals of knowledge and learning and develop you into an equity-minded leader. Here, you become a well-rounded, analytical thinker and dynamic problem solver who can connect ideas across disciplines, communicate effectively and make meaningful contributions across numerous professional fields.
With the Liberal Studies program, create your own degree to reach your dreams, whether graduate school or a professional career in the nonprofit, corporate, public health or education sectors.

### Elementary School Teacher

Teach children in grades K-5 and provide foundational education in subjects like reading, math, social studies and science. Plan and deliver lessons, assess students’ progress, communicate with parents and prepare students for middle school and future learning.

### Human Resources Specialist

Manage an organization’s workforce, including recruiting and hiring, onboarding and training, compensation and benefits, employee relations and compliance with labor laws. Foster a positive company culture and ensure that both employees and employers are supported.

### Technical Writer

Create clear, concise and easily understandable communications that relay complex technical information to various audiences. Write user manuals, help guides, process documentation, technical reports and more.

### Social Worker

Help individuals and families through various life challenges by assessing their needs, connecting them to resources, providing counseling and advocating for their rights.

### School Counselor

Support students as they navigate personal and academic challenges. Provide counseling, academic and career guidance, programs that promote a positive school culture and resources in times of crisis.

## Program Emphasis

Previous
Next

### General Studies

This pathway offers a liberal arts education tailored to your passions and career interests. It is a flexible pathway that builds analytical and communication skills you’ll use in any area and allows you to build knowledge and abilities in multiple areas of interest.

### Pre-Credential Teaching

The teaching pathway offers a liberal arts or interdisciplinary education tailored to your goal of becoming an elementary education teacher. This option prepares you with subject matter knowledge for an elementary classroom based on California requirements.

### Integrated Teacher Education Program (ITEP): Extensive Support

The ITEP for Extensive Support Needs in Special Education degree is rigorous program that combines subject matter preparation for a classroom with an Extensive Support Needs teaching credential in four years.

### Integrated Teacher Education Program (ITEP): Mild/Moderate

The ITEP in Special Education, Mild/Moderate Support Needs degree is an intensive degree that combines subject matter preparation and a mild/moderate teaching credential in four years.

## Courses

The Liberal Studies program serves two types of students: (1) undergraduate students preparing to teach in California public elementary schools, and (2) students with multiple disciplinary interests who seek to enter careers in law enforcement, human services, health professions, and graduate school among others. The program’s core curriculum focuses on gaining understanding of the human condition in relation to contemporary and emerging issues from different human and disciplinary perspectives. You will develop strong critical thinking, communication, and collaboration skills that support the “soft skills” employers expect from employees. Liberal Studies courses are organized around four program themes (social responsibility/social justice, sustainability, creativity, and wellness) that are explored in courses and co-curriculum events.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

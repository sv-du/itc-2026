# Tutorials, Forms, and Policies

## How To....

Follow simple, step-by-step instructions to confidently manage your student account

### View Your Student Account

[**Understanding Your Student Account (PDF)**](understanding_your_student_account.pdf "Understanding Your Student Account")

To better understand your student account—including charges, payments, refunds, and financial aid activity—you can access your account through BroncoDirect.

Simply Log in to your [MyCPP](https://my.cpp.edu/ "MyCPP") **Student Center**, then navigate to the **Finances** section to view your account details.

### Use Your Waivers

**[Employee/Dependent Fee Waivers](https://www.cpp.edu/eoda-hr/departments/benefits/education/employee-fee-waiver/index.shtml "Employee/Dependent Fee Waivers")**

The Fee Waiver Program is designed to reduce or waive state fees for eligible employees to take classes at a CSU campus in order to improve current job skills/abilities (work-related training) or to obtain the education needed to pursue CSU career goals (career development).
Visit [EODA/Human Resources Operations](https://www.cpp.edu/eoda-hr/departments/benefits/education/employee-fee-waiver/index.shtml "EODA/Human Resources Operations") for more information

[**Fee Waivers**](https://www.calstate.edu/apply/paying-for-college/csu-costs/tuition-and-fees/fee-waivers "CSU Fee Waivers")

California Statement University Waivers are authorized by the California Legislature or the CSU Board of Trustees and may reduce the cost of attendance for eligible students.

**[Third-Party Sponsor Programs](../payment-options/third_party_billing.shtml "Third-Party Sponsor Programs")**

Payment of tuition and fees by third-party sponsors, such as vocational rehabilitation.

* [Veterans Benefits](https://www.cpp.edu/orientation/military-veterans/index.shtml "Veterans Benefits") Deferment of tuition and fees is available until veterans' benefits are received.
* [Cal Vet Fee Waiver](https://www.calstate.edu/attend/student-services/troops-to-college/educational-benefits-for-military-connected-students/Pages/dependents-of-veterans.aspx "Cal Vet Fee Waiver") of fees for eligible dependents of Veterans.

### Sign Up for Direct Deposit

Expecting a refund? Make sure you get your money quickly and securely by signing up for direct deposit.

Whether your refund is from financial aid, a tuition overpayment, or dropping/withdrawing from classes, direct deposit is the safest and fastest way to receive your funds—no waiting, no lost checks.

[**Learn how to sign up for direct deposit**](../payment-options/direct-deposit-for-student-refunds.shtml " Learn how to sign up for direct deposit")

### Download Tuition Statements

Your statement—available through **TouchNet Payment Plan**—provides a detailed breakdown of your total tuition and fees, any previous payments, and your remaining balance. It may also include available payment plan options and their associated terms.

To view your statement:

* Login to [TouchNet Payment Plan Site](https://secure.touchnet.net/C20365_tsa/web/caslogin.jsp "TouchNet Payment Plan Site")
* Go to View Statements
* Select Term
* Submit

For step-by-step instructions, refer to the [**Accessing Your Tuition Statement Guide (PDF)**.](../forms-and-policies/statement-procedure-guide-touchnet.pdf "Accessing Your Tuition Statement Guide (PDF)")

### Send Payer Invite (Transact)

Students can grant a parent, guardian, or other designated payer access to their student account by sending an invitation through the Transact Payment system. To invite a payer, enter their email address in the system. The invited payer will receive an email with instructions to create their own login and access the student’s account.

To begin, log in to your MyCPP Portal and follow the steps provided in the guide below.

[Set Up Payer Invite -Transact (PDF)](../forms-and-policies/set-up-payer-invite-transact.pdf "Set Up Payer Invite (Transact) (PDF)")

### Authorize User (TouchNet)

To authorize someone in **TouchNet Payment Plan Page**, a student (or account holder) must grant access to their account through the **Authorized Users** section. This allows the designated person to view and make payments on the student's behalf.

For step-by-step instructions, refer to the TouchNet Payment Page Setting Up Authorize Users

### Order Parking Permit Online

**Parking Services** offers a convenient online portal where you can **purchase a parking permit**, as well as **view, pay, or appeal** any current parking citations.

For step-by-step instructions on purchasing a permit, please refer to the [How to Order and Pay for Parking Permit (PDF)](../forms-and-policies/how_to_order_and_pay_parking_permit_online.pdf "How to Order and Pay for Parking Permit"), and visit the [Parking Services website](https://www.cpp.edu/parking/index.shtml "parking services website") for more information.

### Access Instant Access Complete (E-Book)

The Instant Access Complete program exists to ensure all Cal Poly Pomona students have all the course materials they need to succeed every semester. One flat charge each semester grants access to the required textbooks/homework systems required for all classes a student is registered. On how to retrieved and or opt out of the Instant Access Complete please visit the [Instant Access Complete by Bronco Bookstore](https://www.broncobookstore.com/instant-access-complete.asp "Instant Access Complete by Bronco Bookstore ") website

### Opt Out Student Involvement and Representation Fee (SIRF) Fee

Student Involvement and Representation Fee (SIRF) of $2.00 is an optional fee that California State University (CSU) students pay to support the Cal State Student Association (CSSA). The fee is $2 per term, and it's assessed twice a year. For more information please visit the [Cal State Student Association (CSSA)](https://calstatestudents.org/sirf/ "Cal State Student Association ").

For instructions, download the [How to Opt out the SIRF fee (PDF)](../forms-and-policies/opt-out-of-sirf-fee-process-guide.pdf "How to Opt out the SIRF fee").

### Add a Family Member to Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)

CPP students can grant parents or other individuals access to their education records by completing the FERPA Authorization to Release form through their BroncoDirect Student Center. Students must specify the type of records that may be released and the time period for which access is granted.

For step-by-step instructions, see the [How to Complete a FERPA Authorization to Release (PDF)](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar//forms/how-to-complete-a-ferpa-student.pdf#how to  "How to Complete a FERPA Authorization to Release (PDF)").

For more information about FERPA and student privacy rights, visit the [Registrar's Office](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/student-records/ferpa.shtml#Registrars "Registrar's Office")  page.

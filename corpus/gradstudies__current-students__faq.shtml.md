# Incoming Graduate Student FAQS

## Coursework/Program-Related FAQs:

Expand/Collapse All Coursework/Program-Related FAQs:

### 1. How much freedom do I have in choosing electives? Can we take more classes than the schedule suggests?

This depends on your master’s program. Please contact your program’s [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) or your academic advisor for guidance.

### 2. Who should graduate students contact to request permission numbers to enroll in undergraduate courses that advisors are requiring us to complete (if applicable)?

Please contact the department offering the course.

### 3. What research opportunities are available for graduate students? How do I get internship opportunities and industry connections and placements?

We recommend that you contact your program’s [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) for guidance. Additionally, you can contact the [Career Center](https://www.cpp.edu/career/index.shtml) or [Innovation Incubator](https://www.cpp.edu/ii/micro-internships/about-micro-internships.shtml) for assistance on internship opportunities and industry connections.

### 4. How do we have access to past syllabi?

As a student, your instructor will add the current syllabus to Canvas. If you would like to see past syllabi for reference and in anticipation of an upcoming course where a syllabus has not been finalized, you should contact the instructor to see if they are willing to provide that. As a graduate teaching assistant, you should have access to your previously taught courses over the course of teaching on Canvas.

### 5. Can graduate students teach undergraduate courses?

Yes, in some instances, depending on the program, graduate students can teach undergraduate courses. Please contact your program’s [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) for guidance.

### 6. Do students buy their thesis or project research related items or does their lab purchase them?

We recommend that you contact your committee chair or academic advisor.

### 7. How many units should I take each semester?

Please contact your program’s [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) or academic advisor for guidance. Also, please note [enrollment conditions required for financial aid](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/graduate/newly-admitted-and-current1/keeping-your-aid.shtml).

### 8. How can I get more involved with my master's program since I have a full-time job?

Depending on your graduate program, there are several on-campus graduate student-led clubs and associations that may be available to you. We recommend reviewing [MyBAR](https://mybar.cpp.edu/) to see if any graduate student clubs pertain to you. We also recommend that you contact your program advisor for more information.

### 9. Can we meet with our specific college/program advisors?

We recommend that you email your [program advisor](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) to set up an appointment.

### 10. When do we register for classes and what classes are we supposed to take the first semester?

We recommend that you check BroncoDirect for your registration appointment. For which courses to take, find your [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml).

### 11. Enrollment for classes that are essential for the program are not appearing on BroncoDirect. How do I enroll in these classes? Will new sections open?

We recommend that you contact your program’s graduate coordinator for guidance. Find your [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml).

### 12. I am on the wait list for a few of my master's courses, and every other one is also wait listed. What does this mean for my first semester?

We recommend that you contact your class instructor or your program’s [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) for guidance.

### 13. When do you go to your graduate program department coordinator vs university graduate studies department?

The graduate coordinator will answer questions specific to your program. The Graduate Studies Office will assist with general information on academic policies, procedures, and graduation requirements.

### 14. Do my classes ever expire?

Yes. They expire after seven years.

## Student Support Services, Campus Amenities, Tips:

Expand/Collapse All Student Support Services, Campus Amenities, Tips:

### 1. What is the parking situation? Where can I park? How do I get a parking permit?

Please go to [Parking Services](https://www.cpp.edu/parking/index.shtml) to purchase a permit and learn more  about designated parking areas.

### 2. Can we use the gym?

As a CPP student you can use the gym. Please visit the [BRIC website](https://asi.cpp.edu/campusrec/members-guests/memberships/) for more membership information.

### 3. Any tips to avoid traffic?

You can visit [Transportation Services](https://www.cpp.edu/transportation/index.shtml) for more information.

### 4. Can I have information on Health & Wellness Resources?

We have The Care Center, Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS), Disability Resource Center, and Student Health Services. You can find these and other resources at the [Health and Wellbeing](https://www.cpp.edu/student-affairs/topics/health.shtml) page.
.

### 5. What are some fun things to do in the area? What are some places to eat and study?

For on- and off- campus tips, see [Nearby Attractions](https://www.cpp.edu/aboutcpp/visitor-information/attractions.shtml).

### 6. How do I get a Bronco ID card?

Please visit the [Bronco One Card](https://broncoonecard.com/bronco-one-card-graduate-credential-students/) website for instructions on how to obtain your Bronco One Card.

### 7. Which clubs/extracurricular activities are available?

Depending on your graduate program, there are on campus student-led clubs and associations that may be available to you. We recommend reviewing [MyBAR](https://mybar.cpp.edu/) to see if any graduate student clubs pertain to you. We also recommend that you contact your [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) or instructors for more information.

### 8. Are there possibilities for graduate students to take part in college athletics?

For intramural sports, visit the [Intramural Sports Leagues](https://asi.cpp.edu/activities/sports-competitions/intramural-sports-leagues/) page. For competitive athletics, please contact staff on the [Bronco Athletics Staff Directory](https://broncoathletics.com/staff-directory) page.

### 9. How can I get a campus tour?

For more information and to schedule a campus tour, please visit: [Campus Tours](https://www.cpp.edu/outreach/tours.shtml).

### 10. Are there any available resources for students who are parents?

For resources and support for parenting students, please visit: [Parenting Student Support Services](https://www.cpp.edu/studentparents/index.shtml)

### 11. Where can I find information about CPT & OPT?

For more information, please visit:

* [Curricular Practical Training (CPT)](https://www.cpp.edu/international/students/u-s-employment/cpt.shtml)
* [Optional Practical Training (OPT)](https://www.cpp.edu/international/students/u-s-employment/types-of-opt.shtml)

### 12. Will parking be waived at orientation?

No, parking will not be waived at orientation. All students who RSVP'd to orientation will receive a follow-up email that will include parking information. Please go to [Parking Services](https://www.cpp.edu/parking/index.shtml) to purchase a permit or go to [Parking Permits Pay Stations (cpp.edu)](https://www.cpp.edu/parking/permits/pay-stations.shtml) to learn about a daily parking permit. The parking fee is $5.00.

### 13. Where can I find information about the Veterans Resource Center and the Cal Vet Fee Waiver?

For any questions you may have related to the Veterans Resource Center, please visit [Veterans Resource Center](https://www.cpp.edu/veterans/).

### 14. Where can I find networking opportunities at Cal Poly Pomona?

You can visit the [Career Center](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/) for networking opportunities. You can also contact gradstudies@cpp.edu to hear more information about our Graduate Peer Mentoring Program.

## Financial-Related FAQs:

Expand/Collapse All Financial-Related FAQs:

### 1. How much are university auxiliary fees?

These can be viewed at the Student Accounting and Cashiering Services: [Graduate Student Tuition and Fees](https://www.cpp.edu/student-accounting/tuition-fees/graduate.shtml)

### 2. How does work study work? Is a job assigned to me or do I apply for a job?

You need to apply like any other job. Log in to [Handshake](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/) (through the Career Center) to view openings. The Financial Aid Office determines if students are eligible for any work-study funds.

### 3. What are some tips on financing grad school and helpful scholarship and grant opportunities?

Please see Financial Aid and Scholarships for an overview: [Graduate Students Office of Financial Aid & Scholarships](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/graduate/index.shtml). For scholarships, there are [CSU Systemwide Scholarships](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/scholarships/csu-systemwide-scholarships.shtml) and [Cal Poly Pomona Scholarships](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/scholarships/cal-poly-pomona-scholarships.shtml).

### 4. How do I accept a loan?

Log into your BroncoDirect Account. On the left-hand side, click on the "Financial" tab. Click on your financial aid package. Scroll to the bottom of the page and find "Accept/Decline Awards" to accept loans. For further assistance, please visit the [Financial Aid Graduate Student site](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/graduate/newly-admitted-and-current1/award-notification.shtml).

### 5. Are there on-campus/off-campus jobs for students? I'm looking for a job while in my program.

Please visit the Career Center for assistance. You can log in to [Handshake](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/) (through the Career Center) to see a listing of on-campus jobs.

### 6. Where can I pay my tuition for this semester?

For any questions you may have related to paying your tuition, please contact SACS at: [Contact Us (cpp.edu)](https://www.cpp.edu/student-accounting/contact_us/index.shtml).

## Orientation FAQs

Expand/Collapse All Orientation FAQs

### 1. Will it be recorded?

Yes

### 2. Will it be strictly virtual?

The orientation will be held in person, however there will be a virtual option for students who cannot attend in person.

### 3. Is this mandatory to attend?

No, but highly recommended.

### 4. Are there any required documents I need to bring to orientation?

No

### 5. Can I bring a plus one to orientation?

We do not allow for plus ones to attend orientation due to space limitations.

### 6. Is there a specific attire we need to be aware of for orientation?

Business casual is recommended, but not required. Please dress how you feel most comfortable.

### 7. Will there be gluten-free options at orientation?

If you need to make a special dietary request, please email gradstudies@cpp.edu and we will do our best to accommodate you.

### 8. Is my department orientation different than the CPP Welcome Orientation?

8. Yes, this is a general university orientation, not program specific. Please consult with your [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) on any questions you have about your specific department orientation.

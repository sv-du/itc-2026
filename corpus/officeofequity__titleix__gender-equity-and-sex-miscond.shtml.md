# Title IX: Gender Equity & Sexual Misconduct

## What is Title IX?

**Title IX ensures a safe, equitable, and inclusive educational environment for all.**
 Under federal law, Title IX prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex in any education program or activity receiving federal funding. This includes protection from sexual harassment, sexual assault, dating and domestic violence, stalking, and unequal treatment in academics or extracurricular activities.

Cal Poly Pomona is committed to upholding Title IX by promoting fairness, respect, and accountability. This website provides information about your rights, reporting options, supportive resources, and our procedures for addressing Title IX concerns.

If you have questions or need support, help is available.

## Gender Equity & Sexual Misconduct

### Gender Equity & Sexual Misconduct

The [Interim CSU Nondiscrimination Policy (Nondiscrimination Policy)](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/16328404/latest) is the CSU policy that incorporates the University’s compliance with Title IX and California state law. The Nondiscrimination Policy includes, but is not limited to, the prohibition of discrimination based on sex (including Sex Stereotyping or Sex Characteristics), gender, gender identity and expression, pregnancy or related conditions, and sexual orientation.

The above policy applies to reports or complaints involving alleged conduct that occurred on or after August 1, 2024.

Related to this policy there are sets of procedures for handling formal complaints that fall within the scope of the Nondiscrimination Policy.

The procedures are as follows:

1. [Interim CSU Nondiscrimination Policy - Student Respondent Procedures (effective March 4, 2025)](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/16328290/latest/)
2. [Interim CSU Nondiscrimination Policy - Employee or Third-Party Respondent Procedures](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/16328171/latest/) (effective March 4, 2025)

The procedures set forth in the Nondiscrimination Policy are administrative in nature and are separate and distinct from the criminal and civil legal systems. Pursuing resolution through these procedures does not preclude someone from pursuing legal action now or in the future (noting that statute of limitations may exist for both civil and criminal causes of action). The University’s administrative procedures and the legal system work independently from one another. An individual who reports conduct that may constitute a policy violation and/or a crime may opt to pursue none, one, or some combination of the following processes: the University’s administrative process, making a criminal complaint, and/or making a civil complaint.

To view the **Notice of Non-Discrimination on the Basis of Gender or Sex**and additional resources, please visit the [Discrimination, Harassment and Retaliation Web Page.](https://www.cpp.edu/officeofequity/discrimination-harassment-retaliation/index.shtml)

### When to contact the Title IX Coordinator

A person should contact the Title IX Coordinator or Deputy Title IX Coordinator to:

* Seek information or training about [rights and available actions](https://www.cpp.edu/officeofequity/documents/rights-options_victims.pdf) to resolve reports or complaints involving potential sex discrimination, including sexual misconduct;
* [File a complaint or make a report](https://cm.maxient.com/reportingform.php?CalPolyPomona&layout_id=8) of sex discrimination, including sexual misconduct;
* Notify the university of an incident, policy or procedure that may raise potential Title IX concerns;
* Get information about available resources (including confidential resources) and support services relating to sex discrimination, including sexual misconduct; or
* Ask questions about Cal Poly Pomona’s policies and procedures related to sex discrimination, including sexual misconduct.

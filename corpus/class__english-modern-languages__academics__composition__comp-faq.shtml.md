# Frequently Asked Questions

## GE English Composition & Critical Thinking

If your question isn't addressed below, contact the Composition Coordinator, Dr. Kristin Prins, at kkprins@cpp.edu from your CPP email. Include your Bronco ID number in the body of the email, along with your question.

Expand all

### I am confused about my General Education requirements. Help?

In fall 2025, CPP (along with the rest of the CSU, California Community Colleges, and UC schools) adopted a new General Education pattern. Learn more at the Office of Student Success' [General Education Guide](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/ge.shtml).

### What is the Directed Self-Placement (DSP) survey?

Please see the [Directed Self-Placement page](comp-dsp.shtml) for an explanation.

### What is the difference between Stretch Composition (ENG 1100 + 1101 or ENG 1100M + 1101M) and First-Year Composition (ENG 1103 or 1103M)?

Please see the [Composition Courses page](comp-courses.shtml) for an explanation.

### What is an "M" section (ENG 1100M, 1101M, 1103M)?

Sections with an "M" designation are specially designed for multilingual speakers. These sections have the same kinds of readings and assignments as the monolingual sections (ENG 1100, 1101, 1103). The major difference is that they are taught by faculty with a strong background in linguistics and language acquisition, in addition to rhetoric and writing.

Students whose [DSP results](comp-dsp.shtml) indicate taking an "M" section should do so. Students whose results do not indicate taking an "M" section should **not** do so.

### I think that my DSP results are wrong. What should I do?

The [DSP](comp-dsp.shtml) factors in your previous coursework, standardized test scores, CSU multiple measures placement category, and answers to survey questions about your linguistic background and experiences with and attitudes about reading and writing in English.

We recommend enrolling in the indicated course and, during the first two weeks of the semester, considering whether the pace and the content of the course are a good fit for you. Discuss placement questions with your instructor once they have read your writing.

However, if you have final grades or standardized test scores that haven't been sent to CPP (and so aren't factored into your DSP score), please have them sent to Admissions as soon as possible. Once these are verified, we can update your DSP results.

### My DSP results indicate that I should take a class that is different from what is listed on my major's curriculum sheet or roadmap. What should I do?

If your major's curriculum sheet or roadmap indicate that you should take ENG 1103, but your DSP results indicate taking ENG 1103M or Stretch Composition (ENG 1100 + 1101 or 1100M + ENG 1101M), take what is recommended on the DSP. The curriculum sheet or roadmap is using "ENG 1103" to stand in for "GE 1A," but the way you complete the GE 1A requirement in English Composition should be tailored to you. This is what the DSP does.

An [advisor in your major](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/directories/advising-directory.shtml) will be able to help you plot out a new version of your roadmap to make sure you stay on course to graduation.

### I think that I completed the GE 1A requirement in English Composition through AP credit, dual enrollment credit, IB, or community college course, but my score or transfer credit isn't yet processed by CPP. What should I do?

First, please confirm with an advisor in the [Bronco Advising Center](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/bronco-advising-center/index.shtml) that your test score or course credit does meet CPP's criteria for fulfilling the GE 1A requirement.

If it does and you are getting emails about the [DSP](comp-dsp.shtml), feel free to delete and ignore them. You will be taken off the reminder list once your score or course credit is in our system.

If it does and you are planning to register for a fall GE 1B course during Summer Orientation, you will be able to work with a registration advisor during orientation to get any permission codes needed for you to enroll.

### I did not successfully complete my ENG 1100 or 1100M class. What should I do?

First, retake and pass ENG 1100 or 1100M (whichever your [DSP](comp-dsp.shtml) results indicate). Make good use of your instructor's office hours, meet regularly with tutors in the [Writing Center](https://www.cpp.edu/lrc/our-services/writing-center.shtml), and access any other [student support services](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/directories/index.shtml) that could help you be successful at CPP.

Then, when you have successfully completed Stretch Composition I, take ENG 1101 or 1101M (whichever your DSP results indicate) to complete the GE 1A requirement in English Composition. You may **not** take ENG 1103 or 1103M to complete the requirement.

### I want to take ENG 1100M, 1101M, or 1103M over the summer, but I only see ENG 1100, 1101, or 1103 offered. What should I do?

Summer is the **only** time when students whose DSP results indicate that they should take an "M" section are eligible to take a non-"M" section (ENG 1100, 1101, or 1103). This is because summer sections are taught by faculty who also teach "M" sections — and so are well prepared to support your further development as a writer.

### I started Composition when CPP was on the quarter system. What does that mean in the semester system?

On the quarter system, we had three sequences for completing what was then the GE A2 requirement in Written Communication:

1. Extended Stretch Composition: ENG 100 + 106 + 107 (3 quarters)
2. Stretch Composition: ENG 108 + 109 (2 quarters)
3. First-Year Composition: ENG 110 (1 quarter)

Our multilingual sections were designated by a section number in the 30s.

If you partially completed the Extended Stretch or Stretch sequence, please contact the Composition Coordinator, Dr. Kristin Prins, at kkprins@cpp.edu with questions about placement in our current sequences.

### I took ENG 105: Freshman English II or ENG 130: Freshman English II on the quarter system. What is that class in the semester system?

ENG 105 and 130 are the old course numbers for what then fulfilled GE A3: Critical Thinking. The course is now ENG 2105: Written Reasoning, and it fulfills (as of fall 2025) GE 1B: Critical Thinking.

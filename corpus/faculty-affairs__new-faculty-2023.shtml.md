### Nita Baxani

![](img/new-faculty-2023/nbaxani.jpg)

Music

#### Education

* Ed.D. Columbia University/Teachers College
* Master of Music Manhattan School of Music
* Bachelor of Music Virginia Commonwealth Universityh

#### Research Interests

My scholarly interests center around the role of the psychobiological needs of human beings, with a
particular emphasis on the sense of belonging and play in the music classroom of young children and youth focusing on exploring a community of infants as agents of their own learning within a musical space.

Some highlights:
I've documented infant musical behaviors, creatively imagining them as narratives. Examining infants during play, I interpreted the music-making of children as acts in a narrative musical stage performance.

I've studied what issues of power are raised when studying children at play in contemporary societies, specifically in terms of race, gender, class, ability, and age.

For my dissertation, I continued my observation of young children’s music behaviors and their functions for 7 infants (6-20 months) in home, school, and music class. My aims were to investigate the ways in which infants are musical, how the musical behaviors of infants compare in different settings, and the possible functions that the behaviors serve.

Invited by the Metropolitan Opera in New York City, I and my colleagues conducted research on
Bambin-O, an opera for babies, that combines art, live performance, and infants and their caregivers.

My colleague (in Cyprus) and I currently work with young children and their caregivers and we are currently analyzing data where we are identifying the routines and music making practices that families engage in with their children in the different cultures.

I'm currently involved in curriculum development for Colburn School's Early Childhood Creative Arts Program, as well as LA Philharmonic's Youth Orchestra of Los Angeles program where I am a member of the curriculum task force.

As my instrument is a Full Lyric Soprano voice, I continue to perform when I can.

# Timeline

## 2010 to Now

### Spring 2026

Lanterman Project Advances with Phase I Market Analysis from [Keyser Marston Associates (PDF)](pdfs/cpp-lanterman-market-analysis-final-1-14-26.pdf)

### Fall 2025

On Oct. 31, 2025, CPP issued a Request for Proposals (RFP) for a firm to develop the Conceptual Land Use Plan/Utility Assessment; submissions by interested parties are due by Wednesday, December 3.

### Spring 2025

Kosmont Financial Services to issue report.

### October 2024

CPP selects Kosmont Financial Services to prepare report on the public options available to the university.

### July 2024

CPP issues RFP for financial consultant to explore public financing options to enable to the development of the property.

### December 2023

CPP and ERG agree to pause negotiations as financial market conditions changed due to the pandemic.

### 2022-2023

CPP and ERG negotiate terms to develop and finance the project.

### August 2021

CPP selects Edgewood Realty & Greystar as new master developer.

### April 28, 2021

RFP Addendum #5 Issued.

### April 8, 2021

RFP Addendum #4 Issued.

### March 12, 2021

RFP Addendum #3 Issued.

### February 11, 2021

RFP Addendum #2 Issued, and RFP Interested Parties List Posted.

### January 22, 2021

RFP Addendum #1 Issued.

### January 11, 2021

Request for Proposal Issued.

### May 2020

Restart Master Developer Procurement Process.

### April 2020

Initial Master Developer Withdraws

Cal Poly Pomona was engaged in discussions with FivePoint as a master developer to partner in revitalizing the former Lanterman site into a thriving live, learn, work, and play community.   However, FivePoint informed the university that it could no longer serve as the development partner, due to senior leadership changes within its organization.

### July 2017

CPP retains jurisdiction of property

Cal Poly Pomona informed the CSU Board of Trustees of the decision to retain and develop the Lanterman site and said the campus would issue a Request for Qualifications/Request for Proposals to engage a master developer or team of developers to partner with the campus.

The CSU sent a letter to the Department of Finance indicating that it would retain jurisdiction of the property in support of its higher educational mission.

### January 2017

HOK selected to advise on feasibility

Cal Poly Pomona selects HOK Inc., a predevelopment real estate firm with national and international experience in land-use issues, to advise on the feasibility of developing the Lanterman property. [[3]](https://www-test.cpp.edu/lanterman/timeline.shtml#_ftn3)

[[3]](https://www-test.cpp.edu/lanterman/timeline.shtml#_ftnref3) <https://www.cpp.edu/news/content/2017/01/university-selects-predevelopment-firm-for-lanterman-property/index.shtml>

### October 2016

Urban Land Institute releases study

The Urban Land Institute, retained by Cal Poly Pomona, released a preliminary study of findings and recommendations for the Lanterman property with a focus on historic preservation, housing potential, financing and pertinent approaches to development.

### July 2016

MOU executed

The California Department of Finance and the CSU executed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) indicating the intent to transfer the Lanterman property to the CSU to support its educational mission and stating that the CSU will undertake development planning of the Lanterman property.[[4]](https://www-test.cpp.edu/lanterman/timeline.shtml#_ftn1)  The Department of Finance agreed that the university could assess the site and determine by September 1, 2017 if it could be developed given the historical district constraints.

[[4]](https://www-test.cpp.edu/lanterman/timeline.shtml#_ftnref1) <https://www.cpp.edu/lanterman/pdfs/pages-from-july-24-2018-fin.pdf>

### July 2015

Jurisdiction of property transferred to CSU

Jurisdiction of the Lanterman Development Center property was transferred from the State Department of Development Services to the California State University to further the mission of Cal Poly Pomona. [[5]](#_ftn1)

[[5]](#_ftnref1) <https://www.cpp.edu/lanterman/pdfs/csu-letter-to-ca-dof.pdf>

### May 2015

Chancellor authorized to negotiate MOU

The CSU Board of Trustees authorized the chancellor to negotiate and execute the terms of an MOU with the state to accept interest in nearly 300 acres of real property, known as the Lanterman Development Center.

### December 2014

Lanterman Development Center Closed

The California Department of Developmental Services closed the Lanterman Development Center.

### September 2014

State declares Lanterman surplus property

The State of California declared the Lanterman property to be surplus state real property, and the Department of General Services (DGS) was authorized to transfer jurisdiction of the land to a state agency that would use the property responsibly to serve the best interests of the state.[[6]](#_ftn1)

[[6]](#_ftnref1) [http://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill\_id=201320140SB944](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=201320140SB944)

### February 2010

State holds hearings on closing Lanterman

The California Department of Development Services held public hearings on the recommendation to close the Lanterman Development Center.

### January 2010

Agency declares intention to close Lanterman

The California Department of Development Services indicated to the Legislature its intention to close the Lanterman Development Center.[[8]](#_ftn1)

[[8]](#_ftnref1) https://www.dds.ca.gov/LantermanNews/docs/LDCProposedClosureLetter\_Legislature.pdf

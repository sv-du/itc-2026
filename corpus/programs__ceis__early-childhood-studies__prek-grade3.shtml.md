## The Polytechnic Approach

As an elementary education transfer, you will dive right into courses and fieldwork relevant to your degree. You will build upon your fundamental courses by learning the sociocultural contexts surrounding and supporting young children, then apply your knowledge. Through an intersectional approach to teaching, direct classroom experience with a mentor teacher, service learning, practicum and more, you will kickstart young children’s academic, social and emotional growth and prepare for your dream career in pre-K to elementary education.

### Preschool Teacher

Care for and educate young children and promote their social, emotional and cognitive development. Plan activities and lessons to encourage their exploration, creativity and growth as they prepare for kindergarten.

### Kindergarten Teacher

Introduce five- to six-year-old children to the basics of education, and promote their academic, social and emotional development. Teach foundational skills, track students’ growth and help children adjust to a school setting.

### Elementary School Teacher

Teach children in grades K-3 and provide foundational education in subjects like reading, math, social studies and science. Plan and deliver lessons, assess students’ progress, communicate with parents and prepare students for middle school and future learning.

## Courses

The Pre-K to Grade 3 option is specifically for ADT elementary teacher education transfers. This option helps you as a transfer student obtain the competencies to become a transitional kindergarten (TK) teacher. You will receive a combination of teacher preparation (e.g., ADT in elementary teacher education) and early childhood development coursework. The culminating course requires you to complete 60 hours in a transitional kindergarten classroom where they can apply what you have learned in your courses as you interact with children and families under the supervision of a mentor teacher and faculty member.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

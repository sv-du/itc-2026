# FAQs

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I get a permission number?

**The CLASS Student Success Center cannot issue permission numbers for any courses.**

The department who offers the course you’re trying to add will give you a permission number if possible, and only if you meet the prerequisites for the course. You can request a permission number from the instructor of the course, if they are listed on BroncoDirect, or the department's staff. You will find contact information by navigating to the appropriate college and department website and viewing their "Faculty & Staff" section.

Make sure to provide them with your Bronco ID number and the class/section number of the course you're requesting a permission number for.

### What is academic advising?

Academic advisors provide an equitable and inclusive environment where you can discuss academic, career, and personal goals, ask questions and get resources, and develop the skills that will support your success at CPP and beyond.

Advisors will assist with curriculum, degree/college requirements, graduation requirements, and university policies and procedures. They can also help you to think about your personal, academic, and career goals, and explore the steps to get you there. Lastly, they will serve as an advocate and liaison. They may refer you to other campus programs for assistance.

There are three resources on campus for academic advising:

* The [Bronco Advising Center](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/bronco-advising-center/index.shtml) is a campus-wide center where you can get help with registration, academic records, financial aid, student accounting, and general questions.
* The [CLASS Student Success Center](index.shtml) is the college's advising center where you can get help reviewing your course requirements, planning for graduation, and more.
* The department/program advisors in your major/option are the experts in your specific major and can help you plan your major courses and discuss internships, research, career tracks, and graduate programs.

### What’s the difference between my CLASS Student Success Center advisor and my department/program advisor?

**Department/Program advisors** are faculty members (professors in your department) who are the experts in your specific major and can advise you on major-specific courses, internships, research, career tracks, and graduate programs.

**Student Success advisors** are staff members (full-time advisors) who are supplemental to your department faculty advisor and can assist with any other academic questions or refer you to campus resources. Check out the [Helpful Resources](resources.shtml) section on our website.

### Who is my Student Success Center advisor?

CLASS Student Success Center advisors are assigned by major and, in some cases, by last name. Your advisor may be found in the "Program Advisor" section of your Student Center on BroncoDirect or the "Your Student Success Team" section on CPP Connect.

### Who is my department/program advisor?

Each department assigns their faculty advisors differently. Your advisor may be found in the "Program Advisor" section of your Student Center on BroncoDirect, the "Your Student Success Team" section on CPP Connect. Reach out to your department's staff person if you're unsure who your advisor is.

### Who do I talk to if my Degree Progress Report (DPR) on BroncoDirect is incorrect?

Our advisors can review your DPR and work with the Registrar's Office on fixing any discrepancies.

You can schedule an appointment for this on [CPP Connect](schedule-appointment.shtml).

### Who do I talk to if my Transfer Credit Report (TCR) on BroncoDirect is incorrect, or if I am not receiving credit for a course I took at another school?

Our advisors can review your TCR and work with the Registrar's Office on fixing any discrepancies. They can also help you research articulation agreements (how courses are matched) with other institutions, which can be found on [assist.org](https://assist.org/). If a course does not articulate (does not have a match), an advisor may be able help you submit a petition to the appropriate department for approval.

You can schedule an appointment for these on [CPP Connect](schedule-appointment.shtml).

### How do I take courses at a Community College for credit at CPP?

You can visit the Student Success Central Guide [Taking Courses at a Community College](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/community-college.shtml) for more information about the process. Remember, you can only satisfy lower-division requirements (1000-2000 level courses) with classes from a Community College.

### How do I check the status of my petition?

You can contact us via any method available on our homepage. You will be asked for your Bronco ID number and must specify what petition(s) you're referring to.

### How do I know if I’m ready to graduate?

We strongly recommend scheduling a "Degree Progress Review" appointment with your CLASS Student Success Center advisor through [CPP Connect](https://www-test.cpp.edu/class/student-success-center/schedule-appointment.shtml "https://www-test.cpp.edu/class/student-success-center/schedule-appointment.shtml"). They will let you know what courses/requirements you have left to complete and when you should think about applying. More information about graduation can be found on the [Steps to Senior Success website](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/senior-success.shtml "Steps to Senior Success website").

### How do I apply for graduation?

Apply for graduation through BroncoDirect for the term you plan to complete your final courses/requirements. [The Registrar's Office website](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/graduation/applying-for-graduation.shtml "The Registrar's Office website") has more information on graduation. You can determine if you have already applied by checking the status area at the top of your Degree Progress Report (DPR).

# Frequently Asked Questions

## If you are a student

seeking support services, or resources from Counseling and Psychological Services, please let us know by telephone at (909) 869-3220 during our normal business hours.  Have your Bronco ID number ready when calling.
Counseling and Psychological Services offers a variety of counseling services to help students adjust to college life, cope with personal stressors, and address psychological concerns.  Our services include workshops, group or individual therapy, wellbeing coaching, crisis support, and case management/resource assistance.
Please review our frequently asked questions to better understand the work our department does for students.

### Where is CAPS located?

**Counseling and Psychological Services** is located in Building 66 (Bronco Bookstore Building) Room 116.  Visit our [Contacting CAPS](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/about/contacting-caps.shtml) page to learn more about our location.

### Is this where I talk to someone about academic counseling, or what academic programs the university offers, or what class(es) I should take?

**Counseling and Psychological Services** is often confused with academic counseling.  If you are seeking academic assistance visit the [Bronco Advising Center](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/bronco-advising-center/) for anything dealing with advising or academic resources.

### Can I get a psychological evaluation for a mental health disability or for a learning disability?

Our therapists provide different methods of evaluating for certain mental health concerns.  For disability concerns - including learning disabilities - please visit the [Disability Resource Center](https://www.cpp.edu/drc/)'s website.  Students who believe they have a disability but have not been tested should complete a DRC application to meet with an Access Specialist who can make referrals for testing locations off campus.  **Cal Poly Pomona** does not provide testing for mental health or learning disabilities.

### What kind of services does CAPS provide?

CAPS services include individual therapy, groups, outreach events, and workshops.  Learn more about each of these in Our Services page.

### Why would I use counseling services?

Although family and friends can be a source of support, their relationships with you can make it difficult for them to provide unbiased feedback.  When personal issues interfere with your ability to concentrate and succeed in college, professional counseling can offer an unbiased perspective, support, guidance, and access to resources as you develop new skills, explore options, and solve problems.

### Am I eligible to receive counseling services? Is there a fee?

To be eligible, **Cal Poly Pomona** students must be enrolled during the semester they receive counseling services.  All counseling services are offered at no additional charge to enrolled **Cal Poly Pomona** students as services are funded by the mandatory student health fee already paid through your tuition.  Meanwhile you are enrolled, any [Student Health & Wellbeing](https://www.cpp.edu/healthwellbeing/) campus resources are offered at no additional charge.

### What about privacy and confidentiality?

Seeking counseling is a private and confidential process.  Information disclosed in counseling may not be revealed to anyone outside of CAPS without a client's written permission, unless there is an immediate safety issue.

Standards such as HIPAA compliance continue to guide best practices even in remote working conditions.  Therapists will also consider the implications of Executive Orders impacting laws and procedures for clinical practice in the State of California (e.g., EO N-43-20) that impact consent, confidentiality, and best practices guidelines under "good faith" provisions.

### How do I get started?

You may begin the process by [Making an Appointment](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/our-services/making-an-appointment.shtml) for a screening.  The screening will determine your individual needs and treatment plan.  To schedule an appointment contact us or stop by our office during our [normal business hours](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/about/contacting-caps.shtml).

### How many sessions do I get?

Short-term individual therapy is defined as approximately 6 to 8 sessions per academic year and is most effective with students who are motivated, are open to suggestions and willing to work on them, and are able to self-reflect.  Many students may meet their treatment goals in under six sessions while others may need a few more.  The number of sessions is determined by the clinician/therapist and the current treatment plan.

### Does CAPS provide support letter for drop/withdrawal and retroactive withdrawal from classes?

CAPS only provides support letters for withdrawal or retroactive withdrawal to past/current clients who have been actively involved in treatment or services.  We are not able to provide support letters to students who have never been clients at CAPS.  Please visit the [Care Center](https://www.cpp.edu/broncoscarecenter/) for assistance in obtaining withdrawal or support letters.

### What's the difference between a Workshop and a Group?

Even though some students are nervous at first about joining a workshop or group, many discover that it is the best approach for their particular concerns.  Workshops are designed to provide innovative, strengths-based, practical interventions to help learn tools and coping strategies to deal with thoughts, feelings, and life circumstances.  Workshops may also help reflect more on what one wants to get out of therapy.

Group counseling is a form of counseling in which a small number of people meet together under the guidance of professionally trained therapists to experience growth and change in one's life.  In groups, one can share common problems, safely interact with others, learn about oneself, and help others learn about themselves.  Each semester a new list of [workshops](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/our-services/workshops.shtml) or [groups](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/our-services/groups.shtml) is available at CAPS.

### Who are the therapists?

The [CAPS staff](../about/our-staff.shtml) are a culturally diverse group of highly qualified mental health professionals including licensed doctoral-level psychologists and licensed marriage and family therapists.

### How do I request a therapist to come speak at my class/club/fraternity/sorority/dorm?

To get more information about having one of our professional staff come speak at your organization, please visit our [CAPS Outreach](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/our-services/outreach.shtml) page.  Please contact caps@cpp.edu and we will do our best to accommodate you and your event.  We encourage students, staff, and faculty to let us know what topics they would like to explore.  Please submit requests at least three (3) weeks in advance to ensure a timely fulfillment of your request.

### Are there services available after the office is closed?

Our general services are available during normal business hours, Monday thru Friday, 8:00 a.m. thru 5:00 p.m.  After-hours crisis support is available after normal business hours, 5:00 p.m. thru 8:00 a.m., and all day Saturday and Sunday.  You can access our after-hours crisis support by dialing (909) 869-3220, and pressing Option 2 when prompted.  Be advised that after-hours crisis support should not be used for appointments or for general services inquiries.

### For faculty, staff, and families ... I am concerned about my student. What can I do to help them?

If you believe your student is in immediate danger, or if you require immediate assistance for your student, please contact the University Police Department by dialing (909) 869-3070.

The [Care Center](https://www.cpp.edu/broncoscarecenter/) is the first place to send students who are in distress or are of concern.  Case managers will connect students to resources they need to succeed, support them through crises, provide advocacy, and offer basic needs services.  Please refer back to the [CAPS main page](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/), scroll down, click on the [Refer a Student](https://cm.maxient.com/reportingform.php?CalPolyPomona&layout_id=7) link provided, and fill out the form with as much information as possible.

The Refer a Student of Concern process may take some weeks to complete in order to determine the best course of action to take.  Meanwhile, visit our [Crisis Assistance](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/crisis/crisis-faculty-staff-families.shtml) page designed for student assistance from faculty, staff, or families.

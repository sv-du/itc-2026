# FAQs

## General Information

### Who is eligible to apply for RISE?

* **RISE Up:** Rising 9th and 10th grade California high school students.
* **RISE Above:** Rising 11th and 12th grade California high school students.
* **RISE Beyond:** First-time, incoming freshmen admitted to Cal Poly Pomona.

### How do I apply to the RISE program?

The application for RISE is open now! Apply to the program via [this link](https://engage.cpp.edu/register/RISE2026).

Applications are open through May 15, 2026, with a priority deadline of April 27, 2026.

### Is there a cost to attend RISE?

No, RISE is free for all participants. The program covers housing, meals, workshops and excursions. Students may bring spending money to purchase CPP gear at the bookstore or additional snacks available at markets across campus.

### How will I know if I am accepted into the program?

Once applications are reviewed, accepted students will receive an official email notification with next steps, including required forms and program logistics.

### Can I bring my family to the RISE program?

Parents, family members and supporters are welcome to help students settle into their room in the residence halls and attend designated family events on the first-day of the program. However, RISE is a student-focused experience and family members will not be allowed to stay for the program’s duration.

## Program Logistics

### What should I pack for the program?

Students should bring:

* Clothing appropriate for the weather (it is very hot in Pomona during the summer)
* Toiletries
* Tennis shoes
* Sunscreen
* Personal medications

### What should I wear during RISE?

Students should wear comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing. A RISE T-shirt will be provided and should be worn for specific activities.

### Where will I eat during the program?

Meals will be provided at Centerpointe Dining Commons, Cal Poly Pomona’s dining hall. Some meals may also be catered for special events.

### What type of housing accommodations will be provided?

Students will stay in on-campus housing in shared rooms based on gender identity. Each room will have a bed and desk for each participant. In addition, all participants will be provided with the following items within the residential halls:

* Fitted Sheet
* 1 Pillowcase
* 1 Bath Towel
* 1 Hand Towel
* 1 Pillow
* 1 Blanket

### Will I have a roommate?

Yes, students will be assigned a roommate of the same gender identity. Rooms will be assigned to students upon checking-in for the program.

### What if my student has dietary restrictions or food allergies?

Upon being accepted into the program, students will be asked to fill out a registration form. Please indicate any dietary restrictions on the form. We work closely with dining services to provide meal options for all students.

### Will students have access to laundry facilities?

Yes, laundry facilities are available in the residence halls. However, since the program is only a few days, most students will not need to do laundry.

## Safety & Supervision

### What measures are in place to ensure student safety?

RISE has strict supervision policies, including designated staff, resident assistants and security measures to ensure students’ well-being at all times.

### Am I allowed to leave campus during the program?

No. For safety reasons, students must remain on campus except for official off-campus excursions.

### Will there be program staff available 24/7?

Yes, program staff and residential assistants will be on-site at all times to support students.

### In case of an emergency, how can parents reach their student?

Emergency contact information will be provided before the program begins.

## Program Experience

### What types of activities will I participate in?

RISE students engage in interactive workshops, campus tours, team-building activities, leadership development and social experiences with other students.

### What are RISE workshops?

RISE workshops introduce students to campus resources, student organizations, and academic and career topics to help them prepare for college success.

### Will I have free time?

Yes, the schedule includes structured activities as well as downtime for socializing and relaxing in designated campus areas.

### Can I bring spending money?

Money is not required, but students may bring personal funds for optional purchases both on-campus as well as during off-campus excursions.

### Can I leave RISE before the program ends?

No, students are expected to stay for the entire program duration. If an emergency arises, parents/guardians/supporters must contact RISE staff.

### Will participation in RISE help with college admission?

While RISE does not guarantee admission, participation can strengthen students’ college applications and prepare them for success in the college admissions process.

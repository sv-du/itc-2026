# FAQs

## FAQs

Expand/Collapse All FAQs

### What is a Credential Program?

A Credential Program is a program of professional coursework (including Clinical Practice) which develops the skills needed for serving in the classroom.  This includes coursework in areas such as teaching methodologies, curriculum development and classroom management.

### Which Credential Programs are offered at Cal Poly Pomona?

Cal Poly Pomona offers three Basic Teaching Credentials, two Specialist Credentials, two Certification Programs as well as one Advanced Credential.

**Basic Teaching Credentials:**

* Education Specialist Credential (Special Education): Options are available in Mild/Moderate Disabilities and Moderate/Severe Disabilities
* Multiple Subject Credential (Elementary School)
* Single Subject Credentials (Middle and High School) available in: Agriculture; Art; English; Physical Education; Mathematics (currently there are two options available); Music; Science (currently there are nine options available); Social Science; and World Languages (Spanish).

**Specialist Credentials:**

* Agriculture Specialist Credential - May be added onto a Single Subject Credential in Agriculture.
* Adapted Physical Education Added Authorization- May be added onto a Single Subject Credential in Physical Education, Special Education or onto a Multiple Subject Credential.

**Certifications:**

* Bilingual Authorization Certification- Available in Cantones, Mandarin and Spanish. May be added onto any Single Subject, Multiple Subject or Education Specialist Credential at Cal Poly Pomona.

**Advanced Credentials:**

* Administrative Services Credential(Principals/School Administration) - Requires a Basic Teaching or Services Credential and five years of relevant experience.

### Are there any exams required?

There are several options available in lieu of some of the most common exams:

* [Basic Skills Requirement](https://www.ctc.ca.gov/credentials/leaflets/basic-skills-requirement-%28cl-667%29)
* [Subject Matter Competence](https://engage.cpp.edu/register/?id=a6281279-9b73-428a-92b5-76039a63a4ba)
* [Reading Instruction Competence Assessment (RICA)\*](https://www.cpp.edu/ceis/credential-program/exams-and-subject-matter.shtml)

\*It is important to note that the RICA is the only exam without an alternate option at this time. In addition, it is not required for candidates seeking the Single Subject credential.

***Verification of Subject Matter competence is required for admission into the Credential Programs.***

### What is the Basic Skills Requirement? Do I need it?

The Basic Skills Requirement is mandatory for all educators. Competence in basic reading, writing and math skills must be met by providing verification of the [Basic Skills Requirement](https://www.ctc.ca.gov/credentials/leaflets/basic-skills-requirement-%28cl-667%29).

### What is Subject Matter Competence? Do I need it?

All educators must show comprehensive knowledge and expertise in the subject areas they will be teaching. A variety of options are available to meet this requirement. Verification of the [Subject Matter Competence](https://www.ctc.ca.gov/educator-prep/subject-matter-requirements) requirement is required for program admission.

### What is the RICA? Do I need to take it?

The Reading Instruction Competence Assessment (RICA) is an examination designed to ensure that prospective teachers have acquired the knowledge and skills needed to provide effective reading instruction for K-8 students.  Passage of the RICA is currently required for the Multiple Subject Credential and for the Education Specialist Credential.  For more information on the RICA please visit [http://www.ctcexams.nesinc.com/.](http://www.ctcexams.nesinc.com/)

### How long does it take to earn a Basic Teaching Credential?

It can take one to two years to earn a Basic Teaching Credential. Most students prefer to take some more time and complete the program in two to three years.  Interns are allowed a maximum of two years to complete the program, once issued a University Internship Credential.

### What is an Intern?

An Intern is someone who is employed full-time as a teacher, at a California public school and is concurrently completing a Credential Program.  Interns complete the same requirements as standard students, just in a different manner.  For more information on the Intern Programs at Cal Poly Pomona, please view the requirements here: <https://www.cpp.edu/~ceis/credential-program/teacher-intern.shtml>[.](https://www.cpp.edu/~ceis/education/credential-programs/programs/internships.shtml)

### Where can I see a list of the classes and requirements to earn a Credential?

This information is available here: [https://www.cpp.edu/~ceis/credential-program/index.shtml.](https://www.cpp.edu/~ceis/credential-program/index.shtml)

### How much does it cost per semester? Is there Financial Aid available to me?

For the most current fee information, and for Financial Aid options, please visit the Financial Aid website: <https://www.cpp.edu/~financial-aid/index.shtml>.  Additionally, there is the TEACH Grant available: [https://www.cpp.edu/ceis/credential-program/scholarships.shtml](scholarships.shtml).

### What type of support is available to me while I earn my Credential?

There are a variety of support services available to students at Cal Poly Pomona. Faculty Advisors are assigned to students, according to credential type. Students will be directed to meet with their Advisors for program planning. Additionally, there is a staff Advisor available, so students may obtain general information and assistance. The Credential Services offices also has Credential Analysts, Clinical Practice Staff and a Coordinator available to assist.

### How do I apply to the Credential Program at Cal Poly Pomona?

There are several steps to take in order to join the Credential Program at Cal Poly Pomona:

* Apply to the University online Cal State Apply and complete the **Graduate Application.**
* Apply to the Credential Program. Program requirements and the application can be accessed here: [https://www.cpp.edu/ceis/credential-program/admission/program-application.shtml](admission/program-application.shtml).

### What is the CTC?

CTC is an abbreviation for the **California Commission on Teacher Credentialing**; a state agency that administers legislation governing the preparation of teachers in California, sets standards for candidates and programs and ultimately issues credentials. The CTC website ([www.ctc.ca.gov)](http://www.ctc.ca.gov) has comprehensive information available and students as well as educators are encouraged to visit it frequently for updates and important information.

### Is your program accredited?

Yes! The Credential Programs offered at Cal Poly Pomona, are fully accredited by the **California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CTC**).

### What other authorizations can be added onto my Credential?

There are several authorizations available to add onto an existing Credential.  The most popular authorizations are:

* Supplemental Authorizations
* Subject Matter Authorizations
* Added Teaching Subjects or Areas (Additional Credentials)

Due to the differences in requirements for each of the above categories, please visit the **California Commission on Teacher Credentialing** **(CTC)** website for comprehensive information: [www.ctc.ca.gov.](http://www.ctc.ca.gov)

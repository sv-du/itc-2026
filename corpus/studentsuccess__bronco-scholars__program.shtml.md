# Summer 2025 Program

## Summer Program Options

![Two students with Billy holding sign](img/1-week.jpg)

### 1-Week Summer Transition Program (June 23-28)

Before the start of your math course, you’ll live in the Residence Halls, meet your classmates, learn essential college success skills, get paired with a student mentor, and connect with a personal success coach. You’ll also spend time working on your foundational math skills to prepare you for college-level math, whether you start in the summer or the fall. This 1-week program is free. Meals and housing are included.

![A student gets tutoring in a math course](img/math-course.jpg)

### 6-Week Math Course: June 30-August 8

If you’re feeling anxious or scared about taking college-level math, this class is for you! This 6-week course is free for eligible students (see below for details), and it’s perfect for local students who are commuting to campus. You’ll have access to tutoring and academic support that’ll support you the entire time. Students will enroll in MAT 1051 (College Algebra Stretch I) or STA 1201 (Statistics with Applications Stretch I) and their accompanying activity course depending on major requirements.

![Five students pose with certificates](img/bsp.jpg)

### Do I have to participate in both?

You can choose to participate in the 1-week transition program, the 6-week math course, or both!

### More info about Program Eligibility

×

Bronco Scholars participation is by invitation only, and for CPP students in need of additional support in math. This program is specifically for students who are placed in Category 4 based on the CSU's Multiple Measures. You can visit the [CSU Multiple Measures page](https://csustudentsuccess.org/multiple-measures) and the [CSU Supportive Pathways page](https://csustudentsuccess.org/supportive-pathways) for more information.

Through the 1-week summer transition program, you will experience campus life firsthand with this immersive program. You will:

* Live in campus residential halls
* Learn essential college success skills
* Connext with faculty, staff, and fellow students
* Get paired with a peer mentor
* Meet your dedicated Success Coach
* Spend time working on your foundational math skills to prepare you for college-level math, whether you start in the summer or the fall

In addition, through the 6-week summer course, you will have:

* Take an accelerated path to fulfilling General Education (GE) math major requirement
* Complete 3-4 unit math course in just 6-weeks (compared to 15-week semester)
* Receive embedded course tutor, supplemental tutoring and academic support

The 6-week summer course is ideal for local students who can commute to campus.

### More About Cost

×

Bronco Scholars classes are $203 per unit. There is a $2 student fee. Your BroncoDirect account balance will update with your charges, and you can [pay online](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WtQ2SUZmv_Q) or [in-person](https://www.cpp.edu/student-accounting/payment-options/bill.shtml).

If your Expected Family Contribution from your 2024-25 FAFSA or CA Dream Act application is less than 6,000, you may be eligible for a waiver.

If you do not receive a waiver, you will need to pay for Bronco Scholars classes.

If you receive a waiver but withdraw from your summer courses once they have already begun or you stop attending classes, you may still be responsible for paying course fees. Students who are paying out of pocket must withdraw from classes before the session begins to receive full refund.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is this program required?

Participating in Bronco Scholars is not required. However, we strongly encourage you to consider attending to set yourself up for success your first year.

### Do I have to participate in both the 1-week overnight campus experience and the math course?

You can choose if you want to participate in both or one component.

### Can I work, go on vacation or do other things while participating in the summer program?

For the 1-week overnight campus experience, there is no coming and going: you will be on campus Monday-Saturday. Do not schedule vacations, appointments, and employment during this timeframe.

For the 6-week course, you are expected to attend and be timely to all classes and activities. ​Do not schedule vacations, appointments, and employment during this timeframe.

### What will be the daily schedule for the 6-week math course?

You should plan to be on campus from 9:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. each class day. Your math class will begin at 10 a.m. and meets Monday through Thursday.

### For the 1-week overnight campus experience, what should I bring?

Each dorm is already furnished with:​

* Extra long twin bed​
* Desk & chair​
* Dresser​
* Closet​
* Wastebasket​
* TV connection​
* High speed ethernet connection

Please make sure to bring the following items with you:​

* Toiletries: shampoo/conditioner, body wash, dental hygiene, etc
* Linens/Towels: blanket, X-Long Twin sheets, bath towel etc.​
* School supplies: notebook, pens, pencils, etc.​
* Personal laptop, headphones​
* Recommended: a small amount of cash for vending machines snacks or laundry

### For the 6-week math course, what should I bring?

You should bring:

* Notebooks, pens, pencils and other academic supplies you may need
* Your Math textbook
* Personal items (water bottle, medication, etc.)
* Food for lunch or money to purchase food on campus
* Personal laptop

### How will I get to campus?

You have several options:

* If you drive yourself to campus, you can [purchase a summer semester parking permit](https://www.cpp.edu/parking/index.shtml) ($231) and park in a student lot.
* You can be dropped off at the dropoff circle near the CLA Building.
* You can get a [Foothill Transit ClassPass](https://www.cpp.edu/transportation/classpass.shtml) for free after you enroll in your math course. The Class Pass is accepted on all Foothill Transit Local and Silver Streak buses.
* You can get a discount [Metro bus pass](https://www.cpp.edu/transportation/commuting-to-campus/student-passes.shtml).

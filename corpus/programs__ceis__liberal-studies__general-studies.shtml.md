## About the Program

Are you trying to decide between your many interests? Here, you won’t have to. Pursue interdisciplinary knowledge as you explore various subjects, from arts and humanities to math and natural sciences. With the General Studies pathway, you will create your own degree to reach your dream profession, whether graduate school or a career in the nonprofit, corporate, public health or education sectors.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Develop a broad intellectual base with courses in geology, history, kinesiology and more. Discuss education and knowledge in the context of a diverse and global society. You will gain a well-rounded perspective and hone your abilities as an analytical thinker and dynamic problem solver. Connect ideas across disciplines, communicate effectively and make meaningful contributions across a variety of professional fields.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 4

### Human Resources Specialist

Manage an organization’s workforce, including recruiting and hiring, onboarding and training, compensation and benefits, employee relations and compliance with labor laws. Foster a positive company culture and ensure that both employees and employers are supported.

Card 2 of 4

### Technical Writer

Create clear, concise and easily understandable communications that relay complex technical information to various audiences. Write user manuals, help guides, process documentation, technical reports and more.

Card 3 of 4

### Social Worker

Help individuals and families through various life challenges by assessing their needs, connecting them to resources, providing counseling and advocating for their rights.

Card 4 of 4

### School Counselor

Support students as they navigate personal and academic challenges. Provide counseling, academic and career guidance, programs that promote a positive school culture and resources in times of crisis.

## Program Emphasis

Card 1 of 4

### Community Studies

Take courses in the humanities focused on how humans develop community, including organizational behavior, human geography culture and the arts.

Card 2 of 4

### General

Select from a range of courses in humanities, social sciences and natural sciences.

Card 3 of 4

### Pre-Professional

Participate in classes focused on skills for pre-professional degrees, including professional writing, statistics, natural sciences and law.

Card 4 of 4

### Sustainability

Focus on coursework that emphasis biology, conservation, the environment and bioethics.

## Courses

The General Studies pathway is a fit for people seeking graduate school or a professional career in nonprofit, corporate, public health, education, and more. It is a flexible pathway that builds analytical and communication skills you’ll use in any area and allows you to build knowledge and abilities in two or more areas of interest.
General Studies students select from four emphases: Sustainability, Community Studies, Pre-Professional, and General, and take 10 core Liberal Studies courses with all other LS students--the major's core.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

# Using the Collections

**Please carefully review the following guidelines before your visit as they have changed. You can reach out to our staff with any questions at speccollections@cpp.edu.**

## **Hours**

SC&A is open for research by appointment, Monday through Friday, from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 2:00 PM to 4:00 PM. Please be aware that not all materials are stored in close proximity to the reading room, and we are actively digitizing materials, which may result in some items being unavailable. For more information about SC&A please contact us at speccollections@cpp.edu or visit our [reservation system](https://cpp.libcal.com/reserve/special-collections) to schedule an appointment.

Keep in mind that the library’s [hours](https://cpp.libcal.com/hours/) may vary during holidays and term closures.

## **Location**

Reading room appointments will be held in the Special Collections and Archives Processing and Instructional Lab (Room 4434 on the 4th floor of the University Library) until further notice.

Please note that parking is $8.00 for the day. Metered parking is also available. Here are [directions to the Library](https://www.cpp.edu/maps/) and [driving directions to campus.](https://www.cpp.edu/aboutcpp/visitor-information/directions.shtml)

## **Planning Your Research Visit**

We recommend that all researchers planning to use Special Collections and Archives materials make a reservation at least 48 hours prior to their intended research visit through the library's [reservation system](https://cpp.libcal.com/reserve/special-collections).  We advise waiting until your reservation has been approved before visiting Special Collections and Archives.

## **Guidelines for Use of Materials**

Cal Poly Pomona’s Special Collections and Archives are open to all users who fill out a registration form and present a form of photo identification, subject to the following rules. Advance notice of your visit, although not required, is recommended to ensure availability of materials and staff assistance.

* All readers must fill out a [registration form (PDF)](speccoll_reader_registration_form_rev2024.pdf) in order to view materials from the Special Collections and Archives.
* Readers must present a valid form of photo identification.
* Readers must sign the registration form to indicate their agreement with these rules

### General Reading Room Rules

* All materials are housed in closed stacks and may not be removed from Special Collections. Materials must be viewed in the reading room under staff supervision.
* Food, drink, and gum are not allowed in the reading room.
* Only pencils and laptop computers (or other approved digital devices) may be used for taking notes in the reading room. Ink of any kind is prohibited. If you need paper for note-taking, it will be provided by staff. Additional personal belongings, including personal binders and notebooks, must be stored in a designated area.
* Special Collections staff reserves the right to inspect library materials and personal items prior to entry and upon departure from the reading room.

### Handling of Materials

* Materials must be handled with care to ensure their safety and preservation. Please follow any special instructions given by Special Collections staff.
* No marks may be added or erased on any materials.
* Books, papers, and other objects should not be set on the materials. Special weights will be provided to hold books open.
* Please do not use Post-it notes, paper clips, pencils, or other objects to mark a place in a book or folder. Special acid-free paper strips will be provided.
* White cotton gloves are to be worn when handling photographic materials. Staff will provide them.
* Readers may have one archival box, one oversized flat file, or up to two books at their table at any time.
* Folders should be removed from the box one at a time, ensuring that the exact order of materials within each folder and box is maintained. Please use the provided “folder out” cards when removing folders from boxes. Please report any disarrangement to Special Collections staff.
* Foam book rests will be provided for particularly fragile materials.

### Privacy and Sensitive Information

Researchers using archival materials, whether processed or unprocessed, may encounter sensitive information protected by privacy laws, including social security numbers, dates of birth, medical records, financial details, or personnel information. If you encounter information that seems private or sensitive, please inform Special Collections & Archives staff immediately. Researchers are expected not to copy, publish, share, or disclose any sensitive information, as unauthorized use or disclosure could have legal consequences. Thank you for handling these materials with care and for helping us protect privacy.

## **Duplication Policy**

Special Collections and Archives allows photographic reproduction of its materials for scholarly and general use unless doing so would damage the items or violate donor agreements or copyright restrictions. All reproductions of Special Collections materials are permitted at the discretion of the Cal Poly Pomona University Library.

Special Collections and Archives makes these reproductions for one-time use. Copies may not be sold or placed in other institutions. Copies may not be further duplicated or given to other individuals. The Special Collections and Archives unit reserves the right to ask for the return of copies when the user is finished with them.

## **Citing Materials**

Special Collections and Archives asks that requesters using reproductions of our materials give appropriate credit when quoting from, or reproducing, an item in our collection. If unsure how to cite an item, please ask a Special Collections staff member for clarification. If the item is published or broadcast, the unit also requests a copy of the publication or program, or when that is not feasible, the bibliographic citation.

## **Personal Photography**

If approved by Special Collections staff, users may take non-flash photos of collection materials with their personal cameras for research purposes only. We do not permit the use of personal scanners, tripods, or lights.

## **Fees**

Cal Poly Pomona Special Collections & Archives welcomes individuals to explore and utilize the materials within our collections. These items are made accessible to support research, teaching, and private study. However, it is important to acknowledge that the publication or use of any work protected by copyright, beyond the bounds allowed by fair use for research or educational purposes, requires written permission from the copyright owner.

In overseeing the materials within our collections, the University Library typically holds ownership but not copyright. The user bears the sole responsibility for obtaining these permissions and for any subsequent use of the materials.

In cases where copyright may be explicitly held by the Cal Poly Pomona Foundation or may rest with the university, for example, if the material was created by the institution, a reproduction fee may be applied. Reproduction fees are specifically applicable to materials where the copyright is owned by the university, and permission is granted for one-time, non-exclusive use.

In cases where copyright is held by others, a cost recovery fee will instead be applied to cover staff time for fulfilling digitization requests.

Payments for these services can be made through various methods:

* Credit Card: The University Library Budget Analyst will collaborate with the researcher to facilitate credit card transactions.
* Check: Checks should be made payable to the Cal Poly Pomona Foundation.
* Cash: This payment method is only accepted in person, and it must be the exact amount.

Please be aware that full payment is required before orders can be processed.

It is pertinent to note that SC&A reserves the right to limit the number of items copied from a specific collection for off-site use.

## About the Program

Transform the way you see the world at the molecular level. Investigate chemical processes within living organisms and bridge chemistry and life sciences with courses in molecular biology, enzymology, and metabolic pathways. Get real-world training in techniques used to study biochemical processes through extensive experience in our labs. Understand and manipulate biochemical systems and graduate ready to take on groundbreaking careers in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and medical research.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Analyze the structures of biomolecules like amino acids, carbohydrates, and lipids, sequence DNA with PCR machines, or join a faculty-led research lab to investigate bile acid metabolism. Combine the theoretical classroom study with practical demonstration in the lab and develop your skills as a biochemist. Graduate ready to take on your dream career or advanced studies.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Biochemist

Conduct experiments to research the chemical processes and substances within living organisms for medicine, genetics and environmental science.

### Pharmacologist

Investigate how drugs get absorbed, metabolize and affect living organisms to improve pharmaceuticals and overall health outcomes. Design clinical trials, assess toxicity and drug resistance and more.

### Biotechnologist

Combine the studies of biology, chemistry, genetics and engineering to create new tools and address challenges in medicine, agriculture, environmental conservation and more.

### Forensic Scientist

Analyze crime scene evidence to help solve crimes and aid in investigations.

### Pharmaceutical Sciences

Develop and manufacture pharmaceutical products, including drugs and diagnostic tests, to assist in the treatment of health conditions.

### Clinical Lab Science

Conduct laboratory tests and examine samples to help diagnose diseases and inform their treatment.

### Medical Careers

Prepare for entry into professional programs such as Medical Doctor, Pharmacist, Physician Assistant, etc.

### Environmental Science

Investigate chemical processes in the environment, assess human impact and help develop solutions to environmental issues.

### Government Agencies

Conduct research, work in regulatory agencies or inform public policy from federal to local levels.

### Quality Control and Assurance

Ensure company processes and products meet all necessary requirements and standards. Detect issues in products (QC) or prevent and detect quality problems (QA).

### Chemical Education

Prepare for credential and postgraduate programs and share your passion for chemistry with learners of any age. Opportunities include teaching in elementary and secondary schools, two-year colleges, four-year institutions, or Ph.D-granting institutions.

### Intellectual Property/Law

Assess new chemical compounds, formulations or optimized processes to determine if they qualify for patent protection or use your expertise to review cases of potential patent infringement.

### Graduate and Professional School

Continue your studies with advanced degrees in chemistry or applied sciences or continue into professional programs, including medical, pharmacy, physician’s assistant or nursing school.

## Featured Sub-Discipline: Biochemistry

Biochemistry is an interdisciplinary field where you will combine knowledge of chemistry and biology to explore the molecular foundations of living systems. Students choosing the Biochemistry option complete Biochemistry electives, such as Clinical Chemistry or Enzymology, and take an advanced Biology class that aligns with their interests. The Biochemistry option is a great choice for students who want to gain hands-on experience that support you in pursuing careers where you will apply chemical knowledge to biological systems, such as clinical laboratory science, biotechnology, pharmaceutical sciences or are interested in going on to graduate school.

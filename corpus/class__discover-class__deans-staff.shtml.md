***Dean***

Camille Johnson is the Dean.

Email Dr. Johnson

More on Dr. Johnson

### Dr. Camille Johnson

×

Dr. Camille Johnson is the incoming Dean of the College of Letters, Arts, and Social Sciences. Having grown up in Claremont, she has deep connections to the area and is excited to be returning home. A scholar and higher education professional with expertise in inclusive student and faculty success. As a social psychologist, Dr. Johnson’s research has examined interracial interactions, first-generation college student performance, and analyses of women’s representation in the social sciences. An accomplished scholar and professional leader, she is an elected fellow of the Society for Experimental Social Psychology and the Society for Personality and Social Psychology, for which she also serves as treasurer. In addition, she promoted women higher education as the State Chair of the ACE Women’s Network – Northern California.

Dr. Johnson earned her bachelor’s in Psychology from Carleton College, her master’s in Applied Psychology from Claremont Graduate University, and a Ph.D. in Psychology from The Ohio State University. She completed a fellowship at the Stanford University Graduate School of Business before beginning a tenure-track position at San José State University.

## Dr. Brianne Dávila

***Associate Dean***

Brianne Dávila is the Associate Dean for Academic Programs and Student Success and a Professor of Sociology in the Sociology Department.

Email Dr. Dávila

More on Dr. Dávila

### Dr. Brianne Dávila

×

Brianne Dávila is the Associate Dean for Academic Programs and Student Success and a Professor of Sociology in the Sociology Department. She earned her doctorate in Sociology and Feminist Studies at the University of California, Santa Barbara.  She has a master’s from the same program and received a bachelor’s in Sociology with a Spanish minor from Pitzer College in Claremont, CA.  She previously taught sociology and ethnic studies at Willamette University in Salem, Oregon and worked as an applied sociologist for the Los Angeles Unified School District and Center for Urban Education at the University of Southern California. Her teaching and research interests are in the areas of sociology of education, Latina/o sociology, and social inequality.

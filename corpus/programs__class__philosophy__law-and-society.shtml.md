## About the Program

Interested in making a tangible impact on society? The Law and Society option blends deep philosophical thinking with hands-on problem-solving, focusing on the ethical, social and political issues that shape our world. You’ll dive into the complexities of law and justice, learning to analyze legal concepts and tackle societal challenges with both reason and compassion.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Prepare to immerse yourself in the world of law and society via mock trials, public policy internships and community projects addressing social justice. Whether analyzing environmental laws or debating human rights in a student forum, this option allows you to engage directly with the issues shaping our world. This path doesn’t just prepare you for careers in law—it opens doors to urban planning, public policy and beyond, giving you the tools to drive change and create solutions that improve lives and communities.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 4

### Environmental Policy Advisor

Want to save the planet through law? Work on developing policies to address climate change, sustainability, and environmental justice, using legal frameworks to create a healthier world.

Card 2 of 4

### Public Defender

Passionate about defending others? Provide legal counsel to individuals who can’t afford private lawyers, ensuring fair legal representation for those facing serious criminal charges.

Card 3 of 4

### Cybersecurity Consultant

Interested in defending against digital threats? Work with companies to protect their data and digital infrastructure, blending legal and ethical expertise to ensure compliance and security.

Card 4 of 4

### Policy Analyst

Want to solve large, high stakes societal problems? Apply analytical skills to research issues, identify policy implications and propose solutions.

## Courses

The Law and Society option integrates philosophical study with practical applications in law, social issues and public policy. This program combines a solid philosophical foundation with specialized coursework that addresses moral, social, and political challenges in areas such as law, medicine, the environment, and education. By studying moral and political philosophy, you will gain critical tools for analyzing legal and ethical arguments, preparing for careers in law, business, urban planning, and human services. This option emphasizes the practical application of philosophical skills to real-world problems, providing a strong basis for those pursuing careers that intersect with legal and social frameworks.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

### The Undergraduate Journal of Ethics, Policy, and Social Justice

The Undergraduate Journal of Ethics, Policy, and Social Justice looks for original, thought-provoking work that explores critical issues through a social justice lens. Any undergraduate student can submit a paper for consideration. Interested in taking your involvement further? Become a Student Associate Editor by applying in the Fall and join us for a production class!

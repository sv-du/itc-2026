# About Micro-Internships

![Cal Poly Pomona students looking at career guide](../img/micro-internships/whataremicrointernships-cropped.png)

## What is a Micro-Internship? Short-term, Professional, Paid Experiences!

Micro-Internships are short-term, paid, professional assignments that range from 5 to 40 hours of work in total, can occur anytime of the year, and are highly specific, project-based positions. These projects enable Career Launchers to demonstrate skills, explore career paths, and build their networks as they seek the right full-time role.

## Why Micro-Internships?

![](../test/graphics/photos/students.jpg)

### For Students

* Participate in paid projects with a short time commitment (20-30- 40 hours weekly or within a month).
* Explore diverse career paths while working on captivating assignments.
* Showcase skills to prospective employers and apply classroom knowledge in practical scenarios.
* Develop a professional portfolio and expand your network.
* International Students are eligible to apply.
* Undocumented Students are eligible to apply.

[Get Started](https://www.cpp.edu/ii/micro-internships/for-students.shtml "Get Started. ")

![](../test/graphics/photos/faculty.jpg)

### For Faculty

* Facilitate embedded, paid professional experiences for students within courses
* Collaborate with a community or For-Profit partner to provide a specific deliverable
* Utilize existing collaborations or receive assistance in identifying new ones.
* Parker Dewey performs all administrative duties

[Get Started](https://www.cpp.edu/ii/micro-internships/for-faculty.shtml "Get Started. ")

![](../test/graphics/photos/industry.jpg)

### For Industry Partners

* No administrative paperwork required.
* Parker Dewey operates as a consulting firm, with all students classified as 1099s through Parker Dewey.
* Students commit to strict NDAs, and the employer retains ownership of all work products.
* In the event of dissatisfaction with the deliverable, Parker Dewey offers a full refund.
* Employers can freely hire students as interns, contractors, or full-time employees without incurring any fees.
* Parker Dewey handles payment directly to the student.

[Get Started](https://www.cpp.edu/ii/micro-internships/for-industry-partners.shtml "Get Started. ")

#### **JOB DESCRIPTION**

***This collaboration is tailored to a specific class: GSC 4500***

Purpose: This micro-internship provides students with a hands-on opportunity to investigate seismic activity and subsurface structures at Cal Poly Pomona. The goal is to design and conduct a seismic data acquisition survey to better understand how seismic velocity profiles correlate with known fault lines on campus. This project gives students a chance to contribute to campus safety and research, while applying geoscience techniques in an applied setting.

**Key Objectives:**

* Design and carry out an active seismic survey at a selected fault zone on or near the CPP campus.
* Analyze seismic velocity data to interpret subsurface structural features.
* Correlate seismic velocity profiles with geological fault information to support ongoing safety and infrastructure planning.

**Tasks & Deliverables:**

* **Seismic Survey Design:** Plan and execute a field-based seismic acquisition project.
* **Data Analysis:** Develop and interpret a seismic velocity model of the study area.
* **Final Report:** Prepare a comprehensive report and present the final model to Cal Poly Pomona’s Facilities and Management Department.

**Skills Students Will Gain & Use:**

* Seismic data acquisition and analysis
* Geophysical modeling and interpretation
* Applied research in Earth sciences
* Technical writing and professional reporting
* Team-based fieldwork and communication

### Indoor Mushroom Production: Smart Cultivation in a Controlled Environment | Pomona, CA

×

#### **JOB DESCRIPTION**

***This collaboration is tailored to a specific class: PLT 1120***

Purpose: This micro-internship offers PLT 1120 students a hands-on opportunity to explore sustainable food production through indoor mushroom cultivation. Students will work in teams to carry out critical tasks in a climate-controlled mushroom pod, designed to simulate commercial production systems. Over the course of 20 hours, students will complete one full mini-cycle of oyster or shiitake mushroom production from the substrate prep to harvest while learning the environmental and technological principles behind controlled environment agriculture (CEA).

**Key Objectives:**

1. Apply horticultural principles from PLT 1120 to a non-plant, high-value crop (mushrooms) within a controlled growing environment.
2. Measure and manage environmental variables (temperature, humidity, CO₂ levels, airflow) that influence fungal growth, learning how to monitor and adjust conditions using analog and digital tools.
3. Document and reflect on the lifecycle of the crop and student participation through growth logs, photos, and brief written reflections.
4. Evaluate sustainability trade-offs in indoor mushroom production (e.g., water use, substrate reuse, energy footprint) through a closing discussion and team report.

**Tasks & Deliverables:**

* **Crop Management:** Participate in preparing substrate, inoculating, monitoring, and harvesting mushrooms
* **Environmental Monitoring:** Use analog and digital devices to track and adjust growing conditions
* **Documentation:** Maintain crop logs and take weekly photos to track progress and outcomes
* **Team Reflection & Report:** Engage in a final discussion and submit a team-written report evaluating the sustainability aspects of the growing system

**Skills Students Will Gain & Use:**

* Controlled environment agriculture (CEA) techniques
* Precision monitoring and data collection
* Sustainable production evaluation
* Collaborative problem solving and reporting
* Applied biological and environmental science

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q. What is a Micro-Internship?

A. Micro-Internships are short-term, paid, professional assignments that enable Career Launchers to demonstrate skills, explore career paths, and build their networks. They can take place year-round, typically require 10 to 40 hours of work by the student, and are due within a few days to a few weeks.

### Q. How are Micro-Internships different?

A. Unlike traditional internships that are tied to the academic calendar, Micro-Internships can be used year-round to provide on-demand support for short-term projects (usually requiring a total of 10-40 hours of work). They can be performed remotely and require minimal oversight and commitment. Micro-Internships are utilized across industries and roles, as a precursor or alternative to traditional internships, and as skills-based hiring auditions.

### Q. What is a typical project?

A. Micro-Internships are comparable to those assignments typically given to new hires or summer interns — things like conducting market research, performing financial analysis, and assisting in lead generation. Companies partner with us to provide services across a variety of departments, industries, and organization sizes.

### Q. Are Micro-Internships on-site or remote?

A. Micro-Internships can be either on-site or virtual, depending on the specific requirements of the project.

### Q. Does this replace traditional internship programs?

A. Micro-Internships are not meant to replace traditional internship programs but rather complement and even supplement them. Due to their short-term nature, Micro-Internships can be used year-round to find on-demand project support while simultaneously allowing you to engage and evaluate potential talent.

# Alumni

## PreLaw Alumni

[![headshot of Daniela Ulloa](img/alumni-headshots/ulloa-resized.jpg)

### Daniela Ulloa

Class of 2023](#modal-0 "Daniela Ulloa")

### Daniela Ulloa

×

Daniela Ulloa is a 2023 graduate of Cal Poly Pomona. She graduated cum laude with a degree in Political Science. Daniela began her legal career during her undergraduate studies by starting as a file clerk for a criminal defense firm in Rancho Cucamonga, California. She took a gap year between undergraduate and law school. During that time, she was a paralegal for a family law boutique firm in Riverside, California. Daniela is now attending California Western School of Law in San Diego, California. She is spending her 1L summer working for the San Diego Public Defender's Office and participating in a trial bootcamp. Her drive to attend law school originated from her work at an immigration shelter for unaccompanied children. Daniela's goal is to serve as a mentor for pre-law students, specifically those who are first-generation, like herself.

Her advice for current pre-law students: Get as much experience as you can before going into law school. Any work a law firm or judge gives you will help you immensely when it comes down to hitting the books in law school. You will also have the opportunity to immerse yourself in the legal environment/community and gauge whether law school is the right fit for you.

### Kyle Chao

Class of 2024](#modal-1 "Kyle Chao")

### Kyle Chao

×

Kyle graduated from Cal Poly Pomona in 2024 with a B.A. in Political Science and minors in Music and Data Science. Since graduating, Kyle has spent a summer with the Upward Bound program at USC, teaching Administrative Justice courses to high school students for college credit and playing with various music ensembles in LA. He’s especially interested in Energy and Project Development law, and after his first year of law school, he will work as a Law Clerk at the Los Angeles County Public Defender’s Office during the summer.

CPP has helped him achieve his professional goals through professional-oriented programs like the Judicial Internship course taught by Dr. Menounou.

**Advice for current prelaw students**: prospective law students should try a legal-related internship before applying to law school to see if it's truly the right path. Law is a major time and financial commitment, so it’s important to be sure. I also recommend taking a gap year to enjoy life and recharge before diving into law school and your legal career.

[![Kiana Jokar in front of a building](img/alumni-headshots/jokar460x350.jpg)

### Kiana Jokar

Class of 2023](#modal-2 "Kiana Jokar")

### Kiana Jokar

×

My name is Kiana Jokar, and I graduated from Cal Poly Pomona with a B.A. in Political Science. The department played a major role in shaping me as both a public speaker and a stronger writer. Through the Model National Org. of American States program, I had the opportunity to speak at international relations conferences in D.C., and the Senior Thesis Program helped me hone advanced writing and research skills. One of my favorite memories from CPP was studying abroad in Japan, an experience that broadened my perspectives and enriched my academic journey. I am now entering my second year of law school at Chapman University, where I was awarded the full-ride Dean's Merit Scholarship and the Distinguished Student Fellowship.

**My advice to current PreLaw students** is to find your niche and connect with your prelaw advisors. My advisors at CPP were instrumental in helping with LSAT resources and guiding me in choosing the best scholarship offer from the law schools I applied to. I also highly recommend doing unique and niche activities because my law firm employers always ask about those distinct experiences on my resume, like study abroad Japan.

[![headshot of Edgar Orozco](img/alumni-headshots/edgarorozco.jpg)

### Edgar Orozco

Class of 2020](#modal-3 "Edgar Orozco")

### Edgar Orozco

×

Edgar has graduated from Columbia Law this May of 2025. Since graduating from Cal Poly Pomona, Edgar spent two years between undergraduate school and law school, working for a family law firm in Riverside, California. His interest in law stemmed from a fascination with the legal system and a need for understanding its laws, given his parents migrating from Zacatecas, Mexico, to Riverside, California. Edgar has maintained the same energy and passion for extracurriculars that were rooted since his time at Cal Poly Pomona. At CPP, he enjoyed spending endless hours in the library researching for the award-winning Model UN Team under Dr. Scarcelli. His favorite course at Cal Poly was Dr. Guerrero’s American Presidency.

At Columbia Law School, Edgar has served as Teaching Assistant to 3 professors, Vice President of the Domestic Violence Project, and an Editor to the School’s Blog on Corporate Governance called Columbia Law Blue Sky Blog. Edgar will enter Freshfields Law Firm Fall of 2025, a top firm specializing in Corporate Litigation and Federal Securities Law for companies in Silicon Valley, CA.

**His biggest advice for undergraduate students** is to utilize all of your resources, join clubs, speak to professors during office hours, and to never be afraid of pinging CPP alumni who are in a field of your interest (thank you, Jose Contreras, CPP Class of 2016). He still maintains strong contact with the Political Science Department at Cal Poly Pomona, giving advice to every year’s Model UN team, as well as giving advice to Dr. Menounou’s Judicial Internship course. He invites students to connect with him via LinkedIn for any tips or advice.

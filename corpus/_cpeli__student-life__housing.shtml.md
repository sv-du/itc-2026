# Housing Information

CPELI students have access to the University Housing dormitories on campus and the University Village apartments off campus. Our office works with the Housing and University Village offices to secure rooms for CPELI students prior to arrival. Because these are popular choices for housing, we encourage CPELI students to apply for housing as soon as they receive their visa.

Students who are interested to learn more about Housing can indicate on their [CPELI application](../apply-now/index.shtml) that they would like more information. You can also email us at cpeli@cpp.edu with any questions.

![residential photo](https://www.cpp.edu/cpge/semesteratcpp/img/560.residental-housing.jpg)

## Residence Halls & Suites

Living with American university students and other international students provides many opportunities for our students to speak English outside of the classroom. Cal Poly Pomona University Housing Services offers two on-campus accommodation options – the residence halls and residential suites.

Please refer to the [cost tables](https://www.cpp.edu/housing/futureresidents/housing-costs.shtml) for this information.

[Learn More](https://www.cpp.edu/housing/futureresidents/housing-costs.shtml "Learn More. Residence Halls & Suites")

## University Village

Another popular option is the University Village apartments. Students who are 21 years of age and older may apply to stay at the University Village. These apartments are located next to Cal Poly Pomona and is about a 5-10 minute walk to campus.

## Housing Options

### Residence Halls

The Residence Halls are conveniently located on-campus and are within easy walking distance to the academic buildings, library, computer labs, dining areas, the Bronco Student Center and parking lots.

Features include:

* 2 or 3 students per room
* Hall study room, laundry room, recreation room
* Minimum meal plan required ([more information here](https://www.cpp.edu/housing/futureresidents/housing-costs.shtml))
* Staffed by Residence Hall Advisors and a Residence Hall Coordinator (professional staff member)

### Residential Suites

The Residence Suites are conveniently located on-campus and are within easy walking distance to the academic buildings, library, computer labs, dining areas, the Bronco Student Center and parking lots.

Features include:

* Three different floor plans
* Suite includes living room, kitchenette, full-size bathroom
* Bedroom furnished with bed, bookshelf, desk, chairs and dresser
* Living room/kitchenette furnished with sofa, chair, tables, refrigerator, and microwave
* Minimum meal plan required ([more information here](https://www.cpp.edu/housing/futureresidents/housing-costs.shtml))
* Staffed by Residence Hall Advisors and a Residence Hall Coordinator (professional staff member)

### University Village Apartments

The University Village offers apartment living next to campus. Students who are 21 years of age or older are eligible to live in the Village.

The Village application is available at <http://www.cppvillage.com/>. When the application asks for your Bronco ID, please put your passport number.

**Community Features**

* Apartments house four students, with either two or four bedrooms options
* Furnished bedrooms, living room, and dining room
* Refrigerator and stove/oven are provided in kitchen
* Meal plan purchase is optional from Campus
* Swimming pool, basketball courts, computer lab and recreation center and free laundry room
* Barbeque and picnic areas
* Staffed by Professional Coordinators and Resident Advisors 24 hours a day

### Homestay

*Cal Poly Pomona, the College of Professional and Global Education, and CPELI do not endorse any homestay agencies. Students are responsible for making their own evaluations when selecting housing. CPELI does not manage a homestay program. You can contact an agency directly to apply.*

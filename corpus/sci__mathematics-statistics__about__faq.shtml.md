## FAQ

### 1. Do I need to know my Bronco ID Number prior to calling or emailing?

Yes, please provide your Bronco ID Number. If you are trying to register for a class, please know the section or class number. (See question 3 to find section or class number.)

### 2. How do I find out my Bronco ID Number?

Your Bronco ID number is located on the **[Degree Progress Report](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zXpEbJ3rApg&ab_channel=BroncoAdvisingCenter "link to youtube on the degree progress report")** or on your Cal Poly Pomona Unofficial Transcript.

### 3. What is a section number? What is a class number?

A section number are the two numbers that are listed next to the particular course you are trying to add.

For example, MAT 1060-02 (32976)

* 02 is the section number.
* 32976 is the class number.

### 4. I am trying to search for a course but it is not appearing.

Follow the steps below if you are having trouble **[searching](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y4_SqPdXCPE&ab_channel=BroncoAdvisingCenter "link to youtube on searching for a course")** for sections of a course:

1. Check the term and make sure the current semester is selected.
2. Make sure the "show open classes" box is unchecked. If all classes are waitlisted, checking the "show open classes" box will not list the offered courses.

### 5. When classes are in my shopping cart, does that mean I am enrolled in those courses?

No, click on "[**Continue**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_mGl_c-huY4&ab_channel=BroncoAdvisingCenter "link to youtube in enrolling in classes")," and then click “Finish Enrolling.” Check your class schedule to ensure you are enrolled in the course.

### 6. Where do I enter a permission number?

Click on the class section hyperlink in your enrollment shopping cart. Enter the [**permission number**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kp2Atj5RaPs&ab_channel=BroncoAdvisingCenter "link to youtube on using a permission number") for the class in the permission number box.

If you are waitlisted, you will need to drop the class, add the course, and enter the permission number.

### 7. I am waitlist position 5 or higher. Will I be enrolled in the course?

Students who have not fulfilled the course prerequisites will be dropped and space may become available to students on the waitlist.  If the class remains full, it is at the discretion of the instructor whether or not to overenroll the course; if they choose to do so, you will need a permission number from the instructor to override the class capacity. Alternatively, you can find an open section of the course.

### 8. How do I find the instruction mode for my class?

Click on your Student Center weekly schedule if you are enrolled. Toggle to the "List View" and click on the hyperlinked section number.

From the Class Search page, click on the Class or Section hyperlink for the section you are interested in.

View the [**description**](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/instruction-modes.shtml "link to instruction mode info page") of each instruction mode.

### 9. I received a permission number from the instructor. When enrolling into the course, I received a prerequisite error.

Permission numbers issued by instructors are to override course capacity only. If BroncoDirect does not recognize your prerequisites, contact the Math and Statistics Department via phone (909) 869-3645, email (**mathstat@cpp.edu**), or in person (8-113).

Permission numbers will be updated if prerequisites have been met.

### 10. I took math classes in high school; what math class am I eligible to take at CPP?

High school courses do not transfer as college credit. Click [**here**](https://catalog.cpp.edu/content.php?catoid=65&navoid=5340&hl=%22Advanced+placement%22&returnto=search#Advanced_Placement_Examinations "link to info page on course credit") to view course credit for AP, CLEP, or IB credit. Your advisor will help determine which course to take based on major and [**category placement**](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/new-students/category-placement.shtml "link to info page on category placement").

### 11. I need to take Calculus but do not have AP, CLEP, or IB credit. What are my options?

The Mathematics and Statistics Department offers a calculus placement test. Click [**here**](../calculus-placement.shtml "link to the ALEKS calculus placement test webpage") to learn more information regarding the [**ALEKS calculus placement test**](../calculus-placement.shtml "link to the ALEKS calculus placement test webpage").

### 12. I took a math course at another campus but I did not receive credit on the Transfer Credit Report, and it is not listed on assist.org. What can I do to receive credit?

If the course is not listed on [**assist.org**](https://assist.org/ "link to assist.org"), ccontact your assigned [**advisor**](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/directories/advising-directory.shtml "link to advising directory") and request to initiate a Course Substitution or General Academic petition. The **Registrar's Office** has additional information regarding the difference between which petition to submit.

### 13. Can I take a math course at a community college?

Yes, find a [**community college**](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/guides/community-college.shtml "link to the community college info webpage") and check [**assist.org**](https://assist.org/ "link to assist.org") to ensure you will receive credit for the course. If the course is a prerequisite to another math course, submit an unofficial transcript to **mathstat@cpp.edu** to ensure you will not be dropped from the next math course.

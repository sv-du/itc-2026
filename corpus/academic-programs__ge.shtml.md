# Office of Academic Programs

## The Backbone of a Polytechnic Education

### General Education

General Education (GE) is an interdisciplinary program whose courses are taught and administered by academic departments, but function cohesively to achieve the meaning, purpose, and outcomes set by the university faculty. These requirements are designed to complement the major program to ensure graduates become broadly educated persons who will function as intelligent, active, and creative members of their community.

## Developing GE Courses

[### Policy and Administration

GE is an interdisciplinary program designed to complement majors and ensure graduates become broadly educated persons who will function as intelligent, active, and creative members of their communities.](#modal-0 "GE Policy and Administration")

### GE Policy and Administration

×

[CPP's GE Policy (PDF)](protected/curriculum/cpp-ge-requirements-policy-effective-fall-2025.pdf) is overseen by the Academic Senate's standing GE Committee, which includes [faculty members from every college](https://academic.cpp.edu/senate/committee.aspx?ay_id=30&com_id=6). The committee is supported by the Faculty Director of Undergraduate Studies and General Education who provides resources and guidance, evaluates student petitions and articulation agreements, manages the [CourseMatch](coursematch.shtml) program, and serves as a liaison between administration and faculty on GE matters. The Faculty Director's contact information can be found on [OAP's Contact page](directory.shtml).

Close

[### Course Evaluation Rubrics

All GE courses are evaluated using rubrics to ensure they align to the subject areas and GE student learning outcomes.](#modal-1 "Course Evaluation Rubrics")

### Course Evaluation Rubrics

×

The GE Committee has developed a transparent set of rubrics to evaluate the certification of GE courses. [GE Course Evaluation Rubrics (DOCX)](protected/curriculum/ge-area-rubrics-combined-ay-25-26-11-12-25.docx)

Additionally, GE Area 6: Ethnic Studies courses are first reviewed by the Ethnic Studies Faculty Implementation Committee (ESFIC) and approved by the Ethnic and Womens Studies (EWS) department before being reviewed by the GE Committee. Before submitting a course, faculty should review the course creation checklist.

[Area 6: Ethnic Studies Course Creation Checklist (DOCX)](protected/curriculum/area-6-checklist-5-28-25.docx)

Additionally, departments must ensure they have faculty eligible to teach the proposed courses. Eligibility is evaluated using the following rubric.

[Area 6: Ethnic Studies Faculty Eligibility Rubric (DOCX)](protected/curriculum/area_6_faculty_evaluation_rubric.docx)

[### Recertification

All GE courses must be regularly reviewed to ensure their alignment with the program and with faculty's expectations.](#modal-2 "Recertification (2025-27)")

### Recertification (2025-27)

×

From AY 2025-27, CPP will be recertifying every GE course to ensure they meet faculty standards. Faculty can find a resource guide walking through the recertification timeline, GE program expectations, and ECO examples here:

[Recertification Guide (PDF)](protected/curriculum/recertification-guide-submitters-11-12-25.pdf)

To build a foundation of student success within the GE program, the GE Committee and the Office of Academic Programs ask faculty to review data related to their GE courses. This includes information about enrollment caps, dependent programs, and student success markers such as DFW rates and equity gaps. You may access the OneDrive folder with your CPP credentials here:

[GE Course Data](https://livecsupomona.sharepoint.com/%3Af%3A/s/ap_curriculum/ElmftlpwhhpMsCgg2JY3_RUBIAU7wzc2fMMmN6lqqyUAJQ?e=lzB63Y)

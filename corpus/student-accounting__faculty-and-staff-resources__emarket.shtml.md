# eMarket

## Overview

Student Financial Solutions has partnered with Transact Payments (a third-party vendor solution) to offer e-commerce services to the campus community. Campus departments can establish themselves as merchants to sell approved products or services via customizable online storefronts and accept credit card payments

## Resources

### Benefits

* Increases visibility of departmental services and offerings to campus customers, alumni, and the community
* Reduces administrative burden related to PCI compliance
* SFS will complete one SAQ A report on behalf of departments using Transact E-Market storefronts (does not include e-market checkout integrations)

### eMarket Options

E-Market Storefront vs. Checkout (Options)

| Category | eMarket Storefront | eMarket Checkout |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Purpose & Use Case | Designed to let users browse, select, and pay for items entirely within a hosted site. Ideal for donations, event sales, and departmental storefronts. | Used when item selection happens on the institution’s website, but payment is processed via Transact. Good for applications needing custom workflows. |
| Start Point | Customer starts directly on a Transact (CashNet)-hosted eMarket site (Storefront URL). | Customer starts on the institution’s own website. |
| Item Selection | Happens within the Storefront—user browses by category or item. | Happens on the institutional site—items are selected before being sent to Checkout. |
| Checkout Process | Seamless within the same site; user moves from selection to payment on one site. | User is redirected to Transact (CashNet) to complete payment after selecting items. |
| Payment Methods | Credit card, ACH (if allowed), SmartPay compatible. | Same as Storefront – fully compatible with major methods. |
| Receipt & Confirmation | Built-in confirmation screen and optional email receipt. | Same – confirmation via Transact (CashNet), then redirected to institutional URL. |
| Best for... | Departments that want a ready-to-use online storefront for simple transactions. | Use cases where the institution wants to control item selection and checkout logic. |

### Timelines

New e-market requests may require 8 - 12 weeks to configure and implement, depending on the complexity and current demand for other e-market sites.  This includes information gathering and obtaining a merchant ID (MID) for accepting credit card payments and a bank account.

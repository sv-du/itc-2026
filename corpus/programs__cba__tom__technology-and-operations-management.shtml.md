## About the option

Students in the Technology and Operations Management (TOM) option will focus on the design, implementation, control and improvement of processes and activities that are involved in the daily running of a business.
Heavy emphasis is placed on the study of computer applications for helping managers plan, analyze data, make decisions and communicate. TOM students are provided a broad background in the field, after which they choose one of the following areas of specialization: Supply Chain Management, Management of Technology and Service Operations Management.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Demand Planner

A Demand Planner forecasts customer demand for products, analyzes sales data, and collaborates with supply chain teams to ensure optimal inventory levels and timely product availability.

### S&OP Planner

An S&OP (Sales and Operations Planning) Planner aligns sales forecasts with production, inventory, and supply chain plans, ensuring efficient resource allocation and meeting customer demand.

### Distribution / Inventory Planner

A Distribution/Inventory Planner manages the flow of goods, optimizing inventory levels, ensuring timely distribution, and coordinating supply chain processes to meet customer demand efficiently.

### Process Improvement Analyst

A Process Improvement Analyst identifies inefficiencies in business processes, analyzes data, and recommends strategies to enhance productivity, reduce costs, and streamline operations.

### Management Analyst

A Management Analyst evaluates an organization's operations, identifies areas for improvement, and provides recommendations to enhance efficiency, reduce costs, and achieve business goals.

### Production Analyst

A Production Analyst monitors and analyzes production processes, identifying inefficiencies, optimizing workflows, and ensuring that manufacturing goals and quality standards are met.

### Logistics Analyst

A Logistics Analyst analyzes and optimizes the movement of goods and services, managing supply chain data, tracking shipments, and improving efficiency in distribution and transportation processes.

### Buyer/Procurement Analyst

A Buyer/Procurement Analyst manages purchasing activities, analyzes market trends, sources suppliers, and ensures cost-effective procurement of goods and services to meet organizational needs.

### Transportation Manager

A Transportation Manager oversees the planning, coordination, and management of transportation operations, ensuring efficient and cost-effective delivery of goods while maintaining compliance with regulations.

### Operations Manager

An Operations Manager oversees day-to-day business activities, streamlining processes, managing teams, and ensuring operational efficiency to meet company goals and deliver customer satisfaction.

## Overview

TOM students can get hands-on experience through a variety of organizations, including Operations Management Society (OMS), a business society club that represents the students of the TOM/EBZ department. Students can also join the Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals (CSCMP), a worldwide professional organization focused on the aspects and intricacies of supply chain and operations management.

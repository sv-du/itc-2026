## About the Program

Change how you engage with the world and seek to understand the complexities of the human experience. Explore the foundations of human behavior by delving into developmental, social, cognitive, clinical, and physiological psychology. You’ll also develop critical skills through direct experience in research methodology and statistics, with opportunities to conduct original research.
Whether you’re preparing for graduate school or seeking a career in management, public service, or other fields, this program provides a strong foundation.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Conduct research, analyze your data and interpret your findings. Educate the community during Brain Awareness Week. Join faculty-led research in topics including well-being, actualizing potential, recovering from intergenerational trauma, or social and emotional learning. Learn psychological theories and methods, then apply them to real-life scenarios. Here, you prepare for your career or postgraduate studies by doing hands-on work.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 4

### Clinical Psychologist

Provide therapy, assessment and treatment plans to help individuals with their mental health.

Card 2 of 4

### User Experience (UX) Researcher

Study human interaction with digital platforms to improve user experience and design, blending psychology and technology.

Card 3 of 4

### Human Resources Specialist

Oversee recruitment, employee relations, and organizational development, focusing on understanding human behavior in the workplace.

Card 4 of 4

### Social Media Strategist

Apply psychological principles to create engaging content and develop strategies that resonate with target audiences.

## Courses

Our degree program provides a comprehensive undergraduate education in psychology. You will receive broad exposure to developmental, social, cognitive, clinical, and physiological areas of psychology, as well as specific training in research methodology and statistics. The program is intended primarily as the foundation for entrance to graduate school in any area of psychology but also provides a good background in the science of human behavior for students seeking careers in management in public and private sectors.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

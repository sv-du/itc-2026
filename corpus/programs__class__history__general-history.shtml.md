## About the Program

Understand the complex relationship between the past and contemporary social, political and cultural issues. Develop your research and analysis skills, build historical arguments, and become a better-informed global citizen.
With the General History option, you'll have the freedom to explore your interests or complement your degree with a minor.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Gain insights into the study and practice of history, take broad survey courses of critical periods and lay the foundation for upper-division studies. Then, dive into the exciting world of historical theory, examining different approaches to the field of history. With our well-rounded approach, you will select from courses spanning international regions and periods, the US and Europe, and topical histories including sport and leisure, environmental and public history. Whether you're interested in law, research, museum studies or interdisciplinary fields, you will have the tools to pursue your dream career.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 3

### Attorney

Continue your studies with a juris doctor, then apply research, writing, critical thinking, and argument skills to matters of the law. Work in a variety of fields, from criminal law to intellectual property.

Card 2 of 3

### Researcher

Work in settings including universities or government agencies, conducting deep investigations into historical events and patterns. With this role, you’ll provide insights that inform public policy, education, and historical documentation.

Card 3 of 3

### Historical Property Manager

Preserve and maintain the legacy of historical sites through proper stewardship while sustaining their future through events and education. Historical properties are a hotspot for school tours, weddings, and other special events.

## Courses

The General History option offers a flexible curriculum ideal for students aiming for careers outside of secondary education. This option is suited for those pursuing pre-professional paths in law, business, civil service, or further academic study. It allows you to choose from a wide range of general education courses and electives, providing a strong foundation for careers involving research, analysis, and communication, such as law, foreign service, and various public or private sector roles.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

### History Club

History Club is open to all CPP students interested in History. It exists to offer students and faculty a positive and creative forum to openly discuss historical events, ideas, and related topics while promoting career pathways in fields related to the study of history.

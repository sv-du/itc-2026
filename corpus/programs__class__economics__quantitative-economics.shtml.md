## About the Program

Channel your passion for mathematics into the intellectually stimulating pursuit of applying economic theory. The Quantitative Economics option is designed for students with a strong foundation in mathematics and statistics, offering you a deeper focus on the theoretical and technical aspects of economics.
This program prepares you for advanced study in economics or other technical fields like finance, actuarial science, risk analysis and applied statistics. You'll gain the analytical skills and expertise needed for careers in the insurance and finance industries, positioning yourself for success in fields that demand a high level of quantitative proficiency.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Investigate micro- and macroeconomic theory from a mathematical perspective, apply economic research methods to evaluate previously untested theories, compete in data analysis competitions, and conduct original research under the guidance of a faculty mentor. Here, you will tackle complex problems firsthand and develop the tools necessary for thriving in data-driven environments.

### Data Analyst

Apply statistical techniques, economic theories and quantitative methods to analyze data, identify trends, and provide insights for decision-making and strategy.

### Quantitative Analyst

Develop and apply mathematical models to help businesses, particularly in finance, predict market movements and manage risk.

### Actuary

Analyze risk and uncertainty using statistics and mathematical models, often for insurance companies or pension funds.

### Operations Research Analyst

Use quantitative techniques to improve decision-making and operational efficiency in business and government sectors.

## Courses

The Quantitative Economics option provides a background and understanding of the discipline for students with a strong background in mathematics and statistics. It focuses on more theoretical and technical aspects of the field, and prepares students for graduate work in economics and other technically challenging fields such as finance, actuary science, risk analysis and applied statistics. This option is appropriate for students pursuing employment in the insurance and finance industries.

## What is the Difference?

### Applied vs Quantitative Economics

The Applied Economics option focuses largely on tangible business applications. It will provide you with an ideal foundation for employment in business or government sectors. The Quantitative Economics option focuses more on the theoretical and technical aspects of the field — preparing you for graduate work in economics, and other technically challenging fields such as finance, actuary science, risk analysis and applied statistics.

# International Travel Process

## Requesting Approval for International Travel

**Overview**

In the Division of Academic Affairs, international travel requests must be routed through the Provost's office for approval when they involve the following:

* All faculty and staff.
* All students, whether traveling with or without a faculty or staff member, for academically related purposes, including, but not limited to, field trips, study abroad programs, conferences to present research findings, or to act in some other similar capacity on behalf of the University.\*

\*Note: All requests for student travelers—with or without a faculty or staff member—must be submitted via the request form found on the [Student Travel website](https://www.cpp.edu/student-affairs/studenttravel/index.shtml).

## Prior to Travel

Follow the steps below to request travel approval for the faculty/staff traveler ONLY.

*If traveling with students:*

* First: Submit a request **for yourself** as indicated below, and include the Academic Field Trip Participant List (pdf) with your memo and supporting documentation.
* Second: Submit a separate request **for the student participants** through the [Student Travel website](https://www.cpp.edu/student-affairs/studenttravel/index.shtml), and include the student Release of Liability forms.

## Approval Process

[Step 1

### Prepare Travel Request Memo](#modal-0 "Travel Request Memo")

### Travel Request Memo

×

The memo should include relevant details about the purpose of your travel, anticipated booking arrangements, funding source(s), U.S. State Department [Travel Advisory](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/traveladvisories/traveladvisories.html/) level, and classroom coverage, if applicable.

For travel to a location with a Travel Advisory level of 3 or 4, or on the [CSURMA High Hazard/War Risk](https://www.calstate.edu/csu-system/administration/business-finance/systemwide-risk-management/Documents/25-26-High-Hazardous-Country-List.pdf) list, provide additional justification for your need to travel to a high hazard country.

* [Sample memo (pdf)](international-travel-request-sample-memo.pdf)

Close

[Step 2

### Attach Supporting Documentation](#modal-1 "Supporting documentation")

### Supporting documentation

×

Required for international travel requests:

* Agenda, itinerary, or notice of accepted paper
* U.S. Department of State [Travel Advisory](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/traveladvisories/traveladvisories.html/) information
* Signed [CSU Guidance on International Travel](csu-international-travel-guidance-may-2025.pdf) document

If traveling with students:

* Include the [Academic Field Trip Participant List (pdf)](academic-field-trip-participant-list-oct-2025.pdf "Academic Field Trip Participant List") with the memo.
* Submit a separate request for the student participants using the [Student Travel website](https://www.cpp.edu/student-affairs/studenttravel/index.shtml), where you will include a [Release of Liability (pdf)](https://www.cpp.edu/rms/forms/release-of-liability-2022.pdf) form and a CSU Guidance on International Travel document for each student traveler.

Close

[Step 3

### Submit for Review by Dean or AVP](#modal-2 "Dean or AVP Review")

### Dean or AVP Review

×

An approval signature from the Dean or AVP (or their designee\*) is required.

\*If signed by a designee, include a record of designation, e.g., AIC email.

Close

[Step 4

### Submit for Review by the Provost](#modal-3 "Provost Review")

### Provost Review

×

Create a service ticket in the [Provost's Office Portal](https://cpp.service-now.com/provost_office) and attach the memo and supporting documentation in a single PDF.

If the request is approved, the signed memo will be returned via the same service ticket.

Close

[Step 5

### Create a Travel Request in Concur](#modal-4 "Concur Request")

### Concur Request

×

If travel is approved by the Provost, include the signed memo and supporting documentation with the [Concur](https://www.cpp.edu/fas/university-accounting-services/travel/concur.shtml) request. Once approved in Concur, you may proceed with booking travel arrangements.

*Note: A Travel Request must be submitted and approved in Concur **prior** to booking travel arrangements and departure.*

Close

## After Travel is Completed

Within 60 days after the end of your trip, submit a Concur Expense Report to request reimbursement of travel expenses. The Expense Report must include all receipts of $75 or more, along with appropriate supporting documentation (e.g., conference program, meeting itinerary, or the like).

## Questions?

For questions about travel policies and reimbursement, please contact University Accounting Services at travel@cpp.edu.

For questions about the approval of travel requests by the provost, please contact John Rodriguez, Administrative Analyst, at johnr3@cpp.edu.

## Identity & Need Focused Support

[### I Am First

If you are the first in your family to pursue a college degree, here you'll find a welcoming environment that understands the challenges of being first and will help you navigate university life.](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/oss/i-am-first/index.shtml)

[### Male Success Initiatives (MSI)

Open to all students, regardless of race, color, national origin, or sex, we foster the academic, personal and professional success of all Broncos and seek to enhance the retention and graduation rates of men of color at CPP.](https://www.cpp.edu/msi/index.shtml)

[### NATIVE Pipeline

Open to all students, regardless of race, color, national origin, or sex, the Natives Aiming to Inspire Values in Education (NATIVE) Pipeline program seeks to inspire Native American students to attend college and explore their academic potential.](https://www.cpp.edu/class/ethnic-womens-studies/native-pipeline-program/index.shtml)

[### Project CAMINOS

CAMINOS stands for Cultivating Access and Mentoring through Institutional Networks and Opportunities for Success. We are dedicated to cultivating a college-going culture among students interested in applying to CPP.](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/project-caminos/index.shtml)

[### Project Rebound

Open to all, Project Rebound focuses on serving currently or formerly incarcerated students. We are here to support your academic success, professional development and reintegration to society.](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/channels/project-rebound/)

[### ARCHES

Open to all students, ARCHES (Achievement, Retention and Commitment to Higher Education Success) provides academic support services focused on students with disabilities.](https://www.cpp.edu/drc/arches/index.shtml)

[### EOP

Open to all students, the Educational Opportunity Program (EOP) serves low-income, typically first-generation students, who demonstrate the motivation and potential to success in college.](https://www.cpp.edu/ssep/eop-home.shtml)

[### RAMP

Open to all students, the Reading, Advising & Mentoring Program (RAMP) supports low-income, first-generation colleges students and students with disabilities.](https://www.cpp.edu/ramp/index.shtml)

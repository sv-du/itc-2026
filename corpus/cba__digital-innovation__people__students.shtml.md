# Hill Family Fellows

## Hill Familly Fellows'

![Roman's Picture](../img/roman_arreguin_headshot.jpg)

### Roman Arreguin

Roman Arreguin is a third-year Computer Information Systems major and as a Part-Time Student Operations Manager for both the Student Security Operations Center (SOC) as well as at the Mitchell C. Hill Student-Run Data Center (SDC), is a part of the Hill Family Fellows. In this role, he helps operate the SIEM system within the SOC with the ultimate goal of protecting and supporting the SDC. Through this role, Roman has developed hands-on skills using professional-grade security tools in a real-world environment with tools such as Splunk and Proxmox. After graduation, he plans to work as a security/SOC analyst

[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/roman-arreguin-8962b230b/ "LinkedIn. ")

![Marshall Ung's image](../img/marshall-j-ung.jpg)

### Marshall Ung

Marshall Ung is a fourth-year Computer Engineer and Part-Time Operations Manager for the Mitchell C. Hill Student-Run Data Center (SDC). Under this role, he manages the underlying infrastructure used to provide clubs and classrooms compute power that supports student learning. Prior to this position, Marshall competed in cybersecurity competitions such as the Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition and the Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition. He wishes to put these skills to use and plans to pursue a career in consulting, either offensive or defensive.

[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/marshall-j-ung/ "LinkedIn. ")

![Eric Heng's image](../img/erichen2.png)

### Eric Heng

Eric Heng is a fourth-year Computer Information Systems major at Cal Poly Pomona and serves as a Part-Time Operations Manager for the Mitchell C. Hill Student-Run Data Center (SDC) and the Student Security Operations Center (SOC). In this role, he manages infrastructure projects , explores information technology, and contributes to the organization's growth. Eric enjoys gaining hands-on experience with virtualization, server management, and enterprise security infomation event management software that are rarely accessible in traditional classroom settings. He plans to pursue a career in networking. Eric transferred from Mount San Antonio College back when they were still the Mounties, before they switched to Rizzly the Grizzly.

[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/eric-heng-631468w/ "LinkedIn. ")

![Nich Rosen's image](../img/nich_rosen_headshoteditjpg.jpg)

### Nich Rosen

**Nich Rosen** is a fourth-year Computer Information Systems major and serves as a Part-Time Operations Manager for the Student Security Operations Center (SOC), supported by the Mitchell C. Hill Center for Digital Innovation. In this role, Nich leads SOC operations, and oversees the configuration, maintenance, and operation of industry-grade SIEM systems and other cybersecurity tools within the SOC. He also leads and organizes hands-on training initiatives with the goal of broadening cybersecurity learning opportunities and expanding access for all CPP students. He is passionate about threat detection and incident response and aims to pursue a career as a SOC analyst after graduation.

[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nichrosen/ "LinkedIn. ")

### Maxwell Caron

My name is Maxwell Caron, and I am pursuing a degree in Computer Information Systems. ### Derrick Tran - Former Director of the Student Data Center (SDC), Former Hill Family Fellow

Derrick Tran – is a senior student in Computer Information Systems program and the current director of the Student Data Center (SDC). He is a Hill Family Fellow. He has been a SWIFT club member since 2021 and has been affiliated with the SDC since then. In 2023 he worked at Google/Mandiant in an internship as red team consultant intern. He recently competed in the Global Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition and the National Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition where the team obtained 2nd place nationally. He has been helping with the administrative responsibilities of the SDC, making sure critical systems are up to date. He has also mentored volunteers about IT infrastructure.

![Joe's image](../img/joseph-shin.png)

### Joseph Shin – Former Director of the Student Security Operations Center (SOC), Former Hill Family Fellow

Joseph Shin – is a graduate student in the Masters in Information Security program and is the current director of the SOC. He is a Hill Family Fellow. He has been a member of SWIFT since 2023, he participated in the National DOE CyberForce Cybersecurity Competition and in the National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity (NCAE-C) national competition where the team obtained 2nd place nationally. He is learning to deploy and manage an industry recognized SIEM platform called Splunk to monitor the Student Data Center for security events. He has used this experience to teach and share his knowledge of Splunk to other student volunteers at the SOC and created a Splunk Lab for newcomers to get practical experience using Splunk.

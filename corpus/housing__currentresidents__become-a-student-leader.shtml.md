# Student Employment and Leadership Opportunities

## Join our Team

Learn more about the different student leader and employment opportunities with University Housing Services! While each role has different responsibilities, all students are an essential part of providing a positive experience for students living on campus, and supporting University Housing Services mission and values.

## Employment Opportunities

### Positions Available

To learn about each position offered with Univeristy Housing Services, click on each card below. If you have any questions, please email housing@cpp.edu.

[#### Business Services Student Lead

Business Services Student Leaders operate the front desk of our Housing Office and Package Room. Learn more about the position and apply below.](#modal-0 "Business Services Student Leader Position Description")

### Business Services Student Leader Position Description

×

**Compensation:** $16/hr

**Job description**

Department Overview:
University Housing Services is a residential community with 12 buildings. The department is made up of three areas: Residence Life, Facilities, and Business Services.

The Business Services Student Leader (BSSL) is the first point of contact for residents, parents, and future Cal Poly students.

**Qualifications:** Excellent people/customer service skills; ability to answer phones and greet public; basic computer skills desired; word processing and spreadsheet experience preferred. Must be able to lift and carry all mail packages up to 20lbs. Must be punctual and report to work as scheduled.

**Job Description:**

* Maintain knowledge of processes and procedures related to University Housing Services
* Reception duties- greet guests entering into Housing buildings; provide information regarding UHS policies and regulations; provide information regarding the application process and status; create a welcoming space for residents and visitors.
* Daily interaction with students, parents/guardians, faculty, staff and administrators
* Provide tours of residence halls or suites
* Prepare and distribute on-campus and off-campus mail/packages
* Assist with deliveries and pick-ups to on campus departments
* Enter data into StarRez (student housing data base)
* Answer telephones, take messages
* Maintain confidential files
* Special projects assigned
* Available for special events (weekends)

[#### Conference Services Aide

Conference Services Aides work with our Conference Team over summer to assist our Summer Conference guests. Learn more about the position and apply below.](#modal-1 "Conference Services Aide Position Description")

### Conference Services Aide Position Description

×

**Compensation:** $16.50/hr starting January 2025. All Conference Aides will receive a room and board package for the summer (May 17, 2025 - August 9, 2025) in addition to their hourly wage.

  **Anticipated Hire Date**: Spring Semester 2025

  **Job description**

**Duties & Responsibilities**  The Conference Aide will be responsible for the prepping of building for guests’ arrival. They will also conduct check ins and check outs of those guests. They will be required to work front desk, linen, and any other miscellaneous shifts. The Conference Aide position will be from the end of Spring 2025 to the end of Summer 2025. The Conference Aide must be able to provide great customer service to clients while meeting deadlines, work well under pressure, and act as a liaison between the client and University for all the client needs. All interested parties MUST be available for all MANDATORY Conference Services trainings that will be held on: Friday, 4/25/2025 from 10:00am – 2:00pm and Monday, 5/19/2025 – Friday, 5/23/2025 from 8:00am-5:00pm.

**Requirements & Qualifications**  Student must be currently enrolled full time at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona. Must be in good standings with the University and Judicial Affairs and have at least a 2.0 GPA. During the summer months, this position is a 20-hours a week position. The Conference Aide position is required to live on campus during the summer months. In addition, student must have excellent verbal communication as well as customer service skills. Must be able to lift 15 pounds and stand for a long period of time. A background check must satisfactorily be completed before a candidate can be offered this position. Failure to satisfactorily complete the background check may affect the application status of applicants or continued employment of current CSU employees who apply for this position. The background check consists of the following: Employment verification, education verification, reference checks, and checks of the following systems and databases: National Social Security Number/Address Locator, Felony/Misdemeanor, National Criminal Database, Federal Criminal, Department of Motor Vehicles, and National Sex Offender Registry. Live Scan for all hiring employees.

[#### Housing Technology Support

Housing Technology Support assistants work with our Informations System Specialist and the Housing Technology team to provide service to University Housing Services staff and residents. Learn more about the position and apply below.](#modal-2 "Housing Technology Support Position Description")

### Housing Technology Support Position Description

×

**Compensation:**$16/hr

**Job description**

The Housing Technology Support works with the Information System Specialist and the Housing Technology team to provide service to University Housing Services staff and University Housing residents.

**The Housing Technology Support is responsible for:**

1. Providing first level of support to residents and staff
2. Repairing and maintaining desktops, laptops, and printers
3. Performing routine maintenance
4. Updating and upgrading equipment as needed
5. Installing and troubleshooting printers
6. Troubleshooting and diagnosing concerns of the internet and email
7. Maintaining Microsoft Office products
8. Preventing spam and pop-ups
9. Testing and evaluating existing network systems
10. Changing network user accounts
11. Troubleshooting local area networks (LANs) and wide area networks (WANs)

**Qualifications:**

1. Possess a California Driver’s License
   1. Maintain a clean driving record verified by submitting a California Department of Motor Vehicle Driver History Report
   2. Complete Defensive Driver Training
2. Be proficient in:
   1. Internet troubleshooting with regard to Ethernet cables or wireless access;
3. Have strong knowledge of
   1. Windows OS and Mac
   2. Anti-virus, spam, and pop-up protection;
   3. Network printer configuration and diagnostics;
   4. Computer hardware parts: installing, fixing and replacing;
   5. Smartphones;
   6. Microsoft Office set-up and Outlook Email configuration;
4. Effective communication
   1. Have excellent people/customer service skills;
   2. Answer phones and greet public in a professional manner;
   3. Be comfortable having daily interaction with residents, students, faculty, staff and administrators;
   4. Provide accurate information regarding University and UHS policies and regulations to residents and staff in a timely manner;
   5. Maintain confidentiality;
   6. Complete special projects as assigned
   7. Develop positive working relationships;

[#### Marketing - Graphic Designer

Graphic Designers work in the Marketing department to create print and digital materials for University Housing Services. Learn more about the position and apply below.](#modal-3 "Marketing - Graphic Designer Position Description")

### Marketing - Graphic Designer Position Description

×

**Compensation:**$16.50/hr

**Job description**

Employment in the Marketing department also involves projects for Parking and Transportation Services (PTS) and University Police Department (UPD). Both PTS and UPD operates all parking related services including fees, regulations, the Cal Poly Rideshare program, and Bronco Express shuttle service.

The graphic designer and the web design/development team is led by the Marketing Coordinator. The team designs, prepares, and prints materials related to the marketing needs of UHS, PTS, and UPD.

**Primary duties of the Graphic Designer include but are not limited to:**

* Utilizing desktop design software to design and develop brochures, flyers, posters and other related materials;
* Creating visual displays for campus events;
* Designing promotional materials;
* Assisting in special projects as assigned or needed

**Qualifications:**

**Job Experience**

* Two years job related experience in Graphic Design;
* Knowledge of accepted graphic art standards;
* Digital portfolio (class work is acceptable to demonstrate skills and creativity);
* Knowledge of interactive design principles, standards and theories;

**Knowledge of Applications, Software, and Programs**

* Proficient with the Windows operating system;
* Entry to intermediate proficiency in desktop publishing software programs such as: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, ImageReady, InDesign, Acrobat and Microsoft Office programs;
* Knowledge of Dreamweaver, Flash and web-based media is desirable, but not required;

**Professional Skills and Leadership Abilities**

* Ability to interface with a large number of professional staff and student employees in a shared space;
* Ability to communicate in person, by phone and e-mail;
* A high level of creativity, attention to detail, and the ability to set/meet deadlines with minimal supervision is a must;
* Creativity and ability to focus on specific details;
* Ability to analyze and research editorial content in order to design promotional materials in a creative manner, while maintaining consistency in marketing materials;
* Excellent English verbal communication and writing skills;
* Must demonstrate responsible and dependable behavior;
* Ability to work well with various residents, students, faculty, staff, and high-level administrators;
* Excellent customer service skills;
* High degree of accuracy and attention to detail;
* Motivated, innovative, and shows initiative;
* Excellent time management and organization skills;

**Job description**

**This position is for 2025-2026 University Housing Services residents – this does not include the University Village.**

**Hourly Rate:** $16.50

This position, along with its continued employment, is contingent upon the availability of funding, including work-study allocations, state appropriations, grant support, and other financial resources. It is also subject to compliance with current and future federal and state mandates.

In the event that an offer or role must be rescinded due to funding constraints or future federal or state mandates, the university is committed to working closely with impacted students to explore alternative opportunities and available resources.

Consistent with California law and federal civil rights laws, Cal Poly Pomona (CPP) provides equal opportunity in education and employment without unlawful discrimination or preferential treatment based on race, sex, color, ethnicity, or national origin.

**Position Overview**

As a member of the Residence Life Team, the Program Assistants understands that they are responsible for and will abide by the duties outlined below as assigned by their Lead. This is subject to change depending on community and department needs.

**Requirements**

* Must be a 2025-2026 University Housing Services resident – this does not include the University Village.
* Must be available Wednesdays from 7:00pm – 9:00pm.
* Must be available to work after 4:00pm to attend programs/events.
* Must be able to work evenings and weekends.

**Details**

* Plan, publicize, coordinate, and evaluate educational and social programs during the 2025-2026 academic year.
* Create and market all events through the myBAR system. Enter all attendance into myBAR no later than 24 hours after the event has occurred.
* Facilitate the set-up, clean-up, and tasks during assigned programs as directed by the designated Lead.
* Collaborate with campus departments on the implementation of workshops and activities for the 2025-2026 academic year.
* Publicize and implement Welcome Week 2025 events and initiatives.
* Attend all Welcome Week programs and preparation times.
* Publicize and implement Welcome Week 2025 events and initiatives.
* Market Welcome Week events to recruit participants.

**Qualifications**

* Must be a CPP student with a GPA of 2.0 or higher
* Must have a graduation date of Spring 2026 or later
* Must have knowledge of the department’s mission, vision, values, and goals
* Must have strong conceptualizing skills
* Must be able to work with limited or no supervision and lead other students
* Must be able to take initiative on projects, be proactive, and/or a self-starter
* Must work effectively with a diverse population of residents, students, staff, faculty, administrators, and parents
* Must have excellent communication skills and always maintain professionalism
* Must communicate with the campus community in courteous and respectful manner
* Must have the ability to adhere to strict deadlines

[#### Transfer Thrive Student Ambassador

Transfer Thrive Student Ambassadors work in the Residence Life department with our Transfer Thrive program to support Transfer students in their academic, career, and personal growth.](#modal-5 "Transfer Thrive Student Ambassador Position Description")

### Transfer Thrive Student Ambassador Position Description

×

**Compensation:** $16/hr

**Work Hours:** Up to 20 hours a week

**Position Description**

University Housing Services at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona is seeking motivated leaders to join our team for the 2024 – 2025 academic year as Transfer Thrive Student Ambassadors.

**Transfer Thrive Student Ambassador**

As a direct response to the needs of our Transfer students, University Housing Services (UHS) developed the Transfer Thrive program. Transfer Thrive is dedicated to serving Transfer students through a dynamic living learning community designed to support Transfer students in their academic, career, and personal growth. Transfer Thrive Student Ambassadors offer career, educational, and social programming and workshops. In addition, Transfer Thrive Student Ambassadors are responsible for outreaching and serving as peer mentors to Transfer residents to ensure they experience a sense of belonging and connection.

**Position Experiences & Responsibilities:**

* Cultivate a welcoming and inclusive environment for Transfer residents and student staff to thrive.
* Plan and lead workshops and events on topics related to career development, academic success, and personal wellness in partnership with campus partners.
* Develop and implement passive programming initiatives that address the interests and needs of the Transfer Thrive community.
* Create content and manage Transfer Thrive’s newsletter and/or social media.
* Serve as a peer mentor by connecting with Transfer residents, encouraging involvement, and supporting their transition to campus life.
* Model values of inclusivity and equity, using these principles to guide conversations, programs, and interactions with residents.

**Position Parameters:**

* Currently enrolled undergraduate or graduate student, full or part-time for 2024 – 2025 school year

As part of the hiring process, once a student accepts an offer for employment, the department will conduct a soft background check. This background check includes verifying GPA and grades, a conduct inquiry, and calling references to confirm employment history and/or ask about performance. The employment offer is contingent upon receipt of satisfactory verification of information.

For questions or concerns, email Hailee Shafer at hkshafer@cpp.edu and include "Transfer Thrive" in the subject line {response turnaround is a minimum of three business days}.

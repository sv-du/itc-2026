# Plant Science, B.S.

## About the Major

![Two female students sit on the ground and examine a coffee plant.](../img/2017-coffee-plant-920x570.jpg)

From the food we eat to the clothes we wear, plants have an impact on our everyday lives.

Discover the science behind plants at Cal Poly Pomona — from breeding and propagation, planting to irrigation, and pest management to plant diseases. You'll learn about how science is connected to plants, farming, agriculture, and horticulture.

You'll gain valuable hands-on experience in our campus farm, apiary, labs, and greenhouses. You can learn about large and small-scale production, organic and hydroponic growing methods, soil science, turf management, and arboriculture.

You can learn more about the courses offered in the major — required and elective — as well as general education requirements in the University Catalog link below.

## Career Outcomes

[Discover More About Plant Science Careers](../careers-in-plant-science.shtml)

Card 1 of 5

### Plant Scientist

Studies plant biology to improve growth, reproduction, and adaptation for agricultural or environmental purposes.

Card 2 of 5

### Plant Breeder/Geneticist

Develops improved plant varieties with desirable traits such as pest resistance or climate tolerance.

Card 3 of 5

### Horticulturist

Specializes in the cultivation of fruits, vegetables, flowers, and ornamental plants for commercial or research use.

Card 4 of 5

### Greenhouse Manager

Oversees the growth and care of plants in controlled environments for research, retail, or food production.

Card 5 of 5

### Botanist

Conducts scientific research on plant life, classification, ecology, and conservation.

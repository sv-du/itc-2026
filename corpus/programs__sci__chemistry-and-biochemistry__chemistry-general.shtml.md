## About the Program

Immerse yourself in the study of atoms, molecules, chemical reactions and the makings of matter itself as you bond with others in a collaborative lab environment and formulate solutions to the world’s most pressing issues. Put theory into practice in our state-of-the-art laboratory facilities and join experienced faculty in exciting research opportunities. With the general chemistry emphasis, you will have the flexibility to shape your coursework and choose electives to match your interest in science.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Dive deeper into the classic physical chemistry you’ve experienced in school and expand your horizons into quantum physical chemistry. Learn the theory behind identifying compounds in organic chemistry, then identify those compounds in the lab. Become part of a research lab with a faculty mentor and publish your findings in academic journals. Here, you not only learn theory, you apply it and gain practical experience to prepare you for a career in countless fields.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Research and Development Chemist

Design and develop new products and processes for energy, food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, electronics, and more.

### Forensic Scientist

Analyze crime scene evidence to help solve crimes and aid in investigations.

### Biochemist

Conduct experiments to research the chemical processes and substances within living organisms for medicine, genetics and environmental science.

### Pharmacologist

Investigate how drugs get absorbed, metabolize and affect living organisms to improve pharmaceuticals and overall health outcomes. Design clinical trials, assess toxicity and drug resistance and more.

### Pharmaceutical Sciences

Develop and manufacture pharmaceutical products, including drugs and diagnostic tests, to assist in the treatment of health conditions.

### Clinical Lab Science

Conduct laboratory tests and examine samples to help diagnose diseases and inform their treatment.

### Medical Careers

Prepare for entry into professional programs such as Medical Doctor, Pharmacist, Physician Assistant, etc.

### Environmental Science

Investigate chemical processes in the environment, assess human impact and help develop solutions to environmental issues.

### Government Agencies

Conduct research, work in regulatory agencies or inform public policy from federal to local levels.

### Quality Control and Assurance

Ensure company processes and products meet all necessary requirements and standards. Detect issues in products (QC) or prevent and detect quality problems (QA).

### Chemical Education

Prepare for credential and postgraduate programs and share your passion for chemistry with learners of any age. Opportunities include teaching in elementary and secondary schools, two-year colleges, four-year institutions, or Ph.D-granting institutions.

Slide 12

### Intellectual Property/Law

Assess new chemical compounds, formulations or optimized processes to determine if they qualify for patent protection or use your expertise to review cases of potential patent infringement.

Slide 13

### Graduate and Professional School

Continue your studies with advanced degrees in chemistry or applied sciences or continue into professional programs, including medical, pharmacy, physician’s assistant or nursing school.

## Courses

The General option is the most flexible program. In consultation with an advisor, up to 12 units of electives can be chosen from outside of chemistry to create a truly interdisciplinary program of study. This degree option allows students who have an ADT in Chemistry to earn their degree in 60 semester units upon transfer.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

## Featured Sub-Discipline: Inorganic Chemistry

Research in inorganic chemistry encompasses studying all 118 elements on the periodic table, with applications in catalysis, materials, sustainability, nuclear chemistry, polymers, pigments, fuels, and medicines, for example. Students at CPP are encouraged to conduct undergraduate research with faculty in many of these areas and can learn advanced air-free synthesis, spectroscopy, crystallography and computational methods in inorganic chemistry

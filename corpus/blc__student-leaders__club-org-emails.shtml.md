### February 2, 2026 - Clubs & Orgs: Report Financial Misconduct and Do Not Use Banking Apps

# Reporting Fiscal Irregularities and Prohibited Use of Off-Campus Payment Platforms

Dear Clubs and Organizations,

This is your biannual reminder to ensure compliance with the Administration of Organization Funds ([ICSUAM 03141.01](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/15008510/latest)) and Reporting of Fiscal Improprieties ([Executive Order 1104](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/17952218/latest/)).

**Financial Requirements**

Student organizations are required to deposit and expend all funds collected on behalf of the student organization in an Agency account through the Associated Students, Inc. at Cal Poly Pomona. Student organizations that are registered with the Bronco Leadership Center (BLC) and are in good standing are eligible to open and maintain an Agency Fund Account (AFA) in ASI Financial Services.

Funds held in Agency accounts include membership dues and fees, prizes/awards from competitions, and fundraising income, including monies generated through food sales, item sales, ticket charges for University-approved programs, revenue from university-approved volunteer activities, and similar means. Student organization funds may not be raised through the sale of alcohol or tobacco products.

Upon submission of cash and checks, the appointed Treasurer shall issue a receipt to the payee, identifying the payee’s name, date of receipt, payment type and reason for payment identified.  Copies of these receipts shall be given with the deposit to ASI Financial Services with the deposit documentation for the agency account. Deposits of cash or checks from club members shall be deposited into the agency account within **five (5) days** from the first day cash or checks are received.

Monies collected in the name of the student organization must be kept in an ASI Agency fund. **Cal Poly Pomona does not permit student organizations to maintain off-campus bank accounts, including electronic fund transfer applications such as, but not limited to: Apple Cash, Google Pay, CashApp, PayPal, Square Cash, Venmo, and Zelle.**These platforms currently require the account to be linked to the account holder’s bank account; they are considered off-campus bank accounts.

You can find the complete RSO Finance Manual on the [ASI, Inc. Finance Support webpage](https://asi.cpp.edu/services/club-services/finance-support/).

**Executive Order 1104: Reporting of Fiscal Improprieties**

[Full policy text (calstate.policystat.com)](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/13576706/latest/)

Fiscal improprieties include fraud, embezzlement, or theft, initiated not only by a campus constituent such as a student, university employee, or auxiliary employee but also by an external party. Actions that result in a loss of assets shall be reported as required, even if the loss is later recovered or reimbursed.

If there are any actual or suspected fiscal improprieties, please immediately report them to Jon Saclolol (jcsaclolo@cpp.edu). The campus will investigate any complaint, allegation, or possible evidence of fiscal improprieties.

**Do**

* Report actual or suspected fiscal improprieties. Examples of fiscal improprieties are fraud, embezzlement, or theft.
* Send a report, even if it is less than $5,000.
* Send information to jcsaclolo@cpp.edu.

**Do not**

* Do not delay in emailing jcsaclolo@cpp.edu to report actual or suspected fiscal improprieties.

We appreciate your commitment to upholding the standards of our university and ensuring a positive experience for all. Please do not hesitate to reach out if you have any questions or need further clarification on the above matters.

Thank you,

Bronco Leadership Center

### October 28, 2025 - BSC Room Rental Pricing Update

Dear Broncos,

Beginning July 1, 2026, the Bronco Student Center (BSC) will implement new room rental pricing. While this new structure will apply to all Cal Poly Pomona departments and programs that use BSC spaces and services, **registered student clubs and organizations will still be able to reserve BSC spaces at no cost during standard operating hours.** The charges will only apply when requests are made outside of those hours or involve certain types of partnerships.

Since the BSC’s expansion in 2004, room rental rates have not been adjusted to reflect the significant increase in operational costs over the past two decades. To ensure ASI’s long-term financial sustainability and to continue delivering high-quality event services at the BSC, we must adjust our pricing to better recover ASI’s direct and indirect costs of facilitating events.

A detailed breakdown of the new rates, including a three-year pricing forecast, is available at [https://asi.cpp.edu/facilities/pricingupdates/.](https://asi.cpp.edu/facilities/pricingupdates/)

These updated rates include audiovisual (A/V) equipment and support from ASI staff. **Additional fees may apply** to any event that meets the following criteria:

* Large-scale events that reserve most or all the reservable rooms in the BSC may require additional event staff. All ASI event staff are certified in advanced CPR, AED, and First Aid and trained to respond in emergencies.
* Events that are scheduled outside the BSC’s standard [operational hours.](https://asi.cpp.edu/calendars-hours/facility-hours/?term=fall-2025)
* Events requesting the on-site presence of the Conference & Events Manager or the Events Coordinator (specialized A/V support).
* When a student club or organization partners with a university department or program to   host an event.

If you have questions or would like more details, please reach out to Janet Castro, ASI Conference and Events Manager, at janetcastro@cpp.edu or (909) 869-4452.

We appreciate your understanding and support as we work together to sustain the Bronco Student Center for years to come.

Sincerely,
**Associated Students, Inc.**

### September 5, 2025 - Club/Org Financial Announcements

## Biannual Reminder

*Report Fiscal Improprieties and Off-campus Banks and Mobile Cash Apps are Prohibited*

This is your biannual reminder to ensure compliance with the Administration of Organization Funds ([ICSUAM 03141.01](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/15008510/latest/)) and Reporting of Fiscal Improprieties ([Executive Order 1104](https://calstate.policystat.com/policy/17952218/latest/)).

Financial Requirements
Student organizations are required to deposit and expend all funds collected on behalf of the student organization in an Agency account through the Associated Students, Inc. at Cal Poly Pomona. Student organizations that are registered with the Bronco Leadership Center (BLC) and are in good standing are eligible to open and maintain an Agency Fund Account (AFA) in ASI Financial Services.

Funds held in Agency accounts include membership dues and fees, prizes/awards from competitions, and fundraising income, including monies generated through food sales, item sales, ticket charges for University-approved programs, revenue from university-approved volunteer activities, and similar means. Student organization funds may not be raised through the sale of alcohol or tobacco products.

Upon submission of cash and checks, the appointed Treasurer shall issue a receipt to the payee, identifying the payee’s name, date of receipt, payment type and reason for payment identified. Copies of these receipts shall be given with the deposit to ASI Financial Services with the deposit documentation for the agency account. Deposits of cash or checks from club members shall be deposited into the agency account within five (5) days from the first day cash or checks are received.

Monies collected in the name of the student organization must be kept in an ASI Agency fund. Cal Poly Pomona does not permit student organizations to maintain off-campus bank accounts, including electronic fund transfer applications such as, but not limited to: Apple Cash, Google Pay, CashApp, PayPal, Square Cash, Venmo, and Zelle. These platforms currently require the account to be linked to the account holder’s bank account; they are considered off-campus bank accounts.

You can find the complete RSO Finance Manual on the [ASI, Inc. Finance Support](https://asi.cpp.edu/services/club-services/finance-support/) webpage.

Executive Order 1104: Reporting of Fiscal Improprieties
Ensures that people know they can report if funds, both campus and non-campus, are being misused.

Fiscal improprieties include fraud, embezzlement, or theft, initiated not only by a campus constituent such as a student, university employee, or auxiliary employee but also by an external party. Actions that result in a loss of assets shall be reported as required, even if the loss is later recovered or reimbursed.

If there are any actual or suspected fiscal improprieties, please immediately report them to jcsaclolo@cpp.edu. The campus will investigate any complaint, allegation, or possible evidence of fiscal improprieties.

**Do**

 Report actual or suspected fiscal improprieties. Examples of fiscal improprieties are fraud, embezzlement, or theft.
 Send a report, even if it is less than $5,000.
 Send information to jcsaclolo@cpp.edu.

**Do not**

 Do not delay in emailing jcsaclolo@cpp.edu to report actual or suspected fiscal improprieties.

Sincerely,

The Bronco Leadership Center

### April 17, 2025 - Announcement and Update for Club & Organization Advisors

Hello, Advisors! I hope your week is going well. I also ask that you take a look at these reminders and updates:

* Clubs & Organizations should have submitted their annual budgets to their Council. Budgets **require an Advisor’s signature.**
* Purchase Orders (POs), Disbursement Requests (DRs), for expenses and reimbursement are continually turned in to ASI Financial Services. **These documents require an Advisor’s signature.** If you are no longer serving as Advisor, or you experience difficulty accessing these documents (in OnBase DocuSign or AdobeSign), please let me know.
* Club & Organization, and Council **elections are taking place for the 2025-26 academic year.** A President, Treasurer, Scheduler, and Advisor, must be identified. Current Presidents are required to submit a Club Proposal identifying the 2025-26 Eboard. Deadline for Proposals is April 20.
* New this year: ASI Financial Services requires that an additional student be on file as an Authorized Signer. This is in addition to the President and Treasurer.
* New this year: Lecturers are once again eligible to advise clubs and organizations. 🙂

Thanks for your attention, and enjoy the remainder of the week!

Best,

Anita

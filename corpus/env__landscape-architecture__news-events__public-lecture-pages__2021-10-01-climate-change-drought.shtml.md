# 2021-22 CPPLA Public Lecture Series —

## Landscape Architecture and the Science of Climate Change: Drought

**Date:** October 01, 2021
**Time:** 12:00pm to 1:00pm
**Location:** Virtual

The Department of Landscape Architecture is hosting **“Landscape Architecture and the Science of Climate Change,”** series of talks pairing the research of scientists with the implementation of landscape architects on climate change topics.

The first installment of the 2021-22 public event program “DROUGHT” presents talks by **Alex Hall**, Professor, Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences and Director, UCLA Center for Climate Science; and **Gary Lai**, Principal and Director of Regenerative Design at MIG,Inc. and Board of Directors for USGBC-LA.

**“Landscape Architecture and the Science of Climate Change”**talks are scheduled on the first Friday of each month, 12-1 p.m. PST. (Except April 8, 2022). The 2021-22 program is organized with **Ronnie Siegel**, chair of the Climate Action Committee of the Southern California chapter of the American Society of Landscape Architects (SoCal ASLA) and **Carlos Flores**, SoCal ASLA Climate Action Committee Member and Lecturer at Cal Poly Pomona. Each talk is moderated by a faculty member of the Department of Landscape Architecture at Cal Poly Pomona.

Register to receive the Zoom link at [https://landscapeandscience.eventbrite.com](https://nam11.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Flandscapeandscience.eventbrite.com%2F&data=04%7C01%7Csbgonzaga%40cpp.edu%7Cf199daa45f824b4a4f8e08d97d4bbf90%7C164ba61e39ec4f5d89ffaa1f00a521b4%7C0%7C0%7C637678586548884566%7CUnknown%7CTWFpbGZsb3d8eyJWIjoiMC4wLjAwMDAiLCJQIjoiV2luMzIiLCJBTiI6Ik1haWwiLCJXVCI6Mn0%3D%7C1000&sdata=NTMDbvWhAPGkoSlPhvJr3YPwm%2BiBPzaVj%2FEMysETonY%3D&reserved=0). Future topics are:

* Wildfire (Nov. 5)
* Biodiversity Loss (Dec. 3)
* Flooding (Jan. 7)
* Sea Rise (Feb. 4)
* Carbon (March 4)
* Heat (April 8)
* Food (May 6)
* Extinction (June 3)

For questions, email Carlos Flores at caflores@cpp.edu.

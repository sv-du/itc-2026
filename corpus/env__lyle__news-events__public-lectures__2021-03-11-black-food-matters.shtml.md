# The Lyle Center Presents:

## Black Food Matters: Racial Justice in the Wake of Food Justice

**Date:** March 11, 2021
**Time:** 12:00pm to 01:00pm
**Location:** TBD

![Lyle Center presents. Black Food Matters.  Racial Justice in the wake of Food Justice.  Featuring:  Analena hope hassberg, Cal Poly Pomona.  Gillian Richards-Greaves, Coastal Carolina University.  Kimberly Kasper, Rhodes College.  Judith Williams, Florida International University.  Thursday, March 11 | 12:00pm PST.  Please email crs@cpp.edu for Zoom link](../img/black-food-matters-ig.jpg)

Black Food Matters: Racial Justice in the Wake of Food Justice is a virtual public lecture inspired by the 2020 [book of the same name](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/black-food-matters) by Hanna Garth and Ashante M. Reese analyzing Black food culture and the violence and threats it faces.

Joining the discussion are the following educators, experts and activists: Professor **Analena Hope Hassberg** from Cal Poly Pomona's Department of Ethnic and Women's Studies, whose [research](https://www.cpp.edu/ceis/ethnic-womens-studies/Dr.%20Analena%20Hope%20Hassberg%20.shtml) investigates food insecurity, food sovereignty and the centrality of food in liberation movements; **Gillian Richards-Greaves**, Associate Professor of Anthropology at Coastal Carolina University, who studies the [cultural expressions](https://www.coastal.edu/academics/facultyprofiles/humanities/anthropologyandgeography/gillianrichardsgreaves/) of the African Diaspora; anthropological archaeologist **Kimberly Kasper**, Associate Professor of Anthropology at Rhodes College, who [researches and teaches](https://www.rhodes.edu/bio/kimberly-kasper) on the ecological and cultural legacy of food and medicinal practices; and Florida International University Ph.D. candidate **Judith Williams**, a [McKnight doctoral fellow and former chef](http://gss.fiu.edu/people/graduate-students/judith-williams/) whose dissertation explores anti-black discrimination within Miami’s restaurant industry.

Please join us on Thursday, March 11 at 12:00 p.m. (PST). To get the Zoom link, email crs@cpp.edu.

# Student Life

Student life at Cal Poly Pomona thrives through a wide array of student organizations and clubs that cater to diverse interests and academic disciplines. These groups offer students the chance to connect with peers, engage in meaningful activities, and develop leadership skills. With opportunities for networking, site visits to industry leaders, and hands-on experiences, these clubs not only enrich your college experience but also provide a strong foundation for your future career.

## Student Organizations and Clubs

Student organizations and clubs at Cal Poly Pomona are essential to a vibrant campus life. They provide spaces for students to connect with peers, explore their interests, and develop leadership skills. By participating in these groups, students can engage in networking events, attend site visits, and collaborate on projects that bring classroom learning into real-world contexts, all while building a supportive community.

![](../img/oms-logo-trans.png)

### Operations Management Society

Operations Management Society (OMS) is a business society club that represents the students of the TOM/EBZ department. Our goal has always been to bridge the gap between what students learn in the classroom and reality. OMS hosts a variety of events throughout the year to expand members' networking map, and insight into the field of supply chain and operations management. We strive to assist our members to develop their leadership skills through professional development training among Cal Poly alumni, industry leaders, and faculty.

Join the OMS Club on Tuesdays & Thursdays during U-Hour:

### Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals

Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals (CSCMP) is a worldwide professional organization; an organization being concentrated on the aspects and intricacies of supply chain and operations management.

Our chapter’s mission is to motivate and propel business administration students so that they may reach the acme of their potential as formidable leaders in their respective supply chain career.  We advance student potential by offering facility tours, mentorship programs, guest speaker events, networking opportunities, and professional development workshops.

Students should join CSCMP of CPP to bolster their clout in the supply chain arena by professional networking and gleaning practical knowledge not taught in classroom study.  Furthermore, membership can be retained as a lifelong resource with the greater CSCMP Roundtable even after graduation.

This organization has had presence on the Cal Poly campus since fall 2019.  In that time, we’ve hosted a Six-Sigma workshop, a virtual Covid-19 supply chain speaker panel, and multiple general meetings with keynote speakers.

## Net Working

Networking is a cornerstone of student organizations and clubs at Cal Poly Pomona, offering students invaluable opportunities to connect with peers, alumni, and industry professionals. Through organized events such as guest speaker sessions, career fairs, and industry panels, students can gain insights into various fields, learn from experienced professionals, and build relationships that can lead to internships, job opportunities, and mentorship. Additionally, site visits to companies and organizations provide students with a firsthand look at potential career paths and the chance to engage with industry leaders in their working environments. These networking experiences are designed to bridge the gap between academic learning and professional success, equipping students with the connections and knowledge needed to thrive in their future careers.

## Site Visit

Site visits are a key component of student organizations and clubs at Cal Poly Pomona, offering students a unique opportunity to experience professional environments firsthand. During these visits, students tour companies and organizations, interact with industry leaders, and gain insights into day-to-day operations and career opportunities. These experiences provide a practical perspective on various fields, help students understand industry trends, and allow them to ask questions directly to professionals, thereby bridging the gap between classroom theory and real-world application. Site visits also help students build valuable connections and clarify their career aspirations.

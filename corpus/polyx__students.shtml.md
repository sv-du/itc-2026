## What is a PolyX?

Signature polytechnic experiences (PolyX) at Cal Poly Pomona help students discover real world challenges and solve them using hands-on techniques. Through collaborative learning and with intense mentoring, PolyX brings lessons out of the classroom and prepares students for professional and civic success.

## Interested in a PolyX?

Get involved in a PolyX and start your learn-by-doing experience at CPP! Use the PolyX search feature below to explore the PolyX Hub and find the PolyX that is right for you.

Intense Mentorship

### Intense Mentorship

×

Students engaged in a PolyX will be mentored “intensely” by faculty, staff, industry mentors, and/or peers. “Intense” mentoring is intentional, regular, invasive, in-depth, and sustained. It involves referral to resources for appropriate student populations and mentoring the whole student. Mentorship is tailored to the needs of students, understanding the environment in which a student functions and pairs resources and support to issues that students face.

Close

Dissemination Beyond the Classroom

### Dissemination Beyond the Classroom

×

The results of the PolyX will be published, presented, or publicly accessible in one form or another. In the event that the data of the project is of a sensitive or proprietary nature, the publication may be in a redacted form in Bronco Scholar, and presentations to small groups of selected professionals from the partnering corporate body are acceptable.

Close

Creativity, Discovery, and Innovation

### Creativity, Discovery, and Innovation

×

The PolyX will result in an original work of some sort. This may include, (but is not limited to):

* An original artistic work
* An original research publication that sheds new light on a research question
* The presentation of the results of an experiment that provides new insight in an area of scientific study
* The development of procedures/policies to solve a specific problem
* The creation of a specific product to fulfill an identified need.

Close

Diverse and Multidisciplinary Perspectives

### Diverse and Multidisciplinary Perspectives

×

A PolyX may invite and encourage participation from a variety of disciplines.

Close

Community and Global Engagement

### Community and Global Engagement

×

A PolyX will engage in some way with an identifiable community.

* On-campus communities such as:
  + A specific group within a discipline
  + A specific club or organization
  + An identified subset of Cal Poly Pomona’s students, faculty or staff
  + The campus as a whole
* Off-campus constituencies such as:
  + Specific non-profit organizations
  + Specific corporate partners
  + An identified group or organization
  + A municipal, state or national government

Potential benefits or drawbacks of the project to that community will be considered and explicitly stated at the outset.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-15.png)

Collaborative Learning

### Collaborative Learning

×

While faculty oversight and mentorship play a key role in the project, it should be primarily a student-led effort. Students will participate in generating questions or topics of study, developing answers, defining roles and responsibilities for team members, finding resources, setting schedules and deadlines and asking follow-up questions.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-4.png)

Critical Thinking and Problem Solving

### Critical Thinking and Problem Solving

×

The project will have reflection exercises integrated into it in some way. Through these exercises, the student participants pause to consider what they are learning, how they might need to alter their project’s scope, course or focus and analyze the effectiveness of the methods they are pursuing. The goal of the reflection  includes heightening students’ awareness of the PolyX and the skills they have developed as a result of participating in the PolyX. This will support students’ ability to articulate the PolyX experience.

Close

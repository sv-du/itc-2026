## About the Program

Jump into the study of human movement, health and wellness as you learn about physical activity, exercise and rehabilitation. With biomechanics, exercise science and molecular exercise physiology laboratories, you can learn by doing while serving the community. Through our options in Exercise Science, Public Health and Sport and Social Justice, you can tailor your education to your dream career.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Assist children with motor skills in the Motor Development Clinic or use state-of-the-art screening equipment in the Exercise Science Laboratory. Help others navigate their injuries and conditions, train them in sports and fitness, and teach them healthy lifestyle habits that will last a lifetime. Our comprehensive curriculum and focus on the practical application of this knowledge provide you with the strong foundation needed to pursue your professional or educational goals.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Sports Coach

Help athletes develop their skills, strategize for competitions, and encourage communication and teamwork.

### Personal Trainer

Develop and implement exercise plans for individuals with various goals and needs. Set objectives, assess clients’ physical condition and guide them to their desired outcome.

### Strength and Conditioning Trainer

Create and direct exercise plans to improve the performance of competitive athletes and athletic teams.

### Exercise Physiologist

Help individuals improve their health through education and fitness and exercise programs. Assess patients’ health conditions and develop plans to meet their unique needs.

### Wellness Coach

Assist others in achieving their health and wellness goals. Provide education, guidance and support.

### Biomechanist

Study the mechanics of how humans move and respond to forces, then apply principles and best practices to fields including sports medicine, exercise science or ergonomics.

### Graduate and Professional School

Prepare for programs including: Public Health Specialist (MPH, DrPH), Physical Education Teacher Credential, Adapted Physical Education Teacher Credential, Physical Therapy (DPT), Occupational Therapy (MOT, DOT), Athletic Training (MSAT), Physicians Assistant (PA), Registered Nurse (RN), Medical Doctor (MD), Doctor of Osteopathy (DO), Doctor of Philosophy, Doctor of Education (Ph.D, Ed.D), Clinical Exercise Physiologist.

## Program Options

Previous
Next

### Exercise Science

Prepares you to work as a fitness consultant in a variety of settings, for advanced degree programs in exercise science, or entrance to professional schools in such areas as physical therapy, physician's assistant, chiropractic or medicine.

### Public Health

Prepares you to work at a university or college wellness center, in corporate settings as a wellness professional and to work as a health educator in a wide variety of settings

### Sport and Social Justice

Equips you for work in social justice, with international sport organizations, research and consulting on diversity and sport issues, and for advanced graduate work in the field of kinesiology.

## Learn By Doing

### The Motor Development Clinic

Learn by doing is embedded in programs across campus, and the kinesiology department is no exception with the Motor Development Clinic.
Since 1979, the clinic, located in Kellogg Gym, has been dedicated to improving children's motor skills and helping them fine-tune their cooperation, attention, and social skills. In 2023, the clinic started incorporating a new fitness-based program for adults with disabilities.

## About the Program

Expand your horizons with a theoretical and experimental study of aerodynamics, astrodynamics, propulsion, flight mechanics, systems engineering and aerospace vehicle design – literal rocket science and more!
Through hands-on projects and cutting-edge research that simulate the aerospace industry, as well as internships and job placements, you will graduate with both a conceptual understanding and a portfolio of real-world accomplishments. With program emphases in aeronautics and astronautics, you can chart your course and propel yourself toward your dream career in aerospace.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Design and build satellites, conduct sub- and supersonic wind tunnel testing, fly unmanned air vehicles, launch liquid rockets and more in our ten dedicated laboratories. Showcase your designs and compete in front of industry leaders, including NASA, JPL and AIAA. You won’t just study flight patterns or rocket construction methods – you will demonstrate them with state-of-the-art tools and equipment at your fingertips.

## Career Outcomes

### Aerospace Engineer

Design, develop, and test commercial and military aircraft, spacecraft, satellites, missiles and rockets. Innovate technologies used in aviation, defense and space exploration.

### Mechanical Engineer

Draw upon thermodynamics, mechanics and material science to design, develop, build and test mechanical devices. Create solutions for aircraft and spacecraft components like sensors and landing gear.

### Aviation Manager

Oversee airport operations, flight plans, aircraft maintenance, flight crews, budgeting, and communication with government officials, vendors and contractors. Monitor and ensure compliance for safety.

### Drafter / Computer-Aided Design (CAD) Operator

Prepare the detailed technical drawings and specification sheets required to help build products and structures.

## Program Emphasis

### Aeronautics

Study aerodynamics, propulsion, materials science and structural engineering for the design, development and operation of aircraft and spacecraft.

### Astronautics

Take coursework in physics, materials science and systems engineering for the design, development and operation of spacecraft and exploration of outer space.

## Courses

Our curriculum encompasses both aeronautics and astronautics, offering a comprehensive education to create well-rounded aerospace engineers. Students can choose an emphasis area in their junior year, tailoring their education to their personal and professional interests. Technical elective courses provide our graduates with a unique skill set, setting them apart in the competitive job market.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

## Professor, Aerospace Engineering, College of Science

### Ali Ahmadi

Dr. Ali Ahmadi received his B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from Cal Poly Pomona and a Ph.D. from MIT. He has taught 15 Aerospace courses, conducted research with NASA and the US Air Force, and received teaching awards from Northrop Grumman and Lockheed Martin. He also directs the Subsonic Wind Tunnel and Supersonic Wind Tunnel Lab at Cal Poly Pomona.

## Beyond the Classroom Walls

### Liquid Rocket Lab

The Liquid Rocket Laboratory was established for the advancement and training of students in aerospace rocketry. Students lead and gain experience in the design, manufacture, testing and launch of various rocket projects, which sets them apart and gives them a competitive advantage as they apply for jobs in the aerospace industry.

## Accreditation

### ABET Accreditation

The Aerospace Engineering Department offers an Aerospace Engineering program leading to a Bachelor of Science degree.

The Aerospace Engineering program is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission of ABET, [https://www.abet.org](https://www.abet.org/), under the General Criteria and the Program Criteria for Aerospace and Similarly Named Engineering Programs.

For more information, please visit our [program accreditation page.](https://www.cpp.edu/engineering/aro/abet.shtml)

# Alumni News

## What STS Majors Are Doing With Their Degrees

[![Chrisopher O'Hara](../img/christopher-ohara-green.jpg)

### From STS to two PhDs

Since his undergraduate days at CPP, Christopher O’Hara ('15) had an interdisciplinary and international perspective. He has continued to grow in these dimensions. In October 2024, he received a PhD in Aeronautics and Astronautics from University of Tokyo, where he is a researcher and faculty member working in collaboration with the Japanese Aerospace Exploration Agency. Prior to that, he received a PhD in Engineering in Mathematics and Computer Science from Eindhoven University of Technology (in the Netherlands) in October 2021. In his current position with University of Tokyo, Chris’s team is designing open-source space stations.](#modal-0 "Christopher O'Hara '15")

### Christopher O'Hara '15

×

Chris says that STS has been helpful regarding topics related to policy writing (fair use and responsible use of technology), stakeholder management (balancing concerns, tradeoffs, priorities), and system architecting (who is the system for, what are potential emergent behaviors, and how the development of the technology ultimately affect the world).   He says, “it turns out, STS is one of the most desired forms of knowledge at the system level (all the topics of SDGs, stakeholder management, ethics, standard operation procedures, e.g., require STS skills more than technical skills).

Close

[![Anastasia Zamora-Ortiz](../img/anastasia-zamora-ortizheadshot-copy.jpg)

### Building Trades Council Grants Administrator

Anastasia Zamora-Ortiz ('18) is a Grants Administrator with the Los Angeles and Orange Counties Building Trades Council's non-profit, The Apprenticeship Readiness Fund. She says about her STS degree: "My degree plays a vital role in my career, particularly with this organization. With STS, I can spot patterns in politics, economics, and data trends to predict the elements that influence when our apprentices get assigned to projects. It has also allowed me to take historical context as a juxtaposition for what is going on locally and in federal administration."](#modal-1 "Anastasia Zamora-Ortiz ")

### Anastasia Zamora-Ortiz

×

Of The Apprenticeship Readiness Fund, Anastasia says, "We train the next generation of building and construction apprentices for over 44 local unions and 4 district councils. My daily role involves statistical reports, fiscal analysis and demographic reports. My additional responsibilities also include a level of public relations, including attending press conferences, mingling with elected officials and of course lots of activism as a labor advocate. STS's holistic lens has been very valuable and makes me uniquely suited to this multifaceted role."

[![Albert Fattal](../img/albert-fattal-resized.jpg)

### From STS to the Fed

Albert Fattal ('19) was a nontraditional student at CPP, having numerous years of working experience before enrolling in Science, Technology & Society. He was drawn to STS because he realized it offered a good basis for exploring his research interests in Artificial Intelligence and Blockchain, while being supported by the program’s implementation of CPP’s “learning by doing” approach to undergraduate research.](#modal-2 "Albert Fattal '19")

### Albert Fattal '19

×

Albert's work on his STS senior thesis enabled him to achieve his academic goals of presenting his research on AI and Blockchain at the 2019 International Data Engineering & Science Association (IDEAS) Global AI Conference and publishing a few papers in the CSU Scholar Works database. Following commencement, Albert completed an MBA at Cal Poly and has enjoyed an enriching public service career in Technology at the Federal Reserve in Washington, D.C. He is currently a Research Analyst III in the Program Directions section of the Division of Research and Statistics.

[![Kayla Turnage at Sen. Warnock's Atlanta office](../img/kayla-turnage.jpg)

### Intern with Senator Warnock

Kayla Turnage ('24) worked in the Atlanta Office of U.S. Senator Raphael Warnock in summer 2024. She reports: I learned and experienced so much while working with the Senator’s Team! I was one of only five interns selected nationally for the Session II Summer Program. My primary duties included communicating with constituents, recording their concerns, and compiling reports for senior staff to review. I also developed a policy presentation with my fellow interns about establishing broadband infrastructure in rural Georgia to expand access to telemedicine.](#modal-3 "Kayla Turnage '24")

### Kayla Turnage '24

×

During my internship, I had the opportunity to accompany senatorial Outreach Representatives on several field meetings with various organizations. On one of the outings, I met Dr. John Lipman of the Atlanta Fibroid Center. He is a leading expert in the Uterine Fibroid Embolization (UFE) procedure and has saved thousands of women from potentially receiving unnecessary hysterectomies. He is currently communicating with Congress, advocating for legislation that could improve the lives of others with this procedure. I was lucky enough to observe Dr. Lipman perform the procedure and to have a one-on-one in-person interview discussing the impact of his work. I am currently writing an article to increase awareness about uterine fibroids, Dr. Lipman’s contributions the medical field, and the less invasive UFE alternative.

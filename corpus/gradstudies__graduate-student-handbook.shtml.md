# Graduate Student Handbook 2025 - 2026

## ![The old stables](img/graduate-student-handbook/stables.jpg)Welcome Graduate Students!

This handbook serves as a reference for graduate students, facultyadvisors, and department staff. It covers university policies, student services, financial aid, Graduate Studies Office resources, and keydeadlines. You’ll also find other useful information to support yourgraduate journey, so we encourage you to explore the full handbook.

***Please note:** This handbook does not replace policies in the University Catalog or Title 5.*

## Graduate Studies Office

The Graduate Studies Office (GSO), provides leadership to graduate programs, overseeing the support and academic integrity of these programs. We are committed to advancing graduate students’ current and future
academic success and providing services in a professional and courteous manner.

## Registration & Financial Aid

For questions on course registration, holds, or financial aid, please contact the Bronco Advising Center. Please contact the Registrar's Office for questions on your academic record, enrollment, and degree completion.
The Office of Financial Aid also provides valuable information and resources for prospective, admitted, and current graduate students. View CPP, CSU systemwide, and outside scholarship opportunities, includin g dates and deadlines to apply.

[Registrar's Office](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/) [Office of Financial Aid](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/resources/contact.shtml) [Bronco Advising Center](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/bronco-advising-center/index.shtml) [Bronco Scholarship Portal](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/scholarships/cal-poly-pomona-scholarships.shtml) [CSU Scholarships](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/scholarships/csu-systemwide-scholarships.shtml) [Financial Aid Course Eligibility](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/graduate/course-eligibility.shtml) [Outside Scholarships](https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/scholarships/outside-scholarships.shtml)

Inquire in your department about the availability of graduate assistantships & research positions- graduate assistants work from 5 to 20 hours a week, assisting professors in laboratory classes or as graders in large classes. Advanced graduate students may serve as instructors in some lower division lectures.

[Graduate Assistantships & Teaching Associates](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty-affairs/unit-11-employees/standards-and-information.shtml)

The CSU Chancellor’s Doctoral Incentive Program and the California Pre-Doctoral Program both aim to support students pursuing doctoral degrees and future faculty roles within the CSU system. The Doctoral
Incentive Program provides loans to doctoral students, which are forgiven over five years of full-time CSU faculty service, while the Pre-Doctoral Program offers up to $5,000 for graduate school preparation and opportunities such as summer research internships.

[CSU Chancellor's Doctoral Incentive Program](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty-affairs/temporary-faculty/cdip.shtml)

[California Pre-Doctoral Program](https://www.calstate.edu/csu-system/faculty-staff/predoc)

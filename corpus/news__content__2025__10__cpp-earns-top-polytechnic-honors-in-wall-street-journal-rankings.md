# CPP Earns Top Polytechnic Honors in Wall Street Journal Rankings

* [University News](/news/all-news.shtml?category=22)
* [Student Success](/news/all-news.shtml?category=20)

October 01, 2025 | [samantha gonzaga](/news/all-news.shtml?author=Samantha Gonzaga)

## Share

![Grads from the Huntley College of Agriculture celebrate during the 2025 commencement ceremonies.](../../../../img/news/2025/10/25agriculture-grad_20.png)

Cal Poly Pomona is ranked No. 22 overall in the [2026 WSJ/College Pulse Best Colleges in the U.S.](https://www.wsj.com/rankings/college-rankings/best-colleges-2026) and [No. 13](https://www.wsj.com/rankings/college-rankings/social-mobility-2026?mod=best-colleges-2026) in Social Mobility — making it the highest-ranked polytechnic university in the nation in both categories. CPP was also noted [No. 34](https://www.wsj.com/rankings/college-rankings/best-value-2026?mod=social-mobility-2026) Best Value.

The WSJ/College Pulse rankings evaluated 584 eligible universities, prioritizing student outcomes, including salary gains, time to repay cost and graduation impacts, alongside learning environment and diversity.

The [return on investment](https://www.cpp.edu/news/in-the-news/2025/09/cpp-ranked-top-california-colleges-roi/index.shtml) for CPP students is compelling: Graduates are projected to earn $260,000 more over 20 years compared to peers without degrees.

The university was also recently named the [No. 2](https://www.cpp.edu/news/content/2025/09/cpp-ranked-2-regional-public-university-by-u.s-news-world-report-in-2026-rankings/index.shtml) Top Public School in the West in the 2026 *U.S. News & World Report* rankings, where it also recognized CPP as [No. 3](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/california-state-polytechnic-university-pomona-1144) Regional Universities West — out of 115 universities evaluated in 15 states — and  [No. 3](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/regional-universities-west/veterans?myCollege=regional-universities-west&_sort=myCollege&_sortDirection=asc)  Best College for Veterans.

CPP, where approximately 70 percent of students receive financial aid, is consistently recognized as one of the nation’s best universities for social mobility — in other words, helping non-wealthy students attain marketable degrees at an affordable cost. *Washington Monthly* ranked it No. 16 on its “[Best Bang for the Buck](https://washingtonmonthly.com/2025-college-guide/best-bang-for-the-buck-rankings-west/)” list, making CPP the highest-ranked polytechnic university among almost 200 universities evaluated. The university is also highly ranked by CollegeNET ([No. 17](https://www.cpp.edu/news/content/2022/12/cal-poly-pomona-ranked-no-1-polytechnic-university-in-social-mobility/index.shtml)) and Third Way ([Tier 1](https://www.cpp.edu/news/content/2023/09/elevating-students-cpp-is-top-polytechnic-for-value-economic-mobility-in-new-rankings/index.shtml#:~:text=Cal%20Poly%20Pomona%20is%20also%20the%20highest%2Dranking,Third%20Way%20%E2%80%9C2023%20Economic%20Mobility%20Index%E2%80%9D%20ranking.)).

## Signature Polytechnic Experiences (PolyX)

The hallmark of a Cal Poly Pomona education, Signature polytechnic experiences (PolyX) at Cal Poly Pomona promote students’ discovery of the opportunities and challenges within the field and the development of creative and innovative solutions to those conditions. Through collaborative learning and intense mentoring, faculty and staff can engage students beyond the classroom and prepare them for professional and civic success.

PolyX Submission Instructions

### How to Submit PolyX Application

×

1) Log on to InfoReady (You can log in using your Bronco ID, or you can create your own account)

[Log into Info Ready](https://cpp.infoready4.com/CompetitionSpace/)

![login for polyX](img/polyx-submission-instructions/login.png)

2) Please make sure your profile is set to Faculty and Staff at Cal Poly Pomona after login. You can change your profile settings by clicking your highlighted name on the top right and **setting your primary organization** to "**Faculty and Staff at Cal Poly Pomona"**.

![profile check](img/polyx-submission-instructions/profile-check.png)

3) Return to Home and click on **"Spring 2026 Signature Polytechnic Experience (PolyX) Hub"**.

![red bounding box around link ](img/polyx-submission-instructions/spring2022polyx.png)

4) Click on "**Submit"** below **Application Tools** to start the application process.\*

\*Please review the **Checklist Rubric** under **Competition Files** to ensure your application fulfills the PolyX criteria.

**![Application page instructions on infoready](img/polyx-submission-instructions/applicationsubmit-outlined.png)**

5) Fill out the application under **Details** (See full application on InfoReady).

![Application details on InfoReady](img/polyx-submission-instructions/detail.png)

6) Submit the **Reflection Exercise/Assignment** for your PolyX under **Upload Files**.

![Reflection assignment](img/polyx-submission-instructions/uploadfiles.png)

7) If you're ready to submit the application, click "**Submit Application"**. If you'd like to come back and finish the application at a later time, click **"Save as Draft"**.

![Save or submit your application](img/polyx-submission-instructions/submityourapplication.png)

## PolyX Mini-Grant Application Window Now Open!

The **PolyX Mini-Grant** is designed to support faculty and staff who lead PolyX-designated courses or programs by providing seed funding to sustain, enhance, and strengthen hands-on, real-world learning experiences for students. These mini-grants help ensure that PolyX experiences remain enriching, challenging, and impactful, allowing students to engage deeply with applied learning that connects theory to practice.

Mini-grant funds (**up to $500**) may be used to support course or program needs— such as materials, supplies, activities, or other resources— that advance the Polytechnic Experience and elevate student engagement. By investing in PolyX faculty and staff, the mini-grant program supports innovative teaching practices and reinforces Cal Poly Pomona’s commitment to experiential, poly-centric learning that prepares students for real-world challenges.

The PolyX Mini-Grant reflects our shared mission to enhance the learning experience for students, faculty, and staff alike, while fostering meaningful, practice-based education across disciplines!

Intense Mentorship

### Intense Mentorship

×

Students engaged in the PolyX should be mentored “intensely” by faculty, staff, industry mentors, and/or peers. “Intense” mentoring is intentional, regular, invasive, in-depth, and sustained. It involves referral to resources for appropriate student populations and mentoring the whole student. It is tailored to the needs of students, understands the environment in which students function and pairs resources and support to issues that students face.

Close

![](img/gallery/6.jpg)

Dissemination Beyond the Classroom

### Dissemination Beyond the Classroom

×

The results of this project should be published, presented, or publicly accessible in one form or another. In the event that the data of the project is of a sensitive or proprietary nature, the publication may be in a redacted form in Bronco Scholar, and presentations to small groups of selected professionals from the partnering corporate body are acceptable.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-7.png)

Creativity, Discovery, and Innovation

### Creativity, Discovery, and Innovation

×

The project should result in an original work of some sort. This may include, (but is not limited to):

* An original artistic work
* An original research publication that sheds new light on a research question
* The presentation of the results of an experiment that provides new insight in an area of scientific study
* The development of procedures/policies to solve a specific problem
* The creation of a specific product to fulfill an identified need.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-11.png)

Diverse and Multidisciplinary Perspectives

### Diverse and Multidisciplinary Perspectives

×

Preferably, the project invites and encourages participation from a variety of disciplines.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-1.png)

Community and Global Engagement

### Community and Global Engagement

×

The project should engage in some way with an identifiable community.

* On-campus communities such as:
  + A specific group within a discipline
  + A specific club or organization
  + An identified subset of Cal Poly Pomona’s students, faculty or staff
  + The campus as a whole
* Off-campus constituencies such as:
  + Specific non-profit organizations
  + Specific corporate partners
  + An identified group or organization
  + A municipal, state or national government

Potential benefits or drawbacks of the project to that community should be considered and explicitly stated at the outset.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-15.png)

Collaborative Learning

### Collaborative Learning

×

While faculty oversight and mentorship play a key role in the project, it should be primarily a student-led effort. Students should participate in generating questions or topics of study, developing answers, defining roles and responsibilities for team members, finding resources, setting schedules and deadlines and asking follow-up questions.

Close

![](img/gallery/polyx-4.png)

Critical Thinking and Problem Solving

### Critical Thinking and Problem Solving

×

The project should have reflection exercises integrated into it in some way. Through these exercises, the student participants pause to consider what they are learning, how they might need to alter their project’s scope, course or focus and analyze the effectiveness of the methods they are pursuing. The goal of the reflection should also include heightening students’ awareness of the PolyX and the skills they have developed as a result of participating in the PolyX. This should support students’ ability to articulate the PolyX experience.

Close

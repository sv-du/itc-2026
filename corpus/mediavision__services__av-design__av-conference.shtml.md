# Conferencing Options

## Getting Started

1. ### Room Sizes

   Around the campus you'll find anything from small office-sized conferencing spaces, to larger multi-purpose environments that need to be flexible. This is where you should start to get a good sense of the limitations inherent to a space.
2. ### Collaboration Structure

   How do you run your departments meetings? Does one person usually take the lead and present on progress, goals, and other outcomes; or are your meetings collaborative, where everyone participates in the meeting presenting their finding equally amongst the group? Is it for a larger committee organization, do your meetings operate in a hybrid structure, or do you use the space for a mix of business and events?
3. ### Technology and Assets

   Once you've established a foundational image for a space, the next part is filling it up with the tech to leverage your vision for the space. Would you like to control everything from a single location? Do you need physical or wireless connections for personal devices? Maybe you need the furniture to be modular so you can rearrange the space at a moments notice.

## Example Options

![small conference rooms](av-images/small-conference.jpg)

### for simple meetings

#### Small Conference Rooms

A classic conference room example with a single TV and wireless presentation capabilities. These spaces can also be outfitted with video conferencing capabilities like an all-in-one camera that has built-in speakers and a microphone for Zoom.

![medium conference room](av-images/medium-conference-room-psy.jpg)

### for larger teams

#### Medium Conference Rooms

These are spaces that typically host a larger sum of participants and may require a larger display for presenting. Similar to small conference room, but with a projector instead of a TV. Medium conference rooms can also be outfitted with video conferencing capabilities like an all-in-one camera that has built-in speakers and a microphone for Zoom.

![large conference room](av-images/large-conference-room.jpg)

### for lectures and events

#### Large Conference Rooms

With the additional space, these rooms are typically operate as multi-purpose areas for collaborative meetings, events, cross-department engagements, and many more uses. Often including a single projector or even multiple displays to handle visibility, and integrated audio systems that blend into the room. Large conference rooms can also be configured for remote participation if needed.

# Test Proctoring

![](https://www.cpp.edu/admissions/img/icons/checklist_icon_blue.png)

## Testing Information

Test Proctoring is offered to Cal Poly Pomona students who need to schedule a make-up exam **due to serious and compelling reasons** (i.e. illness). Faculty must submit a **Faculty** **Request Form** for approved make-up exams. For scheduling purposes, Faculty Request Forms must be received at least **48\* hours (2 business days)** in advance of completion date and time. Please note that students registered with the Disability Resource Center (DRC) who need additional accommodations will need to schedule their make up exam with the DRC. The DRC can be contacted at **(909) 869-3333** or **drctesting@cpp.edu**.

Testing Services now offers ALEKS PPL Assessment test procotoring for incoming freshman.

### Test Proctoring Hours

**Monday-Friday 9:00 a.m.-12:00 p.m.**

**Monday-Friday 2:00 p.m.-4:00 p.m.**

**Once the faculty submits their request (at least 2 business days in advance of the completion date):**

* All make-up tests are available Monday-Friday, **by appointment only,** and are scheduled by Testing Services in CPP Connect at least 12-hours in advance of the student's requested appointment time, and are on a first-come, first-served basis.
* Seating per day is limited.
* Check in for test proctoring at the  [**Learning Resource Center (LRC)**](https://www.cpp.edu/lrc/ "learning resource center") in the University Library, 15-2921.
* Students registered with the [**Disability Resource Center (DRC)**](https://www.cpp.edu/drc/index.shtml "link to the drc") and needing additional accommodations will need to schedule a make-up exam with the DRC.

### Faculty Only

* Submit a make-up exam request for your student (**2 business days\* in advance** of completion date and time). **Requests submitted by students directly** **will be denied**. **\***Requests made on Friday after 12:00 pm must have a completion date no earlier than the following Tuesday.
* Only CPP faculty can submit a request form. **CPP account sign-in is required** otherwise you will receive an error message stating that you do not have permission to access the form.
* **Please ensure allowable materials are accurate** before submitting the request form.
* Do not send any other material not pertinent to the exam, especially answer keys, when submitting exams.
* **Testing Services is not responsible for reviewing uploaded testing materials**.
* Proctoring requests for exams that require the use of multiple browsers during the exam or online resources/platforms may not be accommodated.
* Excel files and other supporting materials needed to complete exams must be provided by instructors and accessed by the students through their Canvas course during the exam.
* Uploaded test materials **must be submitted as a PDF**.
* Please refer to the Faculty FAQ for further information.

[Faculty Request Form](https://forms.office.com/Pages/ResponsePage.aspx?id=HqZLFuw5XU-J_6ofAKUhtGVS4WQgth9HsmMxhMgBGCpUMEdRVlNQSzQ4TFY1TjhSQzNERjlUTUpMVy4u "Faculty Request Form. ")

### Proctoring Protocol

* Students must bring a valid photo ID.
* Once the proctored exam has begun, students may not leaving the testing room.
* Electronics not specified as testing material by the instructor or department are not allowed, including but not limited to: phones, smart watches, and calculators.
* Students must adhere to the University **[Student Conduct Code](https://www.cpp.edu/studentconduct/student-conduct-code.shtml "link to student conduct and integrity")** at all times while in the testing room.

## ALEKS Calculus Assessment Proctoring

* Students will need their Bronconame and Bronco password to access ALEKS.
* Students must follow the Proctoring Protocol outlined above.
* Students are encouraged to take and complete an ALEKS Practice Assessment before the proctored assessment. This will give you a baseline and let you know which topics you need to review.
* After taking the practice assessment, the ALEKS PPL system will give you the opportunity to use learning modules to review and improve. By spending time with ALEKS, you will be better prepared and more successful in whatever math class you take in the fall.
* Students who have started an ALEKS Practice Assessment, **MUST COMPLETE** it in its entirety before taking the proctored assessment. The system will prevent a student from taking the proctored assessment until the practice assessment is complete. Clicking "I don't know" on every problem not answered will be recommended before entering the testing room.
* Please visit the [**Mathematics and Statistics Department**](https://www.cpp.edu/sci/mathematics-statistics/calculus-placement.shtml "link to Math and Stats dept ALEKS website") for further information on the ALEKS PPL Assessment.

## Requirements

* All test materials and instructions from faculty must be submitted **for each student** via the Faculty Request Form. **Once these materials are received**, Testing Services will reach out to the student to schedule an appointment.
* Students must present valid photo identification (Bronco ID card or other valid government ID) upon check-in to their scheduled make-up test. Students without valid ID will be denied seating for the test.
* Students should arrive to the LRC **5 minutes prior** to their scheduled appointment. Students arriving late will forfeit their appointment.
* Students who fail to arrive at their scheduled time (late or no-show) will need their faculty to submit a new request form.
* Proctors do not provide scantrons, bluebooks, calculators, or pencils and cannot help troubleshoot technical issues with electronic devices.
* For online exams use of student owned laptops are encouraged.
* For questions email **testingservices@cpp.edu**.

## Accommodations

Students requesting accommodations for disabilities must contact the **[Disability Resource Center (DRC)](https://www.cpp.edu/~drc/)**. The DRC can be contacted at (909) 869-3333 or **drc@cpp.edu**.

## Academic Integrity

Make-up testing will end immediately for students engaging in academic dishonesty and the instructor will be notified. Please refer to the [**Office of Student Conduct and Integrity**](https://www.cpp.edu/studentconduct/student-conduct-code.shtml "office of student conduct and integrity") for further information.

## Student Frequently Asked Questions

### I'm not a Cal Poly Pomona student. Can I take my exam through this service?

At this time, we only offer make-up exams for currently-enrolled Cal Poly Pomona students.

### What if I show up late to my appointment?

Since the proctor cannot leave the room once the exam begins, we cannot accommodate late arrivals. If you are unable to attend the appointment at the scheduled time, notify your instructor to submit a new Faculty Request Form.

### What if I need to cancel or reschedule my appointment?

Email **testingservices@cpp.edu**. If the request to reschedule is within the timeframe indicated by your instructor on the Faculty Request Form, and there is availability, Testing Services will accommodate your request to reschedule. You will receive an email confirmation from CPP Connect with the new day/time. If you do not show to your scheduled appointment or your rescheduling request cannot be accommodated, you will need your faculty to submit a new Faculty Request Form.

### How do I request my make-up exam?

The process begins with your instructor. Your instructor must submit a Faculty Request Form. After the form is received and processed, the student will receive an email with appointment availability. Please keep in mind that appointments are scheduled on a first-come, first-served basis. Reply promptly to ensure you meet your instructor's deadline.

### Can I take the CCE, GWT, or PCPT exams through this service?

Testing Services currently proctors make-up exams for courses only. Please inquire with the respective academic departments directly regarding these other exams.

* CCE - Contact the Political Science Department.
* GWT - This test has been suspended.
* PCPT - Contact the Computer Information Systems Department.

## Faculty Frequently Asked Questions

### How will I get the completed exam?

Faculty indicate on the Faculty Request Form if they want the completed exam materials scanned to PDF and emailed (**within 1 business day**), or if they will collect the physical exam materials from the Learning Resource Center (Library, 15-2921). Faculty must show ID to pick up exams. Exams will be shredded after the appropriate grade appeal date for each term.

### How soon can I expect my student to take their make-up exam?

For scheduling purposes, Faculty Request Forms must be received **at least 48 hours in advance** of completion date and time. After you submit your request for an exam, we will do our best to accommodate your students as quickly as possible.

Seating is limited and appointments are scheduled on a first-come, first-served basis. Please make your students aware of your testing expectations and to check their email frequently for the appointment invite from **testingservices@cpp.edu**and any updates. We may not be able to accommodate same-day requests due to capacity and proctor availability.

### My exam is administered online. What equipment and software options are provided to students?

Testing Services cannot provide technical assistance, equipment (laptops at this time), or help with installing any specialized software for purposes of taking exams. Student personal laptops are encouraged for use in these situations.

Faculty are also encouraged to clearly communicate all technical requirements on the Faculty Request Form and with students prior to them sitting for proctored exams. This includes what software needs to be installed and whether Apple or Windows PCs are preferred.

### How do I provide the exam materials to the proctors?

The materials (PDF) must be attached to the Faculty Request Form submitted. You may also contact testingservices@cpp.edu for additional assistance.

### Can I submit one Facullty Request Form for multiple students?

No. Each student must have a Faculty Request Form with exam material submitted for Testing Services to proctor their exam.

### What if there are changes to my exam after I submitted the Faculty Request Form?

You may email testingservices@cpp.edu with the updated exam or instructions.

### Can the proctor grade the exam?

No, the proctors will not grade the exams. Please DO NOT send any answer keys. All test materials submitted in the Faculty Request Form will be printed and provided to the student during their appointment.

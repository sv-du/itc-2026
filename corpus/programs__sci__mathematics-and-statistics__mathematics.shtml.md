## About the Program

Exponentially grow in your knowledge of calculus, algebra, statistics, mathematical proofs, and more. Make a difference in numerous lives by applying your mathematical understanding in industry or training the next generation of mathematicians and statisticians.
With emphases in applied mathematics / statistics and secondary teacher preparation / pure mathematics, you can shape your education to your dream career.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Actuary

Combine mathematical, statistical and financial theories to analyze financial risks and uncertainties especially in the insurance industry. Help organizations make informed decisions regarding risk management and financial planning.

### Statistician

Utilize statistical methods and tools to collect, analyze, interpret and present quantitative data. Design surveys, use statistical models and software, interpret results, and prepare reports for healthcare, business, government and academia.

### Accountant

Manage financial records and ensure compliance with regulations for businesses, governments and individuals. Prepare financial statements and tax returns, create budgets, forecast financial outcomes for strategic planning, conduct audits, reconcile accounts and more.

### Secondary Education Teacher

Introduce middle and high school students to the fundamentals of pre-algebra, geometry, algebra, precalculus, statistics and calculus. Create lesson plans, present lectures, design hands-on activities, grade assignments and instill a love of mathematics.

### Professor

Teach at the college or university level and help students from all backgrounds develop their mathematical skills. Develop lesson plans, give assessments and mentor students while also conducting research. Propose new theories, seek to prove complex theorems or apply mathematical and statistical models to real-life scenarios.

### Data Analyst

Gather and interpret data, then prepare themit in easy-to-understand formats to help businesses and organizations make better informed decisions.

## Courses

The Department of Mathematics and Statistics offers a flexible major program that can be adapted to serve various needs and interests.
We encourage you to choose electives that will prepare you for careers in your desired industry or graduate study in mathematics or a quantitative discipline in the sciences, engineering, economics or business.
We also recommend you use several free electives to explore a discipline other than mathematics.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

# Career & Professional Readiness Initiative

## Career & Professional Readiness Initiative Explained

### *Prepare Our Students for the Future of Work, Human & Civic Engagement*

Students have sequenced academic and co-curricular experiences supported by coordinated planning among the colleges, academic programs, the Career Center and Alumni Relations.

1. Implement a plan for student success that spans pre-admission to one year post-graduation.
2. Develop a career readiness model that provides students with early and on-going opportunities to engage with alumni, the company, and industry leaders.
3. Advance student learning and civic engagement through initiatives and address community concerns and reginal needs.

[View Model](documents/professionalandcareerreadinessmodel-v1.pdf "Career Readiness Model")

* Signature Polytechnic Experience (PolyX)
  + Project Based Learning
  + Course-based Undergraduate Research Experiences (CUREs)
  + Community Engaged Learning
* **Have Colleges determine ideal foundational knowledge and learning objectives for 2nd year students.**
* **Have departments examine curriculum, target one or two key “2nd year” courses, and create shared experiences that introduce and develop appropriate learning experiences/outcomes that advance project.**
* **Inventory how and how well students achieve fundamental learning outcomes that fulfill master plan objectives:  Written and oral communication; information literacy, critical thinking, and quantitative reasoning.**
* **Develop and embed assignments that introduce and develop “Problem Solving” –an institutional learning outcome that builds off of fundamental outcomes in General Education, disciplines, and academic programs.**
* **Global Awareness**

* **Expand campus employment opportunities for students so that they can work as assistants with faculty, the Library, ASI and on-campus housing.**
* **Help faculty create co-curricular opportunities for students during Winter and Summer breaks.**
* **Help faculty create and offer academic internships that engage students in major-related projects on or near campus during Summer break.**

## About the Program

Ready to turn your love for music into a career? Our Music Industry Studies option will give you a behind-the-scenes look at the music business, from the tech side of production to the marketing and management that drives the industry. Learn how to create and perform music while exploring the economics, culture and legal aspects of the music world. Get real-world experience through internships and collaborations with professionals in the Los Angeles music scene.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Dive into hands-on projects, internships and collaborative classes where you’ll work with your peers to create and perform music, record tracks and explore the business side of the industry.
You’ll have access to unique real-world opportunities, ensuring that you’re not just ready to step into the music world, but to lead it. Whether you want to make music, manage artists or run a label, this program will help you shape your creative and professional future in the music industry.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 4

### Music Producer

Oversee the creation of songs, from recording to mixing and mastering, guiding artists to bring their vision to life by utilizing your Music Industry Studies degree to become a producer.

Card 2 of 4

### Talent Manager

Help artists build their careers, negotiating contracts, booking performances, and guiding them toward success.

Card 3 of 4

### Music Marketing Specialist

Create buzz for new releases, events, and brands by building campaigns and managing social media to get music in front of the right audience.

Card 4 of 4

### Sound Engineer

Manage the technical aspects of recording, mixing, and producing music to ensure everything sounds perfect.

## Courses

The Music Industry Studies option provides a focused education in the business side of music. This program covers essential topics such as music technology, production, marketing, and management. You will gain practical experience through internships and build connections with industry professionals in the vibrant Los Angeles music scene. Key courses include "The Digital Audio Workstation" and "History of Technology in Music," preparing you for various roles within the music industry. This program is suited for those looking to blend their passion for music with business acumen, aiming for careers in music management, production, or marketing.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

## Music Department

### Music and Entertainment Industry Club

The Music and Entertainment Industry Club brings together like-minded students who are interested in the music and entertainment industries. Members gain experience and opportunities to network with Industry professionals at local, regional, and national events. Cal Poly Pomona’s chapter has been extremely active both on campus and in the community.

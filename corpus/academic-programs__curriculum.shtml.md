# Office of Academic Programs

## Building the University Catalog

### Curriculum Development

The University Catalog is the contract CPP makes with students, and ensures our students' education is consistent, equitable, and rigorous. This is the cornerstone of Curriculum Development.

### Expanded Course Outline (ECO)

×

An archive of Expanded Course Outlines is now available to all CPP faculty and staff. The archive allows you to look up an ECO in any given catalog since 2018, which means you can track changes in the course over time.

[Expanded Course Outline Archive](https://academic.cpp.edu/curriculum-development/ECOsV3/defaultv3.aspx)

Before logging into Curriculog, it is sometimes easier to draft an ECO in a Word document. The attached template can be used in isolation, but it is recommended to also look at the Curriculum Development Guide for additional support, guidance, and examples.

[Template - Blank ECO (DOCX)](protected/curriculum/template-blank-eco.docx)

## Curriculum Exception Memos

When curricular changes—reducing a course's units, substituting a course, removing requirements—impact student advising, departments should submit a Curriculum Exception Memo. These memos function like department-level petitions. For more, see ch. 7 of the Curriculum Development Guide.

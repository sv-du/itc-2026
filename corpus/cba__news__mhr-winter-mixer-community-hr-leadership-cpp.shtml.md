# MHR Winter Mixer Builds Community, Showcases Industry Expertise

February 11, 2026

|  |
| --- |
| ![MHR CBA Professors](../img/news/mhr-winter-1.jpg) |
| [L-R] Christina Henderson-Lawrence, Steve Guo, Alex Berberian, Lorena Ornelas-Estrada, Megan Shadrick, Erica Ben, Zeynep Aytug |

The Department of Management and Human Resources (MHR) hosted its first MHR Winter Mixer at the Kellogg West Conference Center and Hotel, drawing together a dynamic group of Cal Poly Pomona students, alumni, faculty, and prospective students for an afternoon of learning, networking, and celebration.

This year’s event marked another successful chapter in the department’s ongoing efforts to strengthen community ties within the MHR program, housed in the College of Business Administration.

Organized by Zeynep Aytug, co-director of the Master of Science in Human Resources and Leadership (MSHRL) program, and Steve Guo, co-director of MSHRL and chair of the department, the Winter Mixer featured a program of speakers, professional development opportunities, and updates on industry trends. The event was co-hosted by the Cal Poly Pomona chapter of the Professionals in Human Resources Association (PIHRA) and proudly sponsored by the Singelyn Graduate School of Business.

"Our goal is to build a strong and supportive MHR community where students, alumni, and faculty can meet, network, share information and experiences, and ultimately grow together. A career in HR and leadership is a lifelong journey,” explained Guo. “This MHR community can serve as a hub for our alumni to support and inspire one another, give back to students who are at the beginning of their careers, and continue learning together."

Singelyn Family Dean Sandeep Krishnamurthy opened the event with welcoming remarks, emphasizing the importance of lifelong learning and the role that community plays in the success of business students and graduates. He was joined by Aytug, who spoke on the importance of academic-industry collaboration, and Lorena Ornelas-Estrada, president of the CBA Alumni Chapter, who highlighted alumni engagement and career pathways for students.

One of the afternoon’s highlights was a keynote address by James Michalski, Esq., a seasoned employment law attorney, who delivered an engaging and informative presentation titled, “California Employment Law 2026: What’s New This Year.” Michalski’s discussion focused on the latest developments in California employment regulations, providing attendees with valuable insights relevant to HR professionals, business leaders, and entrepreneurs alike.

|  |
| --- |
| ![Keynote speaker James Michalski](../img/news/mhr-winter-2.jpg) |
| Keynote Speaker James Michalski, esq addresses event attendees during his keynote speech |

“One rule will get you through: treat people how you wanna be treated. I was interested in HR because the role gives you the ability to help and protect people. As HR leaders, you have a very important role – you’re the eyes, ears, and conscience of the company. You’re as important as the CEO, in my opinion. If you don’t have a good HR leader, your company will crumble under its own weight. No amount of sales can help you, if you have holes in your HR foundation,” concluded Michalski.

In addition to the keynote, the event provided ample opportunities for attendees to reconnect and collaborate. Faculty members were on hand to engage with students and alumni, answer questions about the MHR curriculum, and discuss current research and trends in human resources and leadership.

“It’s about always learning,” said Fernando Galicia (ACC ’16, MHR ’16). “I may not be in class now, but this mixer allows me to see what the industry trends are, what the new California HR laws say, and offers a place to connect with other professionals who are alumni, as well as to meet industry luminaries and reconnect with faculty.”

Several members of the CBA Alumni Chapter board also attended, helping to bridge the gap between students and seasoned professionals. The chapter continues to play a vital role in mentoring students and offering career guidance.

"We have grown from five board members to 18 board members,” CBA Alumni Board President Ornelas-Estrada explained. “The CBA Alumni Chapter is the largest alumni chapter at Cal Poly Pomona, with over 300 active members, so we want to strategically use our resources and events like this one to facilitate opportunities for our students and alumni to connect.”

Attendees enjoyed complimentary food and beverages, along with designated spaces for casual conversation and career-focused discussions. With free parking and no cost to attend, the event remained accessible and welcoming for all members of the CPP MHR community looking to understand the opportunities and paths for future MHR professionals. That future for MHR students at CPP is bright, according to Aytug. “We are proud of our brand-new HR master’s program that combines state-of-the-art HR curriculum, in-person meetings every other week, and ample professional networking opportunities at half the cost of comparable HR master programs in California.”

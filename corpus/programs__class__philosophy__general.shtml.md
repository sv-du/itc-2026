## About the Program

Ready to sharpen your mind and tackle the toughest questions? The General Philosophy option will challenge you to think deeply and critically, teaching you to break down complex ideas and connect concepts across various fields. You’ll explore the big picture, combining logic, ethics, and reasoning to form well-rounded perspectives that can be applied to everything from science and technology to media and beyond.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Imagine yourself presenting research at a student conference or working with a professor on a study that bridges philosophy and tech—your learning here isn’t just theoretical; it’s hands-on and exciting, combining philosophy with other fields, including artificial intelligence, psychology or environmental science.
Whether you’re solving puzzles in cognitive studies or creating compelling narratives in media, the skills you gain in this program will make you a powerhouse of critical thought, capable of navigating any challenge with clarity and insight.

## Courses

The General Philosophy option provides you with a comprehensive foundation in philosophical inquiry, emphasizing critical thinking and reasoning skills. This option encourages you to engage with traditional philosophical questions about truth, knowledge, justice, and reality, while also exploring how these concepts apply to contemporary issues. Through rigorous coursework, you will develop analytical and synthetic skills that are highly valued in various professions. The program’s focus on broad intellectual skills prepares you for diverse careers where advanced cognitive abilities and effective communication are essential. By fostering a deep understanding of abstract concepts and their practical implications, the General option equips you with versatile problem-solving skills applicable across multiple fields.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

## Department of Philosophy

### Ethics Bowl

Ethics Bowl is a team-based oral presentation competition where students respond to questions about contemporary ethical controversies and defend their answers before their opponents and expert judges. Ethics Bowl is a valuable activity for any student interested in ethics, as well as for students interested in careers in policy, government, or law. Cal Poly Pomona has competed in the Intercollegiate Ethics Bowl for a decade with great success, including California regional championships and appearances in the national Ethics Bowl semifinals and quarterfinals.

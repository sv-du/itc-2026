# Cal Poly Pomona to Celebrate Transfer Student Week with Flurry of Activities

* [Student Success](/news/all-news.shtml?category=20)
* [Campus Life](/news/all-news.shtml?category=23)

October 16, 2025 | [christie counts](/news/all-news.shtml?author=Christie Counts)

## Share

![Female transfer student holds Poly Transfer Tshirt](../../../../img/news/2025/10/22poly-transfer-shirts2.png)

Cal Poly Pomona will recognize [National Transfer Student Week](https://www.cpp.edu/polytransfer/transfer-support/national-transfer-student-week.shtml), an annual celebration of our vibrant transfer student community.

Transfer students’ achievements and contributions will be highlighted **Oct. 20-24**. Students can explore valuable resources tailored for transfers, including opportunities to connect with campus organizations, learn about financial literacy and career planning topics, and have some fun during midterms season.  PolyTransfer has collaborated with multiple programs, including [ASI](https://asi.cpp.edu/), the [Career Center](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/), [I Am First](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/oss/i-am-first/index.shtml), [University Housing](https://www.cpp.edu/housing/) and the [Maker’s Studio](https://www.cpp.edu/makerstudio/index.shtml) for this year’s events.

Claire Luevanos, PolyTransfer’s coordinator, shared that the team is excited to celebrate transfer students and encourages them to take advantage of the many events, tools and resources available throughout the week.

“National Transfer Student Week is a reminder that our transfer students play a vital role in the Bronco community,” Luevanos said. “We’re excited to celebrate their stories and connect them with the people and programs that will continue to support their growth.”

PolyTransfer peer mentor and political science senior, Atzary Jimenez-Villalobos, shared that her journey as a transfer student at CPP has been both rewarding and eye-opening. She credits the support and connections she has made in PolyTransfer, as well as being a peer mentor has helped her feel connected to campus and confident navigating its opportunities.

As the upcoming transfer celebration approaches, Jimenez-Villalobos encourages fellow transfer students to get involved in on-campus programs and events to build connections and make the most of their time at CPP.

![Male Chaffey student Admit holds a sign standing next to Billy Bronco.](../../../../img/news/2025/10/25chaffey-college-admits-student6.png)“National Transfer Student week is a great way to connect with community and get familiar with CPP’s resources,” said Jimenez-Villalobos. “One of the other ways is working on campus; the student-to-student engagement with others from different background really helps us build a thriving community.”

The official schedule of events for National Transfer Student Week can be found on [CPP’s PolyTransfer website](https://www.cpp.edu/polytransfer/transfer-support/national-transfer-student-week.shtml). All faculty, staff and students are welcome to attend the following events:

**Oct. 20: The Transfer Table: First-Generation Student Stories**
11:30 a.m. to 1 p.m., Student Services Building 121 (West), MPR 1967/1969
Hosted by I Am First and PolyTransfer, this luncheon celebrates the achievements and journeys of first-generation transfer students. Share stories, enjoy a meal, and connect with peers.

**Oct. 20: Transfer Paint Social**
4 to 6 p.m., Bronco Student Center, Ursa Minor
Join a DIY painting event with free snacks and fellow students. Relax, recharge, and celebrate National Transfer Student Week together.

**Oct. 21: National Transfer Student Week Kickoff**
11 a.m. to 2 p.m., University Park
Celebrate transfer students with a Resource Fair featuring campus programs, services, music, free food, giveaways, and fun activities.

**Oct. 21: FLS Workshop – Exploring Scholarships**
3 to 4 p.m., Student Services Building 121 (West), MPR 1967/1969
Learn to navigate the Bronco Scholarship Portal, craft strong scholarship essays, and connect with campus resources. Snacks provided.

**Oct. 21: Transfer Trivia Night**
5:30 to 7 p.m., Building 73, MPR 124
Test your knowledge, meet other transfer students, and enjoy trivia, prizes, and community.

**Oct. 22: Maker Studio Visit**
Noon to 2 p.m., University Library, Second Floor
Explore hands-on technology and creative tools like 3D printers, engravers, button makers, and more. No experience necessary.

**Oct. 22: Speed Friending**
2 to 4 p.m., Building 52, Room 100
Meet new people through fun, fast-paced conversations and build connections with fellow students.

**Oct. 23: FLS Workshop – Writing Winning Scholarship Essays**
Noon to 1 p.m., Student Services Building 121 (West), MPR 1967/1969
Learn strategies to improve your scholarship essays and access campus support.

**Oct. 23: Transfer Night at the Volleyball Game**
4 to 5 p.m., Kellogg Arena
Cheer on the Broncos, meet other transfer students, and enjoy a taste of campus life.

**Oct. 24: Transfer Night at the Pumpkin Patch**
4 to 6:30 p.m., Cal Poly Pomona Farm Store
Enjoy corn mazes, hayrides, food trucks, a petting farm and fun activities at this festive event for current and prospective transfer students.

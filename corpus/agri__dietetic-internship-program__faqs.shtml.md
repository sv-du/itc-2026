# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## FAQs and Their Answers

Answers to frequently asked questions about the Cal Poly Pomona dietetic internship program.

### Are you participating in the Dietetics Inclusive Centralized Application Service (DICAS)?

Yes, we participate in the Dietetics Inclusive Centralized Application Service (DICAS) as well as D&D Matching.

Please note that you do NOT need to send anything other than the Release Form, Recency of Education (if applicable), and check to Cal Poly Pomona.  We will use the information from DICAS for the rest of the packet.

### Do I need transcripts from all the schools I attended?

Yes. Even though many schools do include information from previous schools on their transcripts, you need to include all transcripts.

### Do I have to change all courses to semester units?

No. Not all courses need to be listed or converted to semester units. But be sure if you have been to both quarter and semester schools, that you choose only one format.

### How will I know if I am selected for an interview?

Applicants selected will be called in early- to mid-February and will be given an interview time. Because of the large number of applicants we interview for our 15 positions, we conduct interviews over two days only in late February. We realize that it may be during school or work hours, but, hopefully, your instructors or employer will understand the importance of the interview and give you time off. Interviews generally take only 15-20 minutes and can be conducted over Zoom.

### Do I have to find my own preceptors?

No, interns are assigned to all rotations by the DI director and receive a schedule at the beginning of the internship. Only interns who choose a free week rotation need to find a preceptor for that rotation.

### If I am selected, how would I be assigned to a hospital? Do I get to choose my hospital?

Interns do not choose their hospital placement. Intern placement is determined by the selection committee. We make every effort to place you close to your home. With the geographic range of the hospitals, this is often difficult. Interns are sometimes asked to commute to their hospitals or to their outside rotations (public health, Head Start, community college, etc.) You may be expected to drive 60 miles one way for this period. While this may be difficult short-term, we hope you appreciate the training needed to become a registered dietitian/registered dietitian nutritionist.

### Do I need to take the GRE?

No.

### Do I need to apply to Cal Poly Pomona?

Not as part of the initial application process. However, if you are matched to our internship, you will have to apply to the university. All interns accepted into the program must apply to the post-baccalaureate credential program through our regular university admissions process. This ensures that you are enrolled in the credential program and allows you to apply for financial aid if needed. You will be given information about this if you are matched to our internship.

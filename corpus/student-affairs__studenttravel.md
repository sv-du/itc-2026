# Student Travel

![2025 Model UN team](img/25model-un.jpg)

## Welcome!

Are you ready to embark on an unforgettable journey? Whether you're traveling for academic purposes or seeking adventure beyond the classroom, this website will help you navigate the process.

Travel will be considered to have a University business purpose if the travel:

* Directly supports a faculty member's project or research program, or
* Is an integral part of the student's degree work, e.g. attend a conference, visit a field site or laboratory facility, etc., or
* Is required for attendance at student‐oriented meetings and the like, or
* Enables the student to attend a conference to present research findings for the University or to act in some other capacity on behalf of the University.

It is recommended that you review the training for the new student travel process.

[View the Training (sumtotal.host)](https://CSU.sumtotal.host/core/pillarRedirect?relyingParty=LM&url=app%2Fmanagement%2FLMS_ActDetails.aspx%3FActivityId%3D145997%26UserMode%3D0)

## Important note

This site is for STUDENT travel only. For guidance on travel processes for faculty and staff in the Division of Academic Affairs, see the [Faculty and Staff Resources website](https://www.cpp.edu/academic-affairs/resources/index.shtml).

> **Persons responsible for submitting travel forms should submit the Step 3 form at least one (1) month before the first day of travel.**

The 3A form is for academic student travel and [approved instructionally related activities (IRAs)](https://www.cpp.edu/cppira/approved-programs.shtml).

The 3B is for campus departments, and [registered clubs and organizations](https://www.cpp.edu/blc/students/join.shtml).

## Student Travel Process

[Step 1

### Gather information

A month and a half before your first day of travel: collect the trip details and forms required for your request.](#modal-0 "Step 1 - Gather Information")

### Step 1 - Gather Information

×

The person who is coordinating the travel should collect the following trip details and documents:

**International Travel**If traveling internationally, each person will be required to submit an acknowledgement that they have read the [CSU guidance for international travel (PDF)](https://www.cpp.edu/academic-affairs/resources/csu-international-travel-guidance-may-2025.pdf "CSU Guidance to Faculty, Students, and Staff Considering International Travel").

**Gather Overall Trip Details**
Dates and times of departure and return
Itinerary (activities, locations, and schedule)
Street address for event venue and hotel
-If your group has traveled to this location at any time in the past

**Gather Traveler Details**
Email addresses
Phone numbers
Student ID numbers

**Emergency contact\* information for each traveler**
Emergency contact phone numbers
Relationship to traveler
\*For Academic Travel, complete the [Academic Field Trip Participant List (PDF)](https://www.cpp.edu/academic-affairs/resources/academic-field-trip-participant-list-oct-2025.pdf)

**Liability Waiver Forms**
Completed [Release of Liability form (PDF)](https://www.cpp.edu/rms/forms/release-of-liability-2022.pdf) for each traveler

For Participants

* Complete all fields: Activities detail, Date/time, Location/Facilities (be specific); Signature, Printed Name, and Date.
  + For participants who are minors, complete the Parent/Guardian information and signature fields also.
* Sign the form with **EITHER** a “wet” or handwritten, cursive signature, **OR** a digitally certified signature with a time/date stamp, e.g., Adobe or Docusign.

  + *Typed or computer-generated signatures cannot be accepted.*
  + *![Physical ink signature or Adobe Sign digital signature acceptable. Typed name with computer fonts, not acceptable.](img/signatures.png)*
* Signature Guidelines: Must be handwritten signature (not typed, computer-generated) – **OR** - Digital signature with time/date stamp (e.g. Adobe, Docusign).
* Submit the entire form (both pages) in pdf format for each participant.

  + *A photo of the signed form does not meet legal standards*

For Requestors

* Review all forms to confirm compliance, and request corrected forms as needed before submitting the Travel Request.
* Scan or merge forms into a single pdf file, in alphabetical order by last name of participant.
  + *Include the entire form (2 pages) for each participant; the signature page by itself does**not meet legal standards.*
* Recheck form(s)/pdf file before submitting.
* Upload the file to the Travel Request.
* Retain the signed ROL forms in accord with [CPP policies](https://www.cpp.edu/rms/releases-of-liability.shtml.)

**Costs for Travel**Transportation details (flights, buses, etc.)
Accommodation details (hotel, hostel, etc.)
Meal arrangements (dietary restrictions, meal plans)
Payment methods and deadlines

**Travel Insurance (if applicable)**Charter or aircraft or bus for purpose of CSU Student air or bus travel shall include indemnity and co-insurance provisions.
For international travel, review Risk Management's [Foreign Travel Insurance Program](https://www.cpp.edu/rms/travel/foreign-travel-insurance.shtml)

[Step 2

### Determine travel type

Decide whether the purpose of your trip is Academic or Non-Academic.](#modal-1 "How to determine your travel type")

### How to determine your travel type

×

Only one person should submit the request on behalf of all who are traveling.

**Academic Travel (use the link on Step 3A)**

* Field Trip (see below for definition and example)
* Conference presentation or attendance
* Research activity
* [Instructionally Related Activities](https://www.cpp.edu/cppira/approved-programs.shtml) (eligible and approved by ASI)

**Non-Academic Travel (use the link on step 3B)**

* [Recognized Clubs and Organizations](https://www.cpp.edu/blc/students/join.shtml)
  + Must be marked Registered and Active by the Bronco Leadership Center.
  + The Scheduler for the Organization should submit the request.

**Field Trip Defined**

An Academic Field Trip Request is required when a field trip is:

* an out-of-classroom, off-campus activity;
* associated with an academic course or university sponsored program;
* at a date, time, and location selected by the instructor;
* not already included on the syllabus at the start of the semester.

EXAMPLE OF A FIELD TRIP

* Enrolled students meet faculty member at a museum at a specific date/time, and this meeting is considered a required component of the course.

EXAMPLE OF WHAT IS **NOT** A FIELD TRIP

* Faculty member asks enrolled students to visit a museum sometime during the academic term. The visit is a course requirement, but the students individually select the date/time for their museum visit, and the instructor is not part of the trip.

### While Traveling

×

When traveling please keep the following in mind:

#### **Save all receipts if you are getting reimbursed**

* **Save every receipt** — meals, transportation, lodging, registration fees, etc.
* Ask for **itemized receipts** that list each item purchased.
* **Do not submit** only credit card slips that only show totals and tips — they are not accepted. You must include itemized receipts which list all items that were purchased.
* Receipts utilizing a credit card must show at least the **last four digits** of the card used.

#### **Prohibited Expenses**

* Expenses that will not be reimbursed

  + Alcohol (pursuant to the campus [Alcohol and Drug policy](https://www.cpp.edu/policies/university/administrative/docs/policy---administrative-affairs-1.5.pdf))
  + Consult the policy links at the bottom of this page for other expenses that will not be reimbursed

#### **Documentation**

* Save all supporting documents, such as:

  + Boarding passes
  + Hotel receipts/folios
  + Event registration confirmations/certificate
  + Event itineraries
  + Meal receipts

#### **Conduct & Safety**

* Maintain professional behavior.
* Follow all **local laws,** [**Student C****onduct, and Student Organization Conduct policies**](https://www.cpp.edu/studentconduct/policies/code.shtml).
* Prioritize your safety — be aware of your surroundings and follow any location-specific advisories.

### Finish any post-travel follow-up

What to do with paper liability forms, and your receipts within 10 business days after you return.](#modal-3 "Follow-up")

### Follow-up

×

**What should I do with the liability forms?**

All supporting documents should have been uploaded to your Travel Request Form prior to travel. Please securely shred these forms once you have verified they all have been submitted. Do not send or email Release of Liability forms to the Bronco Leadership Center or the Provost's Office.

**Request reimbursement from ASI for non-academic travel.**

After you travel, collect all your receipts and submit a reimbursement within 10 business days of returning of the trip.

Submit your receipts and fill out forms to [ASI Financial Services](https://asi.cpp.edu/services/club-services/finance-support/club-forms/).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Who needs to submit a travel request?

For any group or individual travel, forms must be submitted.

For academic travel: contact your department to determine who is responsible for the 3A form.

For non-academic travel, the scheduler of the organization should send the request.

### How long does this process take?

You should start the process roughly a month and a half before traveling. The Step 3 form must be submitted at least a month before the first date that you travel.

### Why does it take so long?

For each travel request, there are multiple departments that need to review. Sometimes, the departments may ask you additional questions before they are able to confirm your request.

### I have not heard anything about my request. What do I do?

The best way to get support is to reply to your ticket. You can go into the [IT Help Desk](https://cpp.service-now.com/ehelp?id=my_requests) and click "My Tickets" to review the status of your request. If you reply to that message, it will add a comment to your request.

If you still do not hear back, send an email to the contacts below.

For Academic Travel: provosts\_docs@cpp.edu

For Non-academic Travel: leadership@cpp.edu

### If I submitted a Step 3 form, I can just go on the trip, since I am done?

No, you need to wait to ensure all areas have completed your request.

* Academic travel
  + You will receive an email that says your ticket "has been approved and is now closed."
* Non-academic travel
  + You will receive an email that says a "solution has been proposed for your ticket."

Once you receive the appropriate message, you can travel.

### Can I get reimbursed for something that I did not list?

If you have questions about reimbursement (non-academic travel), contact [ASI Financial Services](https://asi.cpp.edu/services/club-services/finance-support/).

### What happens if I do not submit a travel request?

Failure to submit a travel request may result in:

* Loss of eligibility for funding or reimbursement
* Organization or individual disciplinary action if the travel is unsanctioned

It’s always best to follow the proper channels to ensure safety, support, and funding.

### I still have an issue that was not addressed here.

We will be happy to assist you!

For academic travel, contact: provosts\_docs@cpp.edu

For non-academic travel, contact: leadership@cpp.edu

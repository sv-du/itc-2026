# Affiliate Faculty

## Meet Our Affiliates

[![Cory Aragon](img/cory-aragon.jpg)

### Associate Professor of Philosophy

#### Cory Aragon

His research focuses on questions of individual responsibility to remedy structural injustice, both material and epistemic, and he works primarily in the fields of contemporary social and political philosophy, feminist philosophy, critical philosophy of race, and global justice. He has been a CCEP Faculty Fellow since 2019 and supervised CPP’s Undergraduate Journal of Ethics, Policy, and Social Justice.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/csaragon/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Lori Barker](img/lori-barker.jpg)

### Professor - Director, MS in Psychology Program

#### Lori Barker

Her primary area of interest is multicultural psychology, and she is the co-author of an undergraduate textbook on that subject titled Multicultural Psychology: Understanding Our Diverse Communities (Oxford University Press). She is also the author of Obama on Our Minds: The Impact of Obama on the Psyche of America (Oxford University Press). Her research focuses on multicultural attitudes, the effectiveness of multicultural education, and the psychological impact of diverse presidential candidates. She recently started a YouTube channel, @LifeLinesWithDrLori, where her goal is to educate, encourage, and empower people about their mental health.](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC0G6F6xFZv9tdM8_VNxnHiw%20 "opens a new window")

[![Armondo Collins ](img/armondo.png)

### Assistant Professor

#### Armondo Collins

Assistant Professor in English & Modern Languages, with specializations in African American literature and rhetoric, and cultural media studies. He is the author of The Black God Trope and Rhetorical Resistance: A Tradition of Race and Religion. He teaches podcasting as a form of multimodal literacy in courses on Rhetoric and Writing for the Professions.](https://www.armondocollins.com/%20 "opens a new window")

[![Brady Collins ](img/brady-collins-higher-res.jpg)

### Associate Professor of Political Science

#### Brady Collins

He specializes in examining labor and housing conditions in ethnic enclaves. His recent work focuses on restaurant and retail workers in the Koreatown neighborhood of Los Angeles. As a Faculty Fellow with the California Center for Ethics and Policy (CCEP), he has coproduced four seasons of the CCEP podcast series. He earned his Ph.D. in urban planning from UCLA, where he was also a graduate of and former instructor in the Urban Humanities Initiative.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/bjcollins/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Will Cowan ](img/will-cowan.jpg)

### Assistant Professor and Environmental Historian

#### Will Cowan

He studies the history of weather and water extremes in the North American West, fusing environmental history, history of science, and disaster studies. Will has contributed to documentaries by Weathered, Off the Beaten Path, and American Experience, and has written pieces for Environmental History, Western Historical Quarterly, The LA Times, and the Library of Congress. He teaches Liberal Studies 4011: “Landscape, Place, & Power,” where students use digital tools and methods to develop their semester-long “Sense of Place” projects.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/wcowan/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Ivana Cvetkovic ](img/ivana-cvetkovic.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of Communication

#### Ivana Cvetkovic

After working as a reporter for daily newspapers in Serbia, her current research and teaching interests are situated at the intersection of media, technology and culture. She primarily focuses on online and social media news discourses , especially discourses on migration, and multimedia message production across a variety of platforms.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/icvetkovic/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Annie Danis ](img/annie.png)

### Assistant Professor of Anthropology

#### Annie Danis

Her research and art explore the intersection of art and archaeology through a sensory approach to historic landscapes. She directs the Amache x Sand Creek Massacre Youth Ambassador Program, an intercultural educational initiative involving young descendants of two significant National Historic Sites in Southeastern Colorado. Together with her Visual Anthropology class, she leads site visits and supervises students and young people in the creation of podcasts, videos, and ArcGIS story maps.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/aedanis/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Amy Dao ](img/amy.png)

### Assistant Professor of Anthropology

#### Amy Dao

Focused on how concepts of care shape economic life. One recent project examined how perceptions of risk, time, and family shaped the campaign for universal health insurance in Vietnam. She plans to work with students on a health-related digital humanities project, building on prior work co-creating the podcast Under One Roof: Multigenerational Households During the Pandemic.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/aldao/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Michelle L. DeJohnette ](img/michelle-l-dejohnette.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of Early Childhood Studies

#### Michelle L. DeJohnette

Teaching antiracism and equity in early education. Her research also focuses on critical theories and culturally-responsible pedagogy. She will collaborate with students in the College of Education and Integrative Studies to launch a podcast on racial inequities in early childhood education.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/mdejohnette/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Philippe Duhart ](img/phillipe-min.png)

### Assistant Professor of Sociology

#### Philippe Duhart

He co-supervises students producing public scholarship around crime and society in short-form videos through the Criminology Media Lab.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/peduhart/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Lauren Furey ](img/laurenfurey.png)

### Associate Professor of Communication

#### Lauren Furey

She advises The Poly Post student newspaper and Bronco Guide magazine, as well as co-leads a Media and Politics Research Team with undergraduate students. Outside of teaching, she researches the portrayal of women in politics by the news media and in popular culture and co-hosts a podcast featuring interviews with women politicians called The Pink State, which won an award of excellence at the 2024 Broadcast Education Association’s Media Arts Festival.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/ldfurey/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Gyasmine George-Williams](img/george-william.png)

### Assistant Professor of Race, Sports and Social Justice

#### Gyasmine George-Williams

An activist and socio-cultural practitioner. She is the author of Love Is at the Root of Resistance: Strategies of Activism, Advocacy and Liberation and the founder of 7Ten Legacy LLC, consulting, publishing and research rooted in advocacy. She equips her students with the tools to engage and develop multicultural competencies, challenge ideologies and formulate a critical lens, and was featured in the Season 4 premiere of CCEP’s podcast, The Multiverse of Misinformation.](https://drgyasmine.com/about/%20 "opens a new window")

[![Sona Gevorkyan](img/sona-gevorkyan.jpg)

### Lecturer

#### Sona Gevorkyan

Sona Gevorkyan is a lecturer in the Department of Architecture in the College of Environmental Design and a licensed architect. She is the founder of SGAO, a multidisciplinary design practice based in Los Angeles. Her research and teaching explore the intersection of materiality, identity, and digital culture, with a focus on AI, fashion, and fabrication in architectural design. She teaches design studies and digital design seminars at Cal Poly Pomona and is currently developing work in 3D-printed ceramics and surface-based storytelling.](https://sgao.us/About%20 "opens a new window")

[![Peter Hanink ](img/peter.png)

### Assistant Professor of Sociology

#### Peter Hanink

Focused on how we as a society respond to crime. His research projects with students have included: analyzing police use-of-force policies, analyzing violence committed against Blacks in South during Reconstruction, experimental studies of how perceptions of dangerousness and criminality are influenced by race, and how rule-breaking is evaluated. He co-supervises students producing public scholarship around crime and society in short-form videos through the Criminology Media Lab.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/pahanink/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Shonn Haren ](img/shonn-haren.jpg)

### Social Sciences Librarian

#### Shonn Haren

M.A. in History from UC Riverside & an M.L.I.S. from San Jose State University. He is CPP’s Social Sciences Librarian and his areas of interest include podcasting (including coproducing CCEP’s podcast in Season 4: The Multiverse of Misinformation), data visualization, GIS, fake news, misinformation and conspiracy theories. For information on his publications, check out his ORCID profile.](https://libguides.library.cpp.edu/prf.php?account_id=111609  "opens a new window")

[![Bianca Haro ](img/bianca-haro.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of Sociology

#### Bianca Haro

Specializing in critical race feminista praxis, education, and youth activism, resistance, and justice. In her course, “The Sociological Landscape of Pomona, California,” students analyze the history and culture of the City of Pomona. She also partners with Gente Organizada, a community-led social action nonprofit organization in Pomona dedicated to organizing and building intergenerational power for youth and immigrant families.](https://cpp.academia.edu/BiancaHaro%20 "opens a new window")

[![Rachael A. Hill ](img/rachael-hill.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of History

#### Rachael A. Hill

Specializing in the history of health, science, and medicine in Ethiopia. Her research interests include public health, the social etiology of disease, the history of science in the global south, African therapeutic practice, and medical pluralism. She supervises DH world history research projects on the trans-Atlantic slave system using the online interactive database slavevoyage.org and mentors the student club Student Initiative for Justice at the Ahimsa Center.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/rahill/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Hyeryung Hwang ](img/ayana-jamieson.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of Global Literature

#### Hyeryung Hwang

An Assistant Professor of Global Literature in the Interdisciplinary General Education (IGE) program in the Department of Liberal Studies. Previously a Marion L. Brittain Postdoctoral Fellow at Georgia Tech, she received training in Digital Humanities pedagogy, exploring how literary data, combined with sociological and literary-critical methods, uncovers social networks in postcolonial literature. At CPP, Dr. Hwang integrates hands-on DH projects into her courses, such as collaborative game narrative design, films and podcasts on racial inequality in digital spaces, and a campus accessibility map, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and enhancing student engagement with the digital humanities.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/hhwang/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Ayana Jamieson ](img/ayana.png)

### Assistant Professor of African American Studies

#### Ayana Jamieson

A mythologist, and a depth psychologist. She is the founder of the Octavia E. Butler Legacy Network, and was featured on NPR’s podcast Throughline. Her work has appeared in the Black Futures anthology. She liaises with the statewide DH@CSU Consortium and supervise EWS student podcast projects.](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ayana-jamieson-phd%20 "opens a new window")

[![Aishwary Kumar ](img/kumar.png)

### Associate Professor of History

#### Aishwary Kumar

Director of the Democracy Institute and the Ahimsa Center. An intellectual historian and political theorist whose work spans South Asian, European, and American political thought, he is the author of Radical Equality: Ambedkar, Gandhi, and the Risk of Democracy and will supervise students engaged in DH work in support of Mutant: The Democracy Podcast.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/aishwaryk/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Carsten Lange ](img/lange.png)

### Professor of Economics

#### Carsten Lange

Specializes in money supply, inflation, central bank policy, and economic impact analysis. Recent projects include an economic impact study for the City of Industry, predicting real estate prices in Orange County with GIS, analyzing California adolescent obesity with GIS, and an analysis of the European debt crisis. He is expert in machine learning and AI including neural networks and deep learning, and a member of CPP’s High Performance Computing Cluster.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/clange/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Dan Luo](img/dan-luo.png)

### Assistant Professor of Comparative Chinese Literature

#### Dan Luo

Her area of specialty is modern and contemporary Chinese literature, cinema, and science fiction. In her scholarship and pedagogy, she incorporates literary, aesthetic, and critical discourses about robotics and artificial intelligence, and explores the ways in which sci-fi thematizes the digital transformation of identity and society. She is the co-author of the Historical Dictionary of Chinese Cinema and has written about Chinese sci-fi as popular media and the simulation of nationalist utopia.](https://www.cpp.edu/class/english-modern-languages/faculty/faculty.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Robert Lewis ](img/lewis.jpg)

### Associate Professor of History

#### Robert Lewis

Specializing in modern European history, with a focus on France, urban history, and the history of sport. He is the author of The Stadium Century: Sport, Spectatorship and Society in Modern France. His new research involves the digitization and GIS mapping of mapping railway networks in 20th-century Europe, and he supervises students’ DH projects oriented around the global history of the First World War.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/rwlewis/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Paul Nissenson ](img/paul-nissenson.jpg)

### Professor and Associate Chair of Mechanical Engineering

#### Paul Nissenson

His main research focus is exploring the role of technology in engineering education. He has created numerous videos for the Mechanical Engineering Department's video tutorial library (ME Online) and is the host of The Engineering Student Experience Podcast.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/pmnissenson/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Lin Ong ](img/lin.png)

### Associate Professor of International Business and Marketing

#### Lin Ong

Her research examines the interaction of business and society, with a focus on consumer vulnerability in digital and financial spaces. A Fulbright Fellow in Malaysia in Spring 2025, she is interested in increasing consumer well-being through effective marketing strategies and public policy. She supervises DH students in digital marketing campaigns.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/lueilinong/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Michael Slaughter ](img/michael-slaughter.png)

### Associate Professor of History

#### Michael Slaughter

He specializes in 20th-century American history, African-American history, and the history of education. He teaches Ethnic Studies Digital History at CPP.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/maslaughter/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Valerie Sky](img/valerie-sky.png)

### Assistant Professor of Applied Linguistics and TESOL

#### Valerie Sky

Dr. Sky’s research explores innovative evidence-based approaches to teaching English to speakers of other languages (TESOL). Professor Sky supervises students’ multimodal projects that focus on linguistics, language learning, and teaching English to adolescents and adults.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/vsky%20 "opens a new window")

[![Denisse Vasquez-Guevara ](img/denise.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of Communication

#### Denisse Vasquez-Guevara

As a science and health communication researcher, she has worked in multidisciplinary research on child nutrition, COVID-19 prevention, chronic illness management, urban accessibility, and sustainable foods, creating educational resources via online platforms. She was included in the Ecuadorean Women in Science Hall by REMCI and awarded as Top Faculty Instructor at the University of Cuenca’s School of Communication. At the DHC, through The Scicom Toolbox, she will provide public engagement tools and training through social media and in-person science communication for culturally diverse audiences.](https://www.youtube.com/%40ScicomToolbox-n6c%20 "opens a new window")

[![Eileen V. Wallis ](img/eileen.png)

### Professor of History

#### Eileen V. Wallis

She specializes in the history of California and the American West; US Women's History; Public History, and the History of US Medicine and Disability. She is the author of two monographs, Earning Power: Women and Work in Los Angeles, 1880-1930; and California and the Politics of Disability, 1850-1970, and numerous articles. She is particularly interested in the intersection(s) of public history with digital technologies and regularly creates collaborative student-led digital projects for community organizations.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/evwallis/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

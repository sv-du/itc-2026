## About the option

In today’s dynamic and interconnected world, employers seek forward-thinking professionals who can adapt to the ever-changing global economy. The International Business option provides students with a solid grounding in the principles of business management as well as interdisciplinary specialization in areas required to understand and react to today’s globalized markets. The objectives of this option are twofold: to provide students with the business knowledge and skills essential for careers in international business; and to provide them with an understanding and appreciation of the culture, language, economics, politics, and history of other parts of the world, with particular emphasis on a geographic area in which the student has a special career interest, or a functional area of focus on the global scene. The curriculum requires completion of the business required courses which provides to all business majors a foundation in the theory and practice of modern business management.

## Career Outcomes

Next

### Economic Advisor

An Economic Advisor provides expert analysis and recommendations on economic trends, policies, and strategies to guide decision-making for businesses, governments, or organizations.

### Entrepreneur

An Entrepreneur identifies business opportunities, takes risks, and creates or manages new ventures, innovating products or services to meet market needs and drive growth.

### Global Account Manager

A Global Account Manager oversees and manages relationships with key international clients, ensuring their needs are met across multiple regions while driving business growth and maintaining strategic partnerships.

### Global Market Researcher

A Global Market Researcher analyzes international market trends, consumer behavior, and competitive landscapes to provide insights that help businesses expand and adapt their strategies globally.

### Import/Export Agent

An Import/Export Agent facilitates the buying and selling of goods across international borders, managing logistics, documentation, and compliance with trade regulations.

### International Banking Officer

An International Banking Officer manages banking operations and services for clients engaged in international trade, ensuring compliance with global financial regulations and facilitating cross-border transactions.

### International Legal Counselor

An International Legal Counselor provides legal advice on global matters, including international treaties, cross-border transactions, and compliance with foreign laws and regulations.

Slide 8

### International Management

International Management involves overseeing and coordinating business operations across multiple countries, focusing on global strategy, cross-cultural communication, and managing international teams to achieve organizational goals.

### Consultant

A Consultant provides expert advice and solutions to organizations or individuals in specific industries or fields, helping them solve problems, improve performance, or achieve their business objectives.

Slide 10

### International Trade Specialist

An International Trade Specialist manages and advises on the import and export of goods and services, ensuring compliance with trade regulations, managing logistics, and optimizing international market strategies.

## Overview

Each student should work closely with the program advisor in identifying career goals and selecting course work most appropriate for goal attainment. The International Business subplan involves the completion of requirements in each of the following areas:
1. Courses in Major required of all Business majors
2. International Business required courses
3. Elective and Emphasis courses
4. General Education

## CEO, Klatch Coffee

### Heather Perry ’04

When she graduated from CPP, Heather Perry’s coﬀee prowess combined with her business education put her on a trajectory to be the ideal CEO of a coﬀee company.
Heather applied her business acumen as she ascended from member to board member to president of the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA), which merged the U.S.-based organization with its counterpart in Europe during her tenure. Networking with coﬀee industry titans around the world through the SCA, trade shows and barista competitions allowed Heather to build Klatch into an internationally known company.
“Yes, there are stressful days, but I love what we get to do, love our team members, love the people we meet through our business,” says Heather, who won the 2003 and 2007 U.S. Barista Championships.

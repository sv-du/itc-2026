## About the Program

Grow your career and feed the future. Here, we emphasize high-impact teaching practices to develop an inclusive and highly skilled workforce in sustainable fruit and vegetable, agronomic and tree crop industries; urban agriculture, controlled environment agriculture; and plant science across the green industries from water resources and irrigation design, arboriculture, and sports turf management to botanical gardens and natural resources conservation.

## The Polytechnic Approach

In the plant science program, you’ll step into a world where your learning takes root in fields, greenhouses and labs. You won’t just study plants and soils—you’ll tackle real-world challenges from sustainable farming to innovative irrigation solutions. As you work hands-on with cutting-edge techniques and technologies, you'll build the skills and knowledge to shape the future of agriculture. Whether you’re in the classroom, on an internship or collaborating on community projects, you’ll be part of the next wave of change across green industries.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Hydroponic Specialist

Design, implement, and manage soil-free growing systems to optimize plant growth and sustainability in controlled environments.

### Irrigation Designer

Develop efficient irrigation systems that conserve water while ensuring optimal plant health and growth for various agricultural and landscaping needs.

### Conservationist

Work to protect and restore plant ecosystems through sustainable practices and habitat management to preserve biodiversity and natural resources.

### Global Product Planner

Oversee the development, production, and distribution of plant-based products on a global scale, ensuring innovation and sustainability in the plant science industry.

## Courses

Our program emphasizes environmental sustainability and the efficient use of natural resources, integrating economic principles essential for maintaining successful plant science businesses. The curriculum includes all coursework needed to fulfill the California Department of Pesticide Regulation’s Agricultural Pest Control Adviser (PCA) education requirements for professional licensure. You will be well-prepared for diverse careers across horticulture, agronomic and specialty crop industries; urban agriculture; plant pest and disease management; landscape and sports turf management; soil, plant, and water resources conservation, as well as roles in government, research, consulting, and further academic pursuits.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

### Orange County Farm Bureau Scholarship

The Orange County Farm Bureau Scholarship was funded by the Orange County Farm Bureau, a non-profit non-governmental organization that promotes Orange County agriculture, to provide scholarships to newly admitted or continuing Huntley College of Agriculture students majoring in Agribusiness and Food Industry Management, Agricultural Science, Animal Health Science, Animal Science, Food Science and Technology, Nutrition and/or Plant Science.

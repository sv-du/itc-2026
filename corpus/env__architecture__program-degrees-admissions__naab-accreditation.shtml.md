# NAAB Accreditation

![miniature model of cabin project](../img/fv_cabin-project-model.jpg)

*Cabin Project Model*

In the United States, most registration boards require a degree from an accredited professional degree program as a prerequisite for licensure. The [National Architectural Accrediting Board (NAAB)](https://www.naab.org/ "National Architectural Accrediting Board (NAAB) (opens in new window)"), which is the sole agency authorized to accredit professional degree programs in architecture offered by institutions with U.S. regional accreditation, recognizes three types of degrees: the Bachelor of Architecture, the Master of Architecture, and the Doctor of Architecture. A program may be granted an eight-year term, an eight-year term with conditions, or a two-year term of continuing accreditation, or a three-year term of initial accreditation, depending on the extent of its conformance with established education standards. Doctor of Architecture and Master of Architecture degree programs may require a non-accredited undergraduate degree in architecture for admission. However, the non-accredited degree is not, by itself, recognized as an accredited degree.

Cal Poly Pomona, Department of Architecture offers the following NAAB-accredited degree program(s):

* [Bachelor of Architecture](https://www.cpp.edu/env/architecture/program-degrees-admissions/bachelor-of-architecture.shtml "https://www.cpp.edu/env/architecture/program-degrees-admissions/bachelor-of-architecture.shtml")(BArch) (150 semester credits)
* [Master of Architecture](https://www.cpp.edu/env/architecture/program-degrees-admissions/master-of-architecture.shtml "https://www.cpp.edu/env/architecture/program-degrees-admissions/master-of-architecture.shtml") (MArch) (non-pre-professional degree + 90 semester credits)

The next accreditation visits for both accredited programs will be in Spring 2031.

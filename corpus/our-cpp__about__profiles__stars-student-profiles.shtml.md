## Aaron Wu

##### Mechanical Engineering Major

Learn about Aaron

### Aaron Wu

---

*“I have always been obsessed with rocket technology. My dream is one day that I can become a real rocket engineer, so doing hybrid rocketry to me is defiantly a great leap forward.”*

---

**Major:** Mechanical Engineering

**Faculty Mentor:** Dr. Martin Mason

**School:** Mt. San Antonio College

Aaron is an International Student from Taiwan. He started his educational journey in Taiwan and wished to complete his dreams of becoming a rocket engineer. Entering the STARS Program has been a fantastic opportunity for Aaron. He has learned an abundance of new information, not only in engineering but in his communication skills as well.

The research project that he is working on is testing 3D printing material, like resin, in extreme conditions to see if they can be used in rocketry. During his research, some of the setbacks that Aaron overcame was the need to fabricate the materials he needs with his own hands rather than blueprints directly to a factory. Despite being used to sending in the blueprints to a factory, the skill of being able to create your own materials is something that Aaron developed in the STARS program. Not only that, but he has also learned how to use building tools like a laser cutter and being able to effectively find replacement part efficiently. These are skills that are usually for his future to work in rocket technology.

Aaron has seen an increase in his confidence regarding future research projects due to the skills he learned in the program. Working with propulsion systems and aerodynamic design will most definitely help Aaron with his future in rocket technology.

“Ambition, perseverance, and patience are three of the most important things I learned from doing this project "added Aaron.

**Advice to Students**

The things he would like for undergraduates seeking research opportunities to know is that the point of conducting research is not to create something useful or valuable, it has to do with the process of developing the mindset of conducting a research project in an academic setting. The purpose of research is to further societies' knowledge of the known and unknown.

## CJ Negrete

##### Environmental/Aerospace Engineering Major

##### \*STARS Summer Program\*

Learn about CJ

### CJ Negrete

**Major:** Environmental/ Aerospace Engineering

**Faculty Mentor:** Dr. Navid Nakhjiri

**School:** Citrus College

CJ is currently studying at Citrus College and will be transferring to Cal Poly Pomona as an Environmental Engineering major. CJ is a proud 33-year-old transfer student that is excited to be transferring to her dream school.  The STARS program has allowed CJ to fully immerse herself in her research project. This opportunity allowed CJ to gain experience in a field she was passionate about and gave her clarity about her future goals. The research program STARS is filled with advisors and associates that are passionate about helping others and supportive of students’ advancement.

With the direction of her faculty advisor, Dr. Yong Gan, CJ researched crude oil absorption through various biomasses. For this project, CJ was able to perform my experiments from home and gather data in a way that felt like it was on her own time and under her own specs. CJ feels that understanding the methods of the experiment allows her to choose which samples to move forward her experiment with.  CJ was grateful to be able to achieve promising results that further her desire to continue future research.

To the students looking to join the STARS program, CJ would say: “Being a returning student, I found myself at the end of my Citrus College career still unsure about what I wanted to do in school. With a few ideas and the support of my mentor, I was allowed to find what I found made me happy and what my passion was. I plan on changing my major from environmental engineering to aerospace engineering. I have found encouragement from my community and the STARS program to pursue my big dreams, and I'm shooting for the stars.”

CJ’s motivation for this research project came from her longtime interest in participating in an environmental project where she can make a difference in the world. This research project is a perfect match and allows her to realize that she can make a difference by putting her ideas out and trying new things.

CJ has acquired a great set of skills over the past few weeks during her research project that is transferable to her educational goals. Those skills include reaching out to an advisor, setting expectations for data collection and creating a timeline. CJ has refined her research and article selection skills to help her push her project forward.

After participation in the STARS program, CJ feels inspired to continue doing research. For her, it has been an exciting and rewarding experience and she would like to continue finding more ways to be involved in research, which includes attending graduate school.

CJ would like to continue her participation in this project throughout the years and wants to make it her senior project. She is invested in seeing this research published in articles.

When CJ recounts her time during the STARS program she says “Everyone should be allowed the opportunity to tinker with new ideas and projects because it is truly inspiring. I would like to thank the SURE/STARS program and the staff involved for not only helping me but countless others, find their passion.”

**Advice to Students**

Communicate when you're unsure about anything or what to do. Ask questions about expectations and how you could assist in the development of the project and don't be afraid to assert your ideas. I would also say do not be discouraged about not getting in if you apply once. Try and try again. I wasn't admitted the first time I applied to the SURE program and now I can't imagine my life without having tried.

## Jose Ortiz

##### Computer Informations Systems Major

##### \*STARS Summer Program\*

Learn about Jose

### Jose Ortiz

**Major:** Computer Information Systems

**Faculty Mentor:** Dr. Honggang Wang

**School:** Cal Poly Pomona

Jose is a Computer Science major here at Cal Poly Pomona. He describes the Student STARS program as a supportive environment where he was able to get immediate and thorough feedback from the Office of Undergraduate Research team. He appreciates the way that the STARS program set him up for success, despite the short 8-week summer program timeline.

Jose’s research project with his faculty mentor Dr. Honggang Wang is about conducting a comprehensive literature review on autonomous driving technologies, and their economic and environmental impacts. They aim to build and demonstrate efficient 3D object detection developed with deep learning Python packages. In his project, Jose will collect and analyze real-life image datasets from industries such as Lyft or Baidu.

To students looking to join the STARS program, Jose says: “I would highly recommend the SURE/STAR program to my friends, acquaintances, ex-roommates, and anyone that maybe taking classes and has any interest in simply getting familiar with how to go about researching a subject in an objective and systematic fashion.”

Jose’s motivation for this research project came from his long-term interest in researching Alzheimer’s Disease (AD). The research that Jose is conducting includes the application of deep learning models to various images, which are collected as a part of Alzheimer’s research. He has found that object detection for autonomous driving or mental degeneration can be applied to further knowledge that would otherwise be labor intensive and time prohibitive.

Although Jose had some previous experiences in his past projects with machine learning, in this project he had to learn a new technique known as deep learning to move forward with his project. Jose struggled at the beginning of this project to get acclimated to the process of deep learning (DL). He was able to tackle his struggles with the literature review, which was the most useful tool to help him become more comfortable with deep learning methods. Jose says that the best way to overcome challenges in research projects is to lean into them.

The most valuable skill Jose has gained this summer has been to not be discouraged by the large volumes of information, and how to comb through it, extracting the information needed for the project.

After Jose’s participation in the STARS program, he feels more confident continuing his research journey. “There is nothing to lose, and everything to gain by participating in the program.”, he adds.

As mentioned previously, the deep learning techniques that Jose learned, can carry over into AD research by applying object detection or image classification, facilitating scientist, clinicians, and researchers with high volumes of data that would otherwise be labor prohibitive.

**Advice to Students**

Don't miss the opportunity. You'll improve yourself in so many ways, your skills, knowledge, reputation as a researcher, your resume will have a great achievement on it, peers will respect you for completing an intense task.

The project that Nicholas and his research team have been working on is developing an Autonomous Solar UAV Drone Fleet. The project has a multitude of different projects, but they hope to have multiple functional autonomous drones that can implement obstacle avoidance. Together, Nicholas and his team decided to use components within the different systems which allowed them to navigate the necessities of cost efficacy, weight, and power consumption of the drones. They all still have a variety of plans which they want to implement in the future.

# OAI Faculty and Staff Summer Conference 2025

## Innovate, Adapt, Lead

### The AI Driven Future of Higher Education and Work

From Wednesday, June 4th, 2025, to Thursday, June 5th, 2025, the Office of Academic Innovation Summer Conference will bring together faculty and staff from Cal Poly Pomona and beyond to explore "The AI-Driven Future of Higher Education and Work." This dynamic event will dive into the intersection of education and the evolving workforce, offering insightful keynotes, engaging panel...

## Plenary Speakers

### Insights from Leaders and Innovators in Higher Education

Join CSU and national leaders as we reimagine teaching, learning, and access in an AI-enhanced world.

[![annette](img/events/summer-conference/keynote/annette.png)

### AI Isn't Magic

Dr. Annette Vee, Professor of English at the University of Pittsburgh, demystifies how large language models like ChatGPT actually work—and how they’re already being used in classrooms by students and instructors alike. This session will provide a foundational understanding of generative AI, offer real-world classroom examples, and explore ways to use these tools collaboratively and ethically in undergraduate education.](#modal-0 "Plenary Information")

### Plenary Information

**Session Organizers:**

Dr. Kristi Prins | English & Modern Languages | College of Letters, Arts, and Social Sciences

Whether or not we use it, students and teachers are both working in the context of AI. How do large language models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT work, and how are undergraduates and instructors using them now? How might we use AI ethically--and collaboratively? This workshop will provide a brief introduction to LLMs and show how faculty and students have been using them in undergraduate courses.

[![chesa caparas](img/events/summer-conference/keynote/chesa.png)

### Digital Transformations in Education

Dr. Chesa Caparas explores how generative AI is reshaping both workforce expectations and classroom practices. As institutions face growing pressure to prepare students for an AI-driven world, this session will spotlight how AI literacy—especially when grounded in ethics—can close opportunity gaps, promote equity, and prepare students to lead in a rapidly evolving economy.](#modal-1 "Plenary Information")

### Plenary Information

×

**Digital Transformations in Education: Meeting the Needs of an AI-Enhanced Future**

**Session Speaker:**Chesa Caparas | English and Ethnic Studies | De Anza College

**Session Organizers:**

Dr. Peter Ross | Philosophy | College of Letters, Arts, and Social Sciences

Dr. Alex Madva | Philosophy | College of Letters, Arts, and Social Sciences

Dr. Winny Dong | Inaugural Senior Director, Innovation and Grants | Office of Academic Innovation

**Session Summary:** There’s little doubt that generative AI tools have seriously disrupted teaching and learning. As industries rush to adopt these new tools, we’re also seeing a dramatic shift in the skills needed to succeed in the workforce, and educational institutions face increasing pressure to prepare students for this AI-enhanced economy. Unfortunately, uneven AI adoption means that not all institutions are equipped to navigate this rapidly changing landscape. This plenary talk will highlight ways that AI literacy curriculum can help overcome digital divides and increase opportunities for students seeking jobs in the emerging workforce. More importantly, the presenter will discuss how teaching AI ethics can provide a solid foundation for ensuring that AI research, development, and implementation remain human-centered.

**About:**

Chesa Caparas is professor of English and Ethnic Studies at De Anza College in Cupertino, CA, where she teaches in the IMPACT AAPI Learning Community and leads professional development workshops on AI in education. She is also a member of CA Learning Lab’s AI Faculty Innovator in Residence Strategy Team and serves as a Fulbright Scholar Alumni Ambassador promoting international research and teaching opportunities to educators across the country. She has a BA and MA in Modern Literature from UC-Santa Cruz and is currently pursuing an MS in Information and Knowledge Strategy at Columbia University. Her writing has been published in *The Journal of Information Ethics*, *The Journal of California English,*ASCCC’s *Rostrum*, and the arts and literature journal *ANMLY.*

Leslie Kennedy, Ed.D., serves as the Assistant Vice Chancellor of Academic Technology Services in Academic and Student Affairs at the California State University, Office of the Chancellor. In this role, Dr. Kennedy provides strategic leadership and creative oversight, enhancing the quality and integration of information technology into teaching, learning, research, scholarship, and the full range of student and academic services across the CSU system. She is responsible for directing the systematic implementation of academic technology initiatives while managing ATS budgets and personnel to foster creative and innovative solutions. Additionally, she advises the Chancellor’s Office leadership on a comprehensive range of academic technology services and initiatives—including systemwide library services, online education, enterprise academic software agreements, learning management systems, affordable learning solutions, artificial intelligence, and web design.

AI in the Classroom

### AI's Impact on Interdisciplinary Writing and the Teaching of Writing

This session explores how generative AI tools are reshaping interdisciplinary writing and pedagogy. Topics include rhetorical purpose, authorship, revision, assessment, and academic integrity. Case studies and strategies for responsible AI integration into writing instruction will be shared to support creativity, critical thinking, and communication across disciplines.

AI in the Classroom

### Incorporating AI into Assignments and Activities

A panel discussion featuring faculty from various disciplines on how generative AI can enhance student engagement through assignments and activities. It emphasizes critical thinking and innovative course design using AI tools.

Card 3 of 7

AI outside the Classroom

### AI on the Job – Smart Tools for Smarter Work

Explore how generative AI tools like ChatGPT and Microsoft Copilot can support your daily work. This hands-on session will demo campus-supported tools, teach practical prompting, and offer time for Q&A. Designed for all experience levels—just bring your curiosity!

Card 4 of 7

AI outside the classroom

### Billy Chat – Keeping It Human

A retrospective on Billy Chat, CPP’s AI chatbot. Learn how it supports student communication, what data it's generated, and how to center human connection in AI-driven solutions.

AI in the Classroom

### AI’s Impact on Interdisciplinary Writing and the Teaching of Writing

Explores how AI tools are reshaping writing across disciplines. Focuses on rhetorical purpose, authorship, assessment, and ethics, with takeaways for responsible integration in writing instruction.

Card 6 of 7

AI outside the Classroom

### Students, the Future of Work, and AI

Covers the impact of AI on industries and job roles, key skills needed for future careers, and how universities can prepare students. Encourages interdisciplinary learning and real-world readiness.

AI outside the classroom

### Using AI Effectively (How Can AI Help Me Do My Job More Effectively)

This hands-on session demonstrates how AI can improve productivity and creativity for faculty and staff. You'll explore tools like ChatGPT and Copilot, view real-time demos, and engage in collaborative activities to rethink your workflows using AI. Come ready to share, learn, and leave with actionable strategies.

### What is the Academic Innovation Summer Conference?

It’s a two-day event exploring **AI’s impact on higher education and the workforce**, featuring keynotes, panels, and workshops.

The conference will be held on **June 4-5, 2025**, from **8:30 AM - 4:00 PM** at **Cal Poly Pomona, Building 162, Room 1001**.

General Admission is **$225 (ends May 22nd at 5 pm)**, with an **Early Bird rate of $175** available until **May 2nd at 5 PM**.

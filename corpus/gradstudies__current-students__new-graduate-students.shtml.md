# New Graduate Students

## Start Your CPP Journey!

Dear Graduate Students,

Welcome to CPP! The Graduate Studies Team is looking forward to your arrival in Spring 2026 and meeting you soon. To help facilitate your transition to our campus environment, here are some resources and next steps for you to follow. Feel free to contact us with questions or concerns!

### Schedule of Registration Windows

After you submit your [Intent to Enroll](https://www.cpp.edu/admissions/graduate/masters-admitted.shtml), you'll be receiving an email from the Registrar's Office before your registration window with specific instructions on how to register for classes. Registration windows for new incoming graduate students for Spring 2026 are typically assigned Mid-October to the end of the Spring Add/Drop Period. Visit the [Registrar's Office](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/registration/registration-new-students.shtml) for more registration information.

\*Note: If you did not register for any classes during the initial registration period, or missed your registration appointment, you may register during the add/drop period for the Spring 2026 semester beginning January 5, 2026 through January 30, 2026 on BroncoDirect.

### Connecting with your Graduate Coordinator

You may contact your [graduate coordinator](../prospective-students/explore-our-grad-programs.shtml) if you need guidance on which classes you need to enroll in for the Fall semester and if you have any questions about your master’s program requirements. You can find your department contact information by [visiting your department homepage](https://www.cpp.edu/academics/departments-az/).

### Checklist for Incoming Graduate Students

[Incoming Graduate FAQs](faq.shtml)

Here is a helpful [checklist (pdf)](../documents/checklist-for-incoming-graduate-students_fall-2025.pdf) for you to review and complete as you transition to your first semester at Cal Poly Pomona.

## Self Service Tools

The Cal Poly Pomona Online Services page features one location to access several popular applications via MyCPP. All currently enrolled students have immediate access to all single sign-on applications, such as BroncoDirect, Canvas, University Library and Lynda.com. To log on, go to [my.cpp.edu](https://my.cpp.edu/) or find the MyCPP link CPP's homepage..

## Bronco One Card

Your [Bronco One Card](https://broncoonecard.com/bronco-one-card-graduate-credential-students/) is your official CPP Photo ID. It allows you to access CPP buildings and campus services, pay for food on campus, or make purchases at the Bronco Bookstore. Once you request, you can pick up your Bronco One Card at the Bronco One Card office located inside Bronco Bookstore (upstairs).  There is a $7.00 fee for your bronco access card.

## Registration

New graduate students can register for classes using [BroncoDirect](https://www.cpp.edu/~broncodirect/), Cal Poly Pomona's online registration system. Log in using your BroncoName and password. Once you are logged in, use the menu to locate the services you are seeking.

# Steps to Become a Registered Dietitian

## How to Become a Registered Dietitian (RD)

There are multiple paths to become a registered dietitian/nutritionist (RD/RDN). The steps may include:

1. Complete a bachelor’s degree and receive a verification statement from a Didactic Program in Dietetics (DPD) program accredited by the Accreditation Council for Education in Nutrition and Dietetics (ACEND)
2. Complete 1,000 hours of supervised practice in an ACEND-accredited program, such as an internship, or coordinated program
3. Complete a master's degree program
4. Pass the registration exam from the Commission on Dietetic Registration
5. Gain licensure in your state of practice (note that California does not have licensure for RD/RDNs at this time)
6. Maintain continuing professional education

Cal Poly Pomona’s dietetic internship is part of Step 2. You must complete your DPD requirements before you start our internship; however, you can apply during your senior year, as long as you will graduate before August. In our program, you will complete the required 1,000 hours of supervised practice and meet all of the competencies required by ACEND to allow you to sit for the registration exam.  You must have proof of completion of a master's degree by the time you complete our internship.

For more information, you can refer to the [5 Steps to Become a Registered Dietitian Nutritionist](https://www.eatrightpro.org/about-us/become-an-rdn-or-dtr/high-school-students/5-steps-to-become-a-registered-dietitian-nutritionist) page in the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics website.

Effective January 1, 2024, the Commission on Dietetic Registration (CDR) requires a minimum of a master’s degree to be eligible to take the credentialing exam to become a registered dietitian nutritionist (RDN).  For more information about this requirement, visit [CDR's website](https://www.cdrnet.org/graduatedegree).

In addition, CDR requires that individuals complete coursework and supervised practice in program(s) accredited by the Accreditation Council for Education in Nutrition and Dietetics (ACEND). Graduates who successfully complete the ACEND-accredited dietetic internship program at Cal Poly Pomona and have a master's degree are eligible to apply to take the CDR credentialing exam to become an RDN.

Ignite your curiosity and explore the science of health and human movement! Dive into exciting discoveries and uncover new ways to improve how we move, live, and thrive.
Select the program option that best fits your interests and career goals: General Kinesiology or Physical Education Teacher Education.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Our Undergraduate programs bring Cal Poly Pomona’s inclusive polytechnic approach to life. This means hands-on learning, real-world projects, and collaborative experiences are built into every step of your education. In our Mind and Heart Lab, Motor Development Clinic, Motor Neuroscience Lab, and Human Performance Lab, you’ll actively apply what you learn while gaining the employable skills needed to solve real-world challenges or preparation for graduate school.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Sports Coach

Help athletes develop their skills, strategize for competitions, and encourage communication and teamwork.

### Personal Trainer

Develop and implement exercise plans for individuals with various goals and needs. Set objectives, assess clients’ physical condition and guide them to their desired outcome.

### Strength and Conditioning Trainer

Create and direct exercise plans to improve the performance of competitive athletes and athletic teams.

### Exercise Physiologist

Help individuals improve their health through education and fitness and exercise programs. Assess patients’ health conditions and develop plans to meet their unique needs.

### Wellness Coach

Assist others in achieving their health and wellness goals. Provide education, guidance and support.

### Biomechanist

Study the mechanics of how humans move and respond to forces, then apply principles and best practices to fields including sports medicine, exercise science or ergonomics.

### Graduate and Professional School

Prepare for programs including: Public Health Specialist (MPH, DrPH), Physical Education Teacher Credential, Adapted Physical Education Teacher Credential, Physical Therapy (DPT), Occupational Therapy (MOT, DOT), Athletic Training (MSAT), Physicians Assistant (PA), Registered Nurse (RN), Medical Doctor (MD), Doctor of Osteopathy (DO), Doctor of Philosophy, Doctor of Education (Ph.D, Ed.D), Clinical Exercise Physiologist.

## Program Emphasis

Previous
Next

### General Kinesiology

Offers a multidisciplinary approach to the study of the complex interactions among physiological, biomechanical, psychological, sociological, behavioral and developmental aspects of human movement in health and disease. Includes additional emphasis choices in Exercise Science, Public Health and Sport and Social Justice.

### Physical Education Teacher Education Program

Prepares students to enter the single-subject credential program to work in the physical education field, teaching preK-12 students. Students are prepared to earn an added authorization in adapted physical education.

## Courses

We offer an undergraduate curriculum divided into two options: Physical Education Teacher Education and General. The General option has exercise science, public health, and sociocultural emphasis areas. These two subplans are designed to meet various student needs and interests.
Most who choose the physical education teacher education subplan will obtain a California teaching credential which qualifies them to teach physical education in the state’s public and private schools at the K-12 level.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

## Learn By Doing

### Motor Development Clinic

Learn by doing is embedded in programs across campus, and the kinesiology department is no exception with the Motor Development Clinic.
Since 1979, the clinic, located in Kellogg Gym, has been dedicated to improving children's motor skills and helping them fine-tune their cooperation, attention, and social skills. In 2023, the clinic started incorporating a new fitness-based program for adults with disabilities.

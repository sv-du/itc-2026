# Schedule of Events

## Fall Conference Week 2025

Fall Conference kicks off Monday, Aug. 18 — and we’re excited to welcome you back! The week begins with a virtual video welcome from Interim President Iris S. Levine, followed presentations, workshops, meetings, and other events and celebrations for faculty and staff. It’s a time to reconnect, recharge and launch the new academic year together. More information will be added in the coming weeks.

### Monday, August 18

**Welcome Video by Interim President Iris S. Levine**8 a.m. | Virtual Event: [Watch President's Welcome](https://streaming.cpp.edu/media/t/1_7qzudxhi)

**Years of Service Ceremony**
10 to 11:30 a.m. | Ursa Major, BSC (in person) and on [Zoom](https://cpp.zoom.us/j/82773611879?pwd=GuPjvLUMH91abM94yFe5CGWA7lWotP.1)

**Paletas with the Provost**
12:30 to 1 p.m. | Ursa Major, BSC

**Academic Affairs Forum**
1 to 2:30 p.m. | Ursa Major, BSC

**Faculty and Staff Workshops**
3 to 4:30 p.m. | BSC

* **AI@CPP – Empowering Faculty and Staff with AI Tools**Location: Ursa Major AB
  Presenters: Laura Massa and Alex Harwood
  Artificial Intelligence is transforming the way we work, teach, and learn—and Cal Poly Pomona is preparing for that future. This session introduces faculty and staff to the university’s current AI tools, ethical guidelines for responsible use, and learn about professional development opportunities. Through interactive activity and collaboration, participants will explore practical ways to use AI in daily academic and administrative tasks. We’ll also preview what's on the AI horizon and invite your input on how CPP can continue to grow as an AI-powered institution.
* **Principles of Community in Action**Location: Ursa Major C
  Presenters: Mark Patterson, Johnathan Grady and Jesus Avalos
  We’ll explore how Cal Poly Pomona’s Principles of Community guide our work and relationships — and how the Ombuds Office supports us in living those values, especially in moments of challenge. Through real stories, small group dialogue, and scenario-based practice, you’ll leave with greater clarity, connection, and confidence in applying our shared commitments. This interactive workshop is co-facilitated by Strategic Learning and the University Ombuds.
* **Bridging Pathways: Building a Career-Ready Ecosystem**Location: Ursa Minor
  Presenters: Larisa Preiser-Houy and Olukemi SawyerrPlease join us in an interactive workshop on CPP’s career readiness and workforce engagement ecosystems. You will participate with other attendees in small group sessions facilitated by team members from the Office of Academic Innovation (OAI) and the College of Professional and Global Education (CPGE). The sessions will focus on the newly developed Career Success Markers and Workforce Innovation and Career Engagement (WICE). Come learn how CPP is building a career-ready ecosystem and the various tools and resources available to you to support the career readiness and engagement of our students and their successful transition into meaningful post-graduation experiences.
* **Stronger Together: Advancing CSU through the Multi-University Collaboration**
  Location: Andromeda
  Presenters: Benny Okiyama and Jon Saclolo
  Explore how the CSU system is uniting all campuses to streamline operations, reduce costs, and enhance service delivery. Discover how collaborative procurement efforts are transforming purchasing processes and increasing efficiency at CPP and across the system.
* **Advancement 101: Building an Authentic Philanthropic Culture with Faculty/ Campus Partners**Location: Lyra
  Presenter: University Advancement
  This session offers a faculty-centered introduction to University Advancement — what it is, how it shows up at Cal Poly Pomona and why it matters. Together, we’ll explore our institutional priorities and how faculty can help shape a culture of giving rooted in authenticity, partnership and shared purpose.

## About Me

Annette Koh's research and practice is focused on placemaking and the politics of public participation.
She/her/hers pronouns.

*Courses taught:*
Planning for Community Change (Fall 2019)
Neighborhood Development (Fall 2019)
Social Context for Urban Change (Spring 2019)
Urban Housing and Community Development (Spring 2019)
Community Planning Studio (URP 4310/4320)

The 2019-20 Community Planning Studio will create planning education workshops and educational materials to increase community engagement in the [East San Gabriel Valley Area Plan](https://planning.lacounty.gov/site/esgvap/ "East San Gabriel Valley Area Plan (opens in new window)"), in partnership with Los Angeles County's Department of Regional Planning.

The 2018-2019 Community Planning Studio students developed a Planning 101 module for geography students at Workman High School in the City of Industry that included a pre-activity on the area's historical context, short videos on key concepts, and other student-centered materials. Students also provided interview and research support for the [People's Map of East San Gabriel Valley](https://planning.lacounty.gov/site/apeoplesmap/ "People's Map of East San Gabriel Valley (opens in new window)") project, and designed other methods of public engagement in planning.

# Finish in Four: 4-Year Graduation Pledge Program

## About the 4-Year Program

![two students at Commencement put up peace signs](img/4-year.jpg)

This 4-year program enables the California State University, including Cal Poly Pomona, to establish to establish pledge programs for entering first-time students who are both interested and able to complete a bachelor's degree in four years. This legislatively-mandated program is limited to students who are California residents and meet specific criteria.

## Eligibility

You must be a California resident and a first-year student entering fall term. You must also meet one of the following eligibility requirements:

* Be a first-generation student
* Was eligible for the [Cal State Apply application waiver](https://www.calstate.edu/apply/paying-for-college/pages/fee-waiver.aspx) when you applied to Cal Poly Pomona
* [Be from a high school](https://www.cde.ca.gov/ds/ad/filessp.asp) located in a community with a population of students that receive free and reduced lunch at a rate of 66% and higher

## Steps to Apply

### Monitor Your Email and BroncoDirect To Do List

If you are eligible for the 4-year, you will receive an invitation to apply. This will be your only chance during your time at Cal Poly Pomona to apply.

You will receive a confirmation email in your CPP inbox when we receive your application. If you're accepted, you'll receive a second email admitting you into the program.

If you're not eligible for the program or miss the application period, don't worry! You will still have access to your assigned academic advisor, and you can still graduate within two years by planning for 30 semester units per year. Visit the Take 30 website to learn more about the benefits.

### Apply for the Program by the Deadline

Apply by **August 18, 2025**.

### Enroll in 15 Units Your First Term

You will register for classes at your mandatory Orientation program. You will also be able to adjust your schedule during your Orientation course registration window and Add/Drop Period.

### Update Your Academic Planner With Your 2025-26 Courses

Use [Academic Planner](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/advising/planner.shtml) to map out your Fall 2025 and Spring 2026 courses.

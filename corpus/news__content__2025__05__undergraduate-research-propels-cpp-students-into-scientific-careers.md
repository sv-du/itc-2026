# Undergraduate Research Propels CPP Students into Scientific Careers

* [College of Science](/news/all-news.shtml?category=6)
* [Research & Innovation](/news/all-news.shtml?category=17)
* [PolyX](/news/all-news.shtml?category=$cat13ndex)

May 27, 2025 | [andrew soliman](/news/all-news.shtml?author=Andrew Soliman)

## Share

![Marissa Postlethwaite](../../../../img/news/2025/05/24science-lab-postlewaite3.jpg)

At Cal Poly Pomona, undergraduate research is more than a résumé booster. It’s a hands-on, career-shaping experience that helps students turn classroom knowledge into real-world skills and lay a foundation for their future.

Students from across different majors, ranging from biology to chemistry to engineering and more, engage in research that strengthens their academic foundations, refines their technical skills, and equips them with experience that can inform their career path.

From researching breast cancer to developing acne treatment medication, students are making tangible contributions to their fields while gaining valuable insights into the world of scientific research.

Marissa Postlethwaite, a [biotechnology](https://www.cpp.edu/programs/sci/biological-sciences/biotechnology.shtml) student involved in breast cancer research, highlighted the professional growth benefits of this hands-on research experience.

"This research opportunity has confirmed my desire to continue working in research,” she said. "Work in this lab has given me a great appreciation for what people do in larger institutes such as the City of Hope and the National Institute of Health."

Niko Lau, a biotechnology student researching acne treatment medication, emphasized the practical benefits and the hands-on opportunity to refine his laboratory skills and techniques.

Similarly, Kristin Lam, a Biotechnology student conducting breast cancer research, noted that the experience, "has benefited me academically due to my prior knowledge here in the lab that I could apply in my classes."

These experiences also help students see themselves as part of a larger scientific community, one that values curiosity, creativity, and real-world problem-solving. Whether it’s through improving public health, contributing to medical advancements, or building a stronger foundation for future innovations, undergraduate research allows students to make their mark.

As part of Cal Poly Pomona’s commitment to hands-on learning, undergraduate research opportunities continue to be a powerful tool for student success, academically, professionally, and personally.

## About the Program

Discover the ancient world through archaeology, the study of human history and cultures through material remains. This option provides a strong understanding of the techniques used to uncover and interpret the physical evidence of past cultures and the research and critical thinking skills needed to shape the future. It prepares you for both professional and academic opportunities in archaeology.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Excavate an actual archaeological site, process and analyze artifacts, and learn the importance of cultural resource management. You’ll engage in fieldwork, research and interpretation of historic cultural materials. This option offers the hands-on experience necessary to prepare you for your career in archaeology.

## Courses

Our Archaeology option offers specialized training in the study of past human societies through their material remains. Building on a common core that includes all major subfields — Sociocultural Anthropology, Biological Anthropology, Archaeology, and Linguistics — this option emphasizes advanced coursework in archaeological methods, theory and fieldwork. It is ideal for students interested in careers in cultural resource management, museum curation or historic preservation, as well as those planning to pursue graduate studies in archaeology.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

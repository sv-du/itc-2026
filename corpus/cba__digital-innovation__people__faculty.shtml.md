## Dr. Indira Guzman

PhD – Director of the Mitchell Hill Center for Digital Innovation
Read more

### More about Dr. Indira Guzman

×

Indira R. Guzman is an Assistant Professor in the Cybersecurity track in the Computer Information Systems department and the new Director of the Mitch Hill Center for Digital Innovation. She received her Ph.D. in Information Science and Technology from Syracuse University as a Fulbright Scholar. She has over 20 years of combined industry, academic, and research experience in information systems. Her research focuses on the impact of digital technologies on organizations and the society. She has conducted research about the occupational culture of IT professionals, diversity equity and inclusion in information systems, behavioral information security, and information security policy compliance. Her work has been cited over 1,000 times, and she has mentored over 50 doctoral students. Currently she is as a research consultant for the ELLAS (Equality in Leadership for Latin American STEM) project funded by the Canadian organization IDRC Grant ID# 109798, elected 2022-2024 President of the Latin American and Caribbean Chapter of the Association of Information Systems (LACAIS), Gold Level ISACA Member, and founder faculty advisor for the Women in Tech (WIT) student club at Cal Poly Pomona.

## Dr. Ron Pike

PhD - Professor Emeritus - Past Director of the Mitchell Hill Center for Digital Innovation.
Read more

### More about Dr. Ron Pike

×

Dr. Ron Pike has worked with students to develop a Student-run Data Center and a Security Operations Center devoted to developing and operating a modern cloud-computing environment to serve education and research. After spending over two decades in cyber education, including the past 12 years of distinguished service to the California State Polytechnic University, Pomona CIS Department within the College of Business Administration, Professor Emeritus Ron Pike is retiring as an educator. The impact he leaves behind at Cal Poly Pomona is almost immeasurable, and his legacy will continue to be felt for many years, due to the dedication, resilience, opportunities and lifelong learning approach he bestowed upon the student culture of Cal Poly Pomona. He has published research on cybersecurity and the cloud computing use as an empowering infrastructure in education. See more information at [Click here](https://www.cpp.edu/cba/news-events/news/articles/dr-ron-pike-retirement.shtml)

## Dr. Dan Manson

PhD Professor Emeritus, CIS Department. Cyber Programs Coordinator
Read more

### More about Dr. Dan Manson

×

Dr. Manson led the effort for Cal Poly Pomona to be designated a National Center of Academic Excellence in Information Assurance Education.Dr. Manson has coordinated the Western Regional Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition and California Cyber Challenge as part of the United States Cyber Challenge. Dan has been co-Principal Investigator on three National Science Foundation grants to support workforce, curriculum and professional development in cyber security, including the current CyberWatch West NSF ATE Regional Center grant. Dan serves on the Academic Relations Committee for the Los Angeles Chapter of the Information Systems Audit and Control Association and is a past president of the Southern California High Technology Crime Investigation Association.

## Dr. Laura A Pohopien

PhD - Avanade Fellow
Read more

### More about Dr. Laura A Pohopien

×

Dr. Laura Pohopien worked with Heba Ramzy, Avanade Global Citizenship Lead, to create Fuel- Technology, Leadership and Innovation conference in 2019. The first conference was hosted by Cal Poly Pomona. Since then, the conference has moved to a virtual platform where university scholars, interns, and high school students from across the globe gather for 1 week to learn, develop personally and professionally, and engage in an epic STEM-based hackathon. This year is Fuel’s 6th conference. The theme: “Imagine what you will do”

Additionally, Dr. Pohopien helped bring to Cal Poly an Avanade-sponsored university-wide AI Hackathon. The inaugural hackathon was great fun for all student participants and plans are in place to continue offering more events like this.

## Bill Luong

System Administrator
Read more

### More about Bill Luong

×

Bill Luong received his B.S. in Business Administration - Computer Information System from Cal Poly Pomona. He works full-time as the System Administrator for the Department of Advanced Computing at Cal Poly Pomona. He is currently a 1st year graduate student in the Masters of Information Security program at Cal Poly Pomona. His main focus is in Linux System Administration. Bill assists the Mitchell C. Hill Student-Run Data Center (SDC) and Student-Run Security Operation Center (SOC) by over seeing and implementing projects. He focuses on managing the virtualization cluster, network infrastructure, and stand-alone servers with the Student Managers.

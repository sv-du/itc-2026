## About the Program

The Biotechnology major is an interdisciplinary program which provides students with a strong background in both biology and chemistry. It provides the theoretical and practical knowledge needed to understand the numerous industrial applications of biological phenomena, while emphasizing the study of cell and molecular biology.
Choose from emphasis in: cellular, molecular, and microbial biology; physiology; biochemistry and molecular separation techniques; food, agriculture, and environment; and policy and business.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Health Professional

Prepare for entry into professional programs such as Medical Doctor, Pharmacist, Physician Assistant, Physical Therapist etc.

### Clinical Laboratory Scientist

Prepare for entry into training programs for testing microbiology, blood bank, hematology, or chemistry in a clinical laboratory.

### Laboratory Technician

Support scientific research and experiments in a microbiology or biochemistry laboratory setting.

### Microbiologist

Research microbial diseases, develop vaccines and pharmaceuticals, check soil and water health, and ensure food safety.

### Biochemist

Research the chemical processes that take place within living organisms.

## Program Emphasis

Previous
Next

### Cellular, Molecular and Microbial Biology

Study applications of molecular biology and microbiology in sectors including food production, technology, and medicine.

### Physiology

Study living organisms at different levels of organization from cells to tissues and organ systems.

### Biochemistry and Molecular Separation Techniques

Study various methods to help you analyze and understand life on the molecular and atomic level.

### Food, Agriculture and Environment

Engage with the practical applications of biology in the critical industries of food, agriculture and environmental protection.

### Policy and Business

Explore the legal and regulatory considerations of biotechnology.

## Courses

The Biotechnology major is an interdisciplinary program that provides you with a strong background in both biology and chemistry and includes an internship in a biotechnology laboratory for practical experience in the field. You can tailor your education through our five emphases: (1) Cellular, Molecular, and Microbial Biology; (2) Physiology; (3) Biochemistry and Molecular Separation Techniques; (4) Food, Agriculture, and Environment; and (5) Policy and Business.
This program also satisfies the admission requirements for various graduate and professional schools.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

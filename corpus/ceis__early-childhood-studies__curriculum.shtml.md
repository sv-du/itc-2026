# Curriculum

The Early Childhood Studies (ECS) Department offers 6 options that advocate for understanding diversity and inclusivity. The faculty use service learning, internships, face-to-face, hybrid, and online pedagogy in order to provide the knowledge, skills, and dispositions to work with children and families during the most critical developmental time in a child's life.

## Practicum Requirement

### Course Information

All students are required to complete a practicum course (ECS 2100/A) that introduces students to working with young children and families in an early learning and care (ELC) setting. In this course students will be placed in an approved ELC settings in which 60 hours will be completed with children and families under the supervision of a mentor teacher and faculty member.

**Practicum Locations**

* Baldwin Park Unified School District
* Pomona School District
* Long Beach School District
* Mulberry Tree Preschool in Whittier

**Information on Practicum**

* This orientation is required for all students who plan to take ECS 2100/2100A or ECS 4100/4100A.
* The due dates for practicum application will remain the same each year:
  + For placement in the Spring semester, the applications are due September 1.
  + For placement in the Fall or Summer semesters, the applications are due February 1.
* Completing this Orientation is only the first step to a complete practicum application. If you complete this orientation on the Application due date, you WILL NOT make the deadline. It is suggested students complete this Orientation at least 10 days before the application deadline. Click here to view the application process.
* Requests to Change a Previously Submitted Application
  + If you already applied and need to request a change to the start term or to withdraw your application completely, please click on the relevant link below. You should receive a response from the Credential Services Office within 24-48 hours.
* [Practicum Change of Term Request](https://engage.cpp.edu/register/ECS-practicum-trm-change-request)
* [Practicum Withdrawal Request](https://engage.cpp.edu/register/ecs-practicum-withdrawal-form)

## Program Options

### Early Childhood Teaching

**Description:** This option provides students with the skills, knowledge, dispositions and experience to work with children ages birth to eight. The culminating course requires students to complete 60 hours in an ELC setting and apply what they have been learning in all of their courses as they interact with children and families under the supervision of a mentor teacher and faculty member.

**Highlights:** Practicum experience in a classroom setting for children ages birth to twelve.

**Career Opportunites:** Teacher for children ages birth to eight

**Sub Core Courses:** ECS 3200, ECS 3250, ECS 3320, ECS 3750, ECS 3600, ECS 3700, ECS 3900, ECS 4200, ECS 4100/A

### Non-Teaching Option

**Description:** Provides students with the competencies to work with young children and families in diverse contexts based on their career goals and interest (i.e., social work, child life, school psychology, counseling etc.). This pathway will provide students with the ability to choose courses within our department to strengthen their desired career path. A capstone course will be completed in which students apply what they are learning via an internship or project.

**Highlights:** Capstone Course: Final Project that includes field work.

Career Opportunities: Advocacy for Children and Families, Foster Care, Child Protective Services, Juvenile Probation.

Graduate/Additional Postbaccalaureate Education: Social Worker, School Psychologist, Child Life Specialist, Behavioral Specialist, Speech Therapy, Occupational Therapy, Physical Therapy, Family Law

**Sub Core Courses:** EWS 2900, EWS 3300, EWS 3600, EMCS 4950

### Multilingual Teaching

**Description:** Provides students with the opportunity to become multilingual educators for young children. California is a culturally and linguistically diverse state with a growing desire to have a strong multilingual early learning and care workforce. Students will be expected to specialize in a language (such as Spanish) so that they can become dual language educators (courses in the English and Modern Languages department and Ethnic and Women Studies Department will be taken to strengthen students language and cultural understanding).  The culminating course requires students to complete 60 hours in a dual immersion early learning and care setting and apply what they have been learning in all of their courses as they interact with children and families under the supervision of a mentor teacher and faculty member. The ECS department is one of the very few programs in Higher Education to offer this unique preparation that is responsive to the growing need for multilingual teacher experts.

**Highlights:**Practicum experience in a dual immersion or multilingual classroom for children ages birth to twelve.

**Career Opportunities:** Teacher for children ages birth to eight

**Sub Core Courses:** ECS 3250, ECS 3320, ECS 3600, ECS 3700, ECS 4200, ECS 3750, ECS 4100/A

### P-3rd Grade Teaching

**Description:** This is specifically for **ADT elementary teacher education transfers.**  This option provides transfer students to obtain the competencies to become transitional kindergarten (TK) teachers. Students receive a combination of teacher preparation (e.g., ADT in elementary teacher education) and early childhood development coursework.  The culminating course requires students to complete 60 hours in a transitional kindergarten classroom where they can apply what they have been learning in all of their courses as they interact with children and families under the supervision of a mentor teacher and faculty member.

**Highlights:** Practicum experience in a classroom setting for ages 4-12. These teachers receive a combination of teacher preparation (e.g., ADT in elementary teacher education) and early childhood development coursework.

**Career Opportunities:** TK- 5th grade Elementary Teacher

**Sub Core Courses:** ECS 3200, ECS 3600, ECS 3800, ECS 4100/A, ECS 4200

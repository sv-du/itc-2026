# Change of Major Guide

## Step 5: Being in Your New Major

When your major and catalog year have been updated on your academic record, you’ll notice it in a few places:

* At the top of your [Degree Progress Report](https://my.cpp.edu/), and in the Major Requirements section of your DPR
* In your unofficial transcript, beginning in the term your change of major was approved
* In your My Academics tab in your [Student Center](https://my.cpp.edu/)

You will also begin to receive department-specific communication, and new college-specific emails if you switch colleges. Here are some things to keep in mind with your new major:

## Know where to find your new curriculum sheet.

Find it on the [curriculum sheet website](https://www.cpp.edu/academic-programs/academic-advising/tools/sheets-roadmaps/index.shtml). Remember to look for the year you switched majors, not the year you came to Cal Poly Pomona.

## Know who your new advisor is.

Your assigned advisor will change when your major officially changes.

## Know about any specific department requirements.

For example, your new department might require permission numbers for specific courses. Ask your academic advisor if you have any questions.

## Get involved in new clubs and organizations within your major.

Your classmates might know of opportunities, but you can always check [MyBAR](https://mybar.cpp.edu/) for an updated list of chartered clubs.

## Start thinking about your future career path.

This might include graduate school, internships and research opportunities. Check out the [Career Center](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/?cppshorturl=career) resources, and look on [Handshake](https://cpp.joinhandshake.com/) for part-time jobs. The Career Center and your faculty advisor may have some advice on how to build your resume.

## Continue to use your Student Success Central planning tools.

You should continue to take advantage of all of the tools in your [planning toolbox](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/advising/index.shtml).

## Consider taking summer courses to catch up.

Your new department’s [summer offerings](https://www.cpp.edu/summer/) might help you complete courses faster.

## If you’ve taken a course that isn’t in your new curriculum, ask your advisor about how it goes toward your degree.

It may count for one of your requirements or go towards unrestricted electives.

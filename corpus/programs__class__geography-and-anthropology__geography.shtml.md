## About the Program

Experience a comprehensive field of study that examines the study of places and the relationships between people and their environments. You will explore crucial contemporary issues like climate change, urbanization, globalization and environmental sustainability, while pursuing with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills. Our program offers two subplans: Geospatial Analysis and Environmental Studies. Both subplans provide extensive geographical understanding with distinct technological and environmental perspectives.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Conduct in-depth geospatial analysis in the Geographic Information System lab or head into local habitats and track human impact through field mapping, systematic sampling and preparing environmental impact reports. With a combination of lectures and hands-on experience, you will receive a robust background in geography for graduate training or careers requiring an understanding of peoples, groups, and their cultural and regional institutions.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 4

### Urban Planner

Develop cities, towns and communities by creating comprehensive plans that balance population growth, housing, transportation and environmental sustainability. Collaborate with stakeholders to make strategic decisions about land use, zoning, infrastructure development and public spaces while ensuring compliance with regulations and promoting the community's social, economic and environmental well-being.

Card 2 of 4

### Geographic Information System (GIS) Specialist

Create, maintain and analyze digital maps and spatial data using specialized software to help organizations make location-based decisions. Gather and integrate geographic data, create detailed maps and visualizations, and provide insights for applications like urban planning, environmental management, or business analytics.

Card 3 of 4

### Environmental Consultant

Provide expert guidance to organizations, governments, and businesses on environmental issues, regulations, and sustainable practices. Assess environmental impacts, conduct site investigations, prepare environmental reports, ensure compliance with regulations, develop sustainability strategies, and recommend solutions for minimizing environmental damage while helping clients balance environmental responsibilities with business objectives.

Card 4 of 4

### Sustainability Specialist

Develop and implement environmental initiatives and strategies to help organizations reduce their environmental impact while maintaining business efficiency. Conduct environmental assessments, create sustainability programs, ensure regulatory compliance, track environmental metrics, and collaborate with various departments to integrate sustainable practices throughout the organization.

## Program Emphasis

Card 1 of 3

### Environmental Studies

Focuses on the interaction between human activities and the natural environment, emphasizing interdisciplinary approach to environmental systems, sustainability and human-environment interactions.

Card 2 of 3

### Geospatial Analysis

Focuses on advanced spatial data analysis and technology, emphasizing Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and Remote Sensing, with strong use of computer technology and data visualization. This track prepares you for careers in spatial data systems and land-use management, equipping them for roles in government agencies and non-profit organizations.

## Courses

The Geography program is designed to provide an understanding of humankind’s cultural and physical environments by examining the dynamic systems through which these diverse settings are changed or sustained. Attention is given to the relationships between population use of resources and environmental and social impacts in rural and urban settings.
You may choose any of three subplans: Geographical Studies, Environmental Studies, and Geospatial Analysis.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

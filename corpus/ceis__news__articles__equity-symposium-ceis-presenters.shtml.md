April 24, 2024

Student Success Center and Retention Coordinator, Berlinda Crawford, and Dr. Eddie Partida, Education Assistant Professor presented at the Equity in Practice Symposium held at Kellogg West on April 19, 202

The [Center for the Advancement for Faculty Excellence (CAFE)](https://www.cpp.edu/cafe/index.shtml), the  [Office of Student Success, Equity and Innovation (OSS)](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/oss/), and [Institutional Research, Planning, and Analytics (IRPA)](https://www.cpp.edu/data/) invite the campus community to join us at the Equity in Practice Symposium. We will celebrate CPP's progress towards student success, share effective practices, and spread the word about campus resources. We’ll also present the [Wall of COOL 2024](https://www.cpp.edu/cafe/cool/index.shtml) awards! (Source: EIP Website)

---

**Berlinda Crawford** presented with a colleague during the *Wall of Cool*. Where presenters shared a poster with their research. *Their presentation was titled: Supporting Student Success Through Inclusive Language in CPP's Academic Policy.*

"In fall 2023, a team of CPP Retention & Graduation Specialists (RGS), including CEIS’ Berlinda Crawford, surveyed CPP students who were currently in the Academic Probation process to better understand their perspective and preference on the “Academic Probation” terminology. Of the students who responded to the survey, 70% preferred a term other than probation. When asked about what feelings students associated with the current terminology, “probation”, 76% of students associated it with anxiety, 70% with shame, and 52% with sadness. As a result of the data collected, the team of RGS’ have submitted an Academic Senate referral to change the terminology of “Academic Probation” to “Academic Notice”, a term that fosters a more supportive and inclusive environment for students facing academic challenges. This change aims to better reflect the educational context and convey a more supportive and proactive approach to addressing academic challenges." Says Berlinda.

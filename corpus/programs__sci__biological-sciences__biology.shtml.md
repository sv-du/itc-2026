## About the Program

The Biology program provides students with a broad view of biological processes, a strong theoretical foundation paired with extensive hands-on experiences in diverse labs.
Choose from emphases in Botany, Cell and Molecular Biology, Microbiology, Neuroscience and Physiology, or Zoology.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Our curriculum emphasizes hands-on laboratory experience to prepare students for biology careers. Students have the opportunity to conduct research in a lab or participate in internships. Examples of research areas include cancer biology, genetics, marine biology, and plant and wildlife conservation.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Health Professional

Prepare for entry into professional programs such as Medical Doctor, Pharmacist, Physician Assistant, Physical Therapist etc.

### Clinical Laboratory Scientist

Prepare for entry into training programs for testing microbiology, blood bank, hematology, or chemistry in a clinical laboratory.

### Plant/Wildlife Biologist

Study the biology of plants and animals, respectively, and work to conserve their habitats and populations. Educate the public about biological conservation.

### Biotechnology Technician

Support scientific research and experiments in a laboratory setting.

### High School Teacher

Prepare for entry into a teaching credential program. Introduce students to the fundamentals of biology. Create lesson plans, present lectures, design lab experiments and encourage an appreciation of the natural world.

## Program Emphasis

Previous
Next

### Botany

Study plant structure, development and function, and achieve specialized knowledge and skills related to plant genetics, ecology, and evolution.

### Cell and Molecular Biology

Focus on life at the cellular level including topics ranging from the flow of genetic information and patterns of inheritance to cellular processes and molecular interactions.

### Microbiology

Specialize in medical or environmental microbiology including immunology, virology, bacteriology, or mycology.

### Neuroscience and Physiology

Study living organisms at different levels of organization from cells to tissues and organ systems.

### Zoology

Gain an understanding of animal evolution, ecology, physiology and development through a combination of lectures and hands-on laboratory and field exercises.

## Community Education

### Rain Bird BioTrek

Rain Bird BioTrek is an educational experience for visitors of all ages. Its greenhouse, gardens and stations provide interactive educational connections to the tropical rainforest, California's indigenous plants and people, and other ancient and present-day habitats. Biology students can get involved in plant and animal care as well as leading field trips for K-12 students.

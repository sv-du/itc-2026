## About the option

Cal Poly Pomona’s College of Business Administration offers a dynamic academic option for aspiring professionals in finance, real estate, and business law. Rooted in Cal Poly Pomona's polytechnic tradition, the curriculum blends theory with practical application, empowering students to become people-oriented leaders in the business world.
Students can tailor their education by choosing an emphasis in finance, real estate, or business law and contract management, each designed to align with specific career goals. Whether mastering financial analysis, preparing for the CFA® designation, meeting broker equivalency requirements in real estate, or diving into the intricacies of business law, FRL students gain the skills and credentials to thrive in competitive industries.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Contract Administrator

A Contract Administrator manages and oversees the creation, negotiation, execution, and compliance of contracts, ensuring legal and financial terms are met in real estate, finance, and legal agreements.

### Financial Analyst

A Financial Analyst evaluates financial data, prepares reports, and provides insights to help organizations make informed decisions regarding investments, budgeting, and financial planning.

### Financial Planner

A Financial Planner helps individuals or businesses develop strategies to manage their finances, including investments, savings, retirement, and tax planning, to achieve long-term financial goals.

### Investment Banker

An Investment Banker advises clients on financial matters, such as mergers, acquisitions, and raising capital, and facilitates complex transactions to help companies grow and navigate financial markets.

### Portfolio Manager

A Portfolio Manager is responsible for making investment decisions and managing a portfolio of assets to achieve clients' financial goals while balancing risk and return.

### Hedge Fund Manager

A Hedge Fund Manager oversees a hedge fund’s investment strategy, making high-level decisions on asset allocation, risk management, and maximizing returns for investors.

### Credit Analyst

A Credit Analyst assesses the creditworthiness of individuals or businesses by analyzing financial data, credit reports, and market trends to determine the risk of lending or extending credit.

### Insurance Underwriter

An Insurance Underwriter evaluates insurance applications, assesses risks, and determines policy terms, premiums, and coverage based on the applicant’s risk profile and relevant data.

### Legal Auditor

A Legal Auditor reviews and examines legal documents, practices, and procedures to ensure compliance with laws, regulations, and internal policies, identifying potential legal risks or issues.

### Real Estate Broker / Investor

A Real Estate Broker facilitates property transactions between buyers and sellers, while a Real Estate Investor purchases, owns, and manages properties for financial gain, often through appreciation or rental income.

### Stockbroker

A Stockbroker buys and sells stocks, bonds, and other securities on behalf of clients, providing investment advice and managing transactions in financial markets.

## About the Program

Become an advocate for all children as you provide them with the tools and support they need to thrive and foster inclusive environments where every child can succeed.
This intensive program allows you to receive a Bachelor of Arts in Early Childhood Studies and a credential in Special Education in four years. This option is for those interested in working with special education children in extensive support classrooms.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Study a broad foundation of principles and practices in special education and learn how to support children with diverse learning needs. Explore methods to create a nurturing and adaptable learning environment. Then, participate in clinical practice in a public school and gain firsthand experience developing individualized learning plans for real students. Upon graduation, you will be ready to take on your dream career in extensive support special education.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Special Education Teacher

Work with students who have diverse learning, emotional, physical and intellectual support needs. Adapt lessons, teach life and social skills, collaborate with support staff and families and develop individualized education programs (IEPs).

### School Counselor

Support students as they navigate personal and academic challenges. Provide counseling, academic and career guidance programs that promote a positive school culture and resources in times of crisis.

### Educational Consultant

Work with students, parents, schools and educational organizations to improve educational experiences and outcomes through advising, research, analysis, development of new policies and curriculum, training, and more.

### Occupational Therapist

Help people with injuries, illnesses or disabilities develop, recover or maintain the skills they need to perform daily activities and work-related tasks to live independently. Assess clients’ challenges, create personalized treatment plans, develop adaptive strategies, teach skills and more.

## Courses

The Integrated Teacher Education Program: Extensive Support program provides you with the opportunity to get your Bachelor of Arts in Early Childhood Studies and credential in Special Education in four years through rigorous coursework and clinical practice. This option is for students interested in working with special education children in extensive support classrooms.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.

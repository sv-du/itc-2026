## About the Program

Become an innovator in a necessity that all humans rely on – food! Apply chemistry, microbiology, statistics, engineering and processing technology to develop healthy foods that taste good, process better, ship well and last longer. Food science and technology allows you to get creative and be analytical. Food scientists are in high demand with great earning potential. Our undergraduate program is the only program in Southern California approved by the Institute of Food Technologists (IFT) and the Research Chefs Association.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Shape the future of food. Learn about the industry and its emerging trends: plant-based foods, sustainable sourcing, and the creation of healthier snacks. Our welcoming, down-to-earth faculty puts a twist on ‘scientist’; they’ll guide you through everything from intercepting harmful agents in food to exploring biotechnical innovations. Students develop and produce products that are sold at the on-campus Farm Store. With real-world projects and a focus on practical skills, you’ll be ready to make an impact in this growing industry.

## Career Outcomes

Previous
Next

### Food Scientist /Product Development

Develop new food products – from formulation to packaging – and improve existing ones, ensuring they meet safety, quality and regulatory standards.

### Regulatory

Help the industry and government agencies ensure that the foods we eat meet all regulatory requirements that make it safe.

### Technical Sales

Work with raw ingredient and flavor suppliers to provide solutions to the food industry when developing new and innovative products.

### Food Microbiologist/Quality Assurance/Quality Control

Examine microorganisms in food to prevent contamination, spoilage and ensure food safety throughout production and storage.

### Sensory Analyst

Conduct sensory evaluations to assess the taste, texture and smell of food products.

## Program Emphasis

Previous
Next

### Culinology®

One of few programs approved by RCA in the U.S. This option blends food science and culinary arts and provides tools to successfully develop new foods for retail and food service consumption.

### General

This option allows students more flexibility in choosing elective courses that could meet the requirements of a pre-professional degree or several minors such as plant-based foods and nutrition, business, nutrition and more.

### Science and Technology

Approved by the Institute of Food Technologists (IFT), this option stresses learning scientific concepts with the application of theoretical principles.

## Courses

The Bachelor of Science degree in Food Science and Technology offers options in Culinology®, Science and Technology, and General.
Our undergraduate programs prepare you for challenging and rewarding careers in the professional fields of Food Science and Technology, with opportunities in the private sector as well as government agencies. Our programs also provide a strong academic background for graduate study and research.
The curriculum, facilities and faculty reflect our department’s commitment to a rigorous, current, science-based undergraduate program that provides the types of skills and knowledge you need to meet your professional goals.

Our undergraduate program is the only approved program in Southern California by the Institute of Food Technologists (IFT) and the Research Chefs Association (RCA).

## Polytechnic Experience

### Product Development Class Readies Students for Careers in Food Industry

The students sit in rows, hunched over their desks and focusing intently on what's in front of them.
They appear to be gearing up for a test, but this isn't your typical college exam. It's a taste test - or in the vernacular of the food science world, a sensory evaluation.
The testing takes place in the kitchen processing labs of Cal Poly Pomona's food product development class. The students tasting and assessing the foods come from across the campus and serve as guest consumers trying out the new products developed in the class.

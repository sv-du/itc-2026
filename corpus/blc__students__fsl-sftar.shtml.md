# Fraternity and Sorority Life

## Sorority and Fraternity Transparency Act Report (SFTAR)

Cal Poly Pomona prepares this information in compliance with the Campus-Recognized Sorority and Fraternity Transparency Act (AB524), California State Education Code, Chapter 5.2, of the Donahue Higher Education Act, Sections 66310-66312.

The SFTAR provides information regarding campus-recognized sorority and fraternity membership, grade point average, community service, fundraising, sanctioned events, current recognition status, and conduct status and data for each academic year beginning with 2022-2023. The report also includes a list of available mental health resources and the residence addresses of campus-recognized sorority and fraternity chapter houses (as defined). The information is prepared and posted online by October 1 of each calendar year.

The Sorority and Fraternity Transparency Act Report at Cal Poly Pomona utilizes the Score Card format for reporting. The Fraternity and Sorority Life Scorecard is a snapshot of each chapter in the governing councils: Interfraternity Council (IFC), Multicultural Greek Council (MGC), National Pan-Hellenic Council (NPHC) and Panhellenic Council (PHC).

The data is gathered from reporting by each chapter and council. If you have any questions or need help accessing this report, please email us at leadership@cpp.edu.

## Resources

### Care Center

The Care Center is the first place to send students who are of concern or in distress. We connect students to resources they need to succeed (both on and off campus), support them through crises, provide advocacy, and offer Basic Needs services.

Building 97, First Floor
(909) 869-5071
carecenter@cpp.edu
<https://www.cpp.edu/carecenter/>

### Bronco Wellness Center

Health Educators and Peer Health Educators provide Cal Poly Pomona students with health education to prevent the development of chronic conditions. Provides resources and programs dedicated to various health topics including but not limited to nutrition, sexual health, mental health, physical activity, sleep and stress management.

Building 97, First Floor

(909) 869-5272
wellnessctr@cpp.edu
[https://www.cpp.edu/health/bronco-wellness-services.shtml](https://www.cpp.edu/broncoscarecenter/wellness.shtml)

### Counseling & Psychological Services

**Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)**advances student success by promoting community wellness, removing psychological barriers, facilitating self-awareness, and cultivating the personal strengths of CPP students through culturally relevant mental health services designed to transform their personal, social, and academic lives.

Building 66, Suite 116
Main line: (909) 869-3220
Crisis counseling (24/7): (909) 869-3220, then press “2”
caps@cpp.edu<https://www.cpp.edu/caps/>

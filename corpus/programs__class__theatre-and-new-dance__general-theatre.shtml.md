## About the Program

Peel back the curtain and immerse yourself in all that a theatre degree has to offer. In the General Theatre option, you will experience our broad curriculum and have the ability to dive deeper into your area of interest, including directing, playwriting, management or theory and criticism. Tailor your education to your career goals and develop the skills to make an impact in the performing arts industry.

## The Polytechnic Approach

Perform on the main stage or fill leadership roles as a designer or producer. Experiment in workshops and projects alongside your peers. Here, you will receive intensive training in all facets of theatre: acting, voice, movement, directing, lighting, costume and scenic design, playwriting, stagecraft, makeup, and stage management. Join your intellectual and academic skills with artistic perspective and graduate ready to take on a career in the performing arts or continue to graduate school.

## Career Outcomes

Card 1 of 4

### Theatre Historian

Become a consultant for museums and archives with an expertise in theatrical artifacts.

Card 2 of 4

### Prop Master

Help create, manage, organize, maintain and catalog props for live performances, keeping track of individual items essential to performances, coming up with new ways to bring life and realism to stories and problem-solving for all things related to prop management.

Card 3 of 4

### Dramaturg

Support the development process of a theatrical production through deep research cultivating a fertile ground for creative discovery.

Card 4 of 4

### Artistic Director

Choose what shows will be premiered at a theatre, hire the essential talent who will host auditions and pick actors for the performances and become a consultant for all high-level creative choices.

## Courses

The General Theatre option allows you to explore a broad range of theatre disciplines and create a customized course of study. This flexible track allows you to focus on areas such as directing, playwriting, theatre management, or theatre theory and criticism. By offering a comprehensive education in both the practical and academic aspects of theatre, the program enables you to develop a well-rounded skill set that can be applied to various career paths. You will be prepared for advanced studies, teaching, or professional work in theatre, television, film, or related fields.

For the most up-to-date information on courses for this program, visit the course catalog or contact an advisor.
